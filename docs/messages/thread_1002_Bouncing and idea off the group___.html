Bouncing and idea off the group...

From: AC | Date: 2005-05-02 21:30:19 UTC

Hi All,

 

I’d like to bounce an idea off you all…

 

I have been approached by a local historical society who is planning their annual fundraiser home tour.  The proceeds of the tour help support the preservation efforts for a Newburgh , NY Revolutionary War era home.  This year they are interested in doing something a little different to entice “new blood” to visit the historic homes and have asked me to conduct a road rally style event.

 

Here are some of the details which are still in a VERY rough form.

 

Number of stops: 6 definitely, possibly 10

Event only stamps

You must attend the brief tour of the home to obtain access/directions to the box which will be on the property.

As it is a fundraiser, there will be a fee of approximately $25 which covers the tours for all the homes and the souvenir book.

Date: Sunday 12 June 2005

 

I have discussed with the person trying to organize this event my thoughts. One of the appeals of letterboxing it that it is a low cost/free hobby for the finders (I’ve tried to convince Lascivious Lion/Box Hound that it’s free, but he yain’t buyin’ it).  And that I wasn’t sure how well that would translate into an “event” for letterboxing.  While I love the idea, I think $25 would be prohibitive for many.  It does however; cover the whole family so that might be a bonus.

 

If you all have any suggestions or tweaks that you think might make this possibly happen, I’d love to hear your input.  Thanks for your time. 

 

Patiently awaiting your input,

 

Talking Turtle

From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2005-05-02 23:10:49 UTC
Allentown, PA has a historical home tour. People do pay some fee to
wander in and out of homes that the owners open up for touring and
viewing. The homeowners typically have some sort of finger food
available to the "guests". This may make the high cost more
palatable. Remember LBNA stands for Low Budget gNarly Activity...

I helped a friend out a couple of years on these and it was much
fun. He had me upstairs and I had a blast telling all sorts of half-
truths like the owner's couch was once owned by Billy Idol, that I
was the owner's chef, "gullible" is not in the dictionary and other
silly things....

From: The Doubtful Guests | Date: 2005-05-03 02:07:38 UTC
Random thoughts on your idea.
 
People like having a passport / visa / souvenir to collect along a tour.  A goal along the way.  (And don't we know it.)  And it is something fun for the kids to do while they tag along.  It would help to have a passport/log made up to stamp into, probably as part of the brochure. 
 
"collect all 10 revolutionary war stamps in your passport log."
 
You might be able to sell it more as a passport / visa / souvenir thing more than as a letterbox.  But still use it to advertise letterboxing. 
 
The house owners probably wouldn't want guests "searching around" for them but you could still give clear directions, "look in the box on the picnic table". 
 
If you did a revolutionary war theme, you could then plant them as a series somewhere else afterwards. 
 
Sounds like fun.
