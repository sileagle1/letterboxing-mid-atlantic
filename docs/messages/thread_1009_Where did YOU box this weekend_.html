Where did YOU box this weekend?

From: Hikers_n_ Hounds | Date: 2005-05-09 00:36:28 UTC
Well H&H in the form of Irene and Dude went camping in the WV panhandle in Glengary with the Commander Cody Caving Club. On Saturday we boogied northeast and met up with Tree Hugger and her daughter Carrie. Tree Hugger is a real West (By God) Virginia Letterboxer from the Philippi area. We met at Deep Creek State Park in MD and found two great boxes, Meadow Mountain and Swallow Falls. Dude and I would have never found Swallow Falls if it weren't for Tree Hugger and Carrie as the park map and clues seemed to contradict each other and Dude was thoroughly confused. Fortunately Tree Hugger has been boxing for awhile now and her "letterboxing 6th sense" as Silent Doug calls it was working well. We found the box on one of the prettiest trails I have been on in awhile.
 
As I've said, Tree hugger is not new to boxing but is not a member of any of the talk lists. I've encouraged her to join our rowdy crew here and assured her a warm welcome.
 
Dude and I also planted a drive by box off of 81 South in MD, just over the MD/Pa State Line, better known as the Mason Dixon Line. Clues are easy and I'll post to LBNA but basically you get on 81 South, as soon as you cross into MD look on your right for the Maryland Veterans Memorial Highway Monument on the right at Mile Marker 11. Pull off on the right at the monument (basically a 4 foot wall with some flags). Face the writing on the monument and notice the large bush to it's right. Past the bush is a thin tree against the fence leaning against another thin tree on the other side of the fence. On the ground behind the thin tree is our Mason - Dixon box.
 
Dude and I are pooped, we drove about 16 hours this weekend and have got to unpack and get ready for work tomorrow. Take care all,
 
H&H

From: Mike Brooks | Date: 2005-05-10 05:46:15 UTC
I did the series of four in Waller Mill Park near Williamsburg. Straight
forward clues, and all present and in good condition.

I found the fourth letterbox and carried it about 50 feet away before
opening it up. I spread out the letterbox contents in one pile and my
supplies in a second pile and start stamping. I finish with the log book and
reach over to put it back in the box when a baby snake, about nine inches
long, emerges from the leaves about a foot from the piles of letterbox. It
was probably harmless, but I still used a stick to retrieve the various
materials and reassemble everything.

The amusing part of this was the spot where I set everything down was random
and I could have easily put the letterbox on the snake itself, or knelt down
on the snake. No noise or rustling of any type. It just slithered into view
and sat there.

It was a lively end to the letterboxing hike.

Mike

Back In The Saddle

From: Nathan Brown | Date: 2005-05-11 02:36:59 UTC
I've been kinda mooping about and lurking lately, But I have been
getting a few boxes.

Four weekends ago I found two boxes, Pit Stop in Aberdeen, MD, and The
Wye Island Osage, which is box one of the Wye Island series on Wye
Island, MD. The Wye Island Cove box was missing.

Three weekends ago I discovered Fire Sprite in ???, PA, and Waiting in
Danville, PA. Both were in fine shape. During the week I was also
first finder on both After the -- --- .-. ... . / -.-. --- -.. . in .-.
. .- -.. .. -. --. --..--, PA and Pateke from New Zealand in Reading, PA.

Two weekends ago I found no boxes :(

But last weekend I was on fire. I headed up the the Leigh Valley and
hit up some major boxing on Sunday. I first found Lessons from the
Jukebox #10 – The Village People. I was the first finder, and captured
the Zymurgy HH for the effort. I released Zymurgy later in the day.
Nice stamp, but The Village People still suck :) I next found some
Scary Vegetables, four of them for that matter. I forgot to mention to
LB, there seems to be a scary vegrant living in the woods near Bad
Broccoli, one should use caution.

Speaking of Lightn' Bug, I headed over to his place next and bugged him
a little bit. He and I headed over to the Lenapi Museum and I found the
box there. He showed me where it was really, since I did not expect to
go for that one and didn't have the clues with me for it. He also
showed me where the last box of Fish in Outer Space is. The box is
still there, bubt the stamp is gone :( We parted ways as he had a soccar
game to get to.

I was not done yet. On the way home I found Tunis Brady (and also
captured Jenny's Bear HH). I was not totally sure I was in the right
park, as the park is not really in the same town the clues say, but the
clues matched up to what I knew about the particular park so I found it.

Finally I headed down the river a little more and found the Just For Fun
series. The first box was no problem. The second was, interesting.
The whole area had gotten hit pretty bad by the flood. Walking along
the trail I found a canoe hung up in some trees. I guess the owner had
found it and couldn't get it out yet, so it was chained to the trees.
The box was in a tree next to a wall along the side of the path. The
path actually leads over the wall. I clambered down atop a pile of logs
and wondered, which tree? the clues said the tree to the right, but
which right? The right facing the wall, or the right facing away from
the wall from the trail. Just then I looked down and there was the box
wedged in the roots at my feet :) The box was a victum of the flood,
however, and after a quick call to H&H, it was decided to pull it.

Well, that has been my most recent adventures, who knows what I'll go
after this weekend?

From: cahillymom | Date: 2005-05-11 12:25:34 UTC
Near, but not quite.
(putting on teachers hat, unrolling imaginary map, tapping imaginary
pointer, and saying, "Now, now, class...")
"Ramapo" is the name of an escarpment, the mountains it forms, the
river in the valley next to them, a town in the valley, and the Indian
tribe that has lived there forever. Not to mention roads, malls, etc.
in the area. Early spelling was Ramapough.
The "Ramapo Mountain People" or "Jackson Whites" are reputed to live a
bit further up the ridge from the State Forest, near and over the
state line. They are said to be the descendants of the Indians and
escaped slaves, among other stories. There is an organized group of
Ramapough Indians up right next to Ringwood State Park. They were in
the news recently when a trail was closed because paint sludge from
the old Ford plant was found there. Not sure if or how they relate to
the other folks.
(takes of hat, puts down pointer, map rolls itself up and flaps like a
window shade...)
As to letterboxing in the area, in addition to the six mentioned,
there are two others in two other parks on the same ridge, all easily
accessed from 287. The views are amazing.
