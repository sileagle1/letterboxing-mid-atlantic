Hi All,

I hope that everybody had a blast at the deuling gatherings (cur the
banjo music...).

Turtle McQuade and I met up in extreme Northeast PA to hunt a
mystery box and visit some cute churches. We had been discussing
meeting up at the NY gathering, but we both came tot he conclusion
that we did not want to subject our kiddos to 4 hour drives each way
(well, NY was 2.25 hrs for me.

So we decided to split the pain (more on that later) and meet in
Susquehanna County, PA. Lone Wolf and Feisty Kitty joined Kirby
Rider as the kid part of our squad. Our goal was to perform a
resuce mission on her "June Bloom" Hitchhiker which had been
languishing in Silent Doug's Stone Face mystery box since late
2003. The rescue was successful and the poor helpless isolated HH
was rescued. The box is in good shape.

Our efforts turned to NJ Caroles "Equinunk Charge" Series - a series
of 5 drive by's located on the grounds of charming little churches
about 15-25 miles N of Honesdale, PA. We got to Pine Mill UMC - a
cute stone building whose masonry was created on site (according to
the older gentleman we chatted with) back in the 1940's. Found the
box and then went for lunch in Equinunk. After lunch we did the
Equinunk UMC and I dislocated my left shoulder retrieving the box
(the kids did not want to reach under the Juniper bushes to get
it!). After it popped back in and we did the boxing thing. Turtle
retrieved the Greyhound 2 HH to bring back to NY. We then went on
to the Union UMC and saw the largest Pine Tree (diameter, not
height) that we had ever seen. From there the Lookout UMC and
finally the Braman UMC.

We also did much exchanging, cross stamping, etc. over lunch. There
was a birthday party for a 90 yr old lady going on near us and they
were all wodnering what those weird people were doing with those
stamps and books....

On Monday, I hope to catch up with Pete and Wanda in Central PA to
find some mystery boxes of the Mapsurfer and True Indigo variety.

Take care,

LB
