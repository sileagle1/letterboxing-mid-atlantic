From: The Doubtful Guests | Date: 2005-05-17 20:08:25 UTC
Ooo, let's get technical about how to classify finds.  Always good for a round of opinions. 
 
Ahhhh, stamps at events cause so many problems with letterboxing "counting".  One can easily argue that if the box is somehow "hidden" you should count it as a "(F)ind".  If it is an event stamp then it is clearly an (E)vent stamp.  If it is just laying out on the table, like traveling event stamps often are, well.... I think some purists would not count it at all. 
 
There were three obvious "Event" stamps:  Elephant with Date, Box of Popcorn, and Yachty Girl's event stamp (did this have a name). 
 
The following stamps were "hidden in plain sight" in closed boxes, or other non-Rubbermaid style containers, and some would count them as "Finds" since they had to be "discovered":
 
Angoscioso la Grandioso
Lion Tamer
Dancing Dog
Hot Ding
Animal Crackers - 1 Find? 12 stamps?
Chunky Monkey
The Letterboxing Book
Spider and Fly
Vaudeville Moon
 
Two boxes were hidden close to the pavillion:
 
The Amazing Fish Girl (from a fishing pole)
Amazing Feats (in a tree)
    This included 4 stamps: Strong Man, Fire Eater, Sword Swallower, and Hula Girl.  1 Find? 4 stamps?
 
Time Magazine Cover - Was just lying out on the table.  I list it as a HitchHiker on AtlasQuest.
 
I will shortly be posting a listing of all the stamps, that I know of, that were at the gather.  I will post a "Clue" on AtlasQuest for the Circus ones for those who want to log them. 
 
We could argue endlessly about the Clue Exchange Game stamps.  You obviously had to work pretty hard to get them and could only get them at the gather.  Work = "Find"?
 
In the end, it has been said that there are no rules in letterboxing so we count them as we see them. 
 
Most truly and sincerely yours,
Phineas Boch
