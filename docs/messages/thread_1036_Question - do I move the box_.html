Question - do I move the box?

From: pamelar123 | Date: 2005-05-19 14:36:00 UTC
Hi gang, I have a question for the group. I've discovered a newer box
planted within a few hundred feet of one of my older ones -- we even
give some of the same information about a key site in the area in our
clues.

It's a big beautiful park with lots of trails and lots to see. I'm
wondering if I should move my box so they're not clustered so close
together. On the other hand, it's nice to get a little more for your
boxing buck -- two stamps for one hike!

Any opinions?

Pam of the Vespa Vikings

Tough call . . . While I enjoy the hiking/puzzle
aspect of the activity as much as the stamp
collecting, and would
rather different boxes be fairly far apart, it is
always an unexpected delight when I come across two
that are
right near each other; almost the same feeling I used
to get when I found TWO prizes in a single box of
CrackerJack ... ALMOST.
Also, you did YOUR part when you staked out the area
and placed your box first. If someone else opts for
the
same territory (perhaps BECAUSE of your placement),
take it as a compliment that you have good taste in
locales.

From: The Doubtful Guests | Date: 2005-05-19 17:07:10 UTC
Two boxes for one hike.  Oh boy!  Some series are planted that close together to begin with. Some people are more likely to visit a series, or an area of multiple plantings, than to visit individual boxes.   
I, myself, would not plant a new box so close to another one, but I don't mind others doing it.  (No rules, no rules.)  When someone planted a box close to one of mine I put a note in the bottom of my clue, "Also look for ____ which is nearby." 
 
Personally I would spend the extra effort on planting a totally new box.  :-)
 
Scarab

Re: [lbma] Question - do I move the box?

From: Lorie Thomas | Date: 2005-05-20 02:33:56 UTC
Please don't!
As a new boxer I'm thrilled to find more than one on a
particular trail.

And just because it's on the same trail doesn't mean
the boxers even know it exists. Without the clues in
hand you'd have to tromp through all the undergrowth,
turn over every rock, and look at every dead tree or
hole.

Now whenever I do trail challenges or just walk the
dog I wonder where the boxes are, knowing they are all
around me!
