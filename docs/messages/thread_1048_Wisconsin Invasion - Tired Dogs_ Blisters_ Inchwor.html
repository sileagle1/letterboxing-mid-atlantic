Wisconsin Invasion - Tired Dogs, Blisters, Inchworms and Ticks

From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2005-05-23 10:38:22 UTC
Hi All,

Had some fun with Wisconsin Hiker (Debbie) and Martini Man (John)
yesterday. Jan if Team Little Dog joined us for a bit of a futile
attempt as well.

After the hardy Wisconsinites (or Wisconsonians or Wisconsoners, I
am not certain) logged like 15+ miles (and untold many boxes) on
Saturday at French Creek and Blue Marsh, they continued to dazzle
with their hiking stamina!!

We did the Nolde Forest Series and tried for Mapsurfer's Old Ned's
Mexican Beer. We were able to make it about 50% through the clues
when we hit an impasse and started trying all sorts of directions,
etc. Having already done Nolde, Jan joined us for the last Shred
MTB box and then for the confuzzled attempt at Mapsurfer. We
evenutally gave up on that one, but have a strategery for future
attempts. We then had some time for exchanges, etc. Jan went off
to see an Elvis entertainer with her fiance, while WH and MM and I
bebopped over to find the Pateke Letterbox.

After that, we employed my geek toy (GPS) to find a LB/Geocahe
hybrid in the Berks County Cemetary - The "Sacred Garden" - I can
provide "boxy" clues for those interested and nto GPS enabled.

From here it was off to find some ... and .-. and Jan's box "After
the .-.- .--..".

There is a new type of Magnetism in Penn's Woods - In addition
to "Tick Magnets", we now have "Inchworm Magnets", as Berks County
woods were full of them, just waiting to land on Martini Man.

At the end of the day, it was time to imitate John Candy in "Trains,
Planes and Automobiles." I then peeled off the boots, found 3
blisters, said in a satisfied tone "Man, my dawgs are barkin'"
Found a tick cruising across my chest at shower time and decided to
estimate our mileage. Nolde - 10 miles, Patake 3/4 miles, LB/Geo -
1/2 mile, -.-.- - 3 miles, Jan's -.-.. - 1/4 mile, for a rousing
estimate of 14.5 miles. No wonder my dogs are stil barking.

Today, we shall meet Gerania up at Hickory Run, to Mapsurf again!

Got Bunny Maps?

We do,

Lightnin' BUg

Re: Wisconsin Invasion - Sacred Garden

From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2005-05-31 16:54:52 UTC
"Sacred Garden" is listed on a geocache website because the planter
is more of a geocaher than a letterboxer. It is considered a hybrid
because it does allow you to trade stamp images as you would with any
letterbox, and it does not have any of the typical geostuff in it.
In that regard it is more like a LB, but its coordinates require a
GPS or a good topo map to find. There are several mystery boxes like
that, espcially when you get to the crux of some mapsurfer boxes.

Anyway, the cemetery it is in is actually more of a potters field, a
term coined by Martini Man that I'd forgotten.

Lightnin' Bug
