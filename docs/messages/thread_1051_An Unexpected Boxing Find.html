An Unexpected Boxing Find

From: louis_d_arc | Date: 2005-05-25 04:09:21 UTC
For those who may not be aware, because I certainly wasn't until I got
there, Longwood Gardens just south of Kennett Square in southern
Chester County has their own take on letterboxing. Within the main
conservatory are hidden five stamps which you can find. A stamp pad at
each box station has also been provided by the LG caretakers. Now,
when I say box station, I don't mean these things are out in the wide
open. A friend and I were there this past weekend, which is how I
found out about this, and had toured the complete conservatory (a
building of no moderate size by any means). It wasn't until we were at
the end and ready to leave when I looked down and there was a plain
unobtrusive black box. Sercurely attached to it was a rubber stamp and
a stamp pad. Well I thought that was the niftiest thing I'd seen at
the gardens.

As we walked out the door there was a brochure stand with many
pamphlets, which no one seemed to be taking any great notice of, into
which five different images could be stamped along with clues as to
where to find the images.

If you're a letterboxing purest, then this would not be of much fun for
you. There are no logs into which you can stamp to prove you've been
there. But since I grew up in that area and spent a day or two each
summer at the gardens I find this to be absolute fun, because they're
including a new hobby of mine among some beautiful flora.
