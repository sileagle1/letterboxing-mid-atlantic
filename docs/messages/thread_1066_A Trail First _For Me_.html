A Trail First (For Me)

From: nattybumppolbna | Date: 2005-05-30 04:05:16 UTC
While hiking a Central PA trail today to place a new box in the lands of my mis-spent
youth, I came across what I thought (at first) was a dog on the path. We eyed each other
for a few seconds. No collar. No friendly owner tagging along a few yards behind. It
didn't look like any breed I know.

Then it took off in the other direction. It had that odd loping gait that you see in nature
films about hyenas and dingos and - oh, just say it - coyotes.

I mentioned it to my Father-In-Law who lives nearby.

"Oh, sure. we get more and more coyotes up there nowadays. Even a few bear,"

I know I sound like a perfect suburban park trail pansy, but should I mention these
potential hazards in the clue?

Natt-

From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2005-05-30 11:18:06 UTC
Bear encounters can range from the "hey I just saw a bear and it ran
off into the woods" (most of the time) to "holy &$@#$, a bear is
after me". A vast majority are of the first type. Bear sees you,
bear bolts.

Nonetheless, most PA State Parks and Gamelands north of Blue Mountain
(even NJ side of the Delaware Water Gap) have some bear and have
signs posted as to the fact that this is "Bear Country". I agree
that you should post that you may run into a bear, or in this case a
coyote.

For a summary of what NOT TO DO with respect to bears:

<<http://www.dcnr.state.pa.us/news/resource/res2005/05-0510-
bearfacts.aspx>>

We had hairy legged it in Hickory Run about 4 weeks after said bear
encounter by the nitwits. We did not bring food with us on the
trails as a precaution. Water was OK.

Lightnin' Bug
