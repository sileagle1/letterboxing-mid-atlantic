New box planted: Happy Wanderer

From: Wisconsin Hiker | Date: 2005-06-03 04:27:57 UTC
Last found/checked: 26-MAY-05

Location: Get yourself to Ountmay Insimay (in the educated porcine
language)
Time: 2-3 hours (~ 4 miles roundtrip with 1041 ft elevation
ascent/descent)
Terrain: Rocky trail

We've enjoyed traveling and hiking ever since we've been
married
(nearly 27 years!) and when we discovered letterboxing, we
enthusiastically incorporated that in our leisure pursuits. To
celebrate this great combination of activities, we placed this box
while we were "wandering" in Pennsylvania.

We were in search of another box when we ascended the AT to reach
this summit (marked by a sign on a tree, some stone steps and 4
concrete pillars). We enjoyed the hike, but were unsuccessful in our
hunt. Hopefully you will have better luck! As we left the summit to
retrace our steps descending the trail, we noticed a utility pole on
the right. We noted the yellow sign with numbers on the pole:
_________ - _________. We'll reference the digits as ABCDE 
FGHIJ.

We then continued down and as we reached the "false summit"
there was
a decision point. If you follow a bearing of BC+I degrees for a few
steps, you'll find yourself standing on a small circular AT
survey
marker. From there, take a new bearing of HG degrees for A+B+C+J
steps, then E+F+J steps at a bearing of ABC  CAH degrees. The
Wanderer is resting in a rocky spot beneath a coniferous tree.
Please rehide him well so he is not found accidentally. There should
be enough materials in the area to provide good cover.

We hope you enjoyed this adventure in the DWG and since we don't
live
in the area we would really appreciate an update on the status of the
box if you find it. We won't be able to check on it for quite a
while!
