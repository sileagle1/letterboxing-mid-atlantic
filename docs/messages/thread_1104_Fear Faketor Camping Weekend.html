Hi all,

We (the family) went camping in Cordorus State Park in
PA this weekend. We were able to visit three of the
boxes and ended up with six stamps (including a HH

very steamy hikes on Saturday, my hubby asked if I
wanted to finish the third box (Mary Ann Furnace)
Saturday evening - what a dumb question - of course!
(I think he's hooked). We had a great time and what a
fabulous park for camping and only 90 minutes from
home! Happy boxing (and camping)!

Brenda in Baltimore

At 09:09 PM 6/13/2005, you wrote:
>Well I've never heard of this place. Dude and I will have to check it out.
>Thanks for the post!!

That's one of my old stomping grounds, going back to my Boy Scout days --
hiking and boating in the spring, summer and fall, and ice skating in the
winter. The lake is manmade, and was created in the late 60s as a joint
project between the state and a local paper manufacturer (that now has
rights to much of the water that flows out of the lake for their industrial
purposes).

When the water level is low, you can see the top of the church steeple that
once stood proudly in the village that was flooded to create the lake (or
so it is told).

And on autumn nights when the moon is full, teenagers dare each other to row out onto
the lake and gaze down into it's murky depths. Only one teenager has ever taken that
dare.

Young Willy Baransky wanted to be liked by the in-crowd so much that he climbed into his
father's rowboat one October night and rowed out on Lake Codorus as the gang watched
from the shore.

He had heard all the stories. Particularly the one about the brother and sister who were
playing hide and seek in the abandoned town on the morning they opened the floodgates.

Willy had heard that when the light of the full moon hits the surface of the lake you can
still see the little girl clinging to the top of the steeple waiting for a rescue that will never
come.

But no one will ever know if Willy saw the ghost of the little girl in the moonlight. He
disappeared that night - never to be seen again. The next morning, his friends searched
the banks for some sign of what became of Willy. They found his boat drifting on the lake.
The oars were missing. But caught in one of the oarlocks was....... a sharpened stainless
steel hook!

That's how I heard it, anyway.
