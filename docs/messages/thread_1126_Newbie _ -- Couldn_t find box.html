Hi all -- I am new to this, so I apologize if I am going about this
the wrong way. (I did check the FAQ but didn't find the answer.)

about this.

From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2005-06-21 12:03:28 UTC
To add to what the mighty Irene has said, you need to be careful with
some of the PCLG boxes. As they are no longer really active in the
boxing world, many of their boxes have gone missing and they may not
have gotten around to updating their site. Many emi-retired boxers
don't bother with updating clues to reflect missingness. There are
several in the Allentown, PA area that have been confirmed missing
several times by respected boxers, yet the clues do not relect that.
Also, starting out with a mystery box is very ambitious and
admirable. Good luck in your searches, perhaps hooking up with one
of us goofballs may be a good way to break into the hobby. Irene's
boxes are cool.

LB

From: gerania93 | Date: 2005-06-21 13:31:27 UTC
Hey, wait a minute, I thought that he was talking about me! As you've
noted, Perfect Circle doesn't have Contact the Placer option on their
site. Within the last year they've done at least some work on their
boxes. Several have been listed as missing. The Moooving Tale clue
had a 'box moved due to construction in area' notice last year. If
you letterbox all summer you might go back to that area in the Fall
and look at the whole thing with new eyes, not merely following the
clues but thinking 'now that looks like a good place to hide a box,
I'll check over there too'.

Gillian

Independence Microbox

Confirmed Missing 04/12/03

Letterboxing Info

Boxes Placed

LBNA Home
LBNA Board

FFF Home
Location     Washington, DC
Date Placed     December 28, 2002
Difficulty    

Easy, drive-up if you can get close parking!
Directions    

Constitution Avenue and 19th Street, NW.

Clues    

South of Constitution Avenue is the Constitution Gardens Park, which includes a small lake. Between 18th and 19th Streets, a footbridge leads to a small island with a memorial for the 56 signers of the Declaration of Independence. After perusing the signatures, head back up the steps to the bridge. Before crossing, you will see a line of trees heading off to your left between the memorial and a low stone wall. Walk along this line of trees until you reach the fifth tree. With your back to this tree, look due north and spot another tree on the far side of the stone wall. Walk directly toward this far tree, stopping at the wall. Directly in front of you, on the back side of the stone wall, the Independence Microbox is nestled in the grass.

Somebody, somehow, traced backwards through the LbNA site last year
and came up with --- yahoo@... --- At the other end of this
address was a nice fellow named Brian, who seems to be the last
vestige of PCLG and the maintainer of the site. We had a
correspondence going about a missing box, and my replacing one that
was ... rotting away, shall we say? I later sent a box status report
to him for one I KNOW was missing, as I found the remnants scattered
about, and got no reply, and the website was not changed. He may or
may not know box status, as he was not the placer of all the boxes.

Editorial comment: This is a very sad thing. Those PCLG boxes were
some of the best I ever found: great clues, fascinating backstories
and places, and really handsome stamps, too. I think they all
stopped boxing before the custom of giving up boxes for adoption
became common.

When I adopted the Martian Maggot boxes (I should have my head
examined) I followed the following procedure:

1) Sent mail to her many times over months with no response. I
believe she moved to Canada in 2003.

2) Contacted Choi with a list of boxes in NJ and PA, who contacted
her at another e-mail address he had for her.

3) Recontacted Choi later to see if he had heard from her. He changed
her LBNA clues to have my name on them. In some ways this is
hijacking because we never got explicit permission. She just doesn't
care anymore. She never responded to any more of my inquiries as to
box locations, hints on exactly where they might be hidden so that
they could be maintained, or permission to access the clues on her
letterboxing website. So I am still in the process of searching for
the boxes and replacing/repairing them.

4) I maintain the boxes staying true to her original intent. Because
Martian Maggot also designed a special web page for most of her
clues, I have kept the clue pointing to her site. Where the clues are
wrong or out of date, I add only enough information to the LBNA page
to correct the error but not change the original clue.

Because PCLG boxes tend to be mystery boxes and the clues are on a
different web site, I recommend following the same procedure. I
believe if nobody from PCLG replies to John's emails and he feels you
are earnest and a good letterboxing citizen, he will assign the clue
over to you.

I am going to check a PCLG box near me and perhaps do maintenance on
it. If it is a good location, I'll start the adoption process.
