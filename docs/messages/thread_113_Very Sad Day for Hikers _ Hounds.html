Very Sad Day for Hikers & Hounds

From: Hikers_n_ Hounds | Date: 2004-05-28 23:32:16 UTC
Hey folks,
 
I just found out that Hikers & Hounds lost one of it's Colorado members, my brother Marty's dog, Chance. Chance was a 7 year old Australian Sheppard and the most phenominal animal I have ever known. When my mom passed in February Marty and
Chance came east for 6 weeks to help us deal with her personal effects. During that time Marty and Chance were members of Hikers & Hounds. They helped us plant the Greatful Dead series in DE, and went on the ill-fated trip to Tyler Park to hunt Muk-Luk's boxes (we never even came close!). I chose the name Hikers & Hounds because everyone in my family has animals and I thought it was easy enough to include whomever wanted to go on trail that day. All of our dogs have the own personal stamps ( a pawprint with thier first initial in it). Chance shared my sister's dog's stamp, Cody. My brother liked letterboxing so much (he is a surveyor by trade and has LOT'S of opportunity to plant) I sent him home to CO with 4 boxes. I don't know if he ever got around to it or not, I'll have to ask him sometime.
 
Marty gave Chance a nice big soup bone he got from the local restaurant. Chance developed an intestinal blockage and never recovered. Please don't give your dogs anything but dog food, and never rawhide. I lost a dog to a perforated stomach years ago caused by sharp rawhide edges.
 
Anyway, it might seem stupid to post like this but we are just heartbroken. When we feel better we are going to carve and plant a memorial box in his honor in one of the many parks we visited while he was here.
 
Animals enrich our lives in so many ways. It's just hard knowing the you will almost always out live them. Give your furry buddy a big hug and kiss tonight. Dude the Wonder Dog will get lots of extra attention from me this weekend. He's just think it's cause he's so great...and he is.
 
Have a great weekend guys.
