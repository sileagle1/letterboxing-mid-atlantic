ticks in southern Jersey

From: dave & diane | Date: 2005-07-16 03:44:15 UTC
I made the Environmental Health Tech at the Newport Navy Base Medical Center
very excited, in fact I think I made his week. After a foray into a couple
of the New Jersey State Forests - Brendan Byrne (formally Lebanon) and Penn
for letterboxing I took the train home and must have missed a couple of
ticks and they got inbedded. I took the ticks over to the doctor's office
because we here in RI know all about the dangers of Lyme Disease. They sent
the ticks off for testing and put me on antibiotics for three weeks. The
Environmental Guy was so excited that I had brought in a "Lone Star" tick.
Guess they're not as common around here as I thought (looked like a dog tick
to me). Turns out one of my ticks had ehrlichiosis. He had to contact the
State of New Jersey and tell them what county it was found in. He was pretty
certain that this is the farthest east that ehrlichiosis has been found. I
guess I came pretty close to making his year. I was never tested myself so I
don't know if I ever had ehrlichiosis. So, watch out down there in NJ.
