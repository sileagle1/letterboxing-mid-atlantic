Hail fellow boxers,
 
I had planned to hunt Silent Doug's Dingman's Falls series but work intruded on my letterboxing plans and I didn't get up to the Poconos until Saturday eveing. However, Sunday I was able to find Silent Doug's Alder Leaf box alive and well in Tobyhanna State Park. A picture perfect day at a nice park with fun trails. Thanks for the box Doug!
 
So today I return to my office after teaching all day and there is a book on my desk with my (work) friend Michael's name in cartouche on the front. I've been talking to Mike for a couple of months about letterboxing as I am sure he would love it. The challenge is he doesn't drive but I promised to take him with me sometime soon. I must say I had ulterior motives for recruiting Mike. He's a very creative person and loves puzzles, etc. I thought I could use him to help H&H find some mystery boxes! So I showed him Fleur de Lis very creative and intellegent Ben Franklin series clues which are not 15 minutes from where we work. Mike and I were supposed to go letterboxing this Saturday, 6/5/04 for this series. His first time out, I was going to show the "rookie" the ropes. We talked about his id stamp and he advised he already has a rubber stamp cartouche of his name, cool! So, back to this book on my desk. I open it up and there are the clues to the Ben Franklin boxes folded in there and 4 OF THE 5 IMAGES!! Turns out Mike couldn't wait and told his room mate and they were out boxing all weekend, and found every box they seached for. Then I turn the page and see the very distinctive stamp of who else...Silent Doug! and Jennifer! Mike ran into them on the trails. Well this guy is bouncing off the walls with glee and was going back after work to get the very last Ben Frankling image. I have created (another) monster! So while I was not as successful as I had hoped to be, one of my recruits had a fabulous weekend and is now coming to the LBMA Camping weekend in July. Thanks to all,
 
Irene - H&H

From: Judy B | Date: 2004-08-02 11:34:43 UTC
FdL - backwards - you left handed too or as idle brained as I -- I'm
always having to carve my stamps twice -- until I started gluing them
to a mount -- then any wordy parts get cut off and I just carve a new
wordy part. But I have improved some, I generally realize my mistake
just after tracing the design -- so now I only use vellum (from the
scrapbooking supplies) and can just flip it over!!! before I lead it
up. Trick I learned in college is to lead/graphite the design and
then with just a couple drops of ligterfluid (in the yellow can) on a
kleenex gently wipe over the graphite. It will keep it from smearing
when you are doing the transfer onto the rubber (for which I use one
of those embossing tools (again over in the scrapbooking dept) with
the tiny metal nib.)
