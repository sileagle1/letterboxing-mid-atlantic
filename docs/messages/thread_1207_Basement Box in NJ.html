aka Wednesday Boxing...

We started out in search of the South Mountain Reserve letterbox from
the basement (in Millburn). Hard hike uphill in this heat (and it was
no Mt Tammany, believe me!) -- seemed a lot longer than it really
was! But no luck finding this box. Has anyone found it recently?
Just curious.

Needed a few easier ones after that so we went to East Hills Park and
had lots of fun on the FitTrail, then headed to Far Hills Pharmacy and
had a deliciously cold root beer float while stamping in.

Ended our day at NJ Hitchhiker Hostel. Picked up two and left two,
including a newly released one, entitled "I Gotta Go". What can I
say? It reflects the gender bias of the B's, the majority of whom
found the stamp and double-entendre of the theme to be so tremendously
clever that they were willing to overlook the fact that it was a store-
bought stamp.

Funny, that last point. When we first started, we picked up lots of B
themed stamps from Michael's to plant. And the more we boxed, the
more we realized how much we vastly preferred finding hand-carved
stamps. So we ended up returning them all, except for the one
mentioned above. Even the personal stamps have gradually been
converted over to B themed hand carved ones (though the B connection
might not be too obvious without explanation...)

No more boxing this week. Too much work to do around here. At least
we had a good two day run!

~ the B's

South Mountain Reservation
This box is Unclaimed.
Placement date: May 1 2003
State: New Jersey
County: Essex
Nearest city: Milburn
Number of boxes: 1

Clues
15 minutes on rocky incline and 20 minutes on paved path (see middle
of clue for lazy driving hint)

On Glen Avenue in Millburn across from near where the bibliophiles
gather there is an opening in the woods marked by a sign that
says "Locust Grove". Drive in here and park.

Bear right, on foot now, onto the Lenape Trail, through the picnic
area, past the roof on legs, and up the hill. Just follow, the
trail's trees are marked in yellow.

When you get to a metal fence, bear right (you're still on the yellow
trail)
until you come to a paved road. Take a right here and as you walk,
enjoy the fresh air.

You will be walking for about twenty minutes, through the white gates
past which cars are allowed, also past a semi-grassy parking area.

You will know you're close when the road widens, and you see an
amazing overlook on the right. (The physically unfit, if good map-
readers, can park here and avoid the hill).

Look at Manhattan, then stand reading the memorial rock bearing
George Bramhall's name. From this rock, turn towards the woods, and
walk into them, crossing the road on its race marker, going between
the two green benches onto the path.

Almost immediately on your left you will see a tall light brown very
dead tree
(just a stone's throw from the benches) it has lost all its branches.

All rhyming aside on the other side of this tree, there are two
stones piled
remove the upper one, and in the tree's hollow you will find the
letterbox. (Please put the rock back before you leave, thanks).

-stephanie

I drove up the South Mountain Reservation last night after work. I
didn't turn over every leaf, but I did turn over every stone near a
tall, dead tree. I forgot Audrey's hint that the tree was VERY
hollow; but every time I finished searching a very dead tree, I would
look up and see another. So I finally ran out of daylight.

There is a large downed tree (light brown) that was probably it. It
is pretty well disintegrated by now. I don't own any tools (ax, hoe,
rake) to bring to bear on the mess that remains.

This answers my question about how long it takes a hollow stump to
fall apart: evidently this one lasted two years, since the box was
planted in May 2003.
