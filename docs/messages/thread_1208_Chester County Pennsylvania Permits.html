Chester County Pennsylvania Permits

From: Jan | Date: 2005-08-04 14:52:07 UTC
I have received some emails from the superintendent of Hibernia Park
in Chester County about two boxes that I have planted there. Here is
the first email that he sent:

Please contact me at the Hibernia Park Office. I am need of more
information as it pertains to your "letter box". It is a requirement
that any "caches" that are placed within county park property be
approved by the Park Superintendent and the proper documentation
submitted.

And a subsequent email:
The "permit", for a lack of a better word, provides the parks
department with information on items that are placed within parklands
for recreational enjoyment such as yours. I think that both the
letterboxes and Geocache are a exciting way to spend time in the park
and support them whole heartedly. This permit can be used for all of
our park sites and submitted directly to the superintendent at that
park for approval. The parks needs this information so if someone
unknowingly stumbles upon these items and reports them to the park
office we know about them and they don't become a suspicious package.

The superintendent that I have been dealing with has been super nice
and very supportive. He emailed me the form and I submitted one for
each of my two boxes that I have placed in this park and they were
approved immediately. I am going to add this form to the forms
section here for anyone else's use. This applies only to the county
parks in Chester county, not any state parks.

Jan of Team Little Dog

From: Jan | Date: 2005-08-04 20:25:47 UTC
I don't know about the Lancaster County parks but if you are
concerned, I would take the advice of the park official that I was
dealing with and call the park and find out. I live in Berks County
and I had already had an appointment set up for next week with a local
official because I was uneasy planting boxes in this particular park
without permission. The Berks County official told me that his main
concern was that boxes not be planted in historic districts and he
would tell me where I could plant boxes. It seems to me that most of
these parks are very willing to work with us and I intend to try to
get permission whenever possible from now on at the county levels,
especially since I have found out that it is so easy.
