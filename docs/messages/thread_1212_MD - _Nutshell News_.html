I just read in my "Nutshell News" (park activities publication) that
on September 10 one of Montgomery County Maryland's regional parks,
Brookside Nature Center in Wheaton Regional Park, is hosting a "Family
Letterboxing Hike." I was surprised to (1) see such a public
letterboxing event hosted by the county and (2) see letterboxing
"endorsed" or "embraced" by the county. Here's a link to the park's
site, but they're not quite up-to-date with all the fall events that
are in their printed catalog:
http://www.mcparkandplanning.org/parks/nature_centers/brookside/index.shtm.


Ironically, one of the parks overseen by this division of the county
is Black Hills Regional Park -- site of Boch's Circus (May Gathering)
and a park that is teeming with boxes. I think there's only a few in
Wheaton Regional Park (which is a lovely facility -- butterfly
exhibit, small gauge train, antique carousel, horses/trail rides,
beautiful gardens and great nature center).

I've taken my kids on several of the Nutshell News programs trying to
get to know the parks better and in the back of my mind trying to
scout out new ideas for box-plants. I always felt like it was this
kind of sneaky, subversive thing -- and here they are leading the hike
for boxes!

It's probably a pretty smart move on their part to enable them to go
over how to best protect their paths and vegetation and what critters
we might expect off-trail.

Maybe we'll be seeing some new boxes in the park and in the area!

Pam of the Vespa Vikings
