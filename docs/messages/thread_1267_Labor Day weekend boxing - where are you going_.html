Re: Labor Day weekend boxing - where are you going?

From: true indigo | Date: 2005-09-06 11:24:09 UTC
We were took Ravenboy back to Gotham after visiting with us for a week. I had a travel bug
that Janila of Team Little Dog had handed off to me that also needed to go to the city. I
knew of a geocache in the same park as my Flat Iron building letterbox and was going to
drop it off.

We walked by to check on the box but there was a woman sitting there. We went to the
end of the park where the geocache is and it was all crowded and blocked with a large
screen tv for folks to watch the US Open. Ravenboy tried to sneak in to get to it but there
was just no getting there without being seen by hundreds of tv viewers.

Grumpy about trying to figure out what to do with the travel bug, we decided to walk back
to the letterbox again. This time some guy in a ball cap opening packages of Rogaine was
sitting there but it looked like he would leave soon so we sat down near him. He finally
read all the instructions, packed it up, and walked away as we realized we were at the
wrong spot! With an added amount of grumpy, we walked down to where the same woman
was still there but we saw her getting up. I encouraged RGBisMe to rush ahead and save
the spot before someone else took it but then noticed she was reaching right where the
letterbox should be.

"She's a BOXER!" we all smiled. It's our first time meeting someone out on the trail for and
we didn't have our stuff for exchanges. It was terrific to finally meet Miss Moon after
months of enjoying so many of her local letterboxes. We exchanged hugs and hope to
meet up at Knoebels for officially exchanging stamps. That was the highlight of our
weekend.

We also tried to find Camp Grandma at Prince Gallitzin State Park and found a likely hiding
place but no box. About 10 miles closer to Altoona we then bushwhacked to a nearby
geocache where someone decided to start shooting their hand gun in our direction for
target practice. With the new knowledge of what a bullets sound like buzzing through
leaves over my head I am resolved to never hike in the woods anytime near hunting
season, blaze orange on my back or not. That was just too frightening.

t

Philly area group is at www.dvoa.org .

Also started considering how to package up the "world's biggest
letterbox" for Knoebels. Will need some really big SPOR!
