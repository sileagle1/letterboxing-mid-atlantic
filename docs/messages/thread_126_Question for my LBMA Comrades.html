Warning to people from out of state : not all places called circle,
like Airport Circle or shown on a map, like Medford Circle have a
traffic circle any more. It has been removed and replaced by a
classic intersection with traffic lights. Hmmm, pull over, check the
map, scratch your head, swear that you must be in the wrong place,
continue driving and get really lost because the road signs and the
streets named on the map don't always match! Also, many, but not all
left and U turns are made via a loop or 'jughandle' on the far right
side of the road. You'll usually figure this out when you're
approaching the traffic light and there's no hope of getting across 3
lanes of traffic to make your turn. Bet money on the fact that if
you're in the right lane, there will be a left turn arrow.

From: A. M. | Date: 2004-06-11 05:34:32 UTC
General warning to everyone on the list regarding
"jughandles:"

If you plan on traveling in NJ, and are accustomed to
getting your directions via Mapquest...be very
cautious. Mapquest does NOT know how to describe a
jughandle. Be wary of any instruction to "fork right
and go 0.05 miles" or anything like that. Make sure
you check the Mapquest route against a decent map
before attempting an unfamiliar route. I've lived
here since I was 3, and I've been burned by trusting
Mapquest too many times to count. ;)

New Jersey: What Exit?

From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2004-06-11 22:59:14 UTC
I hope that I do not offend anybody here, but I am remembering
something from my college days. Back then (early 1980's - yes,
almost an old dude!), when people form New Jersey would meet each
other for the first time, they would find out where in the state they
lived by asking each other "What Exit?". For example, the Garden
State Parkway runs N/S with lower exits being down by Cape May and
upper exits being up by NY State. Anyway, folks could figure out
where in the state other folks lived by this simple system!

I was thinking that this would be a cool letterbox idea; the "What
Exit?" letterbox, to be placed in a public park near an exit off of
one of the major expressways that criss-cross the state.

As I live in Allentown, PA, it may be worth a trip over to the Garden
State to plant this mirthful letterbox.

Thinking happy thoughts,

Lightnin' Bug
