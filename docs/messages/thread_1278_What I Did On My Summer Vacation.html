From: nattybumppolbna | Date: 2005-09-11 14:16:38 UTC
Maybe not so OT since we all have to drive to pursue this hobby/obsession...

I have been using and contributing to http://www.gasbuddy.com and http://
www.gaspricewatch.com/new . They track local gas prices based on the reports of
volunteers on the service. You can enter your location and they will give you the current
prices at nearby gas stations.

As H&H pointed out, high gas prices are a national problem, but gouging is a local one. If
enough people participate in these services, it has the potential to help create a small local
price competition and keep gouging down in your area - particularly if you let the local
station managers know what you're doing.

It doesn't help much once you are on the road, but with the growth of wi-fi networks, it
will.

And if the loan does come through, I have a nice little treasure trail started from one end
of the Outer Banks to the other along Route 12. Between our boxes and the wonderful
contributions of others, there are 16 boxes along a beautiful 100 mile drive.

- Timmy Tourguide
