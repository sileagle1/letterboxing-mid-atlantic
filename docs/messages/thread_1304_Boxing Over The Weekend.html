Boxing Over The Weekend

From: JuneMcAllister | Date: 2005-09-18 16:19:23 UTC
Well, we didn't make the Mystery Gathering, but Tamsyn and Phynstar made the trip to Gotham today and did some urban boxing with me. I smirked at them while they looked for boxes I had already found <EG> and found a couple I hadn't done. Phynstar zeroed right in on one I have looked for twice! Oh the shame!! We trekked the city from morning till dusk and had a great time.
 
And if they hadn't taken Metro North into the city, I might have missed the newly done lower level of Grand Central Station, which has been turned into a huge and really pretty awesome Dining Concourse with food that is actually good, not just fast food.  The refurbishing of the whole place has saved a really great New York City landmark. I had seen some of the new things but not all.  They have really done a great job while preserving the original.

Actually, I'm not entirely new to this list; I had been on it under another name, but then I thought of a cool new trail name and created an ID around it and then subscribed.  Under my old ID I rarely posted, but with an exclusive ID I can be more active.
 
I found the three boxes in the Northwest Branch Trail series, in Silver Spring, MD, and the "Suitable for Quiet Contemplation" box which I had tried to find once before but couldn't.  I found the "Dino" hitchhiker which seems new, I'm the first person to stamp in.
 
BTW, the name "Vagrarian" comes from the writings of crackpot occultist Elliot O'Donnell, who used the term "vagrarian" to refer to a sort of wandering nature spirit.  I like that idea.  Also, it almost sounds like a cross-breeding of "vagrant" and "librarian" and I'm a major book fiend so that's appropriate, plus I read over a huge variety of topics.  So it's a fun name.  :)
