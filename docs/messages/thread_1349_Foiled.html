I nearly managed to entice a fearless, adventurous boxer to the
foreboding Pine Barrens this weekend for some Mapsurfing mischief and
letterboxingbee intrigue. OK, we were going to pick up my boxes too.
Hey, why not? Alas, we were rained out. This was not merely a matter
of getting wet; It was also a matter of getting hopelessly stuck in a
place where few if any towtruck drivers dare to go.

And as H&H pointed out, getting the interior of boxes wet and
dampening the books is not a nice thing to do.

You will be surprised where we have seen tow trucks get to in the
pines. It may cost you both legs and both arms and all your
letterboxes (tee hee) but we have seen them in places where we
wouldn't have imagined them to go. Plus, most of the times when it's
raining in the pines there are usually 4 wheelers out there and most
are usually nice enough to help you out.

Re: Foiled

From: Amy | Date: 2005-10-10 13:52:43 UTC
My letterboxing was also partially foiled this weekend, as I was also
broke down in the middle of nowhere, in the rain, on Saturday. Our
alternator began dying on the highway - no windshield wipers, power
breaks or steering! We coasted off into a tiny town called Accident,
MD, luckily found a garage and waited for our part to be driven in
from
another town.

Back on the road again, we got quite turned around trying to go the
back way, and I mean back way, from Accident to Deep Creek Lake.
While
on this detour we saw some cool wildlife from the car, including a
turkey family and a baby black bear bounding across the road.

We only had time for one box - Swallow Falls Swallow. That whole
park
is magic and I have a new favorite box.
