are rather new to Letterboxing, and are enjoying carving our stamps. I
have a few questions before we find the right spot to hide our first letter
boxes.

boxers, but am not of the etiquette.

by folding 10 index cards in half and stapling the crease. This fit
perfectly in the little box.

I doubt the big corporate mouse will have any indigestion over a
letterboxing stamp. Hiding stuff for the younger boxers is cool,
and I suggest you look up Hikers and Hounds as she has hidden some
kid oriented boxes (the Caterpillar).

You can check your box as often as you like. Like H+H said
previously, sometimes things move a little slowly at first until
people start coming. I was pretty eager at first, but now I get out
to my boxes when I can (which is not as often as I'd like). By all
means, post your creations on the www.letterboxing.org website and
folks will come (well, at least Star Wars Lego and I will).

Welcome to the club (membership is free and open to all, so it is not
hard to get in).

If you are happy with the stamp, then it doesn't matter what the image
is. There are all kinds of boxes out there, so geared toward kids, some
towards families, some towards adults. Some are easy to get, some are
not. It is all up to the placer what they want to do. Have fun with it!

I have some boxes I have not checked on in over a year (I really should
check on them some more). Once a week, once a month, it is up to you
when you want to check up on them.

Hope to see you on the trail, or more likely at a gathering (since I
never run into anyone on the trail).
