Green Lane Park

From: Rainear, Tim TQO | Date: 2004-06-14 00:41:06 UTC

Hi all,

Did not get to box much this weekend.  I was camping with my two youngest girls in Canadensis in the Poconos.

Great weather!  Got in some fishing and hiking.

 

Just to let you know, I will be camping in Green Lane Park with my kids on June 26 and 27.

If you are planning a visit to the park, let me know and perhaps we can arrange an exchange.

 

On the other hand, if you want to look for boxes and need some help with local directions, I'm more than happy to assist you.

There are more than a dozen boxes in the area if you lump in the Perkiomen Trail.

I can help you plot a course to drive to all six of these in a few hours time.

 

If you want to camp, come on down.

Can't beat the price. $6/night (county residents).

They are showing "Finding Nemo" down by the lake on Saturday night.

 

Hope to see some of you that weekend.

Raindear

Heheh! I imagine that Green Lane Park does not have a "goose
restriction" ;-)

While we did not see nary a goose, we did try our bets to sidestep
the goosey-doo! I was talking with a good friend that lives in East
Greenville who told me that you used to be able to swim in the
reservoir, but the goosey problem became a health issue for swimmers.

We had a similar problem at one of the City parks here in Allentown
back in the mid 1980's. The mayor at the time had a bunch of the
Canada Geese rounded up and shipped to South Carolina. Needless to
say, with all of the locals feeding them, the geese came back in even
greater numbers. Canada geese are normally migratory, but not in
good ole Allentown!

One of my personal pet peeves. Many parks (and people) frown on dogs. I am a responsible dog owner and clean up after Dude and keep him on leash. Yet I can't tell you how many of the parks in my area are seriously fouled by Canada Geese. Silver Lake in Bristol, Pennypack  (Veree Road entrance) in NE Philly, Masons Mill in Montco. But you will see people there feeding these "invaders from the north" and you are right, they no longer migrate. I took my kids took Silver Lake years ago and my oldest, who is now 25 was chased by an angry goose becuase he had no food. I love all animals and admire the beauty of these birds. But well meaning humans have created a problem by supporting these large, non-migratory populations. I don't know what the answer is. I'll just stick with my dog, keeping him on leash and picking up after him and getting dirty looks from the people feeding the geese and trying to step around the goose poop. Maybe if they had to clean up after the geese they feed..?
