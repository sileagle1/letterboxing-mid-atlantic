Actually, this is one of Silent Dougs' boxes that I was supposed to
plant sometime ago, somewhere here in Pennsylvania, of my chosing.  I
decided to plant it in Susquehannock (that is a mouthful) State Park.  I
was headed to Lancaster anyway, so I got up early on Sunday morning and
did some hefty hiking.  I have forwarded to clues to Duck, but decided I
would give the folks here a heads up as well, in case anyone was feeling
adventurous.

For those in the know, I checked on a certain other box in that park,
and it was a little waterlogged but the log was in fine shape.  I
resealed it well.  It had not been found since January.

Anyway, here are the clues:

PA State Tile #371: Rabbit

Authored by Silent Doug and Clueless
Planted by Cyclonic

Clue Difficulty: Easy.  Clues are straight forward and direct.  Some
compass work may be needed
Hike Difficulty: Moderate.  Several elevation changes over varying
terrain.  Elevation changes may be very steep.  Some off trail hiking
may be needed.
Time: Allow 1.5 to 2 hours to complete.

Clues:

Make your way to Suspuehannock State Park in Lancaster County,
Pennsylvania.  The park is located in the Southern portion of the county
just off PA 372 and overlooks the Susquahanna River.  The park is named
after the native Susquehannock Indians, of which none actually remain.
On the parks grounds is the Landis House, whose sixth owner, Jacod
Schoff, participated in smuggling escaped slaves through the
"Underground Railroad."  The park has two overlooks, the southern of
which overlokks St. Johnson Island, the world's first bald eagle
sancuary.  The northern overlook looks north towards the Route 372
bridge and Holtwood Dam.  Start here.

Follow the orange blazes downhill.  When you come to a "T" intersection,
head left.

Soon you will come to a trail that forbids horses, follow this trail.

When given the choice to go up or down, take the high road.

Once you make it to the flowing water, follow the trail as it heads
upstream.

Soon, you will encounter a boulder in the path.  Behind the boulder and
across the path is a small tree with a blaze on it pearched atop a
natural pile of rocks.  Under this tree you will find a crevase with a
rock stuffed into it.  Behind this rock you will find your prize.

Enjoy!
