Letterboxing project Northern Virginia

From: theendgang | Date: 2005-11-20 00:49:42 UTC
Today I embarked on a project that I have long wanted to do. I am
working on a letterbox series that will promote a renewed look at the
Civil War that so horribly divided this nation. I will be planting
boxes at different points of interest both well known and some quite
forgotten. They will cover many varied aspects of the war. My interest
in this conflict started at a young age when hearing of My Great-Great
Grandfather's fighting for the Union in the 3rd and 15th New York
Volunteer Cavalry. Some boxes will relate to his diaries. There is no
specific number of boxes to this series. Clues will be posted in
various forms/forums, some public and some not so public. There will be
some that are WOM only. They will vary in difficulty. There will likely
be some twists and surprises along the way.
This is a large project and therefore I would encourage anyone who
wishes to contribute to this, to do so. I will gladly plant a box for
you, or please, plant one with this theme at a location near you.
I planted the first installment today, "Battle of Cool Spring". I
have 8 more ready to go and will continue to add to them. I hope you
all enjoy and find it an interesting adventure. I wish you all safe
trails. Koyote

Dave,
   Wonderful to here from you again! Hope you had a great Thanksgiving.
   I know there were a number of guerilla units, or "partisans" operating in various theaters of the war. I'm not sure specifically to the areas you mentioned, though I will try and find out. Turner Ashby and Mosby were the big ones here in Northern Va. (Loudoun County and Shenandoah County). I haave carve  stamps for both those officers. Ashby's Cavalry was the 7th Virginia Cavalry which was absorbed into the "Laurel Brigade" after he was killed in a firefight near Harrisonburg in 1862. He is buried in Winchester. I would like to do a stamp on Chew's Battery which was part of Ashby's unit. I am also hoping to do a stamp for the Mt. Zion Church (Mosby's meeting place) and Aldie Mill where a significant engagement was fought with Mosby's men.
   Any participation you want to do is fine. I am not able to get in West Virginia much other than occasionally going to Harper's Ferry, which is one of my favorite places. I will attach a WOM I have there in case you ever travel through. It's a tough one physically, but beautiful!
   Let me know what you would like to do. Safe trails.
