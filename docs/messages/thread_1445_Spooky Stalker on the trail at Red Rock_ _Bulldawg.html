Spooky Stalker on the trail at Red Rock! (Bulldawg...was that you today????)

From: gallant_rogue | Date: 2005-12-04 05:02:18 UTC
I'd just finished logging in to a "WOM" LBox when I noticed that I
was being watched. There was a young gentleman nonchalantly "looking
at the trees" strolling on the main trail maybe forty yards from me.
He was way too close for me to try to replace the box I was holding.
I practice discreet letterboxing, so I had the box twenty or thirty
yards away from the hollow stump hiding spot.

"No big deal" I thought and I waited for the gentlemen to move on.
However he appeared to be waiting to see exactly what I was doing.

There is a beautiful river view another ten minutes down the trail
but there was absolutely nothing of interest within 100 yards of
where we were and I was far enough off the trail that I did think
any reasonable person would pay attention to me. But this guy was
obviously checking me out as he was doing a very poor job disguising
it. There was a nearby normal letterbox but he wasn't moving toward
it and I did not see a backpack/fannypack or any place for a log,
inkpad and stamp.

I thought maybe this was the mysterious Bulldawg whom I never met...
but he made no gesture or signal I recognized as a Letterboxer
trying to communicate. So I just decided to "wait him out". I stayed
put while the man feigned interest in the bare empty tree tops.

In a Mexican Stand off neither of us moved for several minutes which
seemed like hours. I stood still and faced him. He made no move and
contiued to gaze up at the trees like there was something of
interest (but there was not).

Finally, seeing that I was not moving... nor pretending to do
anything, not making an attempt to visit, the gentleman then left
the trail and came over to me. Making small talk he mentioned the
cold weather.(Why are you walking over here? I thoight) It then
occurred to me this person was not Bulldawg, not any Letterboxer. It
suddenly seemed so very odd for him to be approaching me off the
trail. Is this guy going to pull a gun and rob me? Mentally I
practiced using my hiking pole as a sword.

He moved toward my right. I turned to face him keeping him directly
in front of me. I asked "Are you waiting for me?" He said no and
stopped advancing... and slowly moved away, but continued watching.

I still dont know if this was Bulldawg a cop or a robber... but I
left the area with the box in my pack, doubling back 30 minutes
later to replace the box. By now the man was gone

If this was Bulldawg... I'm sorry we did figure out who each other
was... if it wasn't Bulldawg... this was downright creepy!
