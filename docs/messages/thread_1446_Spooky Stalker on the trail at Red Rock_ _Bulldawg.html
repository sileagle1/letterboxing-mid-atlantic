I have been to Red Rock many times, and have never had, or heard of a problem. That is a very high traffic park as it is so close to neighborhoods. I always look at it from another point... It's the boxer that looks suspicious. We are the ones poking around stumps, rocks, or whatever, and off trail at times. The same with more remote areas. I was out planting a box at...well, at a place where "controlled hunting" is practiced. Hunting was clearly not allowed at the time I was on trail. I still covered my pack with blaze orange and glad I did. The sound of very close gunfire made it obvious someone was not concerned with particular times to hunt. SAFE TRAILS.

"I shudder to think if this was a lone female letterboxer... "


Actually, this happened to me this summer. Some of you might have seen
the post about it on Mark Pepe's site. I actually was geocaching in an
area less than a mile from my house...and bordering a busy
neighborhood. As I was logging in, a man came up behind me and
was "pleasuring himself." When I attempted to leave, he blocked the
path, wouldn't allow me to leave and rubbed himself on me. I got away
and they caught him based on my description of him and a license plate
on a truck parked by the trail head. A passerby found me blubbering by
the side of the road and called the police.

The worst thing was, he was a repeat sexual offender who didn't even
live anywhere near there. He asked for a trial and got one, thinking I
would back out, but I called his bluff and agreed to testify. He ended
up pleading guilty to the Public Lewdness charge with a one year
probation and the sexual assault charge was dropped. I have a one year
restraining order against him. Although I don't know him, I don't want
to take any chances.

For two weeks, I wouldn't leave the house. I was so creeped out. I
finally ventured forth again, but I am now armed and ALWAYS aware of my
surroundings. If I had seen your creepy guy, and he hadn't moved away
but approached instead, I would have shouted at him like a crazy
person and got the hell out of there.

Just thought I should warn any of you who might approach me mid-trail
for an exchange. *grin*
