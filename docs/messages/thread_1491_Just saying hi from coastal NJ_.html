Just saying hi from coastal NJ.

From: Adam | Date: 2006-01-04 18:29:09 UTC
We just found our first letterboxes last week. Now we are hooked. We
started by taking the kids to look for Mosquito Madness and found it
just as the sun was dropping below the horizon. I sent the kids back
down the trail while I finished stamping in and replacing the box, but
we ended up driving out of the park after dark anyway. The warning is
correct, by the way. It was the end of december and we still saw
mosquitos (albeit very sluggish ones).

We followed this up with Terra-cotta Tangram a couple days later. Lots
of fun and a fascinating site.

We used some store-bought stamps that we had on hand to get started
and still need to make our own. Does anyone have any suggestions as to
what type of material is best?

I think we are on our way to being addicted even though we are just
starting. We are travelling to visit family in PA and northern NJ this
weekend and already trying to figure out how to fit in a letterbox
search between family obligations. We have also started planning
locations to plant our own boxes.

Looking forward to more fun searches,
Adam from Bricktown

--- In lbma@yahoogroups.com, "Adam" <atlantesnj@y...> wrote:
Does anyone have any suggestions as to
> what type of material is best?

Check out www.stampeaz.com for PZ Kut. I've used the ORANGE Grade "A"
and it worked a charm. And it is much less expensive than "pink
stuff" so starting out you can afford to make more carving mistakes.

But if you can't find the "pink stuff" at Michaels (craft store)then
go to www.pearlpaint.com - they have stores in Paramus and
Woodbridge. They don't carry the "pink stuff" on their web site.

I also recommend an Xacto XPL5204 Stencil Knife. http://www.x-
actoblades.com/xacto/xpage4.htm for a picture. I've bought at Total
Crafts - but I'm sure any local craft/hobby store could/should
order/carry them.

Hope to meet you at a gather - have you checked out www.atlasquest.com?

Welcome to the addiction, er... I mean, hobby, pastime, sport, however

have changed their signature stamps several times as a result of their
efforts and are just about ready to change them again...
