Status in Richmond, VA

From: genealogy_va | Date: 2006-01-09 19:24:00 UTC
Hello,

The Knights of Columbus meeting for Virginia, brought me to Richmond
over the weekend. Good meeting, as these things go. I stayed the
night at the Holiday Inn Select nearby, and spent the evening
researching more letterboxes than I had already planned for Sunday.

Status:

Huguenot Park (0 for 3)
Struck out looking for the Huguenot Cross, Huguenot Knot and
Huguenot Dove. My handwritten notes were no match for the
numerous "T" and "Y" intersections in the park. Will try again over
the summer with printed copies of the clues. Nice park though,
right next to the hotel. Wonderful playground for kids on it.

Robious Landing Park (5 for 7)
This is right on the James River, and it is a nice park. Tons of
folks out walking their dogs.

Robious Landing Park LB--needs new logbook. Existing one is
stuffed, and folks are stamping in on a sheet of paper in the
letterbox. Replaced 2 split ziplocks, otherwise in good shape.

Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone: The Castle--new baggie for
the inkpad. Picked up Celtic Quinn's "Mario's Mushrooms: The 1Up
Mushroom HH".

Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone: Sorting Hat--the location
for this one is pretty clear--but no letterbox.

Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone: Snitch--spent a lot of time
looking for this one. Couldn't find a tree with the right knots.
Maybe my handwritten notes again? No box.

Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone: Fluffy--found, and in good
shape. After confirming this one, used the clues to go back for
Snitch again. Still no luck.

Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone: Wizards Chess--new bag for
the inkpad. Pad should be removed, it's making a real mess.
Logbook and stamp in good shape though.

Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone: Sorcerer's Stone--tricky,
but found it as I was throwing in the towel. In good shape, and a
perfect spot.

Deep Run Park (6 for 7)
Wow, what a park. For a country fellow who thinks a baseball
diamond and a place for the kids to play is a park, this was
something. Fountains, nature center, trails, trails, trails.

Food Fight #1, #2, #3 and #4--all in good shape, and a great walk.
Replaced a baggie on #1, and the stump is about shot. Might need to
move it soon. Otherwise, it's a neat 3 year old series.

Donovan's Travels--spent too much time looking for this one, without
success. Knights of Columbo found it a couple of weeks earlier, so
I know it's there somewhere. I knew it was time to move on when
organizing all the fallen pines into a pile started to seem like a
viable option.

Dan the Man's Cool Boxes: Underneath the Tree with White Marks--
somehow I thought looking for this one after Donovan's would get me
back on track--it seemed easy enough. I found it, but only after
circling 187 trees--it must have looked like a madman. My apologies
to all the moms with small kids who were terrified of the big man
muttering to himself on the playground. It could use new bags, but I
was out.

Dan the Man's Cool Boxes: Triple Rocks Box--yep, it's there. All
is well, and might be the smallest stamp image I've found.

There are a lot more boxes in this park, but they will have to wait
for my next Richmond trip in June.

11 for 17 attempted, or 12 for 18 (67%) if you count the hitchhiker.

Richmond is lucky to have these great parks available.

Thanks,
FamilyMan
