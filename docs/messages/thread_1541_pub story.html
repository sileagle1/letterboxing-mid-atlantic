pub story

From: Lorie Thomas | Date: 2006-01-19 14:55:02 UTC
I knew no one and was known to no one. That's how I
got the first piece of the scavenger hunt before the
games got started. PI assumed that the woman on the
corner barstool was just checking out his hot bod when
actually she was ogling the other goodies. ;-)

The Catoctin Crawlers saw every bar except Tuscarora.
That stamp was acquired by half the team while Koyote
marked territory. It's the only pub stamp I'm missing
:-(

After Spanky's we hit Champions, then Red Hot & Blue -
we got stuck here for awhile as the game wound down.
From there we hit Thoroughbreds, probably the only bar
I'll easily be able to avoid on future trips due to
slow service and REALLY high prices, although the beer
sampler was tasty. I recommend the 3 middle beers,
the darkest and lightest of the 5 are just too dark
(tastes like coffee) and light. And don't order a
mixed drink unless you've just won the lottery!

We really got rolling after that. We all had a great
time at Bacchus and I'd love to get back there to try
their menu. That bartender was fabulous.

What's 3.14? A Bible verse? A mile marker? The
length of who's what?
IT'S PI!!! (you idiots!) - Leave it to the teacher to
help us all get our heads out of our butts! We just
were not getting it on our own even though we knew we
needed to go to Mom's Apple Pie. Doh!

The 2 drive-bys were fairly uneventful and quick but
the group really wanted to get to the historic
district, so the team split up to grab Tuscarora and
we met up again at Lightfoots.

Fancy, fancy - neat old building. The last 3 bars are
kind of a blur. Despite the smog, I mean smoke, in
the Downtown Saloon I'd've loved to stay. The band
was fun, the pool tables were active, and the people
were having a great time.

Koyote - That drink in King's Court was a soco and
lime. Also, the backwards dart board stamp came from
Ball's Bluff.

Our team would have done better on the scavenger hunt
if we hadn't been so rocked! Our strategies kinda
fell apart after Lightfoot when the team shrank and
babysitters had to be released. I didn't realize it
was getting so late.

I got 4 of the bone-us boxes and lots of personal
stamps. I even lucked out and got a series that is
soon to be released into the wilds of NJ (I think it's
NJ). Fabulous stamps all around the town! There's
some amazing artists.

The cab ride to the hotel was interesting too. I got
to finish my beer and help close the bar while Koyote
and D of DJ2A grabbed a cab. The bartender fetched me
a cab and it happened to include Koyote and D! How'd
that happen? I was confused but pleased and soon
snoring soundly until the phone rang and HNH and SSB
were asking about breakfast. UGH, it's still the
middle of the night isn't it?

sigh, so up I got, got out of the hotel and joined
those 2 for breakfast at Johnson's. Got another
stamp, had a light breakfast, and headed down the
road. I did end up grabbing a nap about an hour up
the road but was good to go after that.

Thanks again to Poison Ivy for a great time and not
TOO bad of a hangover!

Pictures will follow.
