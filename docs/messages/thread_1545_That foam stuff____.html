I don't recall that being a LBMA topic, but the product makes me think
that maybe the kinda foam used to cut kid kraft shapes could be used
as a stamp mount when combined with a hard surface (wood or acrylic).

difficult to get 100% of the stamp image in the log (unless doing the
printer's method of paper on stamp and rub rather than stamp on paper
and press.) Adding a layer of the cheap foam you buy in sheets, might
be a solution. I usually just use 1/4" foam core board which helps
some.

From: MaryAnn Lockard | Date: 2006-01-23 11:07:04 UTC
No it's not. This is for unmounted rubber die which
are much thinner than carving material. This stuff is
used when using acrylic blocks for a temporary mount
so you don't have to have a block for every stamp you
have. I have a bunch of stamps in my personal
collection I use this way, for art not boxing. I know
what you want but can't think of the name of it. I'll
see if I can it find somewhere.
