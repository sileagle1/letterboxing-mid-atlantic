From: BullDawg | Date: 2006-01-24 01:28:12 UTC
Ok, i am focusing a lot of 2006 on one series that i hope to be
nationwide..and consist of long hikes that require lots of
effort...the details of the seies are yet to come to the public,
but i have so far carved 4 detailed stamps for it, with hikers willing
to plant in NY, oregon, texas, and CT....This will be my focus for
this season, and looking for more hiders of the series (up to 20
stamps)..in return, i can hide a stamp for you in the VA area..this
series will be something different, with focus on the entire
adventure..the hike, the clues, the box, the stamp, and the
book.....These stamps are not to be found by the weekend boxer
looking for a quick find. The best finds are those you earn..Too much
is placed on the "count" (IMO) Quality over quanity...Its a
topic that I love, and again, want to take this to the next
level...along with this, i will do 2 more series in NoVA with "cops
and robbers"..and still have a meeting with Koyote on wed..(with
beers, no doubt)..to start planning a great "event" for Northern
Virginia....that will again, be something
unique....

Im hoping Poison Ivy will plant one for me, in his extreme style
inWest Va, or where he choses....I am trying to get the cream of the
crof involved....Maybe evn Koyote will plant one on his longer hikes,
being crazy like he is...Some of the planters planting so far are
pretty impressive...stay tuned, more details to come, on my site

Peace.....
"the man with the long sleeve white T-shirt, blue jeans, 4-striped
sneaker, and black coat" ....look deep in your logbook, boxers

Bulldawg

From: Hikers_n_ Hounds | Date: 2006-01-25 23:27:57 UTC
Before I get flamed, I am only posting out of concern. Everyone has a right to do whatever they feel is right, and we all know that we box at our own risk, but I can't stress enough how dangerous caves can be to the non-caver. Hell, even experienced, knowledgeable cavers die in caves. But the majority of cave deaths are by non-cavers. People who are not experienced, nor are members of a grotto (an organized caving club). Every year the National Speleological Society publishes a book of that lists all injuries and fatalities and sends to it's members. It's very sobering reading. For more information about cave safety check out this link:
 
http://www.caves.org/safety/
 
And for more information about caves in general and joining a grotto in you rarea visit caves.org
 
I am not lecturing, I just really care about you guys!
 
H&H

I agree and said that in my email below, we all box at our own risk. And I am the LAST person to try to impose my will, or my personal boxing prefernces on anyone. I think boxing should be organic and continue to evolve and grow. I get frustrated with some boxers who complain that boxing has changed and long for the "good old days" when boxers did things a certain way. If you compare us to England, ours is a very different hobby. My only concern is for safety. Even experienced cavers die in caves. Bad air, equipment failure, falls, rock slides, surface rainstorms and cave flooding,  getting lost and hypothermia are just some of the reasons why.So you're all correct, know your limits and listen to your gut. If it doesn't feel right, get the hell out of there.

H&H makes a good point, but one that should be considered on any box. I believe Ryan expressed that on AQ when he set up the section for extreme boxes. You MUST know what you are getting into and what is needed to complete a box. I have boxes that are not for boxers looking for a "lovely jaunt" in the woods. I make no bones about caution needed when it is due. If the box seems too complicated...leave it be. I like traffic at my boxes, but I also like that someone has made a big effort to complete my more difficult boxes. If a box is out for a year or more before it gets a hit than so be it.
   Irene, no flame my dear, it is good to remind us from time to time of safety. And hence, why I always end with... Safe trails.
                                                        Koyote

All kidding aside...as long as it comes with the clear "advisory" I
think it's OK create all & varying degrees of difficulty. Of course
we all want to to avoid leading someone into an extreme or
uncomfortable situation without notice.

That being said... BullDawg's "18 years"... mystery WOM... is
slightly extreme. I spent weeks studying the clue before solving it!
I spent so much time researching that I predicted exactly what the
stamp image was.... once I solved five other box challenges to get
the final clue... I was able to finally verify the correct location
(had to make three trips from Woodbridge to Leesburg which is 30 +
miles each way)... ) and when I arrived at the location I was not
going to back down... but there was an element of extremism. I was a
little uncomfortable and I had to do something I don't normally
associate with letterboxing. ( OK I'd do it again... but there was a
momement I thought I was outside my comfort zone and I could
understand if somebody else might back down)
