Scarab's right, I am a local now...

From: Hikers_n_ Hounds | Date: 2006-01-30 00:48:48 UTC
Just got back from yet another weekend in Ol' Virginny. I have spent a lot of time in both VA and WV but much further south, where the big holes in the ground are (caves). Never spent much time in the northern end of either state until recently. I am having a blast. Great folks, great boxes, great trails.
 
Saturday I drove down to attend Gallant Rogue's 50th Birthday Bash with Sowsewbizzy and had a great time. Got to see Knights of Columbo who I haven't seen since the Mystery Gather, Scarab and Nightowl, Gallant Rogue and Doxie Dolly. The food was incredible, the band was good and we had fun. I did miss my dancing partner though, Mr. Irene, but GR took for for a whirl or two so I got to kick up my heels a little.
 
Sunday I was supposed to exchange with Bulldog but, oh well, but I did see that UFO though so at least one myth has been laid to rest. SSB and I went to Great Falls Park, where she had never been, and poked around there for a bit. It was raining lightly but I don't mind a little drizzle. Keeps the people away. Then we headed to Goose Creek and got ADG's Rusted Butterfly. Does anyone know what that structure used to be? Judy and I were guessing either a grist mill, a lock or maybe a pump house? It's pretty cool.   
 
Then I headed north just in time for "Prairie Home Companion" on NPR. Great show as usual. Hit the Philly area airwaves in time for "Mountain Stage" another of my favorite radio programs.
 
All in all a wonderful weekend with good friends, good music and good food. Doesn't get any better than that!

All sorts of updates

From: Nathan Brown | Date: 2006-01-31 02:25:53 UTC
Well, I have not been too busy of late, but have gotten a few boxes found in the past couple of weeks.

Two weeks ago I road up to Doylestown to go to a friends house.  I got "Boxers or Briefs" (http://www.atlasquest.com/lboxes/clue.html?gBoxId=11861) on the way over, but managed to leave my personal stamp at home, so I'll have to head back there again to stamp in.  It had a nice stamp, but I don't get it???  I met Cetic Cat and Wednsday Bowler on trail, they were after the same box.

I was going to try to meet up with those two again on Sunday, along with Swallowdale, but I simply lost track of time and never made it.

This Saturday I was out at Fort Indiantown Gap, and stopped at Memorial Lake State Park to find "Luck Be A Lady" (http://www.letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?boxnum=17934&boxname=Luck_Be_A_Lady).  Store bought stamp, but a nice walk that gave me some ideas ;-)

Today I checked on some boxes.  "A Nice View" is still in fine shape over looking Berks County.  My Pagoda box was fine, but it seems Penn Tower is missing.  So, I pulled the whole series, and will replant shortly with all new stamps and locations.
