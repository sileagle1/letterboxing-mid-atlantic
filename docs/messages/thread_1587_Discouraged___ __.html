Your frustration is completely understandable. Placing boxes is one of
those things that you just learn a knack for over time, after placing a
few and finding even more. I think everyone here can recount the
mistakes they have made planting boxes and how they have learned from
them. Here are a few of the tips I have picked up over time:

-The smaller, the better. A small stamp in a small box can often do the
same job as a large one, with the added benefit that they can fit in
more places and are easier to conceal.

-Keep it away from trails. Unless you have a specific reason for
planting a box very close to a trail or other heavily used area, it is
best to place the box a few hundred feet into the woods, and use
landmarks or compass work to guide people to the boxes. Personally,
boxes that are hidden in the middle of no where are often my favorites
to find as well.

-Camouflage helps. You can get camo netting in Walmart or other stores
that sell outdoors gear. It really helps in hiding the box.

-Survey the area. Is the area that you want to plant in heavily used?
Is there a lot of trash or bottles around that make it a teen hang out?
Are there signs of flood damage? If it is winter, is there a lot of
dead foliage on the ground? These are questions to ask yourself when
you are looking over the area you want to place your box in.

-Plant off the ground if you can. This will help keep the box safe from
flooding and dry. If you can't, good places on the ground are in tree
roots with rocks over the box, or under boulders with rocks in front of
the box. Stone walls also make great hiding spots. What ever you do,
use rocks to cover the box, they will help secure it from water and animals.

-Don't get discouraged. Boxes go missing for all sorts of reasons.
Don't get discouraged by it. Just have fun!
