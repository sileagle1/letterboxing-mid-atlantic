From: nattybumppolbna | Date: 2006-02-25 15:50:36 UTC
I was warned about a couple of my National Parks boxes by a fellow 'boxer out west.
Apparently they're getting pretty savvy and check the sites and web frequently for boxes
placed in their little fiefdoms.

One suggestion I have used is to not put the actual name of the park - or of any specific
features (i.e. "Old Faithful") in the clue. It won't stop a ranger from cruising lb.org or AQ,
but it will foil a Google search.

If you are web savvy another way to foil them which I have used, is to put the clue in a
picture. Here's one example I have out in the open:
http://www.members.aol.com/nattybumppolbna/postcard.html
The words of the clue are part of the .jpg not html so Google doesn't read them as words,
and thus , doesn't log them for search.

Rather than delete the clues, you can move them and make Google link a dead link. If you
rework the clue - perhaps renaming it in a subtle way - and then delete the original - it
will come up dead on Google. Of course it will live on in other resources, but it should
keep out casual searchers - like your reporter.

Of course, I don't advocate placing letterboxes in any place where they are illegal,
unauthorized or unwelcome.

Natt-
