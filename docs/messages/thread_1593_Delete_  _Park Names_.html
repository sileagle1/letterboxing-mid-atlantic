> This brings up another train of thought, mentioning the
> name of parks and other landmarks in clues. Good idea, bad idea?

My first few boxes included park names, but I got a few emails from
people asking for info about the park, (e.g. how to rent a shelter,
etc.). This made me realize that anyone looking for park info was
coming across my clues which I did NOT think was a good idea.

I now only make an indirect reference to the park name (for
example: "fill in blank for park name: Old Mother _______ went to the
cupboard to get her poor dog a bone"), but oftentimes also include an
address or directions to the park. In other words, it's not a mystery
box, it just doesn't specifically call out the name of the park to
avoid having the clues show up on Google results.

Wisconsin Hiker
