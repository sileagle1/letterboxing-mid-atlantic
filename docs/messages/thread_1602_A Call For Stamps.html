A Call For Stamps

From: nattybumppolbna | Date: 2006-03-01 20:47:51 UTC
First: If you haven't voted in the poll to determine the Rally date, please do so now.
Thanks.

If this Road Rally Thing Happens... and I'm hoping it does... I am going to need some
help.

Like last year, I'd like to put the word out for carvers to donate stamps to the cause. We
had some really beautiful work last year and I would hate to penalize the participants by
inflicting only my work on them. So if anyone is interested in carving a stamp for the
Rally, please let me know.

I was pleased with the results of the broad theme of "cars." It gave us a great variety:
everything from sculptured hood ornaments to political statements to action and
adventure and puzzles. So I will leave that as the theme for this year's rally.

If any of last year's stamps have not been planted elsewhere, they would also be welcome.

Speaking of donations... If you have a prize you would like to donate to the cause, please
contact me. True Indigo and RGB were very gracious in (quite unexpectedly) donating
prizes to last year's rally and made the stamp prize and the Humanitarian award possible.
And no, that's not why they won.

I have come up with a way to open the rally up to more competitors this year. My biggest
fear - which came to pass - was bunching up at boxes. It's hardly fair to those who must
seek and find for others to just follow you in to the box. Perhaps my new method will let
more people play and still keep it fair.

Other developments for this year:

No bullet-proof vests needed.
No tropical storms allowed.
More awards.
More devilry.

If the folks who attended last year have any comments, criticisms suggestions please feel
free to contact me off-list so I may improve the event for this year.


All the best,

Natt-

An interesting idea. I wish I could come up with a better way, but here are my concerns:

My first and foremost concern for this is safety. Anything having to do with driving cars is
just asking for trouble. So I must balance my desire to let everyone play with reducing the
chances for mayhem.

After driving the route many, many times last year I still got one of the directions wrong
on the instructions. Go figure. Despite otherwise good directions we still had missed
turns, U-turns, flat tires and other difficulties in the biggest rain storm of all time.
(perhaps an overstatement, but not by much)

Mathematically, if we have 20 teams going for 8-10 boxes, it virtually guarantees
bunching up with a shotgun start. And as great minds think alike, I believe that many
teams would pick the most efficient route and increase the bunch up. In addition, if you
all leave at once, who will keep me company in my lonely, lonely cabin by the lake?

I liked the way everybody had time to hang out and visit while waiting to go or waiting for
the teams to return. It was fun to see people cheer the returning teams as they crossed
the finish line.

MaryAnn, please don't feel I'm picking on you. Quite a few people have expressed similar
concerns off-list. I think I have a way for as many as 25 teams to play, and I think that is a
manageable number for me. If people can start earlier we can get more teams in, as we
have a hard out-time at the park.

So stick with me. If this way doesn't work, we may have to make it a TRUE Road Rally
where the competitors must travel from a start point to an unknown destination by
directions alone. Now THAT would be mayhem!

Natt-

Does that work? I guess I got about 10,000 miles of reverse to do before I trade in my
van.

Like a Road Rally, accuracy is favored over speed in this contest.

Knowing the exact route and location of the letterboxes, I run the route prior to the rally.
In theory, since I know where I am going, I should have the "perfect" time. If someone
does it faster (in theory) they must be speeding or cheating. The winner of the rally is the
team that comes closest without going UNDER the judges "perfect" time. So you can go
fast if you like, but if you beat the judges, (or are observed speeding or driving illegally or
dangerously by our mysterious friend) you lose.

Mileage was taken only to be used as a tie breaker, so spin your wheels all you want.

As someone else noted, by the time the third or fourth crew got to the second-to-last box
last year, they didn't have to follow the complex compass headings, they just followed the
footsteps in the rain-soaked grass. There will always be some unforeseen, unfair element
to this hybrid "sport." So I will have to beg your forgiveness and remind you that if you are
only in it for the trophy, you might be missing the point of the game. (Although they were
mighty fine trophies, if I do say so myself)

I think we might be able to do as many as 20 teams this year. I think more than that
might be unmanageable for many reasons.
