Report: Wolf Gap, Guardian of Old Schloss, Iron Furnace

From: Todd White | Date: 2006-03-19 17:02:35 UTC
Hello,
 
Yesterday, my 9 year-old son and 12 year-old daughter joined me for a climb up to Old Schloss, in very rural Shenandoah County.  Unfortunately, both of these boxes (Guardian of Old Schloss and Wolf Gap) seem to be missing.
 
But it is a spectacular hike, if you are so inclined.  And it could sorely stand some letterboxes up there, which I'll think about if I ever recover.  4.4 miles round trip--2.5 hours up, 1 hour down.
 
I wouldn't bring any younger children, though.  The views, and the drop-offs, are spectacular.
 
Also, Iron Furnace at the Elizabeth Furnace area near Front Royal, VA, has only had one finder outside of my family (thank you Cyclonic).  It's a great place to spend a few family hours, and plenty of trails nearby in need of more boxes. Easy to find, too.
 
Thanks,
FamilyMan

South Orange NJ - Boxes Retired/Planted

From: Hillel | Date: 2006-03-19 19:54:22 UTC
So I always was a little nervious with the "Boy Scout Area Waterfalls" box, because it seemed to be hard for people to get to sometimes.  Then yesterday I went to check on it, and its a good thing I did, because the box was just sitting there completely uncovered out in the open.  I decided to retire it.
 
However I did have a series of 5 boxes (4 plus 1 bonus), in my backpack at the time, so I decided to just plant that in the same park.  The clues have been posted to LBNA and Atlas Quest, so happy hunting.  Keep in mind some of these boxes are Microboxes so they may be a bit smaller than what you are looking for.
