I have received a couple of mails about what transpired to get this
box as this was a thread a while back. I post the update here.

I suppose I should comment/update on the "Loudoun County Letterboxes
pulled" thread of a month or so ago. I met with the Park manager a
while back after she contacted me that she pulled all of my boxes and
those of Bulldawg's. She has pulled boxes from ADG and DJ2 in the
past. At the meeting she expressed her concerns with boxes being in
sensitive areas in the park. A main point was just that she was not
informed of the boxes. She also has 2 sanctioned geocaches which are
not working out due to cachers coming in after hours and bushwhacking
into the woods from the road and at night. A sheriff's deputy
followed 2 cachers into the woods one night about a month a go and
just about arrested them until their story was explained and park
management was notified. They were lucky. The manager's policy right
now is to allow the single letterbox. Any other boxes found will be
pulled and the placer notified. She reads the logs and if any finds
indicate the boxer was in after hours or on holidays, the box and
it's contents are destroyed. She monitors the LBNA site regularly
(which is how we got snagged), and has met with a council of park
managers in northern virginia to inform them how to moniter for
letterboxes. She did indicate that some managers were not overly
worried, but most just did not know about boxing.
She had indicated to Bulldawg and I that she would consider
a "sanctioned " letterbox. I met with her and proposed a box that
would promote either the historic or nature aspect of the park. My
plan was,and is, to have a sanctioned box which Pam, the park
manager, assists in planting. We actually hiked for the plant
together. I then suggested that the clues be only available at the
visitor center and they be numbered so that the amount of traffic to
the box can be monitored. This way the box casn be moved if there is
too much traffic to the site which is off trail. She became very
enthusiastic about the project and has printed out a nice brochure
for the clues. She is also having the park's web site updated to list
letterboxing as one of the "programs". The brochure also gives some
letterboxing "rules" and etiquette, so new boxers can be given a good
start. The box is limited ( this one for about a year) at which point
it will be retired and I will make new one with a different theme and
clues.
Future boxes will be for less amount of time. This one is out
longer due to it being the anniversary year of the French and Indian
War, and the park has a number of activities planned with that theme
including a re-enactment.
It worked out great. I get to plant boxes "legally", and she gets
a new family activity to promote the park. I hope this is a stepping
stone to allowing for 1 or 2 more boxes at the park.
Safe trails,
