Weekend trip to Philadelphia -- time to letterbox?

From: cpascott | Date: 2004-06-29 14:35:30 UTC
Hi all! I've joined this group temporarily to ask a question about
letterboxes in Philadelphia. My GF and I are travelling to Philly
for the Independence Day weekend. Although I've been to Philly a
number of times, my GF hasn't spent any time there, and thus we'll be
doing a lot of the traditional tourist things during the day.

We have both discovered letterboxing over the last few weeks, and
would enjoy finding a couple of Philadelphia letterboxes while were
there, although (as aluded to) our time available to letterbox will
be short. I figure that once museums, etc. close, we may have some
time in the evenings, or, if we have enough energy, early mornings.

I'm aware of some letterboxes in Philly from the LbNA database,
specifically:
* The three Space Traveler boxes in Wissahickon Park.
* The 5-box Ben Franklin Series
* The Eastern State Peniteniary letterbox
* America's First Zoo letterbox

We're most intrigued by the Ben Franklin series, but wish to know how
realistic it is to find some or all of these boxes for a weekend
traveller staying in the city without a car. We'll be staying at the
Club Quarters in Independence Park.

I think we're planning to go to the Eastern State Peniteniary anyway,
so we'll look for that one, but having more than one would be nice!

Is the Ben Franklin series possible for our scenario? Can it be done
after hours (evenings or even at night)? Lastly (and most
important), does anyone know the status of these boxes? Given the
time constraints, we certainly do not want to hunt for boxes that are
MIA!

Many thanks!

CPAScott and Anjabanja
Weymouth and Brookline, MA

Those boxes are all there, but as for not having a car..don't know.
It'd be a LONG walk or bike ride. And I don't know how you'd get
there without a car, other than SEPTA, but as an out-of-towner you
probably don't want to use public transport. I wouldn't get these at
night, either...some parts of the park (lower than where I placed the
boxes, but they're still part of the park) can be pretty dangerous at
night.

I'd go for the Wissahickon Park ones - you can walk to all 3, and
they're a nice walk :)

There's also one in the Manayunk section of Philadelphia, accessible
by the R6 Norristown train, called "Boards and Bridges".

FdL
