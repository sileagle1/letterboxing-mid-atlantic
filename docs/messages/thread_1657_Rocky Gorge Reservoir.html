Rocky Gorge Reservoir

From: wood thrush | Date: 2006-04-04 14:00:55 UTC
My contribution on some older boxes/what I did this weekend...

For a few months I have been working on the series of boxes planted
at Rocky Gorge Reservoir (Laurel/Scaggsville, Md.) in 2002 by
aweehedgehog and dewlovers. Of the eight boxes listed at LBNA, I
have now found five, safe and sound and in good shape. They have not
had much traffic over the years, but they are to be recommended.

Some notes:
You'll have to be a bit of an archaeologist; many of the landmarks
are now altered or disappeared---but things can be worked out.

Both "Scotts Cove" boxes survive, as do all three "Supplee Lane at
Rocky Gorge Reservoir" boxes.

The three that have been giving me fits make up the series "Rocky
Gorge Reservoir." Again, the landmarks can no longer be found; I am
fairly certain I've been in the right locations, but all three have
eluded me.

I don't *think* either placer is still in the game, but I could be
wrong about that. Has anyone found or attempted these three boxes
recently and could share insights?

Many thanks, and if you get out this way, I think you'll enjoy the
challenge of finding the five boxes that I know still remain!

wood thrush

My next "older boxes" challenge is Chessie Lover's early 2003

the Duckett Dam. I've negotiated the four "Easter Bunny Trail" boxes
(with mixed results), but "The Dam Trail" five boxes are a real
challenge, involving some very faint trails, bushwhacking, steep
slopes, "jumping a ravine" and so on. I set out to try them but was

Thanks to both Whisper Foot and Martini Man. Both boxers who have
stamped in before me on many a box in Maryland. That must have been
some whirlwind tour you took in 2004, MM---your stamps are everywhere
in logs!

Re the "Dam Trail" boxes, I had the same feeling you did, MM---that it
would not be wise to attempt them. It just did not look like there was
any kind of real trail there, I had only gone 1 for 4 on the "Easter
Bunny Trail" and being so close downstream to the Duckett Dam really
did make me feel like it would be trespassing, what with 9-11 concerns
about dams, this being the D.C. metro area, and everything. I didn't
even get far enough along to see the "no tresspassing" signs and I
felt that way. I know the Duckett Dam (which you can see from I-95)
has recently been listed as one that authorities are especially
keeping an eye on.

As you wrote, it just didn't seem like a good idea at the time.

wood thrush

Also many thanks to you Donna! You are right about the penchant for
natural disaster in this area. I work at the Univ of Md and live in
Columbia, so the day the tornado came through in 2003 I drove home
right along its path while it was going through. Between the flooding
along the Patuxent (detrimental to the Easter Bunny Trail) and the
downed trees in the reservoir area, I live in a real weather magnet.

Let me also say that box 1 of the Easter Bunny Trail lives! So if
you'd like to stamp a piece of history and you're in the Laurel area,
there you go!

Also, I am looking forward to getting to some of your boxes "out west"
this coming spring and summer. And Dead Duck Chuck's stamp cracks me
up every time I run across it. :-)
