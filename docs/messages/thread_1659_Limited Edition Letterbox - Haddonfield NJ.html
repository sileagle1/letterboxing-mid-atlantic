Hi Folks:
This may not be everyone's cup of tea: but I am making a letterbox
available during a special event being held during Holy Week only.
This is a non-denominational Christian labyrinth experience. This
contemplative multimedia walk takes about one hour - you can read
more details below. I have led this project with a small group at
our church for the past 5 years. It is a wonderful experience. To
combine my love of this labyrinth and my love of letterboxing, I
decided to offer a special limited edition letterbox for
letterboxing visitors. Everyone is welcome to walk the labyrinth!
Details about the labyrinth follow. Letterboxers who choose to walk
the path may ask any Labyrinth worker for "Station 13" when they
have completed their walk. The Labyrinth Letterbox will only be
available during Labyrinth hours (see below). I plan to be there as
often as I can, so if you visit, do ask for Monica. If I am there, I
would love to exchange with you! If you can answer this question, I
will also share my personal traveler with you: What does the
word "ouroboros" mean?

The details about the event follow:
highlysung of Team SuddenTurkey

The Labyrinth: A Personal Journey

Holy Week, April 9 - 14, 2006

The labyrinth, an ancient symbol of renewal and transformation, is a
meditative, multi-media walk with activities along the way that help
you to focus on your own personal spiritual journey.

Lutheran Church of Our Savior, Parish Hall
204 Wayne Avenue, Haddonfield, NJ

Hours of Operation:

Palm Sunday, April 9: 12 PM - 4 PM

Monday, April 10: 2 - 8 PM

Tuesday, April 11: 2 - 8 PM

Wednesday, April 12: 6 - 8 PM

Maundy Thursday, April 13: 10 AM - 6 PM

Good Friday, April 14: 10 AM - 6 PM

Participants can come at any time during labyrinth hours. The CD-
guided walk takes about one hour. Meditations, background music and
instructions are on a CD, which you listen to on headphones while
carrying a personal CD player provided by the Labyrinth Team.

Call Monica at 856-429-5122 for more information. You may call to
reserve a time for your walk, but reservations are not required. If
you would like to bring a group (youth group, prayer group, etc.),
please call and we will set up a time to accommodate your
group.Invite your friends and family! There is no cost.

Questions and Answers about the Labyrinth experience.

What kind of activities are along the path? Will I feel
uncomfortable?

The activities are as simple as watching a video, planting a seed or
leaving your footprints in the sand. Of course, if you feel
uncomfortable with any activity you can feel free to skip it. Your
participation level is at your own discretion. No one will be
watching to see what you do. There is no right or wrong way to walk
the labyrinth.

I'm afraid I'll feel self-conscious, thinking other people are
watching me. Will I have to interact with others?

No. You travel through the canvas labyrinth design wearing a walkman-
style CD player and going at your own pace. Others going through the
labyrinth will be occupied with their own journey. If you like, at
the end of your journey you may stay to chat with others about your
experience or you may want to leave and continue your thoughts in
private. It's up to you.

Do I need to prepare in any way before coming? Should I bring
anything?

No advance preparation is needed. Just set aside one hour for
yourself. You will probably want to wear comfortable clothes, and
you will be invited to remove your shoes (if you like.) You may also
bring your own walkman-style CD player if you like. We will have
players available here for your use.

What will I get out of this labyrinth experience?

It really depends on what you want to get out of it. If you are
tired and stressed with the distractions of life, you might enjoy a
gentle hour of relaxation and reflection. If you are yearning for a
closer relationship with God, an hour of focus and contemplation may
help to bring the closeness you desire. If you are struggling with
emotional hurt and pain in your life, this hour can be a healing
experience, helping you to let go of pain and anger. If you're not
sure of what you're looking for, an open mind during your walk could
light a pathway to your soul!
