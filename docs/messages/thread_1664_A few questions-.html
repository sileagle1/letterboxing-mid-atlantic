From: Sara | Date: 2006-04-14 13:21:30 UTC
Hello,

I don't think we've posted here yet, but we've been reading along
for a week or so...

My name is Sara and my boyfriend is Dave and we are team
*HappilyEverAfter* .. I am from South Jersey (right outside Philly)
and the boyfriend is from Long Island. Right now we are finishing up
our fourth and final year at University of New Haven in CT.

So.. in a few months we hope to be boxing with the lot of you! :)

But for now.. we are spending all our free time boxing in CT.

So... if anyone wants to box in CT we know where a lot of boxes are
and of some great parks that are loaded. Do you know where in CT you
will be traveling? NE? NW? South? or just in general? The state is
bigger than it looks on paper! :) and there are probably more boxes
per square inch in CT than anywhere else!

Feel free to ask us anything about boxing CT.. we'd love to share
our favorites. 3 places worth checking out off the top of my head..

Barkhamsted, CT (two state parks and over 15 boxes)
Hamden, CT (Sleeping Giant Park is lovely! and over 8 boxes there)
Mattatuck State Park (Home of the leathermans cave & a couple boxes)

FYI - if you go to
http://dep.state.ct.us/burnatr/forestry/centen/letterbox.htm

CT State actually will send u a patch if you collect 5 of their 30
boxes (each in a different state park) TeamHappilyEverAfter is
shooting for all 30... then we get a walking stick compliments of
the state!

Hope that helps.. don't be afraid to ask for more info, I could talk
about boxin' for hours :)

Sara of *HappilyEverAfter*

I was in CT last summer.
We stopped at Mt. Tom and looked for Bray Loop
Metamorphasis - found the notebook but the stamp was
gone and things were scattered. Hopefully the owners
have replaced it.

We also found the LB on the Beau Bridges Trail.

Other than that we ended up in Vermont (Quechee Gorge)
and then stopped at Chicopee SP in Massachusetts on
the way home.

All 3 were good leg stretchers after several hours on
the road.

Happy hunting

From: warcat1976 | Date: 2006-04-15 01:32:11 UTC
If you want to visit a "letterboxers paradise", go to Mansfield CT.
The town is fabulous- lots of parks all close to each other, nice old
cemetarys, 3 popular old-time general stores (right out of the
movies). And lots and lots of boxes! You will love it! The stamps
are really wonderful-each one better than the next. The next town
over even has a drive-in moveie theatre with 3 screens. We picked up a
town map with all the streets on it and mapped out a great route.
