From: rallymastr | Date: 2006-04-29 14:07:55 UTC
It will be a little different this year. We will be at the Green Lane Ambulance Company. It
is right up the street from a small town park. Last year's setting was perfect, but there is a
bike ride in town I am desperately trying to avoid.

The rally consists of a meticulously described route to 5 - 12 locations throughout
Montgomery, Berks, & Lehigh counties. The route includes clues to boxes planted at those
locations a short distance from the parking area. The circuit takes between 2-3 hours to
complete. The objective is to complete the circuit as accurately as possible. The judges
have established a "perfect" run time, following all traffic rules, speeds, etc. You must
attempt to match the run time as close as possible without going under. Faster
contestants are disqualified. In addition there may be unannounced boxes along the route
that contestants can find for bonuses and prizes. I say "may be" because there were not
last year. I won't know if there will be this year either, but I like to keep my options open.
There may be additional bedevilment provided by other competitors that is not officially
sanctioned by the rally, but is fun nonetheless.
After your circuit, most people hang out and enjoy each other's company until the final run
has returned and the winners are announced.

So you tell me. Will your teenagers like it?
