From: wpastfarm | Date: 2006-04-22 02:29:30 UTC
Peters Valley is part of the Delaware Water Gap National Recreation
Area, on the Sussex County side. There are very few boxes in Sussex.
Not sure if the lack of boxes is due to lack of boxers in that
relatively sparsely populated part of the state, or because of the
federal parkland.

Either way, Peters Valley is worth a visit. Their fall fair used to
be at the valley -- looking at the website now, I see that it has been
moved to the Sussex Co fairgrounds. If you go, there is (or at least
used to be) a drive-in restaurant at the corner of Rts 15 and 206,
with decent hotdogs served by carhops on rollerskates.

I think the boxes that ARE in that area are all older boxes, from
back when LBing was an add-on activity for some very serious hikers.
Thus they are all on the very tippy-top of the Kittatinny Ridge. If
I ever build up the stamina to get up a hill without huffing and
puffing, I'm going after them...

There's a bunch of over-lapping/abutting parks out there. (DGNWA,
Worthington, Stokes) I think the best way to get a good idea of the
trails would be through the nynjtc maps of the area (#s 15-18)
