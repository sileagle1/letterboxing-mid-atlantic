From: Hikers_n_ Hounds | Date: 2006-04-29 14:50:49 UTC
Hey there fellow LBMAer's,
 
Team New Hampshire is thinking of heading to the Keystone State in August for some camping and letterboxing and has asked for some suggestions. Unfortunately my beautiful Bucks County Pa is woefully short on campgrounds, I think we have 2 or 3 privately owned that aren't the greatest. So I am throwing this out to you folks for suggestions. I am now thinking they might be better off going to French Creek State Park? I would like to keep them within an hour or so of Tyler State Park as they might want to do the Battle of the Bands, but I really don't know that for sure. Anyway, give Dave your suggestions and he can decide. He can also let us know when they will be heading down so we can get together with them for greetings and exchanges. Team New Hampshire is Dave and his fiance, and thier baby and her two adorable little boys. I had so much fun hanging out with them last time I was in the Granite State. Can't wait for them to see our neck of the woods.
 
HnH

From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2006-04-29 16:36:51 UTC
French Creek is also a place laden with boxious material, if one is
either willing to exercise one's legs or mind. Allentown is abotu 1
hour North, but seldom visited by boxing hordes.

Nockamixon also has camping, and is closer to Tyler.

French Creek's camping ranging from Modern Cabins with all amenities
to a modern RV campground all the way down to primitive fields for
tent camping. The DCNR (state agency) does take reservations, which
are necessary. All state parks in PA do require reservation making.

LB

I never realized how many day use only parks there are up there. The
couple that let you stay over offer group sites or cabin rental only,
but I'm sure you know that already. So, when the going gets tough, I
love to say that, the tough go to NJ!

http://www.state.nj.us/dep/parksandforests/parks/bull.html
Department of Environmental Protection

Bull's Island is just north of Lambertville. It's not far from
Washington Crossing, Tyler or you. Checking the map I notice that
Nockamixon/Haycock aren't too far away either.
