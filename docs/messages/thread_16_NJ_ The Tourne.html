letterboxing in PA state parks

From: Silent Doug | Date: 2004-03-25 21:40:26 UTC
I heard from a letterboxer in Western PA who was contacted about their
letterboxes in a state park there. The letterboxer had to fill out a form
to get approval for the letterboxes, so I started doing some research.

The PA DCNR web page doesn't have anything on letterboxing, but they do
have a geocaching policy (and I assume it also applies to letterboxes). I'm
going to followup with the park down the road from my home to check on
their letterbox policy, but in the mean time, I got this response from the
DCNR regarding their geocaching policy:

>The following webpage contains our state park geocaching guidelines:
>http://www.dcnr.state.pa.us/geocaching.aspx
>
>If you plan on placing a geocache in a specific state park, I would
>recommend contacting that park office and speaking to the park manager for
>further information, as he/she will have a central part in making the
>determination on whether or not to approve of the geocache. In addition,
>you'll be required to submit a Geocache Identification Form, which you can
>also download from the above website.

It's interesting that they provide "official geocache" stickers to be
placed on any "registered" geocaches in PA state parks. These are the same
stickers that you can purchase from the geocaching.com web site.

Has anyone else heard from a PA state parks official or had any contact
about letterboxing?

While it's an extra step that must be taken when hiding a letterbox, I'm
not opposed to their policy. At least they're not behaving like the NPS and
banning letterboxes altogether!

Doug

From: Rainear, Tim TQO | Date: 2004-03-26 00:10:19 UTC

Doug,

I did not get permission from Green Lane Park or the Perkiomen Trail commission to place boxes there.

However, after the fact I communicated with the park commissioner and he was not opposed to the practice.

He did ask me to fill out a special use form of some kind which essentially identifies the exact location of the box.

I understood this to be more for safety issues should anyone get lost trying to find a box, they would know where to go look for them.

 

Not sure any aspect of that makes sense.  The PT boxes are in view of the trail so that should not be an issue.

And if you get lost looking for a box, knowing where the box is will not help the rescue squad find you.  If you follow my logic here.

 

Anyway, I never got around to filling out the form.

The Nature Center at Green Lane is very supportive and knows I have 4 boxes surrounding their facility.

They have asked me to put on the letterboxing beginners class in July.

 

On a different note, Peace Valley nature Center has expressed a desire that we DON'T letterbox in their park.

They consider themselves a nature preserve and do not want to encourage off trail walking.

Raindear
