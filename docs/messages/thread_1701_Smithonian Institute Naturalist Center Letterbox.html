Smithonian Institute Naturalist Center Letterbox

From: theendgang | Date: 2006-05-03 15:26:15 UTC
The Smithonian Institute Naturalist Center in Leesburg Virginia
recently posted 2 letterboxes at the center. I completed one box which
was a fun find. This box is a small store bought stamp. The other box
is in a different section of the center which is not for kids under 10
years old. I contacted the placer who works there and is also in charge
of books at the Museum of Natural History in DC. She told me the store
bought stamp was a last minute idea, and I recommended a hand
carved stamp be done to replace the store-bought stamp. She has asked
me to carve one for her. Woohoo! carving for Smithonian! The other box
apparently is hand carved.
I had been to the center before with my kids, and it is a wonderful,
hands-on, place for kids to see and learn. Ther are wonderful things to
do there and a great way to keep young minds occupied. Last visit, my 3
and 5 year olds put together a human skeleton. There is also a deer
skeleton that can be put together. New twist on puzzles!
The back room for kids 10 and up is full of skeletons and science
tables. Kids are able to use microscopes and do experiments.
I highly recommend these boxes if you have younger letterboxers in
your group ( or even if you don't). Oh...It's FREE!
Safe trails! Koyote
