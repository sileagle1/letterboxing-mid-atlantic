From: thedoubtfulguests | Date: 2006-05-07 01:25:57 UTC
A lot of letterboxers are shy of publicity in general. In any case
showing where a box is and showing the stamp is definitely a
letterboxing no-no without permission of the author. You could write
and ask the author's permission, or, plant your own letterbox, or,
fake it. You could just film up and down some paths and pull a box out
of a hiding place that isn't really left there.

Scarab of the Doubtful Guests

From: June | Date: 2006-05-07 02:52:30 UTC
Hi,
 
Welcome to our unique hobby. I hope you have fun. Here's the but...........no, you don't film the way to the hiding place of someone else's box. This is pretty basic stuff. If you have their permission, it's a free country and you can do what you want I guess,  but I can't imagine anyone letting you show the way to one of their boxes, (or their stamp) especially if it's a video blog that will be online for anyone to see. That pretty much destroys the whole point of the hobby.
 
If it could be done as a mystery sort of, with fade-outs, having landmarks camoflauged, maybe. But you still must get the permission of the box's owner. On the whole, it's an idea I am against. I **do** appreciate you asking about it and not just going ahead and doing it like some might have.
