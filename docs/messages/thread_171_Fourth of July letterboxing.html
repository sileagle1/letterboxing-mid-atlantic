Bucks County box report

From: Silent Doug | Date: 2004-07-06 12:06:20 UTC
Here's a report on some letterboxes in Bucks County that I logged (some
with Jennifer) on Sunday:

In Tyler State Park, I found the remnants of the Creekside box, but the
stamp and log were missing. I left a replacement log in the box. The
landmarks here have changed due to major trail maintenance, so the clues
aren't very accurate anymore (there is no gate and we found one trail sign
that had been removed during the trail work and was sitting on the ground
out of view). Schofield Ford Covered Bridge #1 and #2, were logged, but
some of the landmarks for #1 have changed significantly (notably, there are
14 Safety Zone signs now, not 3).

Jane Austen: Jane Austen and Jane Austen: Persuasion were both in fine
shape. Jane Austen: Mansfield Park was confirmed missing.

In Washington's Crossing, I logged Bowman's Hill Tower and Winahawcawchunk.
We found Abstract Guitar, but not Cheshire Cat. (Irene, I don't know if the
compass reading is right, but part of the problem is knowing where to stand
"before" the fireplace -- do you mean in front of the hearth or on the
trail at the Y or next to the chimney?) (Incidentally, we tried to find
your Neshaminy boxes last week but the park gates were closed 45 minutes
before sunset! We'll try again soon.)

I also found Victory but then was too exhausted to go on to Captain Moore's
Insignia.

We also found the Wally Hitchhiker and the Hitchhiking Hare hitchhikers,
giving me 11 for the day.

Doug

From: shaneodom2003 | Date: 2004-07-06 12:11:29 UTC
Well,
As for me and mine, we got out here in the Annapolis MD area and went
to hit the Downs Pk boxes. Lots of rain pushed us to try out for Swan
Lake/Waterford park instead. Lovely place for a box and lots of fun
in the rain, dripping along, kids in tow, umbrella strapped to the
stroller, three boxers huddled under the umbrella, trying to stamp
while fending the baby's little grasping hands away from the
ink, "Watch her! Oh boy, shes got it on her finge...no her face..make
that her hair!",,, and of course, "Hold the umbrella still, move
over, watch the drips! There getting on the journal! Oh well, a few
runs will add to it!". Just a bit of a note to the placer, I am
already carrying a repair kit, and the box seemed abit in peril. It
is rather close to the water and the water was very high! So, we
double bagged the contents, I put some of the about letterboxing
flyers in it, and then we bagged the box itself before rehiding. The
box itself was one of the sandwich boxes, maybe not Glad, but
something like that, one of the cheaper ones that come in the multi
packs. I was looking at those myself for placing and noted that they
just don't seal well. The box was slightly open when we found it.
And, just like on our first two boxes, another boxer had been there
that day! This is driving us crazy! We sure hope we run into someone
sometime! Oh well, thats boxing!,
Much Peace & happy trails,
Shane "Pathmaker" Odom, Father Woodwose
