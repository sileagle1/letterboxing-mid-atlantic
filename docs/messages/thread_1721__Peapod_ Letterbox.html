From: JJ | Date: 2007-06-07 12:33:23 UTC
<<I have seen so many changes which I feel are not bettering the hobby that
I have come to the conclusion that maybe it might be better to just go back
to hiking only. >>

The great thing about being actively involved in a hobby (as opposed to a
spectator sport) is that by your involvement, you are constantly helping to
influence it. Everything about how we box has been guided and shaped by the
wonderful people we've met (or whose boxes we've found, or who have found
ours) along the way, from where we plant and the materials we use to how we
carve stamps and write clues. So to me, quitting because of disappointment
and change is just letting others get the best of your passion...why let
them smother the little light you carry for this hobby? I commend those who
have been doing this for a long time. You've seen the changes and helped to
divert the negative ones and shape the positive ones to make this something
that can continue to grow and thrive.

<<So, in the meantime, I have just backed away from a lot of those things
that I don't care for, letterboxed my way, and ignored the rest. >>

Which is exactly what I mean -- that's how you influence something for the
better.

That being said...we've never quit, but we do take long breaks. :)

Puddleby (of 4Rhines)

Have you ever quit letterboxing? was Re: "Peapod" Letterbox

From: Audrey | Date: 2007-06-07 14:06:08 UTC
Rick, that is an excellent question for discussion!

I've considered quitting being a letterbox PLANTER. Every day spent
maintaining (checking, replacing...) is one that I'm not spending
searching, unless there's one really close to my plant. And I take my
plants too much to heart to abandon a box. So I have this pressure
hanging over my head to maintain them. At what point does the pressure
outweigh the fun? It's like the old "all work, no play" thing. "All
plants and no finds makes a letterboxer... what?"
I wouldn't think you can really "quit" being a letterboxer when it
comes to finding them, though. You just scale the level of activity to
fit the rest of your life.

Aud
