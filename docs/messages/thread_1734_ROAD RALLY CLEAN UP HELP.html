Okay, here's the story...

I'm gathering up the boxes from the road rally. I'm trying to get all the bonus boxes and
additional eeevil as well to return to the owners.

So I park at the Salfordville Post Office - as I have many times while scouting and planting
- and head up the trail. Now the Postmaster, who has the post office in his home, sees me
do this all the time. He can't be happy about my parking in his driveway and, unlike other
denizens of the Perkiomen Trail, I only ever stay for a few minutes.

This time, he actually comes to the window and gives me a look. Knowing I will be brief
and not return, I hustle up the trail and gather my box and two others left behind at that
location. In the process of doing so, I get a sliver of wood in my hand. Using my trusty
Leatherman tool I remove the sliver but manage to cut open my hand in the process. (It's
one of those cuts on flexible skin that just bleeds and bleeds and won't close up because
the skin won't lay flat.)
On the way back to the car I notice - Wow, this is kind of different - a letterbox with a
wallet in it. I open up the box, and sure enough, inside is a wallet with a stamp in one
section and a log where the bills usually go. Pretty ingenious, right?

Now picture this: You are the Postmaster of Salfordville, PA. Some guy - who, let's face it,
looks like a terrorist - keeps parking in your lot and sneaking off to the local hiking trail.
But he's not hiking or biking and he never stays very long. One day, you look out your
window and here he comes - walking back to his car, rifling through somebody's wallet
with a bloody knife in his hand. What would you do?

Well, I didn't wait to find out.

I bet he's got a really great story to tell, though.
