Identifing other boxers

From: nattybumppolbna | Date: 2006-06-05 17:56:57 UTC
I know I am "different" from many other 'boxers. For those of you who have met me, that
goes without saying. But I try to be as un-letterboxer as possible when out on the trail.
Sure I mutter, but it's usually profane and thus discourages nosey dog-walkers etc. Maybe
it's a hold-over from urban 'boxing or some of the more "guerilla" boxes I do, but I usually
have a prominent alibi - camera, bird book, tree book, ball - to give noxers an idea of why
I am there without asking.

I don't wear letterboxing regalia while letterboxing because I occasionally need some
"plausible deniablity." And IMHO it's a little like wearing the T-shirt of the band your
playing in on stage. No judgements here, just my own twisted mores in play.

Perhaps that's why I am more than a little frustrated with 'boxers who 'fess up right off the
bat.

"What are you doing in there?"
"Looking for a letterbox."
"A what?!"
..and three days of staking out the lobby of a major US corporation go down the drain.

I am always reminded of the tale of the couple who went in search of the letterbox in the
Smithsonian. When it wasn't there, they asked a guard about it. He helped them search
until they found a letterboxer in mid-stamp-in with the box. Then the guard confiscated
the box. (There is another version of the story involving Homeland Security and a body-
cavity search afterwards, but I'll stick to the more plausible story.)

So as the resident curmudgeon, may I suggest that there are times for being "campground
friendly" and times for playing it cool.

My .02

Natt-

From: nattybumppolbna | Date: 2006-06-13 21:03:45 UTC
I will admit that sometimes my clues even baffle me. Not long ago I got an email from
someone who was constantly baffled by a box of mine. After many, many good faith
attempts, I thought I would extend a little mercy hint. Well it seems I misplaced my clue
key and had to re-work the whole puzzle myself. My memory not being what it used to
be, it took some time. And I can assure you that I was cursing myself as much as any of
you have.

I personally don't count a box as an "P" until somebody finds it - so tough clues are a
double-edged sword for me. I have a box in North Carolina
http://www.members.aol.com/nattybumppolbna/blanket.html
that has been up for almost 2 years without a finder although many have tried. Often I
want to make it easier, but it's an easy clue already and I'd hate to cheat the first finder of
a sweet sweet victory.

Perseverence is the key. I remember cracking my first Mapsurfer with the kind help of
Fleur de Lis. After days and days of ciphers and codework, it turned out the key was in
studying his other clues.

I can't get out as much as many of you. And I suck at carving. The only joy left is the
clue-ing.

Anybody take a shot at RALLY RUNNER yet?
