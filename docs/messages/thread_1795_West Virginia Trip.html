From: BullDawg | Date: 2006-06-19 14:33:26 UTC
Back from the West VA camping/boxing trip with Koyote. We struck out
on boxes, but found some cool ones. Thanks to HnH for pointing us to
the Lazy A campground.....was perfect for us. And thanks to Poison
Ivy, for letting us invade his house so early the following
morning...still gotta get our minds together on a project. Other
than a brief battle with road-sickness, things were smooth. We'll be
back.....Added a box for you guys out there in Cacapon State Park....

Still bummed I didnt see any guys playing banjos on their porches, but
did hear a guy cranking the theme from "Dukes of Hazzard" at the
campsite....a true classic moment.

Yeeeee-Haaaawww!

From: Hikers_n_ Hounds | Date: 2006-06-19 23:54:36 UTC
One year when my caving club was at Lazy A there actually was a local Bluegrass Band using that tiny room between the mens and ladies showers to practice in. We were down in camp and I kept saying I heard music, finally my son and I walked up and damned if we didn't get to sit in and listed to thier practice session. They were fantastic. They had a competition the next day and I never found out how they did, but it one of those wonderful unexpected treats that you never forget. My son didn't care much for bluegrass before that but it really opened his eyes and mind.
 
Glad you guys had a good time. Did you go after any of the Berkely Springs boxes?

we mainly did a few drive bys on 81 and winchester, hit a few trails in Cacapon State Park, went to Ivy's..then attempted a few more quickies in shepardstown, and martinsburg..wasted a lot of time driving looking for places...kinda dumb we didnt bring a WVA map, or find the parks location before heading out...that proved costly...Not to mention missing on a few of Grey Squirel's..one of which was a real bizzare miss, like the trail had been re-done
 
as for bluegrass..the only thing that scares me more is classical......"the prefered music of serial killers"
 
2 days in West Virginia....and we didnt spot 1 "hottie" (shocker right?...well I guess Ivy's pretty hot))......but saw only one grocery store!.......funny how something only 1.5 hours from home is like another country.
 
still some great views, and the drive out through Harper's Ferry is amazing....I hadnt been to West Va in 10 years, except for those nice gentlemen's clubs!..Ill be back again, with the kids for camping
