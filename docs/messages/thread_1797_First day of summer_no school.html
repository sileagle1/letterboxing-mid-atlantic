First day of summer/no school

From: the_beeeez | Date: 2006-06-22 00:47:17 UTC
To celebrate the solstice and other various events, we headed to the
Pine Barrens. We sweated our way around the neat Bridges of
Burlington County series by the von der Insels, and also picked up the
5 Bridges of Burlington County and Ong's Hat boxes. A stop at Atsion
Lake provided some much-needed cooling down. We have many more boxes
to find down that way and we'll be using the letterboxing/lake
combination as much as possible.

Oh, and our new signature stamps and trail names (only took us a year)
made their official debuts today. I (Queen B) am now boxing with
sheep/dog, Barn Cat and Blankie Boy.

-- Queen B (the B's)

From: Suzanne Coe | Date: 2006-06-22 12:11:13 UTC
We were having a wonderful time.  The blue trail was somewhat overgrown but we found it without too much trouble.  We hiked along enjoying the scenery and occasional wildlife (some nesting quail & tapirs).  At first when we reached the box site we thought it was missing, but then--there it was, stuck to the side of the rusted metal plate.  I had it in my hand....
 
...and then I woke up!  Whew--obviously I was going through letterbox withdrawal--must be a reaction to the frenzy of Canada and Stampin & Campin.  So later that morning I headed out to find "Princess" in Newtown Square--easy walk with cute clues from 2 young ladies.  The route back took me past "Bob Urban's Field of Dreams" (see Jan, I have a mapping program in MY car too!--I just have to figure out how to get it folded back up again =)  That was a nice hike on a very interesting trail.
Both boxes in fine shape, and I have fed the addiction for the moment....
 
Sheba
