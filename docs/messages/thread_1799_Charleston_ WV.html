From: Hikers_n_ Hounds | Date: 2006-06-24 14:58:27 UTC
What's involved is whatever you want it to be. That's the beauty of this hobby. You make it your own. Bascially, pick a date and a spot. Before you select your date you should check out the events link on Atlasquest to make sure you're not picking the same date as another local gathering. Or maybe a larger gathering further away that a lot of LBMAer's are planning to attend.
 
In the Mid-Atlantic region we've got the Knoebels Gathering coming up Sept. 9th, and Letterboxing Bee usually hosts a Boxtoberfest Gathering sometime in October. Sowsewbizzy is hosting a huge Games themed gathering in DE in October as well.Scarab did a Mid-Winter Ice Breaker for a couple of years but decided not to hold it last year so as not to effect Poison Ivy's Pub Crawl. Natty Bumppo has hosted a Road Rally the last 2 springs.  Several boxers worked together to bring us a Circus gathering and a Mystery gathering that were both incredible. I've hosted 3 very low key gatherings, I'm just too damn lazy to put much effort into it. My method is to select an area I like a lot with lot's of boxes already there and just pick a date. I love to camp so we've done that at two of my gatherings.
 
Your best bet would be to suggest several dates on this list, or post a poll, and see what most folks pick. Then you can ask folks to donate stamps or boxes that you can pre-plant and post clues. OR you can carve and plant yourself. You can pick a theme, or not. Both Atlas Quest and Silent Doug's website, www.letterboxing.info have lot's of help on this subject. But do what YOU want to do. That way your gathering will be like no one elses. It doesn't have to be big, or thematic. Anytime you get a couple of letterboxers together everyone has a great time. 
 
We've got the greatest bunch of people on this list who enthusiastically support each other and our efforts. Figure out what you want to do, when you want to do it. You'll get lot's of help I'm sure.
 
H&H
