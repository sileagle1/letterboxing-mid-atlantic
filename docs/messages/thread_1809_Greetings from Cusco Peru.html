Greetings from Cusco Peru

From: gallant_rogue | Date: 2006-06-26 17:25:58 UTC
I am sitting in a little internet cafe just off the Playa de Arms.
It cost about seventy cents an hour (plus cervesas) I managed to
plant a letterbox on the Inca Trail and have one more to plant
tomorrow. To my knowledge this is the first letterbox in the
country... which is kind of hard to believe because there are so
many hikers here from all over the world. The Inca Trail makes the
Appalachian Trail´s Roller Coaster seem easy.... but I´m glad I
hiked the that part of the AT for practice... the altitude here is
absolutely brutal. I´m at about 11,000 feet now. Allow at least
three or four days to adjust to the thin air before starting the IT.
Today I am resting up from 10 rough days of travelling around Peru.
I have a ton of pictures from my primary target... Machu Pichu...
but alas... my camera has been borrowed ... and I don´t know by
whom... so I lost many other picures... will have a gallery of
photos somewhere next week when I return... but if you want to check
out the first Letterbox in Peru... go see Intipunko over at AQ . . .
the first finder gets a LBMA patch!

ps Bulldawg & Koyote... did someone actually beat you guys to
Spirit or did you just not report the find? Congrats to Hikinghams
for the find at Spirit!

Back in Virginia !! ( was Greetings from Peru)

From: gallant_rogue | Date: 2006-07-01 17:17:28 UTC
Ahhh... so nice to be home!... I'm not ready to start orgainizing a
pub crawl this week. Let me recover then I'll get right on it. P.I>
set the gold standard. In the mean time... sign me up for the BBQ.
After two weeks of trekking & exploring amazing Peru I'm back in my
beautiful Virginia. I'm pleased to have planted the first two
letterboxes in Peru. They are both in spectacular and secure
locations and I DO think they will be found soon.... maybe sooner
then some of my unfound boxes here in Virginia. Peru, with its
thousands of trails and incredible archeological sites, is a premier
destination for hikers... which we all know is a close cousin of
Letterboxing. The Hiking Expeditions Business is an important
industry in Peru. I met hundreds of hikers from all over the world.
So here is an open invitation to all my NoVa LB associates... I've
many picture cd's and Peruvian music on cd... so if you want to come
over for a multimedia presentation of Gallant Rogue's Incan
Adventure... email, call or just come on down! I don't go back to
work till Wednesday and just busting to share my pictures. My dvd
player reads the jpeg files so I'm reviewing the pictures on my big
tv screen. I was at Machu Pichu for the solstice sunrise and
captured a photo of the first sun ray passing thru the temple
window... which happens just one time a year... I feel like Indiana
Jones!
