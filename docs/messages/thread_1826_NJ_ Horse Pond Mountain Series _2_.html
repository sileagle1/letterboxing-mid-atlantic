Horse Pond Mountain Series (2)
Placed 07/01/06
Ringwood, NJ, Passaic County

Clues: Easy
Terrain: Difficult
Orientation: Difficult
Time: 2 - 3 hours

Park at the end of Lake Riconda Drive, which is off of Stonetown
Road in Ringwood.

Notes:
While the trails are blazed adequately, due to lack of use some of
the trails are not obvious. This makes for difficult orientation
especially in the beginning.
This is a demanding series requiring multiple climbs.

BURNT MEADOW Letterbox

At the end of Lake Riconda Road is a telephone pole that has two
blazes on it, the blue diamond Highlands Trail and the red triangle
Stonetown Circular. Go to the left of the telephone pole picking up
the merged trail, which I'll simply refer to as the red trail. Red
trail will shortly begin to climb Harrison Mountain. You'll
eventually notice power line off to the right of the trail. Soon
you'll come to a clearing that the power lines cross. Pick up red
trail on the other side of the clearing. After a brief decent
you'll do another much shorter climb. You'll eventually come to a
tree that indicates to make a right for the Highlands Trail. From
that blazed tree go 13 paces at 160 degrees. There'll be a large
rock to the left, and a hole made by a fallen tree. To the right of
the rock is a smaller rock, underneath it, in the front is another
rock and under that is what you seek.

Please hide box completely hidden from view after stamping in.

HORSE POND MOUNTAIN Letterbox

Go back and take the right picking up the blue diamond Highland
Trail/ white blazed Horse Pond Mountain Trail, simply referred to as
the white trail. White trail will begin to descend and then will
cross a small stream. Shortly the trail will then take a left on to
a woods road. White trail will begin to climb woods road and then
there will be blazes indicating to take the white trail off the
right of the woods road. White trail will begin climbing. You'll
eventually come to a clearing, continue on white trail. White trail
will pass the start of the yellow blazed Burnt Meadow Trail. Stay
on white trail and you'll shortly come to a viewpoint. Enjoy the
view and when done locate the campfire near the middle of the
trail. From campfire go 14 paces at 320 degrees to a rock outcrop.
Underneath it, under another rock is what you seek.

Please hide box completely hidden from view after stamping in.

Return the way you came.
