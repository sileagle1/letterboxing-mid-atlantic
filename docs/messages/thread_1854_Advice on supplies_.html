Advice on supplies?

From: the B's | Date: 2006-07-15 21:59:45 UTC
We are planning a series of microboxes which requires a specific color
per box. We got pretty far with the "ink dots" sets we found at
Michael's but we are still missing a couple of key colors (why would
they repeat green in the sets, anyway?)

As far as I can tell, there are two mini-ink pad brands: Versacolor
cubes and Colorbox Cat's Eye. Looks like both will fit in our chosen
containers. Anyone have experience, good or bad, with either one? Or
is there some other great product out there I am missing?

I would love to order these in conjunction with more carving blocks
but, so far, I have not had any luck finding a supplier that carries
both things. Again, I feel like I'm missing something!

Thanks in advance for the help. We are pretty excited about this
series -- we think it might be the first of its kind, at least in NJ --
and want to get it planted asap.

-- Queen B (the B's)

Re: Advice on supplies?

From: Jan | Date: 2006-07-15 22:19:42 UTC
I purchased about 8 Cat's Eyes when I was at the Artway in Maryland last
month and I wish I had puchased about a dozen more.  They are easy to
use, the colors are vibrant, and they are small enough to fit
comfortably in my pack.  In case you can't tell, I highly recommend them. 
I love my Marvy Markers but they are big and the caps keep falling off. 
I take them along with me for boxes that need them but use the Cat's Eyes
as often as possible.

Jan of Team Little Dog

You are correct that StanzOn is a fast drying solvent ink. It is not recommended for fabric but it is acid-free, archival and is recommended for paper. It will work on all most any surface. The only time I have had problems with it was on a surface that I had use alcohol inks.
