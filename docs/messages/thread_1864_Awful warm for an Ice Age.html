Awful warm for an Ice Age

From: Nathan Brown | Date: 2006-07-19 16:45:12 UTC
I've been meaning to write this for a while, but, well, you know me :)

Last Thursday I left before the crack of dawn and headed up to Mt.
Haycock, not really a mountain, but close enough for Bucks County.
Divine Lotus' Ice Age series has had me chomping at the bit ever since I
saw them in Myke's logbook a while back.

So I get there at about 6, put on the recommended bug spay, a good thing
too, since the woods are thick with mesquites this year, and headed up
the mountain.

I found the first three with no trouble, all in excellent condition. I
could not find the next two, I just could not spot the landmarks, and
then the fun really began.

The path kinda disappears, and you are left knowing that you need to get
to the top of this giant pile of rock, but not knowing just how to do
this. Add to the the already oppressive head and humidity, a hallmark
of Pennsylvania Julys, and you are in for a tough go at it.

But, I was prepared, I had plenty of water (hiker's rule #1, 2, and 3,
bring water), and slowly clambered up the rocks. I got to the top,
found I was about a hundred yards from where I needed to be, but from
there I found the last two boxes with ease.

More sweating, grunting, sliding on rocks that I was trying to climb
down found be back at the bottom in pretty short order. I was drenched
in sweat, covered in dirt, sure that I had rolled in poison ivy more
then once, but elated at my finds, and looking forward to returning to
find the rest as well as other boxes that may be hiding on that there
mountain.

Today I will update my logbook so you can see these wonderful stamps, if
you wish.
