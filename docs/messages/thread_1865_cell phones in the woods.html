Re: [lbma] Re: cell phones in the woods

From: dave & diane | Date: 2006-07-21 03:10:53 UTC
Fortunately Richmond, RI is findable with service. Dave and I were hiking in search of a couple boxes in that part of Arcadia Park last November and the daylight disappeared. You can imagine how hard it is to see blue blazes on trees in the gloom of on-coming evening.  Especially when they alternate with yellow. We knew enough to stop when we felt we had left the trail and called 911. The cell phone not only found a signal, but they were able to locate us by the GPS thingy in the phone.  While the State Park does not have rangers on duty (in November on a weekend after 4PM) they did tell us which way to hike to get out of the woods. Fortunately, there was only a small stream to ford on the walk out. So with wet feet and wounded pride we found the forest road and the lot we parked our car in! Said thanks to the State Trooper who met us at the car and went home to rest our weary bones.
While we were prepared to spend the night in the woods - so glad we didn't have to. We now have one of those wind-up flashlights in the backpack. That way I don't have to worry about "when was the last time you checked the batteries?"!
We went back the next day to finish the series and found the spot where we had stopped(I took a photograph with my digital camera). Turns out we hadn't left the trail afterall and we were within 3 feet of a blazed tree!
We had totally under-estimated how long it would take to hike the given mileage, not to mention the stops at the boxes for stamping in and admiring the stamps/boxes and surroundings.. That's why I really like it when planters give you an idea of how long it should take to do their hike.
Diane

From: Hikers_n_ Hounds | Date: 2006-07-21 11:23:03 UTC
Boy does this bring back bad memories from last summer! Sheba and I were hunting a mapsurfer box in a very remote area. the woods were very deep and no one else was there. No cell signal, no path eve. Just blazed trees. The blazes are old and faded and many of the trees had been knocked down by a hurricance. We were about halfway to the box when I really got concerned about losing the light and getting stuck in the woods for the night. the blazes were hard enough to find in full sun and would have been impossible to find in the dark. I had taken a compass bearing of where the car was so we could have nushwacked our way out, maybe. Anyway, we decided to bail and come back another day. I'm really glad we did. But you bring up a good point.  Dude and I have gotten stuck in the woods at least 3 times after dark. Fortunately they were woods I knew pretty well and I am always prepared to spend the night. But my experience is that leterboxing always takes me a lot longer than I originally estimated.
