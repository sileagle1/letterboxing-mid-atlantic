Bulldawg boxes, and my future...

From: BullDawg | Date: 2006-07-29 22:43:50 UTC
OK..it was a year ago on July 31, 2005..that I found my first
box..thus is the last weekend to post this before my anniversary...the
clues for "a dawg is born" will be released on 7/31/2006, and is a
small tribute to what this hobbie brought me, and a thanks to the bud
that brought me to the sport, Koyote....we have had this talk, he
knows my gratitude...It was planted at the "stamp & camp" gather, and
released to those folks that attended.,,,but not to the public til
7/31/06

after that, I was motivated to plant like crazy, (I thought that was
the idea.....to plant like crazy, its not).....and with Gallant Rogue
as the biggest finder of my boxes...thank you, for the effort and all
the words we have had....

many whom know me in the area, know I am a man of mystery..and after a
year of this hobbie, I can see why folks bore of it, and disappear
after a brief time (no offense). I have tried to bring a lot new to
it......All of my boxes are about to be retired, except a few..like
the American Indian Culture series, Ball's Bluff and a few
others......others will be pulled forever over the next few
weeks....and some clues have been pulled already...As the hobby
evolves, so will I.... It's a simple game, with simple idea.....but I
am a complex man, and like complex games, clues and boxes. I feel we
all need cooler clues, and make this game more challenging...not just
about dartmoor, but Americanized.

To ALL finders, and reporters....this hobby has opened my eyes to
hiking, and nature...and for that I owe my bud, Koyote.... He showed
me letterboxing, but that was the tip of the iceburg, in the greater
scheme of hiking. I have further realized that quanity is not
quality.....and some of my boxes have made people think, and evaluate
their lives....Its an awakening, and that is what draws me to
it....and why I want others to come to my boxes. I want a place of
reflection, or appreciation of life. I feel after this year, that I
have actually grown as a person, and realized a lot about myself.
better yet, I have seen things on longer hikes that I would have never
seen in my Life, especially on the Appilachain Trail in VA. It puts
life in perspective..and made me rethink A lot of stuff...that is what
I take from this, and is my motto: "It's not about the box, but the
place the box has taken you to"......the stamp is always secondary,
and after about 8 months of boxing, and hiking Great Falls VA..I
realized that.

after this one year, I have met a few boxers and ....and I just wanna
say thanks for you warm welcomes: Hikers and Hounds, Ms Scarlet,
SewSowBizzy, all the END GANG!, geckospot, Tempis Fugit, J & R, Poison
Ivy, funky feline, spidey, all day dream, cyclonic (for the ride), and
WINK and Tar Heel....my party buds

Id say 75-85% of my future plants are for the locals I trust, or for
folks that want long hikes, extreme, or adult boxes

thanks again to all the locals and lurkers
