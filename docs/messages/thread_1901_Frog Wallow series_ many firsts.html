My first carvings, series, and planting.
Take water.
Keep moving or use bug repellent. The deer flies and
skeeters aren't too bad if you keep moving.

Enjoy!

Pack water and a snack, this is a long hike. This
series is placed on the longest trail in the Delaware
Trail Challenge. Stop by any Delaware state park
office for a list of trails and your own trail
challenge card, as well as a trail map. Don't forget
the water!
http://www.destateparks.com/Activities/trails/challenge.htm

To find box #1 start the trail challenge hike on the
Swamp Forest Trail. Proceed clockwise around the pond.
Pass the beach and disc golf course. Pause in the open
space at the base of a stand of pines and find the 2
LARGE 3-fingered trees. Walk between these giants
toward a 2-fingered tree by a stream. See that smaller
nondescript tree on the right? Your prize is there.

Return to the trail and continue around the pond.
Watch for a large flat rock on the left just before a
bridge. Isn't that a great place to rest? Once you're
rested, face the rock and look into the woods at
11:00. From that tree go back about 10 steps (placer
is short!) to the 2-trunked tree on your right. Box is
under rocks. Watch out for the groundhog burrows. T

For box #3 return to the trail and continue. Pass the
boat ramp. Cross 4 wooden bridges but do not take the
bridge that prohibits horses. Take the horse trail at
this point to the rock pile on the right. With your
back to the rock pile go to the fallen tree across the
trail. This prize is under the tree, on the right,
close to a rusty fence post.


NOTE: Before you set out you must read and agree to
the Waiver of Responsibility and Disclaimer.
