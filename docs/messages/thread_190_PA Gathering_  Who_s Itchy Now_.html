PA Gathering: Who's Itchy Now?

From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2004-07-11 23:26:56 UTC
Howdy LBMA! By now I am sure that all of you have find memories of
the event organized by Hikers and Hounds.

While we were down at Washington's Crossing we joked about re-naming
the park the "Pennsylvania State Poison Ivy Reserve (PSPIR)". With
that in mind, I was wondering how many folks came down with a case of
poison ivy...

I'm thinking of making a stamp with a poison ivy plant that I can
incorporate into a postal letterbox to mail to folks that came down
with it. The problem is, I have NO clue what goes into a PLB,
although I know poison ivy pretty well. I'm thinking of snail-
mailing it out to victims from the "PSPIR gathering" once I see what
a PLB looks like (I'm on a couple of mailing lists). I cannot
guarantee when that will be, but whoever was smitten by the "PA state
flower" and responds to this board or me (via email) with their email
address and itchy tail from our gathering, I'll add you to the list.

I'll email you for your snail mail address once the stamp is carved
and send the itchy PLB on its way.

Leaflet Three, Let It Be!

Lightnin' Bug

From: nature77girl77 | Date: 2004-07-12 18:22:07 UTC
Just wanted to say we had a GREAT time camping and gathering!
(Those of you that didn't camp really missed out!) It was great to
meet so many boxers from other states and see some stamps that we
don't see much of in our area (CT). Thanks so much Irene!

Regarding the poison ivy, we didn't get any, but we also wore
jeans. If you think you've come in contact with poison ivy though,
you should rub the juice of a Jewelweed plant on the exposed area.
(We boxed with someone who sat in poison ivy and helped her find
some Jewelweed - did it work?) I believe someone posted a pic of
Jewelweed on the national listserve.

Thanks to everyone for a great time!

Tali & Alexander (NE CT)
