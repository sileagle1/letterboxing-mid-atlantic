Hi,
Posted this to AQ, and wanted to pass it along here as well.

For anyone hiking around the Massanutten Mountain area/ Elizabeth
furnace area; I ran into not one, but two bears today on ajaunt up to
Buzzard Rock. The first one was a good 700 lbs and the biggest bear
I've seen. He was kind enough to run off when I started whistling and
waving my arms ( I had a goal to reach afterall). His departure was
welcome so as to not to continue wetting my pants. The second bear was
a few hours later and much smaller. I was able to get some pics for the
kids. The bear attack last month was on my mind, though that person
provoked the bear. Just a heads up that our bear friends are active in
the area. Use your heads and common sense if out and about in the
woods. Safe trails.
Koyote

From: hurdhut@aol.com | Date: 2006-08-16 23:19:34 UTC
 I have thought about bells, but, to be honest, I want to be able to get close to seeing the animals in the wild if I can. I feel comfortable enough to do so. I would have booked back the other way when I encountered the first bear, but the trail was rocky and he would have caught up to me easily. I briefly thought about the camera, but we had pretty much stumbled into each others path and I didn't want him so close as to think he was out of options.
  Just so you know Irene, and I'm sure Dude is very protective; the bear attack in this area involved a mountain biker who goes out with his dog almost daily. The dog got whiff of the bear and went to play or fight, but tangled with the bear making it feel it was backed up. The mountain biker heard the dog barking then yelping. He jumped off the bike and went into the brush to find the bear holding the dog down. The guy, apparently not thinking well, charged the bear which turned on him ( duh!). The guy and his dog ended up getting away, fortunately. The dog will have the scars to prove his valor judging by the pic I saw. The Ranger that was interviewed didn't seem to put any blame on the bear. :)

Oh my - I was trying to talk my husband into hiking up there yesterday! I'm terrified about running into a bear or wild beast. I clip on a bunch of keys on my backpack to make noise. From: gerania93 | Date: 2006-08-18 13:06:30 UTC
I sing when I walk a trail alone, not Offspring, but I sing so badly
it scares pretty much anything away, people too. I carry a whistle
now so that I can make loud noise if I have to. For anyone who doesn't
know, the International Mountail Distress Signal is six blasts of a
whistle followed by a silence. The response is three blasts. Stumbling
around, crying and saying "I want my Mommy" aren't quite as helpful
but much more comforting.

Gerania
