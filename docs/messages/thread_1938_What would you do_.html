What would you do?

From: the B's | Date: 2006-08-24 01:39:18 UTC
We have to admit: we love PCLG boxes, and go after them whenever
possible. Last summer, we found "Coward" in terrible shape. The
stamp was missing and the logbook was so soaked that we couldn't
turn the pages. We did our best to dry it out but it was a lost
cause, along with that beautiful stamp.

This summer found us in the environs of the box again, and we
decided to check on it. We were the last ones to log into the book,
over a year ago (although I believe others may have found it since
then, based on the location we found it in). On the theory that it
wasn't doing much good where it was, we made the tough call to pull
it for some maintenance.

The rest of the B's are upstairs with a hair dryer, drying out the
logbook (that part alone has been fascinating; Wingfoot was the
first finder, Wanda and Pete are in there as well -- 15 finders, at
most, in all that time). We have managed to dry out and separate
the page with the original image, which was absolutely amazing.

What to do now? I have contacted the placer but based on other
experience, I am not expecting a reply. Should we just put it
back? Would it be bad form to try and recarve a replica of the
stamp and replace it?

-- Queen B
