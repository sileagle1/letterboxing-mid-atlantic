Knoebels Kampin' Kudos

From: Hikers_n_ Hounds | Date: 2006-09-11 23:49:47 UTC
Kudos and thanks to all who attended the Knoebels gathering. It was great to put some new faces to stamps and see so many old friends. What a nice surprise to actually meet that sainted woman, Mrs. Lightnin' Bug! and the other littler' bug I hadn't met yet. Plus all of the other folks I hadn't yet had the privilege of meeting. So cool. My personal favorite attendee was my little sister (46) Deb and her dog Cody, who are the other half of the Hikers and Hounds stamp image. Deb and Cody have only ever been to one other event so I was very happy they came. It was out on trail with Deb, Cody and Dude in Jan 04 that we stumbled across a box not re-hidden well and discovered letterboxing.
 
We had a blast hanging out with eveyone but the highlight of the weekend had to be when Deb and I, along with team Clue Hound all rode the train...with our dogs! We had so much fun!! True Indigo took pictures and I am hoping they turned out okay. Dude just loved it! Then we had a nice relaxing time around the camp fire with Crayola Posse, EBBB (Erin) Team Clue Hound, The Von der Insels, Sheba and Cyclonic. If I forgot anyone I apologize profusely and blame the beer Cyclonic made me bring and drink.
 
My sister brought her guitar and we were trying to think of sons to sing when Sue from Team Clue Hound suggested "Little Black Egg" but she couldn't remember the words or tune?. Well who the heck has ever heard of this?! I teased her mercilessly all night, making up my own words to the tune of "Oh Susannah" and "Jimmy Crack Corn", telling her there was no such song. This morning she emailed me and lo and behold there is a song Little Black Egg by the Nightcrawlers and the Lemonheads! Plus a video I couldn't play. Whoda thunk it!
 
We really had a great time, thanks again to all,
 
Irene and Dude, Deb and Cody
Hikers and Hounds

From: JJ Rhinehart | Date: 2006-09-13 01:42:28 UTC
We had a fabulous time at Knoebels. Nothing is nicer than being hugged by
someone you didn't know yesterday! :)

It was great to put faces with trail names. Our girls were charmed by all
the nice 'boxing people (and pets) we met, and my only regret is that I
didn't snap a photo of RGBisMe teaching 20-month-old Rina how to make her
first SPOR out of gravel and chunks of blacktop. We did get a few great
pics of "how to use a reach for a box as a fake photo op," which I'll post
in the photo folder asap, and we learned some carving secrets from the pros.
And the very best part of the whole weekend was running into Wanda&Pete on
the trail later -- what a small, serendipitous world we live in -- we shared
clues, a hike and some wonderful conversation. No small irony that they
have 11,000+ finds and we have 11! (Ok, so it's actually 16 or 17 now, but
still...!) Generous, encouraging, friendly people like we met this weekend
are clearly the driving force behind this wonderful pastime.

We are really looking forward to seeing many of you at more gatherings in
the future, and getting to know you all better. And lots and lots of
letterboxing!
-4Rhines
