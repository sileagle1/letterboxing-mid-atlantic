Welcome!

From: fleurdelislb | Date: 2004-03-08 00:25:12 UTC
If you've recently stumbled upon this site, welcome!

I've frequented the LBNE site for awhile now but as I don't visit New
England often I can't quite relate to the clue sharing, stories, etc.
I thought that those of us in the Mid-Atlantic states would enjoy
such a place where we can share ideas, clues, and stories about
letterboxing in our own neck of the US.

In case you haven't noticed, I have not included a database as part
of our group - it's too controversial a topic on LBNA and elsewhere.
It'd like this to remain as peaceful as possible without it starting
out in a name-calling fest over a database!

So once again, welcome. Make of this what you will, and hopefully it
will be an enjoyable spot for Mid-Atlantic Letterboxers to stop in
and talk about LBing.

Fleur de Lis

Hi! I'm brand new to letterboxing!

I live in NY state but I'm interested in Mid-atlantic areas
also...my brother and his fiancee live in DC, my parents in NVA.
I'm looking for a way to introduce this to them when we visit for
EAster week. I have two girls ages 3 and 6, and we all love being
outdoors. Sometimes we have a hard time finding activities that
span the generations...It seems like letterboxing could be an
activity we would all enjoy doing when we are done visiting all the
museums we can stand in DC!

I'm also a Brownie troop leader and I'm exploring ways letterboxing
could be used to keep the girls busy in the out of doors...so many
of the traditional camp skills I learned as a girl seem quite
dangerous when 25 1st graders are involved!

From: fleurdelislb | Date: 2004-03-13 20:32:28 UTC
I'd say just tell them you're all going for a walk/treasure hunt and
then give them the details on the walk. I did that at Thanksgiving
with some extended family, and by the time we were ready to leave, 8
people had piled/squished into my sister's little Volkswagon!
Everyone was very excited! I just looked at letterboxing.org and
there are plenty of LBs in DC, so you can just pick the ones that
appeal to you and are family friendly and off you go! :) Have fun!

Thanks for starting this group. As someone who lives in North NJ
its great to here about boxes around this area. Its also great to

I also wanted to share an update. I went out to find the boxes in
Hartshorne woods park today. I found the first five but couldn't
find the last one aka "Old Glory". The other five boxes where in
good shape. I also went to look for the "Sandy Hook Lighthouse" box
but to my dismay I found that the Mt. Mitchell ScenicOverlook park

anyone who was thinking of going to look for it. I'm not quite sure
