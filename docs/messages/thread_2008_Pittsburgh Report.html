Pittsburgh Report

From: Nathan Brown | Date: 2006-09-29 03:49:07 UTC
So, I did do a little boxing when I was out in Pittsburgh this weekend
to help Tommcat move and pick up some tires for a friend's Duce and a half.

On the way out I found both of Flat Tire's boxes along the Turnpike,
which gave me some great ideas for planting some more micros of my own.
Both were in fine shape and well worth the quick stop.

Farmer Brown and I went hunting for a few over the weekend.

First we found the "Mystery Animal Series #3" box in Moon park.
Honesty, it was a dissappointing hike with a store bought stamp at the
end. Fine for kids though.

We also headed to Frick Park to get a few. The "East Ridge" box was
also a store bought, but the hike was nice and well worth it. We got
the first two boxes of the "Pride of Pittsburgh" series, very nice
stamps. We elected not to get the rest, which are in a different part
of the park, and instead got "Promenading Pooch", a store bought but
again a nice stamp.

We skipped the rest of "Pride of Pittsburgh" to head to Kennywood, which
turned out to be closed, but that was just as well since the skies
opened up when we got there.

I did some scouting around, found some places to plant later this year,
like down at Fort Pitt and Old Economy Village, so hopefully by December
Pittsburgh will have some new boxes.

For the B's, I highly recommend "Pride of Pittsburgh"
--
