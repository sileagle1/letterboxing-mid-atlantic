NJ Flood

From: gerania93 | Date: 2004-07-14 20:54:43 UTC
Bev, Alyssa, fellow Southern NJ 'boxers, are you folks OK after the
1000 year storm of '04? I was in N.E. PA for a family wedding over
the weekend and drove home Monday evening. MY aprox. 2 1/2 hour trip
took over 4, poor visibility, rain heavy at times, reduced speed
limit on turnpike. The drive from the turnpike to my house should
take 15 to 20 minutes and lasted for over 2 hours. Had to take one
detour and turn around and try another way at road closings three
times. I drove through some scary flooded intersections. When I
finally got home, I couldn't get 'home'.

I live on a dead end street and the intersection closest to my home
was flooded too. I tried to drive in but had to stop when the car
started making nasty gurgling noises and threatened to drown.
Luckily, I was able to back out of that mess and park a couple of
blocks away. I lost my cell phone recently, so my son cut through
yards and waded knee deep water to go to the house and tell my
husband to go and pick me up in his big pickup. Except dad wasn't at
home. A couple of people walking by asked if they could help but they
didn't have phones either. Pretty weird for this area. Finally, a
guy, a driver stopped and let me use his phone, but Hubby's not
answering the cell. I guess my plan B and plan C sounded pretty thin
to this man so he offered to give my son and me as well as another
woman from the neighborhood a ride in his really big SUV. He took me
right to the door which I appreciated because most of my front yard
was flooded. Turns out that he is a retired volunteer fireman from
the area and I promptly forgot his name when I got into the house and
shut the door. Bummer!

PS Hubby was fine. He didn't think that I was coming home till the
next day and went out with his friends. He got my voice mail and said
that he'd be right home, 15 minutes. HA! Took an hour.

Hmmm. This sounds like a stamp to me. Maybe a HH.

Gillian

From: letterboxingbee | Date: 2004-07-15 12:00:23 UTC
Hi Gillian,

Luckily where we live in Moorestown we are elevated high enough on
the water table that we would only get flooded out if other homes
were completey under water. Which is a SAD way to explain things,
but that was a major factor in deciding where to purchase our house.

I am completely thankful that the sump pump was a trooper and did not
fail us. It went off every 25-30 seconds but it did not quite. :)
We did get about 3" water in the basement a few years ago because one
sump pump failed and that was not fun!! So I can imagine what others
down south Jersey are going through. My heart truely goes out to
them.

Glad to hear you finally made it home. A few extra hours spent
getting home is truelly worth getting home safe and finding your
house is ok.

I can't get to check on my boxes til maybe mid next week so if anyone
finds one of mine missing or soaked please let me know and I will
make replacement boxes as soon as I can.

That HH idea sounds pretty cool.


Thanks for your concern Gillian. Glad to hear your ok.
