Geocachers!

From: the B's | Date: 2006-10-03 21:13:02 UTC
While working away on the plethora of DE clues this afternoon, I
received the following message about a mystery box we placed in VA
last year:

"Message:
Our Jamestown 2007 committee came across your letterbox this past
Friday. It is in very close proximity to our geocache location for
2007. It is in fine condition and we enjoyed looking at your stamp of
***. Our geocache goes on line January 1st., and we did not know if
you would like us to move your letterbox somewhere else on ***, that
would be less obtrusive. Please e me any time. Thanks."

My first reaction was along the lines of, "What the &*()%#? We were
here first. Find your own darn hiding spot!"

Then I looked into Jamestown 2007 and it is a huge big deal. And I
have to give them credit for not trashing our box without letting me
know.

Problem is, I pretty much have the only hiding spot in that location,
at least as far as I can remember. And can I really trust them to
hide my box as well as I did? I am leaning towards just pulling it,
but it was a decent little box in a box-less area.

Thoughts, please?

-- Queen B of the B's

Re: [lbma] Geocachers!

From: Stephen Dargitz | Date: 2006-10-04 12:16:43 UTC
I would suggest in your response to the geocacher(s) that you try to set up a phone call.  Frequently warmth and tone are not captured in written communication; i.e., you're more likely to persuade them on the phone.  Their tone is absolutely awful in writing and perhaps they didn't mean for it to come out that way.
 
Without letting on what you are trying to find out, try to determine whether or not this is:
 
"Nice letterbox.  Shame if anything happened to it." or
 
"We are the official Jamestown 2007 committee and [are planning]/[have been planning for several months] a geocache event in connection with our festivals and we suspect a lot of inexperienced cachers may damage your letterbox.  Heads up."  or
 
"I'm involved in Jamestown 2007 and also wanted to plant a geocache and your letterbox is near the spot I just scouted out."
 
Your response may be different depending on context.  QOCMike is active on Atlas Quest and does both letterboxing and geocaching.  He may also have some insight.

From: leabhar314 | Date: 2006-10-04 16:06:39 UTC
Hi there,

I live here in the middle of Colonial history-land (Jamestown,
Williamsburg, Yorktown), and I can attest that the 2007 event is the
hugest tourist deal that has gone on down here since the bicentennial.
The planning has been going on for years, and there are associated
events happening already.

I agree with the phone call suggestion. But realistically, if your box
is left anywhere near where their cache is going to be, it probably
won't survive. Letterboxes can to fall prey to geocachers even in the
best of circumstances, imagine what will happen with a bunch of
tourists for whom this is their geocache introduction.

If you decide you want to move it, or relabel it or something, and I
can help, let me know. (though you'd have to send me the clues, I
looked through the VA mystery listings and I confess I haven't been
able to determine which box this might be)

~ Leabhar
