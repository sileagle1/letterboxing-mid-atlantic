Cross-PA Trip

From: the B's | Date: 2006-10-04 15:22:57 UTC
We dealt with the ginormous car show in Carlisle, buckets of rain
and construction and accidents, 400 miles each way, and in the
middle of it all managed to letterbox. Nothing stands in the way of
priorities!

After the Peter J. Camiel rest stop (great stamp), we intended a
quick on and off in Carlisle but were stopped dead in our tracks by
the car show. We still managed to pick up RGBisme's wonderful "Mary
McCauly" (enjoyed the surroundings greatly) and Pointer Party's "NH
Lady Slipper Finds a Home in PA." The latter featured a stamp
carved by Uneksia that is truly stunning. Don't forget your markers
for that one.

Then on and on and on the PA TP -- a brief stop at Somerset to walk
the dog, then out to Zelienople. The rain was coming down pretty
hard so we chose drive-bys. We were not disappointed with the
Harmonist Cemetary. One of the most unique places we have ever been
taken lbing, even in the rain.

Onto family in OH, and our mystery box planted. I hope to get the
clues up later today. It is dedicated to my aunt, who is currently
battling cancer, and she is very excited about the box. She can't
wait until it gets visitors -- unbeknownst to her, I am going to ask
visitors to leave her a note as well. The clues are going to be
pretty easy as a result but it was great (and a great surprise) to
see her so full of anticipation. I might even have to post a
spoiler on the Great Lakes List to get someone out there soon!

Back and back again, with just a quick stop in Beaver Falls for a
couple of almost drive-bys in pretty spots. But our thoughts were
turning to preparation for the DE games, and we worked on making
good time home.

Thanks for all the suggestions, to those who sent them to me. We
are planning several more trips out Harrisburg/Lancaster way to pick
up all the neat boxes there. In the meantime, I am working
feverishly to get us turned around in time to head south this time.
See some of you in a few days, jars of pickles in hand.

-- Queen B of the B's
