...COASTAL FLOOD WATCH REMAINS IN EFFECT FROM FRIDAY EVENING THROUGH

A COASTAL FLOOD WATCH REMAINS IN EFFECT FROM FRIDAY EVENING THROUGH
LATE SATURDAY NIGHT.

AN ONSHORE FLOW WILL STRENGTHEN ON FRIDAY AND CONTINUE THROUGH
SATURDAY. THIS WILL BE DUE TO AN INCREASING PRESSURE GRADIENT
BETWEEN LOW PRESSURE DEVELOPING TO THE SOUTH AND HIGH PRESSURE
ANCHORED TO OUR NORTH. AS A RESULT, IT IS POSSIBLE THAT MODERATE
TIDAL FLOODING WILL OCCUR DURING TIMES OF HIGH TIDE ALONG THE
ATLANTIC COASTLINES OF NEW JERSEY AND DELAWARE, ADJACENT BACK BAYS,
DELAWARE BAY AND RARITAN BAY STARTING FRIDAY EVENING AND LASTING
THROUGH LATE SATURDAY NIGHT. THERE IS ALSO THE POTENTIAL FOR
MODERATE TO SEVERE BEACH EROSION.

THE COMBINATION OF HIGH ASTRONOMICAL TIDES, DUE TO THE FULL MOON,
AND AN EXPECTED STRONG NORTHEAST WIND MAY GENERATE HIGH TIDES IN
EXCESS OF 7.5 FEET ABOVE MEAN LOWER LOW WATER.

HIGH TIDES OCCUR ALONG THE ATLANTIC COAST AND RARITAN BAY BETWEEN
7:30 PM AND 9:00 PM FRIDAY, BETWEEN 7:45 AM AND 9:15 AM SATURDAY,
AND BETWEEN 8: 15 PM AND 9:45 PM SATURDAY. HIGH TIDES ALONG THE
ADJACENT BACK BAYS OCCUR A COUPLE OF HOURS LATER.

HIGH TIDES ALONG DELAWARE BAY OCCUR BETWEEN 8:15 PM AND 11:30 PM
FRIDAY, BETWEEN 8:45 AM AND 12:00 PM SATURDAY, AND BETWEEN 9:00 PM
SATURDAY AND 12: 30 AM SUNDAY.
