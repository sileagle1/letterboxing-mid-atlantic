From: Nathan Brown | Date: 2006-10-09 02:50:57 UTC
Judy B wrote on 10/7/2006, 12:20 PM:

> This morning I received a call from Sheba (Suzanne Coe) that her
> daughter Death Moose (Laura Coe) was fatally hit by a car as she was
> crossing the street last night. Many of you have met Laura at gathers
> and know the pair were frequently on the trails together.

I don't really know how to describe the sadness I feel over this. Irene
called me Saturday afternoon with the news. She was in tears over the
phone, and I thought it was Dude. Nothing could have prepared me for
the shock of this sad news.

It breaks my heart to hear of Laura. She was such a beautiful person,
full of life. She was the type of person that marched to her own beat,
very independent, and growing into her own being. She was like a
blooming flower, wonderfully different, strong willed and bright.

It truly is not fair to Suzanne and her family to have Laura ripped from
their lives. Sue, who if you don't know her, is such a smart, cheerful,
kind and loving woman. Every time you meet her she has a smile on her
face. For this to happen seems unreal.

Those who know me know that I am not usually a very emotional person,
but when we stopped on Saturday, all I wanted to do was hold her, tell
her how much I loved her, tell her how much she and Laura meant to me,
to us as a family, because we all have truly become a family. I ended
up being totally speechless, letting the moment speak for itself.

So Suzanne, I just want you to know that you and Laura are in my heart,
that anything I can do for you is considered done. You have family
here, and we are with you and will forever cherish the memories of Laura.
--
Nathan Brown

AKA Cyclonic
Penncoasters.com

www.ricksantorum.com

Our feelings do mirror those that Nathan has put forth. We are all

Laura Evaline Coe, age 12, of Claymont, DE, went to be with the Lord
suddenly on Friday, October 6, 2006. Laura was a 7th grade student at
Hanby Middle School. She was a member of Girl Scouts of the Chesapeake
Bay, Troop 1453. Laura enjoyed drawing, reading, writing and hiking.

Laura is survived by her parents, Timothy E. and Suzanne E. Coe; a
sister, Sara E. Coe; a brother, Eric E. Coe; paternal grandparents,
Darrell (Bert) and Brenda Coe; maternal grandparents, Walter G. and
Elizabeth S. Fraser; paternal great-grandmother, Evaline Gilpin. She
is also survived by a host of aunts, uncles, cousins and friends.

A visitation will be held on Thursday, October 12, 2006 from 10:00 am
to 12:00 noon in the Strano & Feeley Family Funeral Home, 635
Churchmans Rd., Newark, DE 19702 followed by a service celebrating
Laura's life at 12:00 noon. Burial will follow in Arlington Cemetery,
Drexel Hill, PA. In lieu of flowers contributions in Laura's memory
can be sent to the Girl Scouts of the Chesapeake Bay, 100 W. 10th St.,
Suite 610, Wilm., DE 19801. For directions or to express an online
condolence please visit www.strano-feeley.com

Strano & Feeley

Family Funeral Home

302-731-5459

Laura Evaline Coe, age 12, of Claymont, DE, went to be with the Lord
suddenly on Friday, October 6, 2006. Laura was a 7th grade student at
Hanby Middle School. She was a member of Girl Scouts of the Chesapeake
Bay, Troop 1453. Laura enjoyed drawing, reading, writing and hiking.

Laura is survived by her parents, Timothy E. and Suzanne E. Coe; a
sister, Sara E. Coe; a brother, Eric E. Coe; paternal grandparents,
Darrell (Bert) and Brenda Coe; maternal grandparents, Walter G. and
Elizabeth S. Fraser; paternal great-grandmother, Evaline Gilpin. She
is also survived by a host of aunts, uncles, cousins and friends.

A visitation will be held on Thursday, October 12, 2006 from 10:00 am
to 12:00 noon in the Strano & Feeley Family Funeral Home, 635
Churchmans Rd., Newark, DE 19702 followed by a service celebrating
Laura's life at 12:00 noon. Burial will follow in Arlington Cemetery,
Drexel Hill, PA. In lieu of flowers contributions in Laura's memory
can be sent to the Girl Scouts of the Chesapeake Bay, 100 W. 10th St.,
Suite 610, Wilm., DE 19801. For directions or to express an online
condolence please visit www.strano-feeley. com
