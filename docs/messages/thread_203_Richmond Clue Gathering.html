Richmond Clue Gathering

From: Mike Brooks | Date: 2004-07-18 09:24:20 UTC
I'm not yet sure about making that gathering, but here are some thoughts
about the Clue series that might (or not) be helpful.

The rope is the toughest of the weapons. I was second to try the ones in
Dorey Park, but I was FTF on the rope. The other boxer going for all the
FTFs missed it. As a minor hint, I recall joking that the leaves they had
originally covered the rope with turned to sand.

I'm having trouble with landmarks in Poor Farm Park. I'm getting ready for
my fourth visit there. In fairness, I haven't been just letterboxing while
there. There is also a seven part geocache (#1 has the lat/long for #2,
etc.). Since I print maps and don't carry a GPS for that hobby, I have to do
those one at a time, so I'm only getting a couple of boxes on each visit. It
took two visits to see the landmarks for #1 and #4 and I haven't found the
landmarks for #3 in three attempts.

Remember that there is a fourth park involved. I know where it is and it is
the most central of the four. I know Dorey and Poor Farm have shelters. I'm
remember that the fourth park had shelters the last time I was there, but I
haven't been there in over ten years. I haven't been to Powhite yet.

I did all five in Dorey in about 100 minutes, which included 9 holes of disc
golf. Knowing the trails at Poor Farm, those boxes are doable in about 3
hours. The owners posted an estimate of two hours or so for Powhite. The
fourth park is fairly small, so three should take more than 45 minutes
there. I think it's possible to do the series in a very long day. Figure 7
hours of actual walking, plus an hour between each park (30-40 drive, 20-30
rest and eat). The proposed date in October is still in Savings time, so
that will help some.

For camping, Pocahontas is the nearest state park. There is a private
campground near the interstate just south of Ashland, but reports about that
place vary, with negatives outnumbering positives. With careful shopping,
you should be able to find an acceptable motel under $40, even on a
Saturday.

I know biking is OK at Powhite and Poor Farm, but I'm not sure about Dorey.

Mike
