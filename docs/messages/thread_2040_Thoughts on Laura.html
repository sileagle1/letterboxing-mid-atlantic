Re: Thoughts on Laura

From: Myke Scholtes | Date: 2006-10-13 00:36:46 UTC
MizScarlet & H&H,

Thank you for your kind words. I forget who coined the term "Brother
Buzzard," but I absolutely love it. It was very difficult to deliver
that sermon today, but I'm so glad I was able to help in that way.

Today was a GOOD day, I think. Not an easy one, not a happy one. But
a GOOD one.

MizScarlet, I gotta say, you're 100% right: "It's not just finding
tupperware in the woods, it's finding the people who find the
tupperware." That's the whole thing.

I think I have to break down here a bit. When I discovered
letterboxing in January 2004, I immediately loved it, and I loved
doing it ALONE. I was a solo boxer, although I dragged some friends
along from time to time. I had no interest in meeting all of you, or
even knowing who you were in real life...I thought the anonymity was
part of what made it cool. I loved how I could kind of, sort of,
connect with other people in this distant way. Then the Silent One
emailed me and brought me into the fold. I went very hesitantly and
tentatively to Boxtoberfest 2004, and Team Lightin' Bug immediately
grabbed me and said, "Let's go looking for boxes!" And then, slowly,
I met so many of you. And slowly, I've begun to care for and love so
many of you. I still enjoy boxing alone a lot of the time, but
there's something about being in this community that's beyond words.
How else could Cyclonic and I get along so well? He and I are about
as far apart as two people can get on the political spectrum, and we
both feel pretty strongly about our views. In another venue, perhaps
he and I would have written each other off. Nonetheless, I view him
as a dear friend.

And that's just one example. How do we do this? How does stamp
carving and treasure hunting do this? How has this hobby, this HOBBY,
touched me in ways as deep as I've ever felt in church? How do I have
the nerve to mass email this to so many people? I don't know, but
thank God all this is true.

I am honored, awed, and humbled right now. I am in the presence of
greatness. This group is just incredible. And you know what, I think
a lot of us have noticed this recently. Laura has helped us realize
this. "A child shall lead them." Thank God for Laura!

Let's continue to be incredible to each other, and especially to Sheba
over the next weeks, months, years. Let's continue to be this source
of joy and hope, this conduit of all the great things people can be.

Peace,
Brother Buzzard

* doesn't this guy EVER stop preaching? *

Sheba is the strongest, most amazing woman I know. I hug her as we come
into the home, and she looks up and says to me, "You DO have a tie!" I
find this amazing.

I know I am taking this a bit hard, more then I would have ever thought
I would. I don't know what it is about the whole situation, but I find
even now I have tears welling up at the edges of my eyes, just barely
being held back. I cannot imagine what Sheba is going through, but one
thing is for sure, she has people here that love her and care for her
very much, and I truly believe that is helping her get through this.

The Mainline Maven, Mizscalet, you have hit on something that I think
often gets overlooked. Those carefully planned (or in Irene's case,
just thrown) boxes have people behind them. They have a story all their
own. It is often said that to find some of the mystery boxes you have
to get into the head of those who planted them, think like they think,
see things as they see things. But that can be said of any box. They
each have a story to tell, not only about themselves, but also about
those who have left them.

I am glad that we have managed to pull those people together. We all
from all walks of life, and all have our own story to tell. We all
share in the joy of a milestone reached, and the sorrow of a lose.

I think that if there is anything to be gleaned from this, it is that
was are not alone, we are a family, and though we may disagree, may
fight and argue, we will always be connected by those pieces of plastic
left in the woods.

Laura has truly left us a gift, let us not squander or forget it.
