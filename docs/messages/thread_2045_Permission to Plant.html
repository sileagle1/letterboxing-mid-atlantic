Permission to Plant

From: Nathan Brown | Date: 2006-10-13 18:10:51 UTC
Well, some good news from Lower Bucks today.

First, Debbie of the Radetoan's and I met with Bensalem Parks and Rec
this morning, and they pretty much gave us the go ahead to plant in
Bensalem Township Parks. All you have to do is send Parks and Rec the
clues when the box is planted and let them know what park it is is so
that public works doesn't trash it.

Debbie is the front person on this one, and she lurks here on the list,
so now that she is outed she can give the details ;-)

Also, today I offically recieved permission to plant in Neshaminy State
Park. This was kinda an experiment to get to know the proccess on it.
The DNCR has a form that needs to be filled out and the box needs to be
approved then. It took about two weeks all together, it would be faster
if I had scouted a location ahead of time though. In any case, next
week you can expect to find the Neshaminy Hitch Hiker Hostel in the
park. Time for you to get to work Irene, I've already warned them.

--
Nathan Brown

AKA Cyclonic
Penncoasters.com

www.ricksantorum.com

Hi! My family and I are fairly new to letterboxing (a little over two
months) and we are enjoying every minute of it. We have visited some
of the boxes of the people who post here and every one has been a
wonderful adventure. We call our team "Radetoan" which stands for RAy,
DEbbie, TOmmy, and ANnie. Tommy and Annie are five and two. Because
we live in Bensalem Twp, Bucks County, we thought it would be great to
plant some boxes in our home town. The problem was that if any of the
township employees happened to come across the boxes here, they would
throw them away. So my husband suggested that we get permission to
plant before we did anything. Any way, long story short, after a
couple of letters to Parks and Rec and some help from Hikers and
Hounds, and Cyclonic, we now have permission to plant in any of the
parks throughout the entire township. However, a copy of the clues and
the name of the park must be sent to Sabrina Stancil of Parks and
Recreation at the township building. The address can be found on the
bensalem township website.

I'm looking forward to meeting you all on the trails. And Cyclonic...I
