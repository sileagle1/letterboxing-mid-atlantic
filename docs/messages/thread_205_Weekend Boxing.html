From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2004-07-18 18:50:23 UTC
Howdy All!

Fun in the Finger Lakes of NY with the Lightnin' Bugs....

Well, Mrs. Lightnin' Bug and I just got back from the Finger Lakes
Wine Festival at Watkins Glen Racetrack. The little bugs were with
Oma for the weekend. We had a blast, and the boss even partook in a
few hunts [In exchange for a visit to a bead shop in Luzerne, PA on
the way up - Not at all a problem or concern for an enlightened
bug! ;-) ]

Anyway, the Wine Festival was a blast - That and a couple visits to
some Lightnin' Bug family favorite wineriess resulted in some
consumption and perhaps a few purchases. The Letterboxing was fun as
well, and Mrs. Lightnin' Bug realized that this was something
that 'she could get into' as she is a big fan of solving mysteries.
The downside is that Mrs. Lightnin' Bug now has a nickname - The
Skeeter Pincushion! While I was only tapped about 3 or 4 times, poor
pincushion was tapped at least 20 times (we stopped counting) and we
had to buy some hydrocortisone cream. Maybe a skeeter magnet stamp
to accompany our personal traveler stamp.

All told, we found 3 boxes together:

Blueberry Patch - Hector, Finger Lakes Nat Forest (should be renamed
skeeter patch!!)
The Middle - Finger lakes mystery series (mystery, but very easy if
you know the area)
Sacajawea Stumper Geocache Letterbox Combo - Watkins Glen State Park -
Caution NO STAMP! Took a card with a sort of stamp and will
consider that the stamp... %-|

On Sunday morning, I ventured off while the Mrs. snoozed to find 2
more:

Spencer Crest Nature Center, Corning, NY
Turtle Pond, Corning NY

This is a cool little preserve on the south side of the mountain
south of Corning, NY.

Rain forced us to pass on Carantouan Springs in Waverly, NY on the
way home. Regrets.

Hope everbody had a good weekend. Now to get over an incipient
migraine headache...

Lightnin' Bug

Weekend Boxing

From: Art Vandelay | Date: 2004-08-08 21:19:04 UTC
Sorry.  Should have posted here (Thanks Irene).We had such big plans for boxing yesterday.  Went to Bucks County to move my college son from his summer dorm to his new dorm (he hurt his back last month and we didn't want him to do any heavy lifting himself).  Actually we had planned almost the same boxes as "highlysung" and thought maybe we'd meet.  Well, the move took much longer than we expected and our plans went out the window.  We ended up doing the 2 boxes in the Mercer Hunt in Doylestown which we found without much trouble.  We had followed "Big Mac" in the search and may have actually caught a glimpse of him when we started out (if he rode a bicycle).  There was a wedding reception going on at Fonthill.  Actually it was our anniversary yesterday but since we had no time to celebrate, we'll celebrate today instead.
Also went to Dollar Tree in PA for 1st time.  Biggest dollar store I've ever seen- picked up some of the Milan Classic boxes someone wrote about.  They are good.  Also bought the one and only 2 pack of spiral bound index cards they had.

Today we went out for a huge anniversary meal- we were stuffed.  Afterwards we walked it off with a search for Rifle Camp box in W. Paterson NJ.  Clues said the box was planted April 6, 2003 but the clues were listed on the "new" boxes on August 5, 2004.  We were the first to sign the logbook.

Cat

the only volcanic-based rocks in NJ, and boy, does it ever look it.

vanished. Anyone know if the box is still there?

Friday. LOL. I thought the "not for the faint-hearted" was to be
taken together with "wear good shoes" to mean a long hike. NOT. And
now I know. I'm faint-hearted.

I got my car out of the shop on Friday (for less than the estimate!) and immediately went mad, driving all over the area like a thing possessed.
 
On Saturday morning I drove down to Calvert Cliffs State Park in MD and found the box there.  I stamped in but soon some people came down the path!  I quickly stashed the box and sat down and looked thoughtful while scribbling in my notebook; I watched them out of the corner of my eye, but I doubt they even noticed me!
 
I got things properly squared away after they were gone, and proceeded to the beach where I enjoyed the fine sea breezes and hunted for fossils.  I left when hunger overcame me!
 
I had wanted to hunt down the boxes at St. Mary's City and in Point Lookout, but I had to get back to Washington for my evening plans.  Hopefully next time....
