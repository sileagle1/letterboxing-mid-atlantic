From: Hikers & Hounds | Date: 2006-10-22 21:27:47 UTC
Oh please! If you're going to have a camp fire then dang it, MAKE IT A FIRE!!!!!!!!! Woo-hoo! Thanks to Bev and Rob our hosts and everyone who came out. I had a lovely time. I missed my best friend Dude though and think this might be my last gather that he can't go to (well, except the Pub Crawl of course. Love my dog, but LOVE to crawl!).
 
I got to meet several new (to me anyway) boxers and enjoyed chatting with all of them so much! And re-aquainted myself with a couple I met two years ago who have successfully hunted most of my boxes and it was so rewarding to see them all so pretty in their beautiful log book. What a treat!
 
LOVED Miss Moon's trip report, LOL! Just remember that old song Miss Moon "I found love on a two way street, but lost it on a lonely highway"...be careful of those stuck-in-traffic-on-the-bridge romances, they rarely last.
 
As far as the guffawing and carrying on in the parking lot, all I can say is we did Sowsewbizzy proud! Thank goodness Miz Scarlet has such slender arms 'cause I was looking around for a coat hanger or maybe just a real big rock! Hey, the owner is in the forgiveness business so it's a given I could have gotten away with it! Yeeeeeha!
 
And lastly, I have spent two memorable evenings around the campfire with the Von Der Insels and have decided they are deliciously demented! What a freaking riot. First they get us all drunk with hot mulled wine and the next thing I know we are all singing cartoon theme songs. Dave smoked us all by knowing all the words to "Under Dog".
 
What a great time! Oh yeah, I actually did a little boxing too, go figure!
 
Thanks to all,
 
H&H

At gatherings we exchange personal stamps, one reason
we love newbies at gatherings is new stamps. Some
people grab a few local boxes and some boxes are
planted just for gathers. Some of us just sit and gab.

contribution. It's a great time to put faces with
stamps you may have seen in boxes. Little ones are
most welcome. The pub crawl being an exception.
