Since this is my hometown, I did some checking, and didn't turn up
anything in the Hanover newspaper.

But I did find that the September/October issue of the Friends of
Codorus State Park newsletter included a story on letterboxing, which
I found a bit surprising (which you'll see as you read it below). It
may have accounted for some of the increased activity in the area.

Here's the text of the article:

>Letterboxing
>
>Thus begins a 'treasure hunt' that I think is one of Codorus State
>Park's unique features. If you've never heard of "Letterboxing", I
>think you'll find it can be a great family outing.
>
>Go to 'Silent Doug's Letterbox' website on your computer for the
>full riddle. The web address is www.letterboxing.info - copies of
>the riddle are also available at the park office. Follow the clues
>and you will find Doug's letterbox. Inside you will find a stamp and
>a book. You bring a stamp of your own to stamp Doug's book, then
>sign it and use his stamp to mark one of your own. You need to bring
>your own inkpad.
>
>When you return, go back to the website to report that you have
>found the cache.
>
>If you enjoy this hunt, there are Letterboxes EVERYWHERE!!
>
>It is a fun game. You can nickname yourself and hit the letterbox
>trail using a stamp that becomes your signature! This is great
>experience in learning to follow directions. Make sure you read each
>one at least twice and understand which way he is directing you
>before you take the chosen trail. Doug points you to some pretty
>unique features of the trail. He is very observant about peculiar
>shapes of trees.
>
>The last time I checked, this box was still in place. Some have been
>stolen, a sad ending for those excited about the end of their
>treasure hunt. Please place the box back just as you found it so the
>next players in the game can find it.

Here's the complete issue:
http://www.friendsofcodorus.org/PDF/SeptOct_screen.pdf

SD
