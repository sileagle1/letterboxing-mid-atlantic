Redden State Forest - DE

From: Judy B | Date: 2006-10-25 19:22:01 UTC
Stopped by the office on way back to VA today and picked up the TWO
Forest Finds they had pulled. They also had "The Heart" "The
Cross" "The Dove" and a fourth by someone ?? -- rather water damaged
and I didn't have my LetterBoxing gear to stamp in . . .

Seems when one of the boxes was found the ranger went online and saw
there were others in the Forest!!! Promptly went out and pulled them.
Plus they had a big ammo geocache and were still looking for another.

The lady at the desk was very nice and impressed with the Smokey Bear
stamp. I asked if they were discouraging visitors to the forest and
even tho they don't charge an entrance fee, might want to consider
allowing LetterBoxes with application . . . the State Parks do!

She thought they were a good idea and didn't harm anyone! I added
with application they could control that they were not way off trail
(hazardous). She might mention it up the line. Told her I would
check back next Spring (since I almost go by the place on my drive to
Skogen).

Judy B
sewsowbizzy

Jessica had 4 boxes at Redden, yes.

When we found them over the Memorial Day Weekend, I recall that at
least one or two were barely hidden, but I figured that the state
forest did get the kind of traffic that a state park does, which is why
they were still around.

I hope that the state forests are willing to copy the parks' approach
to letterboxes. However, parks cannot allow a box in any section that
is designated as a nature preserve, and if a state forest has that
status...

Agreed with Otis' Friends - we have emailed Jessica about her boxes and
received no reply. She may not be very active at the moment.
