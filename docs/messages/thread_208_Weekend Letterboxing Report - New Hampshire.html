Weekend Letterboxing Report - New Hampshire

From: Hikers_n_ Hounds | Date: 2004-07-18 22:36:54 UTC
Well I'm back in Pa after a wonderful weekend in beautiful New Hampshire. My friends and I went out and found 9 boxes (woo-hoo!) and met up with several local NH boxers for exchanges. These people were so nice they actually drove (with us following behind them) to a couple of the boxes for us. Wow! I also planted our very first hitchiker, Sailin' Shoe in honor of one of our favorite bands, Little Feat. So all in all it was a rousing success. We found:
1. Valley Street Cemetery
2. Amy Marcy Cheny
3. Franklin Peirce
4. Deering Overlook
5. Mine Falls Park
6. 1886*
7. Gilson Road
8. Blue Bears Adventure - 2 boxes
 
All boxes were great, in great locations. I must make a special comment about Blue Bears Adventure. These clues were so much fun to follow and the park was so beautiful that if you get the chance to go to NH, make sure you hunt these. Check out the clues on the homepage, how original!
 
While there I also was inspired to create a hitchiker and mail to my friends to set loose for me. I think I will call it the "New Hampshire State Bird" it will be an image of THE BIGGEST DARN MESQUITOYOU HAVE EVER SEEN. I am not kidding folks, I have never seen or felt anything like these mesquitos! They are HUGE, drink gallons of blood and actually followed us into our car so they could continue feasting on us. I have been camping my whole life and been bitten millions of times, but I must have over 100 red welts, and that's while using bug spray! I was reading Lightnin Bugs account of his wife's trouble with mesquitos in NY this weekend. Tell her it could have been worse, she could have been in New Hampshire! But in spite of all that, I can't wait to go back!
 
Hope everyone had a great weekend!
 
H&H - P12, F41, X60, HH 1(planted), PLB1 (Planted)

From: rpboehme@yahoo.com | Date: 2004-07-18 23:19:04 UTC
We feel your pain. The Finger Lakes had much rain recently, so there
were many places to breed and we were fresh blood, so to speak.

Were these MH mosquitoes yellow with stripes?? The ones that hounded
the Mrs. looked like that. The NYS skeeters were definately bigger
than the PA skeeters. I should have remembered the upstate NY
skeeters, having spent a couple summers up there during the college
years and going on several hikes.

Nuthin', but nuthin' beats the misery of encounters with black flies
though. They laugh at DEET! Black flies need cold running water to
breed, so their peak season is actually earlier (like May-June in the
Adirondacks) than peak skeeter season (more like July). Anyway,
unlike skeeters, which drink you blood though their 'straw', black
flies sit on your skin and slice it open. They wait for the blood to
pool in a drop and slurp it up. Nasty big welts, itchy too!

Lightnin' Bug
