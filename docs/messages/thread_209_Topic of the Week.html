I sent out my first PLB (Apologies) last Fri - and am waiting to
receive the three for which I have signed up. I think they are a
nice SIDELINE to the LB'g Adventure. They do take a lot of time to
prepare - it is generally more than a stamp & log in a sandwich box.
I saw one at the PA gather and it also was a box of humor. Sometimes
there are treats (since the number of stampers is pre-known). But I
think the best part is that folks who may not be able to go hiking
can be part of the fun. Just like virtual letterboxing (but that's
another TOTW). And I'm not saying this just because I'm a Postal
employee (at least for another 8 months!) and appreciate the revenue.

From: Hikers_n_ Hounds | Date: 2004-07-26 16:09:27 UTC
Sorry FdL am jumping in here with a problem I am experiencing on the new boxers list and need the opinions of my "homies"
 
On the New Boxers list I inadvertantly started a big honkin' fuss about putting log's in each box for a multiple box series. I innocently stated that on our next series, which will be 7 boxes aimed at kids, we will only be using 1 log in the very last box. We are also starting work on our "Holy Grail of Letterboxing" series will wil be 4-6 boxes and Carmen and I decided the same thing. Maybe a log in the first box to welcome boxers, and a log in the last box to thank them for playing.  Well, Some folks are pretty upset about this and gettin' pretty frisky in their posts implying that I am lazy (which I am, so good for them for noticing) and that I am somehow "cheating" my fellow boxers of the fun that they get stamping into each box and even more importantly, of counting a box as a find. To which I want to ask "sez who?" Did you not print the clues, find the box and stamp your log? Then WHO says it's not a find just cause there's no log? The International Letterboxing Council? What is the ILC's address so that I can write them for details? It's not that I am too cheap to buy logs, I have no problem with that and I certainly am not advocating everyone do this, it's just something Carmen and I decided. Of course I will put this in my clues so those purists who feel they can not in good conscience count 7 seperate boxes with 7 seperate stamps as 7 seperate finds simply because there aren't 7 seperate logs can avoid these abominations at all costs! Heresy! Treason! Stay away!
 
So anyway, my questions to you folks are, would this upset/insult you and can this really affect someone's count? Would your enjoyment of the series be diminished by not having a log in each box? Since you folks are in my planting area I do not want to insult or upset anyone else. Maybe I will put a log in each box but tell folks I don't expect them to stamp into each one if they don't feel like it. But that kind of irks me 'cause now I feel that I am being forced to change my mind and I am a very stubborn person. But if it really is in the rules and will mess up people's counts or spoil their fun then that's not cool either. 
 
I must say I am constantly surprised at the things LB's get so worked up about and how often I manage to put my foot in my mouth with this hobby. A new person asked a question, I gave them my 2 cents and have gotten a $1.50 butt kickin for it. Oh well, that's what I deserve I guess.
 
Off topic - Dude is pretty darn sick and I am a mess. He hasn't been able to hold any food down since Saturday night. He was fine all day Saturday on the trails. I will probably be taking him to the Vet tonight. Dude is my best friend in the whole wide world and even though he is 14 and I know what's coming I am not ready to lose him yet. And I know he's just a dog but keep him in your thoughts.
 
H&H

From: cahillymom | Date: 2004-07-26 17:27:38 UTC
Irene,
No rules.
Whatever.
In this case, whatever suits the series. I have a series of four
with a book in each, and in going back and reading entries, realized
they're a little close together for that. By the third book, folks
have run out of things to say and just stamp in. The fourth book
mainly shows how many haven't found the third.
My Tangram series has one book, which allowed the other boxes to be
much smaller and easier to hide. Again, very short hikes between
boxes.
I've found series where you had to hike at least half an hour
between boxes. They, IMO, deserved separate books, and had them.
If you think one book will do, go for it. How on earth could we,
sitting cozy at our keyboards, tell what suits the site?
