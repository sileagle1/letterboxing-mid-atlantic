Re: OT: Best Hiking in PA

From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2006-11-12 00:10:03 UTC
Hey Natt,

It all depends upon what you are looking for. The views from
Susquehannock SP over the Susquehannah river are excellent and really
short strolls from the parking area.

If you are looking for the more challenging, I highly recommend the
hike up to Pulpit Rock or the Pinnacle. You will be rewarded with
some of the best views in Eastern PA.

An excellent box related view can be found at "Ashram with a View" by
JBZFarm. It is also a climb, but my favorite view of the Susky
river. It is my favorite box-related view.

Big Pocono, home to some boxen, some other things, has a view form
one of the highest elevations in eastern PA. It is road accessible
through the end of huntin' season. You can see into NY from there on
a clear day.

Cheers,

LB

Some memorable hikes from backpacking trips and hikes in PA:
- The Black Forest Trail in the Tiadaghton State Forest. It has some great vistas (including the Pine Creek Gorge), hiking along steams in dark hollows and an old growth hemlock tree. Very rugged terrain and going either steeply up or sharply down most of the time.
- The Appalachian Trail in the Michaux State Forest. I was struck by how pleasant the hiking was (not nearly as rocky as the rest of PA) and all the quartz. One cliff face had a vein of it coming down.
- The Northern half of the Perkiomen Trail in Montgomery County.
- Ringing rocks park in Bucks County. The only hike that requires a hammer (the rocks really ring like bells) and a great waterfall that you can get close to.
- The Appalachian Trail Â from PA 309 to Bake Oven Knob. I might be biased because I've hiked to so often. But I love Knifes Edge, Bear Rocks and Bake Oven Knob for rock scrambling and views.
