GladWare not fireproof

From: Audrey | Date: 2006-11-13 04:09:21 UTC
Well, the verdict's in. Not only does GladWare crack in cold weather,
it melts when heated. At least we think it was heated. It was
definitely melted. (see photo added to Misc. folder) Best guess is a
small fire in the vicinity of Terra-Cotta Tangram box #4. There was a
gaping hole in the side and lid of the container, the edges were all
collapsed in and fused to each other and to the baggie. And the stamp
came off its backing, but was otherwise fine. Go figure.
Anyway, Tangram series is now safe in new Rubbermaid.
Aud

From: Clueless | Date: 2006-11-14 03:48:32 UTC
Doug and I found a rubbermaid box earlier this year that had protected the contents remarkably well through what appeared to have been a pretty decent fire.  The stamp and logbook were usable, though the box was quite melted, as were parts of the bags.  The box was buried under some rocks, and pretty much firmed up our decision to plant boxes that way ourselves from now on.
 
My cream colored hiking pants, on the other hand, did not survive the forest fire so well.  As the path had burned in the fire, it took us quite a while to locate the box, stumbling through charred brush.  Then I actually sat on the root of the tree, moving around a bit to get the box rehidden well.  Then I looked down and around at my pants and...ohhh...not so pretty.
