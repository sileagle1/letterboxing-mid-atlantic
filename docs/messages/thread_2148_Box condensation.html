Re: Box condensation

From: Judy B | Date: 2006-11-19 23:55:53 UTC
The dessicant pack discussion has been held on the Big list - not
sure if actual condensation - wetness - is the mission of dessicant
vs humitdy before it condenses.

But a good double bagging that folks actually take the time to 100%
seal should work. When "The Sailboat - aka Winnoa Senior - was
completely under water during the Oct 6 deluge in DE, WI Hiker found
it on Monday after the waters receded with 1/4" water in the box and
no water in the bags or damage to contents.

When a box gets a lot of visitors and the bags are constantly open'd,
the seal will lose its umph (see today's Post Travel section on quart
bags for luggage) even when it appears to be sealed.

Being the wonderful LB'g group we are, maybe we could take the
initiative to regularly RePlace bags AND annotate the log when that
was done. Then 6-10 visitors later a finder would know - Time to
Replace the Bags. OK, so maybe it is too early for New Years
Resolutions!

Judy

Walnutport is a town near the Lehigh Gap in PA. There was a box
