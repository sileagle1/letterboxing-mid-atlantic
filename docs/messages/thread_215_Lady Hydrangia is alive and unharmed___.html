Subject: [lbma] Re: [LbNA] A Sad State of Affairs - question for the group

Dearest Neighbors,

Her Ladyship senses the possibility of jumping to a conclusion lacking
in veracity. How can one be sure that the last person to stamp in a
logbook is responsible for the state in which the next person finds a
box? Would it not be possible that the box could have been discovered
and left along the trail by a non-boxer? It sounds as if the four of
you who have built cases based on circumstantial evidence should
perhaps confer privately to determine that you are indeed considering
the same suspect. As it is your public speculation has thrown into
question the reputation of every father and son letter boxing team in
the mid-Atlantic region. In the event that you all have come up with
the same suspect remember that your case is still based on
circumstantial evidence.

Several of our comrades in boxing in New England have expressed the
opinion that vandalized or removed boxes often are not the target of
malicious intent or lackadaisical re-hiding but a poor hiding spot. If
you find your boxes frequently go missing ask yourself if it could be
your own fault for choosing a poor location. Her Ladyship's "Real
Story of Steam Power" box is not usually concealed as we initially
left it. This tells Her Ladyship that we should have chosen a better
spot. Her Ladyship thinks it unfair to hide a box in the middle of an
open area under some twigs and leaves then expecting it to remain
concealed. Her ladyship feels that just a tad more effort should go
into choosing the place of concealment. Consider also that these boxes
are covered in human scent which animals are keenly adapted to
locating and will expose boxes in the hope of finding food which they
have, through operant conditioning, come to associate with human
scent. Small mammals will often push and drag their uncovered
treasures about in an attempt to open the box and, lacking the
opposable pollex of the human digital appendage, leave it unopened and
distant from it's hiding spot. Perhaps your suspect merely leaves an
enticing scent on the boxes he or she finds. Her Ladyship hopes you
will temper your accusations with compassion and all due demurral to
our nations time honored principal of a presumption of innocence.

Respectfully submitted,
Lady Prisspott
