Newbie, first box found and a question

From: carissanboys | Date: 2006-11-25 18:32:27 UTC
Hi everyone! My family and I just began our letterboxing journey and
went out today and found our first letterbox. It was the Bay
Bridge/Sandy Point letterbox and we had a wonderful time. We had
never been to that park before and what a beautiful place!!

My question is that we found a hitchhiker in the box. I had read a
bit about hitchhikers and was tempted to take it with us as we're
planning on finding another letterbox tomorrow. But since it was our
first time and wasn't completely sure about etiquette, we left it
behind. Should we have left our stamps in the hitchhiker's logbook
or is that simply for the letterbox's that it stays with? And is
there a way to search for info on the hitchhiker? It did have an
email address but I forgot to write it down (we have 3 boys ages 2,
4, and 6 and since it was our first time, there was a lot to remember
to do!). I'm assuming hitchhikers are kind of like word of mouth
boxes...not really a way to find out more.

Another reason I didn't take the hitchhiker along is that the box
we're planning on looking for tomorrow hasn't been found yet (at
least not recorded on atlasquest.com) and I was afraid to put it
somewhere that it might not be found again. If you pick up the
hitchhiker, are you suppose to let the owner know where you placed it
or just where you found it?

Thanks so much for the help. I'm very excited about this new hobby
for our family and look forward to getting to know you all.

From: Judy B | Date: 2006-11-25 19:00:48 UTC

WELCOME to LBMA - a great group of hilarious folks - who hope to meet you/all at a gather sometime soon (check AQ often for gathers in the area).

There is a HH yahoo group you might review some of the posts there for answers:
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/letterbox_travellers/

And there is another group for newbies that might be of interest:
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/newboxers/

Personally, I think you should only stamp in a HH if you are taking it with you to move along.  Whether you leave it in a popular box or an obscure box makes no difference.  I also report to the planter where I found it (if I can remember) and where I left it.  Not sure what info they post on the HH talk list.

Well, you have certainly found the right activity for family fun.
