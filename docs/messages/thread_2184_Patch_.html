From: Nathan Brown | Date: 2006-12-06 17:51:50 UTC
kpiletterbox wrote on 12/5/2006, 6:55 PM:

> I am also planning a gather that will be
> announced in the local newspaper, it will be a workshop to get
> people interested in letterboxing in the area. I'd like the
> Pittsburgh area to become sorta like the Tyler State Park area,
> where it seems you could store enough food to serve a battalion with
> all the tupperware lying about. I will do like a two hour tutorial
> on the basics, and provide clues to a freshly planted letterbox just
> for the event.

Now, I am the one that is usually all for publicity, but I don't think
this is such a good idea. If I am approached by a newspaper or TV, I am
fine, but I don't think going out and looking for publicity is the right
way to go about it.

What I would suggest you do, instead of having a workshop that you
advertise, start to work with your local parks. Really get to know the
ins and outs of the hobby as well as the ins and outs of how the parks
in your area work, and then if one of the parks wants to do something,
set it up through them. This is a slower approach, but in the long run
a better one.
