From: Hikers and Hounds | Date: 2006-12-11 00:25:41 UTC
Mr. Irene, Dude and I were in Delaware last night for our caving club's holiday party. We stayed over at our friends in Newark and then headed up to Valley Garden Park, one of my favorite places on the planet, to do some maintenance and boxing. Re-vamped "Long Strange Trip" series, added 2 more boxes to it, Truckin' and Playing in the Band-the event stamp from last weekends gather. Grabbed the wedding box in there but ran out of steam before we got Under the Fairy Moon and FOO's box (Friends of Otis). Which means Dude and I must head back ASAP after the holidays to get those 2 plus all of the new ones popping up all over the area.
 
Every time I do maintenance on these boxes, I run into boxers. This time it was the Chambers family from Hockessin out enjoying the day. They are newer boxers brought in by the Family Fun article and were so much fun to chat and exchange with. I have invited them to join us on this list and hope to see them here soon.
 
I see the list was very quiet today so hopefully everyone was busy enjoying themselves on way or another. Take care and box softly!
 
H&H

I actually went out yesterday, and since Trogdor
wanted to sleep in, I did a solo trip way out west to
Montgomery County, MD. Had an absolutely lovely day
of near-solitude, exploring a couple of parks courtesy
of Yachty Girl and the Energizer Bunnie.

I did come across one "murder" scene. Climbed up a
hill for a "Far, Far, Away" box and came across a
ziplock bag, chewed into pieces. Came around the log
and found a second ziplock bag, similarly torn and
chewed. Then there was a tupperware lid. Then the
bottom of the box ten feet away. And nestled in the
oak leaves, the logbook, fortunately not too mangled
and not too damp. Couldn't ever find the Donkey
stamp, although it might have been there in the leaves
somewhere. Or in the gullet of a ...was that the
lingering scent of eau de SKUNK?

My first crime scene, and it was shocking.
