Re: [lbma] What makes you choose a location to plant?

From: Otis' Friends | Date: 2006-12-22 09:41:37 UTC
I've done both, but I try to match the stamp to the location if possible (which can mean finding the right location or carving the right stamp).  In other circumstances, the stamp and the clues will be integrated together, and the location just needs to be a good location and not related in any way.  ("Good location" can include any of the following: nice hike, interesting place, not in plain sight, pre-existing hidey holes, etc.)  When everything comes together, it's a moment of boxing zen.  But you need not feel any obligation to match clues, stamp, and/or location.  Some of the most popular boxes do not.

Re: What makes you choose a location to plant?

From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2006-12-22 11:24:22 UTC
It truly depends upon the purpose. Sometimes the location drives the
stamp (like the soon to be planted Velvet Elvis), sometimes I'll have
a particular stamp that needs a location (like my recent ambigram
boxes) and it really does not matter. But my favorites are where the
clue concept (as in eevil boxes) drives the stamp and sometimes the
location.

Some people "choose" to only find stamps that match a particular
location, but by the same talking they exclude themselves from a lot
of creativity.

So, you can take your pick as there is no hard fast rule.

LB

Re: What makes you choose a location to plant?

From: nattybumppolbna | Date: 2006-12-22 13:29:28 UTC
The Ancients (well, Scarab, mostly) speak of not seeking the place of planting, but
allowing the place to choose you.
Late at night, around the campfire, over a flagon of mulled cider, after the Newbies have
found their boxes and left in their minivans, the Old-Timers (well, Irene, mostly) speak in
hushed tones of the boxes that call to them on the trail. They speak of walking down back
trails, off the beaten path, and spying a tree stump, or a SPOR, or a bark pile and knowing
- just knowing - the box was there without ever consulting the clue.


Be wary of planting in sensitive ecological areas.
Check the geocache listings - don't plant too close or the treasure-raiding hordes may
think your stamp is a trading toy.
If you plant in dangerous terrain, let people know in the clue so they can safely gauge their
abilities.
Know that terrain changes. Streams flood. A great winter plant may be inaccessable
through summer undergrowth
Most importantly, follow your bliss, young grasshopper. Some of the best 'boxes came
from people changing the rules.
Rules? Did I say "rules?" What rules?
Make it fun. Make it the kind of box you wish someone had planted for you.
