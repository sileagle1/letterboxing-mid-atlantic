Where are you boxing this weekend?

I was thinking that I have two windows for boxing this weekend:
Saturday (7/24) Morning at Green Lane with Thomas (have a couple to
find yet and some others to research including Chin Knees and the new
ones H+H provided at the Gathering). I'm on kid patrol in the
afternoon, as Mrs. LB is doing a beading thing at Kalmbach Park in
Macungie (a future LB plant!).

Sunday morning is Mass, but Sunday (7/25) Afternoon is full of
possibilities. I'm thinking maybe a return to my old hiking grounds
of the Pinnacle and Pulpit Rock (complete with time saving
shortcuts). This one, being more strenuous, would be sans Thomas.

Down the road, someday, I'd like to tackle some mystery boxes as I
think that I figured out exactly where the boxes are - Silent
Doug's "Stone Face", the Mapsurfer's "Chester County Compass
Conundrum" and almost totally solving "Speaking in Ancient Tongues"
(sorry, I am German, not Basque, but the internet is helpful). If
anybody wants to trade notes or involve me in a mystery solving
partnership, please let me know.

Any takers?

Lightnin' Bug

From: Hikers_n_ Hounds | Date: 2004-07-25 13:37:13 UTC
I did some box maintenance yesterday and then Dude and I hunted for Brandywine Springs boxes planted by HSC Pride. Brandywine Springs Park is interesting in that it used to be a commercial amusement park until about 1915 or so then closed down. The forest has re-claimed the area but there are still foundations and railroad tracks, etc all around. And along the trail there are pictures showing what the park used to look like in certain areas. Pretty interesting. Found the first box totally flooded so dried it out and did repairs. Never found the second box. I am pretty darn sure I was in the right spot at the right tree but the tree has so badly desintegrated into wood pulp it was impossible to search efficiently. I emailed the placers to find out what the deal is. Today is putter around the house day. Happy Hunting!
