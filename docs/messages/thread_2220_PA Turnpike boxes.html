From: kpiletterbox | Date: 2006-12-29 20:12:24 UTC
I highly recommend holy turnpike as it is a great carving in a cool
site. There are also two more at rest stops, one in somerset called "I-
76 Somerset PA" and one called "Who is Peter J Camiel" they are both
active, as I have held both on the 22nd of December.

If you stumble upon the "America's First Superhighway" boxes (they are
not listed on AQ or LB.org, they are on a private website) I know the
westbound one has been muggled, as I held it's shattered remnants in my
hand. I also could not find the east bound one, so if it's there I
followed the clues wrong.

Thanks to Cyclonic, we found the clues on lb.org and deciphered no
problem for a recent trip on the PA TP. The only difficulty was that
my father (Games grilling man of Two Cooks) and I are directionally
challenged and messed up west vs east bound, and so stopped at the
wrong place whilst westbound. We found various unmentionables but
obviously no box, muggled or otherwise. Of course, we didn't figure
it out until we had passed the westbound stop while travelling in the
correct direction.

re: Who is Peter J. Camiel?
One of our favorite boxes of all time.

When I was a kid, we often travelled the TP on the way to visit
relatives in OH. My imagination was always taken by this place, and
we pointed it out to the kids on our recent trip because it is so
unique. Glad to see I know have a good reason to stop (as if I didn't
before! but somehow never did).

From: the B's | Date: 2007-01-11 03:09:24 UTC
We just did that trip not too long ago (October, to be exact).

We took a brief side trip to Zelienople, which is north of Pittsburgh
but not too far off 76. I highly recommend the "Harmonist Cemetary"
box as a very cool place to visit -- unlike anything we had ever seen.

A little closer to home, but a nice place to make your first pit stop
and stretch your legs, the "NH Lady Slipper Finds a Home in PA" box,
in Carlisle, is one of my all-time favorite stamps. Just gorgeous. We
also got the "PA Women's Series #1 -- Mary Macauley" stamp in
Carlisle, which is a great stamp by Crayola Posse.
