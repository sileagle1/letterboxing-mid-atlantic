Hitchhikers with a Goal - A Great Race

From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2004-07-24 23:46:16 UTC
Here's something for you HH fans to ponder...

The idea actually came from a couple of geocachers, but plagiarism is
the finest form of flattery.

A father and son team had a couple of "travel bugs" that they were
racing from Duluth, MN to Allentown, PA. A travel bug is kind of a
hitchhiker for geocachers, with folks logging their progress from
cache to cache. Well, I got wrapped up in this when I found one of
these travel bugs in a combo geocache/letterbox that my son and I
found. I got the one bug to Allentown, PA first (that's where we
live) and the race was won!

Anyway, is there any desire to try this sort of 'race' in the
letterboxing community? Here's what I have in mind - a couple of
letterboxers create a couple of hitchhikers that want to come home -
to a specific geographic area. These hitchhikers will be placed in
close proximity to start the race (like a couple of boxes in a
series) and the sent on their merry way. The first one to arrive at
their destination wins.

If anybody is interested in exploring this possibility, please let me
know.

Team Lightnin' Bug

Looks like we have a minor global conspiracy!!

So far, I count in Fleur de Lis, Hikers and Hounds, Judy B (sew so
bizzy, maybe) and Team LB. Amanda has volunteered to be out
planter! The trick would be to get the HH's collected and to Amanda
during one of her visits. This could be something we gradually work
towards. Another idea would be to mail them all to a common person
and they mail it out to Seattle. H+H has also proposed taking them
up to NH for a shorter race.

Let's begin by answering Judy's very pertinent questions. These are
just some off the cuff ideas, so please interject if you think they
are flawed.

1) The log book in each HH would say what the race is about, so that
people will know to git it along!

2) I am thinking point Z ought to be a city or at least close to
it. Amanda made a great suggestion about originally planting in the
same starting city. May I suggest that these be LB's with high
vistation rates so that things move along quickly.

3) As many different LB'ers as we can cram in ought to participate.
The more the merrier.

4) Prize? The one the geocachers did were for family pride (it was
father against son!). I guess we can cobble together a prize, like a
certificate that decalres that the winner is no longer a 'reviled
newbie' ( ;-) sorry, we are not on the main list) or some such
silliness. We can certainly brainstorm that one!

Maybe we ought to try a theme for the HH's - like fast or slow
things. Fast can be rockets, falcons and such. Slow can be slugs,
amoebas, a dial-up connection.

I think that I have opened an interesting can of worms on this one,
but hey is seems liek a fun can of worms!!!!

I am certain we'll be doing lots of scheming,

Lightnin' Bug
