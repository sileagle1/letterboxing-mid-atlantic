Well,
The gang and I took two grandmother Woodwooses to Mt. Vernon,
George's little ole parcel down in VA way today. We visited the Mt.
Vernon Trail box planted in Ft. Hunt Park. Cool box with log going
back to early '02. The oldest box we have seen so far, and planted by
Hike it or Bike it folks. The third of theirs we have found, so
thanks to them for good boxes. The Log was somewhat full, there is
room on the back of the pages, and I forgot that I had a back up log
with me, so we stamped on the backs. Next visitor needs to take a
replacement as I think I understand that the "Hike/Bike" folks are
from north of here and do guerilla placing, i.e. place and don't get
back to check on them much or at all. Nice spot for a box.
We also did a placement. I noted that as the Mt. Vernon Estate is not
managed by the NPS then I decided to stash one there ON THE GROUNDS!
DA DA DA DUMMMMMMMMMM! Not sure why nobody has gone for this before
but "George's Little Box" is in place with clues soon to follow. Now
the grounds get upwards of a million visitors a year and are
amazingly well maintained so finding a good spot was tricky but we
did it. G for George is the word. Hope ya'll take the oppurtunity to
say howdy to our mini-box, and the many others in the area. We did
not get a chance to visit Huntley Meadows Park, but it appears that
there are a bunch there. Look forward to hunting some more there
sometime in the future.
Much Peaceful Trails to all,
Shane "Pathmaker" Odom
Father Woodwoose
