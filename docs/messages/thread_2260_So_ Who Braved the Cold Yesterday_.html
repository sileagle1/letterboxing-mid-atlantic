Re: So, Who Braved the Cold Yesterday?

From: true indigo | Date: 2007-01-21 19:41:43 UTC
The Crayola Posse dedicated the weekend to the letterbox education of Girls Scouts and
their leaders. With the Hi-tech Hide and Seek badge there are a lot of leaders who know
nothing about letterboxing yet are trying to share badge requirements.

We spent 3 hours yesterday at the Small Valley Program Center sharing basic and helpful
information to a group of 25 young ladies and their leaders. We were glad we had come
the week prior to hide some letterboxes as it allowed us to write up the clues in advance.
This gave us time to talk on the trail about being discrete, respecting the land, and
replacing found letterboxes properly. We had enough adults to spilt the girls into smaller
groups thus encouraging less impact on the trail and more chances for hunting discretely.
They were real troopers braving the 0° wind chill. Their enthusiasm about carving stamps,
unique ideas on stealth approaches while seeking, and overall interest made the day seem
much warmer.

Today was another success sharing a similar workshop just for leaders at the Synergy
2007 weekend. I was able to impress upon the women the importance of taking time to
understand the activity fully before trying to rush through badge requirements. They were
excited to see what artistry, time, and effort are put into letterboxes.

A big thank you to Silent Doug for allowing me to use his outline of a similar program a
few years ago and Janila for the sample of her carving. Thanks also to RGBisMe for running
the laptop slideshow while I talked.

true indigo

From: Hikers and Hounds | Date: 2007-01-22 00:48:58 UTC
Cyclonic and I didn't actually box, but rather did some boxing errands today. The Allegheny Art Supplies store in Newtown, PA ordered "the pink stuff" for us so we figured we had to go buy some, plus picked up a couple of other things.
 
Drove over to BCCC to see if my Tyler Hall box is still there, haven't heard about it in awhile and Sunday is the best day to be on campus. Lo and behold it's still there! I never imagined it would've lasted this long! The Compass Box is gone though, lots a grounds work going on where the box was. Then stopped in Tyler to check on Disc Golf as I got an MIA report but it's there, the people just looked in the wrong spot. Cyclonic scoped out a spot for a potential box or two.
 
Well, when in Newtown....ya gotta go to Isaac Newtons, right? So did that, then surprisingly enough we started talking about beer ( our 2nd favortie subject after letterboxing) and I remember my son Carvin' Carmen raving about this place in Joisey, Circle Liquors in Pennington, right on the circle. We decided to see if it was open and it was! What an incredible selection of micro-brews from all over! We stood in front of the cases, mouths agape, like kids in a candy store. An adults candy store. Plus, the coolest thing was you can buy just a single beer for $2 so you can try whatever you like without committing to a whole six pack. We were in there quite awhile. Gotta get my Red Hats gals there.
 
The stopped at Banres & Noble (not letterboxing related) but wanted to pick up a copy of an adorable book my sisters friend got me for Christmas called "Bats at the Beach". I LOVE great childrens books and loves bats and this is the best of both! I highly recommend this book to kids of all ages. I am giving this one as a gift a young friend who is also fellow bat lover. I thought of him as soon as I read it. Plus picked up a copy of Games magazine, whiich I really like. I never read it until the article was in it, what a great magazine full of all kinds of brain teasers.
 
So no real boxing, but had a lot of fun hanging out with one of my favorite boxers. Plus we did talk boxing all day, plans for gathers, for boxes, etc. And we were outside in the cold. SO we almost boxed.
 
H&H

Re: [lbma] So, Who Braved the Cold Yesterday?

From: Mary Erickson | Date: 2007-01-22 01:52:45 UTC
Trogdor (11 year old son) and I did get to Annapolis
this afternoon and found both boxes we were looking
for, with a hitchhiker in each one. It began snowing
as we were looking for a mysterious stump at Box #1.
Trogdor went right to the box while I was having fun
in the brambles 20 feet away. How does he do that?
He's like a little letterbox hound.

By the time we got to the state park where Box #2 was
there was serious snow in the air, so we found the box
then hiked the 1/4 mile back to the car to stamp in to
save those numb little fingers (and keep the logbooks
dry, of course). Then another round of brisk hiking
to replace the box. It gave Trogdor another chance to
annoy a large flock of Canada Geese, and we saw 10
deer leaping across the roads. Otherwise we had the
entire state park to ourselves, go figure!

We forewent the two geocaches we were planning on
[usually a chance for Trogdor to do the techie thing
he loves] because the roads were getting a little
treacherous. We probably passed 10 different
ditched, wrecked, and TURNED OVER cars. Trogdor said
it was the creepiest thing he'd ever seen. But we
just took it at sane speeds and fortunately didn't
have to get up on 695 around Baltimore where the 8-car
pile ups were, according to the accu weather station.

Home safely, filled in the journals, logged in the
finds, sent emails to placers [I REALLY enjoy getting
replies to those, by the way...], and I am now at 100
on AtlasQuest, yay! So I spent a while looking for
all those new secret F-100 boxes, and probably spotted
about 3 within my easily driveable radius.

So that was a great boxing day. Have another box 80%
ready to plant, and ideas ideas ideas for these indoor
days.

And here's a question...Why do we find so many
hitchhikers that have NOT been stamped into the box's
log, and even more often don't have the box's stamp in
the hh's logbook? I've learned to check, because it
felt horrible to bring one home and then find out the
last placer never logged in at all, so there was no
record of that last box.

Mommo
