will be funny soon

From: brendan_hickey13 | Date: 2007-01-22 19:43:55 UTC
Syner and I found Silent Doug's Panther Swamp box on Saturday, amid
snow squalls and blasting 30 MPH winds that drove the wind chill to -
5 degrees. We had no trouble finding the box and it was a nice walk,
though removing our gloves to stamp in was an act of dedication to
the hobby.

While looking through the log, we saw that Lightnin Bug had found it
on 12/30. Below his stamp was written, "56.8 miles at 215 degrees
from this spot."

The trails in this part of Promised Land have little numbered sign
posts - I figured that they referenced tree stands though we did not
see any. However, the Panther Swamp box is in the section of trail
between 56 and 57, so what if the signs are mile markers? Then the
box is about at 56.6 miles, and a bearing of 215 degrees from the box
points to a left turn in the trail.

"Hey, Lightnin Bug left a new box out here," we thought with
excitement. We looked for awhile on Saturday but with light fading
and a birthday party waiting, we bailed out before finding anything.

We returned on Sunday morning, estimating distance based on time and
probable walking speed. Once in the general vicinity, we searched
every stump, hollow, exposed root, and pile of rocks for nearly a
mile along the approximate bearing of 215 degrees heading away from
Silent Doug's box. "It would have to be something obvious to a
letterboxer," we decided.

We can now say that such an effort amid the brush at Promised Land
takes about 2 hours. After all of that, still no box. We left
disappointed but determined to narrow the search and find the box on
our next trip.

Don't follow us. We're lost.

After an email exchange with Lightnin Bug, I can now tell you exactly
what is at 56.8 miles on a bearing of 215 degrees from this box:

Lightnin Bug's house

Yeah, he had a new GPS and was playing with it.

Syner and I are sure that this is funny, just isn't yet for us. :)

Psychiker
