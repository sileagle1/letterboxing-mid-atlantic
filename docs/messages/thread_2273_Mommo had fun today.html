From: tworstaggering | Date: 2007-01-25 03:49:59 UTC
What we do for our children...

To keep my boxing buddy, Trogdor, duly entertained during a day of
letterboxing I have learned to throw in some geocaching along the
way. He likes his techie gadget and NOT having to do the artsy
fartsy part of letterboxing, i.e., stamping in. BUT he's willing if
it's spread out a bit.

We homeschool, so we took advantage of a reasonable weather day here
in Maryland to have a full day of P.E.

We were having a great adventure with several box and cache
successes until we came to a certain geocache spot. Sparing you
letterboxers the geo-details, we basically spent twenty minutes
poking into every nook and cranny around two huge fireplaces in a
picnic shelter. As it turns out, I ended up having to get this
little micro-cache. It entailed climbing up onto a picnic table,
then hauling my middle-aged self up onto a ledge above the
fireplace, then balancing up there and stepping even further up onto
a little wooden shelf, then reaching all the way up to my full five-
foot-two and pawing around sight unseen in the eaves hoping not to
run into creepy nasties, and lo and behold, there it was. And of
course my son captured this maneuver on film.

As we were leaving the site, thankfully back down on terra firm, I
told my son, "I hope you look back someday and think, 'I had a FUN
MOM!!!'"
