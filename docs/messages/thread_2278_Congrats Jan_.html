From: Jan | Date: 2007-01-30 00:16:41 UTC
I am still recuperating from our experience on the mountain
yesterday - Haycock Mountain that is. Sheba and I decided to make the
trek for the Ice Age series. Maybe we should have figured that
something was bound to happen when we ignored the snow flurries and
light drizzle on the way up to the mountain. And maybe another hint
might have been the slippery rocks as we began our walk to box number
one. But, we are women! We are tough! So we moseyed along the
trail, quickly picking up boxes one through four. (Oh, box number
three proved to be my 800th find!)

Somehow we seemed to totally miss box number five. Maybe it was
because we began to focus more on the ice which was coating the rocks
as we hit the higher elevation. When we hit a particularly narrow
rock which had a thick layer of ice and a nice drop down its side, we
decided that no box was worth this. So we turned around and started
to head down only to discover another trail heading up the west side
of the mountain. It proved to be a much easier walk and we quickly
ended up at box number 7 and also were able to get box number 6. We
then decided to take the same easier trail back down the mountain.
But being good little letterboxers, the call of box number 5 was just
too strong. So we thought that if we just headed east from the easy
trail, we would eventually reach the original trail and could search
for number 5.

What we didn't realize was that this mountain is one big boulder
field. We kept climbing over one boulder after another, always
expecting to see the trail after the next one. At one point we ran
into a nice young man who was there just to climb the rocks. He
informed us that we were actually between two trails and that the only
way to reach either of them was over more boulders. He also verified
that the parking lot was southeast of our location. We decided to
head southeast still hoping to cross the original trail. But it was
not to be. Almost two hours later, we finally crossed over one more
boulder and actually could see houses in the distance. We limped our
way to civilization, not having any idea where we might be.

Fortunately, there was a man out in his yard who informed us that we
were on a road which was directly parallel to the road that the
parking lot was on. What he didn't tell us was that it was another
mile and a half away. I was never so happy to see my little car as I
was yesterday.

I think that, yesterday, both Sheba and I were convinced
that we never wanted to see that mountain again but both of us have
already agreed today that we will make another trip to find that
elusive box number five.
