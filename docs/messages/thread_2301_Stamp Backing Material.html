What I do is I mount them on a strip of basewood with a layer of fun
foam between the rubber and the wood. The foam gives the stamp some
give and helps get rid of the dead spots.

At Wal-Mart they have fun foam that is sticky on one side, which is perfect.

Basewood can be gotten at any craft store. It is heavier then balsa,
which is too light and fragile. when choosing the wood, take a good
look at it and pick one that is nice and flat and level. Most are
pretty good, rarely have I come across one that was warped.

Stamp Backing Material vs Stamping Method

From: Judy B | Date: 2007-02-12 04:36:56 UTC
When in doubt about getting a clear mounted stamp image just take
your impression using the "printing method" (as I call it.) This is
also good for those really big (>3x4") stamps.

Place rubber stamp - image up - on LEFT side of log
Bring right page over top (keep it steady as you lower it)
Rub back of page with your hand
Gently lift the page back up and remove the stamp

This becomes a little more tricky when using a large log with
multiple impressions per page.

In which case, place rubber stamp image down on the page
Close the log
Flip over so you can open to back of page - exposing it for a hand job

At least a large mounted stamp won't be in multiple pieces when you
find it!!

I also like the foam core so I can stamp the image on it and then
cover in clear packaging tape -- so finders can easily see what color
ink pens to use ;^) and which way it is up (unless I glued it on
wrong -- and I use the Quick-Tite super glue -- cause times awasting.

We always stamp the image onto the first page of the logbook that we
place in the box. We figure people can look at that to see color
suggestions, rather than looking at the back of the stamp. It saves
the trouble of flipping the stamp over and back repeatedly when you
are coloring, to see what color goes where.
