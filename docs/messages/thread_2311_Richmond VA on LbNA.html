Message to Webmasters,

I just received a message from a fellow boxer regarding the location
of Hanover, Virginia on the letterbox map. Hanover is directly
adjacent to Richmond city. It is in central Virginia and at least an
hour from northern Virginia if not an hour and a half. I am curious
to know the reasons why the location has been changed? People who
live around Hanover will not know the boxes are there since they will
not think to look in northern Virginia to find boxes that are in
central Virginia.

drewclan@... replies:
Hi! This Richmond area gives us fits! What you see now is based on
input from letterboxers who live in that area. But nothing is perfect
and we're always ready to improve things.

I think that y'all ought to get together on your regional yahoogroup
or at a Gathering this spring, and come up with a coherent breakdown
of the area from a local perspective. We will do anything we can at
LbNA to help make the maps more reader friendly and more usefull to
the people that use them. Thanks for the note, Jay in CT

Re: [lbma] Richmond VA on LbNA

From: EllBee | Date: 2007-02-19 01:42:46 UTC
We're in that area, and oddly enough were just talking about the way Hanover was placed on the LbNA map.

Decidedly, Hanover is in the Richmond Metro area. My husband is the manager of the Richmond public radio station and has been working with local Emergency Preparedness folks regarding an emergency notification/information system. Hanover is among the area cities/counties that are participating in this program and is well within the broadcast area for the station.

I'd agree that some Richmond-area folks might miss boxes listed in Hanover if they're not included in the Central VA section, especially if they're newer boxers who haven't been navigating the site for long. Spotsylvania, Stafford, Fredericksburg listed in the NoVA region - fine. Hanover on the other hand is so close to Richmond that you can't even call it a bedroom community.

JMO,
EllBee
