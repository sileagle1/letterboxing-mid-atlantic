Re: [lbma] Re: VA Division on LbNA - Proposal

From: EllBee | Date: 2007-02-20 22:41:14 UTC
Okay. Central VA:
Hanover - yes
New Kent - well, it's a bedroom community for Richmond, but I bet it's also a bedroom community for Williamsburg/Tidewater.
Charles City - I definitely associate this county with the Williamsburg area.
    However, looking at a map, New Kent and Charles City could be llisted with Richmond - it could go either way.
Louisa - sure. You have to traverse Louisa county to get from Richmond to Charlottesville so if C'ville is Central VA, then so is Louisa. Same for Fluvanna and Goochland, if they're not already in Central VA.
Nelson County should be moved out of the Central VA region and into Shenandoah - no mountains in Central VA.

Southwest/Roanoke:
Pittsylvania - I'm on the fence on this one. By rights there would be a 'Southside VA' region, but there aren't any major population centers and darn few boxes. Overall, I'm in favor of keeping Pittsylvania & Danville in Central VA, barring feedback from the folks who have planted boxes in those areas.
Alleghany - yes. It makes no sense at all for this to be in the Central VA region.
Botetourt - yes
Bedford - yes
I'd also add Amherst to this region, or to the Shenandoah region. The mountain thing again.

Just my $2...
EllBee

I meant to also add that moving Hanover to NoVA region makes little sense on another front - there are so incredibly many boxes listed in the NoVA region that adding even more is just going to make it harder to navigate. If anything, the NoVA region should be subdivided to make looking for boxes more manageable. I'd suggest splitting off the Fredericksburg/Stafford/Spotsylvania area.
