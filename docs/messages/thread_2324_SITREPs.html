Okay, in an interest in reviving what appears to have been an older,
wordier tradition, here is my sitrep from yesterday. If this is too
much detail, feel free to let me know and I'll rein it in next time.

Saturady afternoon was still springlike, so I went with HoneyPig to
try the Rockburn Branch Trail boxes. There was a lot of ice; we were
skating along the road and dear HoneyPig [14 yo daughter] discovered
that she really could use a pair of boots since tennis shoes have no
traction. Whoops! Thar she goes again!

Then we did the ENTIRE purple loop before we realized we
had NOT seen "the arch that's not a larch." I ignored HoneyPig's
look that was pleading for mercy, and I somehow convinced her to GO
AROUND AGAIN. I spotted what "used to be" an arch, maybe, and sure
enough we found that 1st box. Kind of like finding a Maine box, I
suppose [Go down the road until you come to where the Fishah's bahn
was before it buhned down two yeahs ago..."]

Then we wandered WAY UP STREAM looking for a dam before we figured
out we must have missed a junction, so we bushwhacked downstream,
found the dam, took the compass reading and found...a completely
empty tupperware box under a rock. Right place, obviously, but no
note, no log, no stamp. Don't know what to think there. Meanwhile
HoneyPig figured out that the ice over a certain little pond really
wasn't that thick. Thar she went again!

We found the SITE for box 3, but maybe it's buried under a ton of
dirt and snow, although we kicked up a fair amount looking for a
rock somewhere. No box in sight. However, right at the place where I
thought for sure the box SHOULD be was a frozen carcass of a blue
jay. I teach a homeschool/high school biology class and we're doing
birds in 2 weeks, so that beautiful specimen is now in my freezer.
[Scared my 17 year old, who was looking for a frozen pizza, heh heh.]

So 1 complete box, 1 empty, and 1 surprise treasure! And a lot of
good exercise and a daughter with a really cheerful spirit to put up
with it all.

And exacto knives come in handy for removing bramble splinters from
your own thumb.

Mommo
