Nice report, Mommo.  I’ll take a shot at writing up our Saturday outing.

 

Our Saturday errands took us toward Lancaster, PA, so the Scattered Showers took a small detour to Adamstown to explore Evilmommy territory.  We stopped at the library first.  Crossing our fingers that a 7 year old and a 3 year old could handle words like “quiet” and “discrete”, we went inside.  A few minutes spent in the children’s corner hopefully diffused any suspicions of the patrons and staff before we stole off to the reference area.  Rainmaker was a bit frustrated, not finding the box where he thought it ought to be, but I spied it the minute I looked at the shelf and laughed at what I read.  I’m sure it’s a mommy thing…  After stamping in, we returned to the kid’s section, planning to read a book to the kids as a treat.  Instead the kids had a pre-lunch meltdown.  The youngest was hauled out of the library crying “I want my daddy to read to meeeeeee.”

 

The next box was nearby on State Game Lands.   The parking lot was unplowed but empty.  We pulled into the existing ruts, hoping that the minivan would make it out later.  We half-walked, half-slid along the trail, following some established footprints and wondering if they would lead right to the box.  The box was straightforward to find, at least in this season, and with the snow mostly frozen, we didn’t leave many tracks of our own.  Our minivan successfully pulled out of the parking lot, and we were off to lunch at Dave’s Diner.  The roasted chicken sandwich and the “shorebird” sandwich were really good.

 

There was plenty of daylight left when we got home, so we decided to plant a box as well.  SkatingHippo gave our youngest a box for his birthday in December.  The location was scouted a month ago, so we returned to shuffle over the snow and ice to hide it.  We hope it’s a family-friendly box.  Its biggest shortcoming would be pulling the children away from the playground equipment long enough to find the box.

 

All in all, we had 2 finds, 1 plant, and a chance to Get Out.  Not bad at all.

 

Chris, of the Scattered Showers
