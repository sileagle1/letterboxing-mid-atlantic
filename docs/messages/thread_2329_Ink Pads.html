From: Rick from Boca | Date: 2007-03-02 16:52:27 UTC
Well, I've forgotten an ink pad before, but I certainly wouldn't
blame anyone other than myself.

However, I've noticed in the past few boxes planted by newbies that
there were ink pads included. These stamps were nothing special that
would require a special color of ink, either.

I've just double-checked the Getting Started page on LBNA, and the
only one who says you should include an ink pad is Sojourner in her
Letterboxing 101 article. Neither "Start Finding Letterboxes"
nor "Getting Started (Creating a Letterbox)" mentions an ink pad.

There was a box in Florida where the clues said something like "no
ink pad is included, as in the Mid-Atlantic custom," and I was like,
really?

From: Heather Siple | Date: 2007-03-03 20:36:10 UTC
Oddly enough, I was out at Silver Lake Park, in Dover, during that big deep
freeze. My fingers were numb by the time I found the incredibly easy plant,
and I was keeping my ink pad warm in my pocket. But, I found the box in
very good shape, containing an ink pad that was definitely not frozen! No
one had signed into the log in over a week. It may be messy in the Spring,
but it looked good last month!

I have found, however, that most of the ink pads I've found in letterboxes
are dried out. I leave makers in most of mine because they seal better and
because I have been known to leave my own ink behind by accident!
