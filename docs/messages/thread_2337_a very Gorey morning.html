a very Gorey morning

From: Suzanne Coe | Date: 2007-03-07 17:43:05 UTC
Last Saturday Pink Panther invited me to come boxing with her in Allentown.  Not Lightnin Bug boxes though--this time it was Indigo Vulture's new Edward Gorey series.  (Well, ok, we did find Great Allentown Fair also.  But you can't go up there & NOT hunt a LB box--it's like a rule or something =)
 
When we started out it was colder than we'd expected--a good thing because that meant the mud had frozen, but it did present some challenges.  Like setting box #1 down & watching it slide gracefully down the hill!  We overshot box #2 & ended up in a bottle graveyard, where we found the most bizarre thing I'VE ever seen letterboxing--false teeth in a tree....  (PP thought about taking them home to Stan, but we couldn't find the lowers. So he got a rock.) 
 
It looked like the trail had been washed out after the bridge, but we were able to pick it up without much problem on the other side.  (There had been quite a bit of flooding & parts of the disc golf course were still under water--although there were still people playing on it!)
Actually the only thing we found to complain about was that we did NOT like the approach to Lumpy.... All in all an excellent series--loved the stamps.  A boxer from Allentown had found the first 2 before we got, but we can claim first fiinders for the series as a whole. 
 
On the return trip we called LB to ask about the clues to Great Allentown Fair, hoping he could save us some steps back to the car.  I was surprised to hear him admit that he didn't have them memorized!  I think another good rule would be that LB should not plant anymore boxes until he's memorized the clues to the ones he already has out there, so that when people call him from the trail he can tell them where to go....  (he listened politely when I explained this, but I think it went in one ear & out the other).
 
We did get some hints on reverse engineering it though, so it was a short walk to the Fair, another fun box...  I was glad I was with PP because she's good at hearing SPOR under the snow, and she'd brought her shovel to dig it out with!   Due to the recent discussion, it was interesting to note that ink in the original colors was provided.   (If I recall, the South Mountain series contains ink as well; didn't see any problems with the pads.)
 
We were amazed on the walk back by the change in the creek--the water was full of huge slabs of ice floating downstream (we thought we saw a couple chunks of asphalt too).
And the mud had melted into--well, mud.  So I gues it was a good time to be heading home.   Although there was another box I'd have done if I'd had time, and I didn't even get a chance to stop at Yocco's (stupid work!).

The whole "inkpads bad in winter!" thing is a bit of urban legendry
or opinion. Most of the issues are because sloppy boxers may not
reseal the baggies well and too much liquid leaks in. Hence,
exploded mess. Of course, pigment pads rarely run. I've had pads go
almost 3 yrs now without many problems, except with poor resealing.

On an aside, I only include inpads when the stamp calls for a
specific color, so sorry tut-tutters, not every one of my boxes has
an inkpad! ;-)
