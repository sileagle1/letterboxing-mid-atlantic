From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2007-03-10 12:20:38 UTC
These are the same folks that Otis mentions. They are running a
small boutique boxing biz in the Harrisburg, PA area. The boxes are
unusual in that they were originally planted, but then pulled and
turned into semi-virtuals. You needed to go on one of the
adventures, gather the pertinent information about the area and then
email them back with a scanned in image of your stamp, with your
address. At that point they would send you the box stamp imagesvia
mail.

Pink Panther, Black Swan and I sought and found the information
needed for several of the boxes in the St. Anthony's wilderness and
I think the Crayola Posse also found some of them. We had a blast
in the rain, as it was the very beginning of the floods of June 2006
and we were almost stranded at the epicenter. The creek that we
needed to drive over (little bridge) had risen a couple of feet in
the several hours we were there. Very cool area though, but not for
those seeking instant grat. The hikes were nice and long, 5 miles
plus.

The coolest thing is that I received a holiday card from them now
with a stamp.

LB

From: true indigo | Date: 2007-03-10 13:05:16 UTC
I was asking the same questions about a year ago when it was determined that the owner
of the business was in nearby Millerstown, PA. Someone used the "Who Is" look up tool:
http://www.register.com/retail/
whois.rcmx;jsessionid=530299D50A606A8B07C1BB312EC6B3D4.euapp03

Letterbox adventures business was discussed on a blog and Atlas Quest message boards:
http://www.stampinthebox.com/2006/03/just-say-no.asp
http://www.atlasquest.com/boards/messages.html?gThreadId=7577
http://www.atlasquest.com/boards/messages.html?gThreadId=4108

The owner had grabbed my attention (around 2/2006) before her business was known to
me. She was last finder of a few letterboxes I checked in on that were not replaced well. I
wanted to touch base with her by giving her some informational links and searched for her
trail name. I found her registered on LBNA soon after with a plant of her own at Little
Buffalo State Park and in her own town.

RGBisMe and I found her box in the state park and it was filled with a lot of advertising for
letterboxadventures.com on the pen and scented eraser stamp. We were surprised we
hadn't heard of it and tried to check out the link when we got home. On 2/18/06 the
name was registered but the web site was not yet up. We forgot about it until other
letterboxers starting making a lot of noise about the business at the beginning of March
2006. I was mostly shocked that it was started up by someone who had done very little of
what I would consider market research.

On 3/4/06 we went to find her second box with Maga11 & Toolman Luke. Same
advertising and AC had been to the box before us. It think it was soon after this time the
owner pulled both of these boxes.

Knowing there was a lot of controversy surrounding these boxes, we thought we would
check out the locations of a few letterboxes that were listed just on her web site.
http://trueindigo.blogspot.com/2006/04/twenty-five-mile-weekend.html

Although I wasn't 100% clear on my blog as to where we were, we hunted her Devil's Race
Course and 4 other nearby letterboxes. Yes, the stamp was made from a scented eraser
but was a nice little carving and the locations as well as the journey there were top notch.
There was clear advertising for the business in the 5 boxes we found that day. Lightnin'
Bug has since found some of them but the physical boxes were pulled and requirements
for the stamps are more like virtual.

That's the info I have. My opinion? If it is a well run business and there is enough need for
it, it will thrive. I have no need for it. Personally creating what letterbox adventures sells
prepackaged is a big part what makes the hobby fun for me.

true indigo

From: Lock Wench | Date: 2007-03-10 14:34:22 UTC
Yeah, this group showed up last year, listing links to  boxes without the owners' permission. After being BLASTED from all sides, they took the links down and went into hiding for awhile. 

They were basically a site selling supplies, albeit quite boring ones. Ex. fruit scented erasers for carving with their logo on them.  The market prevailed and they didn't get many takers. Bravo for them, though, for regrouping and coming up with a unique way to make a buck off of letterboxing. The guy selling workshops to educate teachers how to letterbox the Valley Quest way makes THOUSANDS. Why not this poor sap?

There seems to be a lot of people going the "make a buck" way recently. I have no problem with it as long as they provide a true service to letterboxing. Therefore, I don't mind paying as a premium member to Atlasquest, as I believe it provides a good service. I buy PZ Cut and carving tools from Stampeaz.com, as I believe Webfoot provides a good product at a great price, and her service matches none. A recent addition to Cafepress started offering t-shirts and other stuff and got in hot water because they listed designs for events...without asking the event planner.  They also were in great need of a proofreader as their site was riddled with spelling errors. Not what you want to see in a company who is printing your message on a shirt or other item....lol.  But some of the designs were cute, so maybe if they get their act together, they can make a few bucks. I probably wouldn't wear one of their shirts except to a gather, as I don't believe in SHOUTING letterboxing everywhere I go.
