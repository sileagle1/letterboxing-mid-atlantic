From: Hikers_n_ Hounds | Date: 2004-07-29 23:26:04 UTC
...as my boss would say. Down the road apiece, maybe late fall, I was thinking of doing a series and planting it in Tyler State Park in Newtown, PA. Tyler is HUGE and to the best of my knowledge there are only 2 boxes there, or rumored to be there, I think planted by Mukluk. I hunted but found neither...but again, that's me! Anyway, I was thinking it might be something we wanted to do as a group? Like the stuff CT is always doing. I love music, everyone in my family are musicians, except me but hey, someone's gotto be in the audience right? We can't all be on stage.
 
Anyway here's my idea for whomever is interested. I'm looking at getting it together maybe in Novemeber, or maybe it could be an over winter project to plant in the spring, who knows. It's called "the Battle of the Bands" and will feature stamps donated by folks of thier favortie bands. You can plant them your self, heck we can have a group planting day, or send them to me and I will plant it for you, then email you the clues so you can post your own box. H&H will of course be planting the Grateful Dead, we also already have a Led Zeppelin stamp and are working on String Cheese Incident and possibly Radiohead. I know letterboxingbee had mentioned doing a Pink Floyd stamp so she might want to include that in this.
 
Anyway, just the nucleus of an idea for our area. Put it on the radar if your interested and we look at it again in a couple of months.
 
H&H

At 07:26 PM 7/29/2004, you wrote:
>Anyway, just the nucleus of an idea for our area. Put it on the radar if
>your interested and we look at it again in a couple of months.

Oooooh, cool -- to add a little
indy-alternative-beach-bar-cover-band-and-so-much-more flavor to the
project, Jennifer and I will do Love Seed Mama Jump (whom we're
incidentally going to see tomorrow night). And maybe Pearl Jam, too, she
says, because the cover of Alive would make a great stamp. That means I'll
have to come up with one of my own now, too, which is going to take some
thought....

Also, Turtle McQ did a couple of hitchhikers that travel along with
mini-CDs that she burned using her PC. Each mini-CD features a song by one
of her (and my) favorite folk performers, Kris Delmhorst. The idea is that
people take the hitchhiker home with them and then listen to the CD before
returning the hitchhiker to the next box. She got permission from Kris to
do it, and some people have even reported that they've bought CDs as a
result of hearing the song on the mini-CDs, which is nice. Anyway, it's
just another little idea to get the creative juices flowing!

Doug

returning the hitchhiker to the next box. She got permission from Kris to
do it, and some people have even reported that they've bought CDs as a
result of hearing the song on the mini-CDs, which is nice. Anyway, it's
just another little idea to get the creative juices flowing!

Doug
