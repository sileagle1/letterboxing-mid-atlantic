From: Silent Doug | Date: 2007-04-03 16:14:35 UTC
Friday-Sunday, August 3-5, Clueless & I are teaching a weekend-long
workshop on letterboxing for the Appalachian Mountain Club at their
Mohican Center near the Delaware Water Gap. There will be classes on
coptic stitch bookbinding, needle tool carving, planting, hiding,
mystery clueification, and two hikes of 5 miles or so, including
finding some of the existing boxes in the Water Gap area and others.
We're aiming to jam the weekend full of surprises and fun for both
new and experienced boxers. As you may know, Clueless has been a
long-standing volunteer and hike leader for the AMC, and this is part
of an effort that they're making to increase usage of their Mohican
Center, so it's a worthy cause. The registration fee includes lodging
in the center's oh-so-luxurious bunk beds along with any stinky
Appalachian Trail thru-hikers who might still be making their way
north at that point (and I suppose you could camp in your own tent,
too) and breakfasts and Saturday night dinner and the traditional
Mohican raucous after-dinner/late-night musical singalong
folk-till-you-drop entertainment.

We'll have more details later if anyone is interested....

From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2007-04-03 18:27:08 UTC
Most of the trails in the vicinity of the Mohican center do not
involve serious climbs, as it is pretty much at the top of the
Kittatiny Ridge and your car can be your legs. It is only when one
tries to hoof it up from the Delaware river level that one gets that
old 1300 ft elevation gain!

Indigo Vulture and I had much fun there last summer re-enacting "This
is my Rattlesnake Swamp Trail" with much gusto. Forget the Lost
Clues, as the box appears to be lost as well! In the words of
Stephen Colbert "That Box is dead to me!"

It is really a very cool place and worth the trip just to hang out.
Catfish pond is home to many a swimming bear.

sloof lirpA!

(well the bear part)
