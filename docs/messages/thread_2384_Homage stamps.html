Homage stamps

From: Lea Shangraw Fox | Date: 2007-04-10 23:58:39 UTC
At our recent West Coast gathering I introduced the concept of Homage Stamps and they
were quite a hit. Only problem was I wasn't sure quite how to use them. I started out by
putting them in boxes where I thought the Object of the Homage would see them. Then I
snuck some in to people's log books. But neither of those methods were useful in getting
them noticed by everyone who was an Object. So, how do you folks do this? And do you
have them registered anywhere on Atlas Quest? I think there will be more West Coast
homages soon....

-- Princess Lea

P.S. The 4 I did were A Panda from Seattle, Porks Anoynymous, QuadrupleSaj and Young
Blue, and LizaScenic

From: Hikers and Hounds | Date: 2007-04-11 11:17:26 UTC
Miz Scarlet's right, it does have possibilits! Polka dots, polka dancing, the minds runs riot with ideas!
 
To answer Princess Lea, orignally the concept was started by the knuckleheads in LBMA who used to try to stamp their Silent Doug homages in boxes they thought he would find. A difficult task as Silent Doug had pretty much found everything there was to find around the Mid-Atlantic region but it got easier as new boxes were planted.
 
Now what some so is go to a gather that the honoree will be attending and stamp into the event board with the homage stamp before the honoree stamps in, or on the sly when they're not looking. Someone usually brings it the thier attention.
