From: stevelignowski | Date: 2007-04-12 13:16:30 UTC
For my first letterbox, I decided to try to find Gabriella's Flag.
It should be easy; I'd been there before, and the directions didn't
look too hard, right? The day started out overcast, and it actually
snowed a little, but then the sun came out. Beth got home from work
around 11, and we got to Eagle Rock around 12:15. Beth decided to
stay in the car-- she wasn't feeling well-- so I set out on my own.
Well, I found the yellow trail to follow into the woods easily
enough, and I thought I was doing pretty good. The ground was very
muddy, and there were many pools of standing water both on the trail
and off. Several times the path was flooded and I had to track
around until there was another dry stretch. I had to be careful not
to slip when I was going down the hill. I think where I went wrong
might have been where the clues mention "a tree in the middle of the
trail ending a bed of rock." I don't recall seeing that (-- or
looking for it-- for that matter. Pay more attention to the clues
next time!). Anyway, I got to what I thought was the bottom of the
hill at the end of the rocky trail, and I looked around for
the "large tree directly on the left", but there were a lot of large
trees, and it would've been easy to miss a "large rock" under all of
the leftover leaves. Someone had built a rough shelter out of limbs
against a tree. There were a few bridges over small creeks, too. I
went back up and down a few times, before it was clear I either got
something wrong, or the clues were too vague. I'll give it another
try when the weather is warmer, and the ground is drier. Maybe I'll
try contacting the placer to ask if I made a wrong turn, but I think
I'll give it one more try before I give up and cry "uncle."

The Pakrat
