YAHOOOOOOOOO!!

From: cluelessboxer | Date: 2007-04-24 01:38:01 UTC
Well, I had a big LBMA 'boxing weekend, and I am practically giddy
with joy.

While The Silent One was at a conference, I got out in the awesome
weather and did some boxing with my buddy Skip. More importantly, I
did some planting & maintenance.

First: replanted the MIA Ghost of Graeme (stamp by Doug). Afterwards,
I realized that all the other boxes that had been are no more. Anyone
know what happened here? I had permission on my box and was never
told otherwise, but suddenly felt a bit suspicious afterwards.

Next: on to...well, I can't tell you (it being a mystery, or should I
say "mystery", since it won't be tough to solve), but I FINALLY
planted Jane Austen's Sense and Sensibilities. Two boxes on an
awesome hike, if I do say so myself. Need to write the clues, which
will be a little time consuming, but still! It's finally planted!!!

Had to pick up a box myself, at this point, then hit Salad Works, one
of my favorite eating spots (nothing like it in Connecticut).

Energy replenished, on to Tyler, where I planted Doug's XTC box.
That's right, we committed to B.O.B. and then not only didn't
contribute, we then moved out of state. I finally planted my
contributions two years ago, and this weekend got Doug's box in as
well. I totally know you didn't think we'd follow up on this
commitment, and were worried about the void it left at Tyler.

Of course we picked up a few boxes while we were there, then onwards
to replace the MIA Northanger Abbey. With this box replant, my Jane
Austen series - started THREE YEARS AGO - would finally be complete.
We got there, only to realize I had miscalculated the time and we
needed to leave so I could pick up Doug from his conference. I was
THIS CLOSE to having my series done, the heavy weight on my shoulders
lifted, the long wait for my fans (hee hee) over!! Arg, arg, arg.

Needless to say, I left visiting the relatives early the next day so I
could come back and finish this off. I know my priorities. So that's
it. I am ecstatic to say for the first time ever, the Jane Austen
Series is done. I just need to write the clues, so nobody report
anything missing until that's done, ok? And by the way, if you want a
good laugh, you need to get the new Northanger Abbey stamp and hold it
next to an image of the old. What a difference three years of carving
makes.

Did I mention I finished my Jane Austen series? Thanks for listening!
