Second box attempt.....

From: stevelignowski | Date: 2007-04-25 19:27:02 UTC
Since they recently re-opened for 2007, I thought I'd give the
Liberty Hall box a try. It's right next door to where I work, so it
was just a short walk at lunchtime. There was a large crowd there.
When I went into the Visitor Center to pay the fee to get in, I was
told it was a funeral group. The woman also asked if I was part of
the Kean College art display, so I would get student rates. I said
no. It's nice to know that at 38 I can still be mistaken for a
college student. The fee for access to the "grounds only" had gone
up to $5, so that part of the clue is now a little out of date.
Anyhow, I made a beeline for the "house in the South East corner".
It was very easy to find, since I had studied a map before I set out,
and I had a pleasant little hike past a fairly large bed of
daffodils, through a meadow with literally thousands of dandelions
and finally past a pond complete with a weeping willow. There were
also what appeared to be rhubarb plants growing all over the place.
I got to the house, which had an outdoor privey to the side (I wonder
if it was still functional?), and circled around to the east corner.
I took the specified 15 paces into the woods and turned right. There
in the ground was an erupted root, with a cavity covered by an old
piece of concrete, just like the clue said. Aha, I thought,
finally! I moved the piece of concrete, and found nothing there.
Given the size of the cavity, a box would have been hard to miss.
Still, I ran my hand around inside, but still nothing. I replaced
the piece of concrete the way I had found it, and left empty-handed.
Since it had only taken me about ten minutes to get to the box site,
I took a little walk around and found a waist high hedge "maze", and
some raspberry canes. There weren't very many plants in evidence in
the gardens, probably still too early for anything except bulbs,
yet. I sent an e-mail along with photos of the house and root to the
placer to make sure I had the right spot.

The Pakrat

Box to be Retired

From: Pete Mandeville | Date: 2007-04-26 03:19:07 UTC
It is with much regret that I announce the retirement of my first plant,
Red Sox Nation - A. I tried announcing this before but I think the message
was swallowed up by the Yahoo server. Anyway, the stamp is a depiction of
the fusion between the Boston Red Sox and the Wilmington Blue Rocks.
Unfortunately, the Blue Rocks are no longer the Single A affiliate of the
Red Sox. Therefore, I will be retiring the box after this weekend. I think
those who have found it enjoyed the stamp. So, here it is . . last chance.

Good luck,
