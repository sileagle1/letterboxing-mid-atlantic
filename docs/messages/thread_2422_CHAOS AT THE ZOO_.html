CHAOS AT THE ZOO!

Remember: You are being watched! Little eyes and big eyes are just waiting to watch you
find or replace these boxes. Please use caution. Have an excuse: Tie your shoe, drop
your keys, lose your frisbee. Please take the boxes away to stamp in and then return to
their original spot. Please repack carefully and be sure to include the string that helps you
extract the contents. You do not need to stray out of bounds for any of these boxes. The
Zoo charges an admission fee.

Further enjoyment of these boxes relies on YOU and your stealth skills as a letterboxer.

It is said, good fences make good neighbors. So steel fences must make great neighbors.
They sure make a great place to hide magnetic microboxes.


ANACONDA

The Anaconda has escaped from the Reptile House! He passed up the giant tortise
because the shell always give him indigestion. But that Southern Ground Hornbill?
Mmmmm! That's tasty! As you sit on the bench peeking through the fence at the
Hornbill, know that the Anaconda waits behind the fencepost by your right foot.


CHEETAH

Out on the savannah, a warthog stands. In the bushes just 14 fence posts to the
north, a Cheetah hides watching his prey.


THE TIGER

Who let the cat out? Who? Who? Who? Who? The Tiger slipped through the open
gate and headed south. He left the Tiger Terrace behind and even passed up the promise
of a nice horse dinner. He was not even interested in ice cream. He was hungry for
freedom! And he was looking for the one word he had learned in all his years at the zoo:
TIXE
The big cat crept south past the place where the humans paraded their offspring by
the buffet. What's that smell? Camel! It's been years since this feline had Middle Eastern
cuisine. But the taste of freedom would be so sweet. Behold, the TIXE! Up the ramp on
the right. Oh no! Another steel gate. Freedom denied!

The Tiger waits. Soon, when someone opens that gate, he will pounce! Until then he
hides at the base of the last post on the ramp fence. Just across from the sign of the
wheeled human. Beware!

ZEBRA

Find the map of Africa where the zebras and giraffes frolic with lions and
hippopotami. The zebra hides behind the first fence post to your left.
