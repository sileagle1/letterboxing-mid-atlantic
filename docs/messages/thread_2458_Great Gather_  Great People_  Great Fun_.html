Re: [lbma] Great Gather! Great People! Great Fun!

From: Nathan Brown | Date: 2007-05-21 15:26:38 UTC
Now that I have gotten some sleep...

Thanks to everyone who came out. I am really glad you had a great time,
and I think I will do it again next year.

It looks like we had more then 50 attendees, and more then 20 folks
camped, which is just amazing. Folks came from all over, PA, Joisey,
The Lower Three, Virginia, New York (both the city and state), and the
little state with the big name, the State of Rhode Island and Providence
Plantations.

The night boxing got high praise from all who participated, and some
folks actually did crack open Davy Jones' Locker.

While it was a ton of work, I am by far not the only one to thank.

Boxes came from The Royal Fish, Celtic Cat, Indigo Vulture, and Hikers
and Hounds. They were all great. Several stamps from MizScarlett,
including the fantastic Starry, Starry Night, were including in the
night boxing. SewSowBizzy made her famous coffee in the morning, and my
room mate Matt prepared his now famous burgers. So everyone came
together to make this gathering happen.

--
Nathan Brown

AKA Cyclonic
Penncoasters.com

From: Chris Bamberger | Date: 2007-05-21 17:01:08 UTC
Thanks so much to Cyclonic for organizing this event! Other than the occasional rain (not our fault!), the weather was perfect. We had a great time meeting people, exchanging stamps, and finding boxes. We hope to visit the park again to find more of the boxes. The kids seemed to have a great time seeing Dude and climbing trees with the other youngsters (there was no river to fall into!). I've been inspired to start carving again with some new stamps for the family and perhaps travelers for the kids. My kids were also fascinated with Davy Jones' Locker though they might not have the patience to solve it.

One of the best things about going to the gather was unadvertised - all that time hiking and playing outside managed to reset the kids' internal clocks. Before the gather, they were waking up around 5:30-6am. Today when I left the house at 6:45 am, they were both still in bed! Thank youuuuuuuu!
