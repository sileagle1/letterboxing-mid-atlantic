Third time's the charm....

From: stevelignowski | Date: 2007-05-25 02:24:50 UTC
On my second trip to Eagle Rock, I went to look for "We Will Never
Forget" first. Not much to say about this one except that it was my
first found box ever! It was quite close to the south wall between a
tree trunk and the large brown boulder mentioned in the clue, under a
SPOR. It was in sad shape, though. About the only undamaged things
in it were the stamp itself and the markers used to ink it. The log
pages were a sodden mess, and it wasn't even in a box. The torn
ziplocs were in an equally torn plastic shopping bag. Still, it was
well hidden. I left the old log there, not really knowing what I
could do to salvage it. I did leave a new spiral bound logbook in a
new double lock freezer bag. I couldn't seem to get the double sided
stamp to line up right, even though I tried twice.

Next I went for "Gabriella's Flag" again, and this time I found it.
Did I feel foolish! The first time I'd looked for it, I was WAY off
track. I guess I hadn't been paying attention to the yellow blazes
on the trees, and had gone straight at the fork instead of turning
left to follow the yellow trail. There was a yellow blazed tree
which had rotted in the center and fallen across the path, but it was
easily climbed over. After that it was very easy to find the blue
trailhead. I followed it and found the "tree in the middle of the
trail ending a bed of rock". Up ahead, at fifty paces I found
the "large tree directly on the left" and sighted a number of large
rocks at 280 degrees. I walked past them, and as I turned to go
back, I saw the box, partially exposed, in a space underneath one of
the larger boulders. This box was in much better condition. I
didn't take much time to look through the logbook (maybe next time
I'm up there), except to note that I was only the second person to
find the box this year, both of us in May. I hid the box so that it
was not visible, went back to my car and returned home. All told it
took maybe 30-40 minutes to find both boxes, stamp in and be back on
the road. Not bad for my first real letterboxing experience.

The Pakrat

One year

From: Otis' Friends | Date: 2007-05-25 04:04:02 UTC

It's been nearly a year since our first (Sheba's Brandywine M&M box) and second (Mother Duck's Carousel Camper) finds (when we didn't even have a signature stamp), and I'd have to say it's been quite a lot of fun.  We've pretty much lost the full contingent of five (except on rare occasions), but the baby and I make a good team and occasionally have other helpers.  Thanks to everyone who gave us great boxes to find and good examples of how to behave ourselves - or not.  I can't say we'll mark the occasion with anything that spectacular, given that the following day marks a considerably more significant anniversary for two of Otis' Friends, but we do hope to strike out into new territory this weekend, pick up a few far-away mysteries, and -- finally -- plant a series that has been gathering dust for several months waiting for me to go to just the right spot.  See you on the trail -- or will we ;-)
