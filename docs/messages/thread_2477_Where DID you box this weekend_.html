From: EllBee | Date: 2007-05-29 14:09:24 UTC
Oh, man! I do *not* envy you having to deal with the crowds at VA Beach on Memorial Day weekend! Yuk!

We had our worst boxing weekend on record. On Sunday, we tried for 3 easy drive-bys in Poolsville, MD only to be utterly foiled by masses of poison ivy. On a positive note, we did get to tour the old one-room schoolhouse. We'll try again in the winter...

On Monday we boxed locally at Pocahontas SP in Chesterfield, VA. We weren't really expecting to find the boxes we were hunting, and we didn't. We were hunting a series planted by Ladybug and the Itchy Butt Girls back in 2000. We found box 1 and could not find box 2 a couple of years ago, and were unable to attempt boxes 3-5 at that time as they were expanding the campground and access to the section of trail where the boxes were was cut off. We tried again yesterday - the trail is open again although one of the loops is now part of the campground. The trails are so poorly maintained that we were hopeful that the old landmarks would still be in place, and in fact every ladnmark was still in place, even when they should not have been due to the changes to the trail, but the final landmark was, of course, gone. Since boxes 4 & 5 piggyback off of box 3 all three are now inaccessible. Well, anyway, it was a nice hike...

EllBee

I had a wonderful weekend out !

Sheba and I headed out to the State College area for the weekend,
staying at SB Elliott State Park and we got tons of boxing in.

On the way out we went up through the Altoona area. We stopped at
Through Creek State Park where we got Balanced Rock, which is on a short
walk along a fantastic trail. On the way there we stopped at this
funky, interesting little Museum, the Broad Top Miners Museum, which was
in an old movie theater. We then visited Chimney Rock Park where we got
Slinky. Then we hit the Amusement parks in the area, Lakemont Park and
Delgrossos Amusement Park. We finally finished the day with Halcyon
Days, a great hike with a 1000 foot climb. We finally came off that
mountain at 9PM in the dark.

Needless to say, we did not get an early start on Sunday, rolling into
Bellefonte at about 11. Bellefonte is a beautiful town, and the county
seat of Centre County. There we took our time and found Chimney
Swifts. We found Bush house to be missing, as well as Inverted Jenny.
We then ventured out of town to find Anna Keichline, an excellent box
BTW, and spent a long time trying to figure out where Wild Iris was,
exploring the Fisherman's Paradise Hatchery before almost giving up when
it dawned on me where the box might be, and it was there. At least we
had no problem finding Red Poppy.

Finally getting into State College, we sat down at Creamery and had some
ice cream and met up with Lotus and Leafcutter before heading off to dinner.

I really have to thank Crayola Posse for hosting a little get together
to a local brewpub, Ottos. It was just a fantastic time, and I just
could not thank Terri and Jim enough.

After dinner Sheba and I did a little tour of the campus and picked up a
bunch of the Huntin' boxes. We finally finished up at about midnight,
though there are still more boxes to be had.

Monday was Mount Nittany day. We found four of the five Mystery Tour
boxes, but were not prepared to bushwack to the last, though I am pretty
sure I know where it is. Zion was our major goal, and Sheba found it
without a problem. We did not find Fibonacci Spiral, but we did find
the new Archaeobirder box as well as something else.

We then headed down to Boalsburgs where we said goodbye to Crayola
Posse, visited the 28th Division Shrine and found Thanks A Lotte. We
finally got home exhausted, but boy, when a great weekend.

--
Nathan Brown
