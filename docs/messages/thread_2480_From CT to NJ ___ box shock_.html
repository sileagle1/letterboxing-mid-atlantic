From CT to NJ ... box shock.

From: Sara | Date: 2007-05-30 02:06:24 UTC
Hello everyone!

We are not new to letterboxing, but we are new to boxing in the PA/NJ
area. We got our start in Connecticutt, where we went to college. We
graduated last May and moved back to New Jersey (where I grew up). I
can't believe how sparse boxes are on my side of the river! We are
across the river from Philadelphia.. and boxes here are slim pickins!

We did have a good a Memorial Day weekend for boxing.. we went to
Nockamixon State Park and got a few boxes.. we did the Ice Age Series
and definitly got lost on the way to the peak, and on our way back :)
It was a hoot, but we never found 4 or 5 because we couldn't even see
a trail between boxes 3 and 6 :)

Does anyone know any boxes with beautiful mountain views within 2
hours drive of Philly? My poor fiance David is dying in flat South
Jersey after spending 4 years in the mountains of CT! We love HARD
EXHAUSTING hikes with a prize at the end... any suggestions?

Thanks!

Sara

Team*HappilyEverAfter*

P.S. We are planning on getting more boxes in the area by planting
like crazy this summer in Southern Jersey :)

From: divinelotus73 | Date: 2007-05-30 03:36:09 UTC
A couple of good hikes but they may be a bit out of your two hour
driving range:
Pinnacle in Berks County, PA ( I did this in my pre-boxing day-
excellent hike)
Hawk Mountain (just around the corner- although I am not sure if the
box is still there but the hike is worth it either way)
Mt Misery at Valley Forge (this is that terribly hard, but it's a
nice hike)
Also try Lehigh Mountain in Fountain Hill and South Mountain in
Emmaus- nice boxes nice hikes.

Good Luck & glad you enjoyed the Ice Age Series.

Lotus

From: Audrey | Date: 2007-05-30 11:17:53 UTC
I second what Carole said. Also, there are two more ridges that have
nice plants and views; the Ramapo escarpment (runs along the east side
of 287 in Morris and Passaic Counties) and the first Watchung Mountain
extending from Somerset Coounty all the way up into Bergen. It's not a
really high ridge, but you get NYC skyline views from the top, which is
cool.
For long, hard hikes, try any of the older boxes by Wingfoot.
For a "sampler pack" of NJ terrain, do my Physiography series: one box
in each region working you from the flats all the way up to the
Kittatinny ridge.

From: Otis' Friends | Date: 2007-05-30 11:33:03 UTC
One feature of AtlasQuest you will like (if you haven't already used it) is the ability to do searches for hikes of a minimum length or minimum elevation change.
What everyone else said.  If you don't go to North Jersey, a good bet is to head to the Blue Mountain ridge, though there are a few shorter but steep climbs closer to Philadelphia (Wissahickon Valley, French Creek - take the Mill Creek Trail, Susquehannock State Park in Southern Lancaster County - actually, there are several ravines leading to the Susquehanna in Lancaster County with interesting hikes).
One of the nice things about Pennsylvania is that the state parks have web sites and it is easy to pull a map (often with contour lines) from the internet.
Browse here:  www.dcnr.state.pa.us/stateParks/
