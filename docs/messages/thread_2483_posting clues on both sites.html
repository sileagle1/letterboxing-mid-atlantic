From: Michael Scholtes | Date: 2007-06-01 02:58:12 UTC
I'm thinking about an issue that just sort of came up on the big list.
There was a brief conversation about posting clues on both LBNA and
AtlasQuest. Apparently posting on AQ used to mean your clue was
automatically added to LBNA. I never knew that. (It's not true
anymore...that was the original point of the post on the big list.)

Anyway, I'm thinking about the fact that so many of us post the same
clues on both sites. Why exactly do we do that? I certainly do it.
Recently, all of my clues are HOSTED on my own webspace, but I post
links on both of the main repositories. But it just occurs to
me...why exactly am I doing that? Are there people who just check
only one site? Certainly some new folks might only know of one of the
sites, but frankly, many of my recent boxes aren't exactly newbie
boxes. (e.g., I notice nobody's found Nobody.)

I sometimes think about Kant's categorical imperative, which states
that you should only act in such a way that you could wish EVERYONE
acted that way. Well, if everyone acted the way I do, then both AQ
and LBNA would have exactly the same list of boxes. What's the point
of that?

I'm just thinking out loud. I don't have any agenda or even an
opinion, yet. I'm just curious what you all think about this.

Or...is it just me who posts everything on both sites?

Thanks!

Indigo Vulture

Kant?  We're talking about posting clues on the web, not moral choices!
 
One could as easily ask why some post clues on one web site and not the other.  Some writers only publish in some outlets and not others.  There are a number of different reasons (profitability, creative control, reach, relations with management, etc.) and I suspect the same is true here.  Atlas Quest has bells and whistles that I often enjoy, but I'm sure we all have our own dislikes about it as well.  Were it not for clues posted to LBNA but hosted on Atlas Quest, I'm not sure when I learned of Atlas Quest.  I think about the only time I host clues only on Atlas Quest is when I want to keep the traffic down, and it has its own imperfect ways of doing that.
 
For me, posting to both sites keeps my options open so that I can outmaneuver The Man.
