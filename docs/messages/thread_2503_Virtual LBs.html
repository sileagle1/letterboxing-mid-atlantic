From: Jan | Date: 2007-06-13 11:28:54 UTC
Funny you should ask this! On a previous post, I mentioned that I was
considering quitting letterboxing because I don't like a lot of the
changes going on. One of the many changes that really bother me are
people who "plant" and "find" virtual letterboxes and feel that they
are now "real" letterboxers. I am not exactly sure why this is even
part of letterboxing but it is very popular among those who don't seem
to want to go outside.

I loved Nathan's signature line "real men don't do virtual
letterboxes" although I might have amended it to say, "real
letterboxers don't do virtual letterboxes."

Jan

LBMA has always been open to individual preferences and not cowered to
the "elite". I think VLB is an avenue for the physically challenged
to play this derivation of 'hide & seek'. The fact that there is
usually an online stamp image & log in the end is the only similarity
to LB'g as it was originated. Besides, those online don't leave the
boxes a mess and in the open -- who are we to care they also don't
want to get ticks and poison ivy!

From: Jan | Date: 2007-06-13 13:09:58 UTC
I hope that no one took my comments as condemnation of those who do
virtuals - just that I don't think that these are really letterboxes,
just as I don't think that postals are real letterboxes. Virtuals
are something that I have tried to get Out of Sight interested in
because they don't require her to go outside and find her way
around. They are fun side games to play but in my mind not
letterboxes.

I did try several virtuals a year or two ago to see what they were
all about - and a hand carved stamp image was NOT part of it. Most
of the ones that I did simply had a picture from the internet as
your "reward" for finishing the puzzle. In fact, I remember emailing
one of the "planters" because there was not anything at the end, it
just ended.

At least with postal letterboxes, you are pretty much guaranteed a
hand carved stamp for your efforts.

Jan
