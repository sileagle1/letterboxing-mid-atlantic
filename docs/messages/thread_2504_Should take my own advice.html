Should take my own advice

From: Heather Siple | Date: 2007-06-13 14:10:43 UTC
Did I not just say that the best protection was jeans and long sleeves?  I went out yesterday to do some boxing in Lancaster with Squirt and Miss P, wearing shorts, with the swim suits in the trunk for later.  Figured I knew what poison ivy looks like and if the bugs started biting I'd pull out some bug spray, but the trail was paved, so I wasn't worried about ticks. 
 
EXCEPT we did end up off the paved part and the weeds that were sticking out across the path for several feet turned out to be stinging nettles!  Not knowing what they were, we waded through them.  All three got stung and Miss P got it in the face while crawling under a fallen tree across the path.
 
Fortunately, we also carry an ammonia stick for just such emergencies!
 
Mother Duck

> I finally got a day to letterbox, so I headed out to Bombay Hook
for the Pierre's Vinyl Cut series. I stopped in the vistor's center
to see if the Parson's Point trail was opened and it was. No eaglets
this year. The lady warned me that there were reports of flies. I
thought I was ready because I had brought my 25% deet spray. When I
hit the first trail, I got about 30 feet before I could not take the
flies, so I got back to my car, put on a long sleeve shirt and
resprayed myself. I have never seen so may flies they kept getting
between my eyes and glasses but I kept on. I found 2 of 3 of the
boxes and really enjoyed the tower on Bear Swamp trail. The breeze
kept the flies away. It was a good thing that there has been so much
discussion about tick removal, as I was covered in deer ticks. So
much for Deet working. I may have to be a cold weather letterboxer,
because I really hate ticks.& nbsp; But it was fun to get out and
box. And towards sunset my husband and I went back out and there was
so much wildlife, it was amazing. That's what I love about
