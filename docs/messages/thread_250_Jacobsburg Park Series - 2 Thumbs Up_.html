From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2004-08-04 16:58:58 UTC
Howdy All,

Team Lightnin' Bug is back in the house. Tom and I just spent 3 days
camping at Akelaland (Cub Scout camp in Jonas, PA). We had a
wonderful time and hit the newly restored Jacobsburg SP series. We
enjoyed hunting these boxes and the visit was well worth the effort.
for those of you naturalistically inclined, the trails take you
through some of the largest Hemlocks I have ever seen, along a very
nice stream, a couple of good climbs along the Bushkill Creek ("try
not to look down to much Thomas!" "OK Daddy, but I still glimpse),
and finally through some beautiful purple Monarda. This was about
Tom's 10 am limit.

Anyway, we'd like to thank Silent Doug and Jennifer for restoring
these 'pioneer' boxes.

One final thing, when compared to Jacobsburg SP, what is the
difficulty of the 3 Fall Series (Factory, Deer, etc.)? We'll be
passing through there on the way to the family vacation spot and Mrs.
Lightnin' Bug and a younger, less stable kid will be coming along.
Tom and I want to seek and find, but we want to be sure Mommy and
little guy will be able to do it.

Thanks again,

Lightnin' Bug
