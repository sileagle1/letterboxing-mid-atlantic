My Boxing Experience

From: shaneodom2003 | Date: 2004-08-05 01:53:28 UTC
Hello all,
Papa Woodwoose from MD here. I wanted to write a quick comment just
to share how much I am enjoying this little hobby that some folks on
here seem to have, (IMHO) worked hard to support in the past years.
Tonight I am binding a small journal for the first time. Made with
blank index cards, end papers, board covers, and a sewn binding. I
have learned several interesting and enjoyable handcrafts since I
took up boxing just this summer. Not a rarity, as my wife and I are
full time handcrafters, making sculptural leather masks, and other
artwork, at Renaissance Fairs and other festivals. But boxing has
been esp. sweet in the lovely areas it has lead. I tend to pick up
something and figure it out. Boxing has got me carving stamps, and re-
exploring my graphic libraries. I brings me back to stencils and
their applications in studios. I have learned more about
blockprinting and linoleum(sp?) carving. The speedball nibs are a
wonderful carving tool that I wish I had know about a couple of years
ago. And now this little book-making bug has bit me. I am taking the
covers off all my hardbacks and looking at what method the spine is
made! And driving my lovely partner a bit crazy in the process.I have
always loved books, understood much about how they are made, but
never made one myself. I love handcrafted things of all kinds, and
all things are handcrafted at some point. It all started with an
artisan somewhere. So thank you to all the boxers who have supported
this and thanks to all the boxers yet to come who will send it down
the years.
Much peace,
Make treasures,
Woodwose
