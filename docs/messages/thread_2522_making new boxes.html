From: wood thrush | Date: 2007-06-22 03:26:17 UTC
Hi Cindy, I will try to answer the "what kind of information sheet
should I include" question. This is one of those areas where you will
find people have different ideas about what works best.

You can find the the original, lengthy and often found version in the
files section of the Yahoo newboxers forum: http://f1.grp.yahoofs.com/v1/
MDt7RoECdDjoxTRs7ZHoO3dqPdZ5GbcqMVkKQZgFm5lWK8kxGH6K64U-
UFBEPnAgOEs0VkLUhxkECi6Gr_aEB7FKn57EFBHVN8GjUQ/aboutletterboxing.pdf

Or a business card version designed for people to take home with
them on atlasquest:
http://www.atlasquest.com/toolbox/forms/infosheets.pdf

Personally I include neither of these in my boxes! My main concern is
people who discover the box accidentally or who find it while
geocaching. So what I include is a laminated "warning sign" that
I make sure is the first thing people see when they open the box.
It says:

THIS IS A LETTERBOX
This box is part of an international outdoor pastime called
Letterboxing. It is NOT a geocache and does NOT
accept items for trading. Please do not disturb it.
Return it exactly as you found it. You are invited to
participate in this pastime. For more information see
www.letterboxing.org.

My sign isn't particularly inviting, is it?
My biggest concerns as a planter are that a non-boxer will
find it and throw it away, or that a cacher will find it and
trade out the stamp. So that's why I made a "warning" rather
than an "inviting" sign.

There are many variations on the warning sign and on
the information sheet too, and I am sure my colleagues here
on the Mid-Atlantic list will have their own takes on what
they prefer!

wood thrush

Good questions!

 

I don’t know if the notes are effective, but I think it helps to have something in the event that a geocacher or muggle finds the box.  (On a side note, we were hunting a letterbox in Allentown, PA, under a boomerang rock, and my daughter found a not-too-well-hidden geocache less than two feet away.  We need to carry a few trinkets for occasions like this.)  This is what we’ve put in our boxes lately.

 

Greetings!

 

You have found a letterbox, not a geocache.

Please hide this at least as well as you found it

so others may enjoy finding this in the future.

 

If you have never heard of letterboxing, you

can read about it at http://www.letterboxing.org/

 

If you enjoyed finding this letterbox or

find it in need of first aid, please email

makerain@...

 

I want to add something about not trading items from the box once I figure out the right wording.  Also, I think a link to a letterboxing website and a contact email are important.

 

What people like best – an interesting hike, interesting clues, unique stamp, perhaps a convenient place nearby (rock, log, picnic table) to stamp in.  What people don’t like – boxes not properly sealed, soggy logbooks, leaky inkpads, biting insects, poison ivy.

 

As always, your mileage may vary. J

From: Audrey | Date: 2007-06-22 14:54:24 UTC
"What features have you found that people like the most and which get
a negative reaction?"

There's a big difference between "like the most" and "the most people
like". You can't please all the boxers all the time. I don't even try.
I would rather get one "wow!" in a logbook from boxers I know and
respect, than a book full of comments from strangers.
If you copy the features of your own favorite finds, those that <made>
them your favorite finds, you will be pleasing the boxers most like
yourself.

Aud

You have found a letterbox, not a geocache.
