NYC and Mermaids

From: Nathan Brown | Date: 2007-06-25 02:06:35 UTC
Oh yes, it is that time of year again, time for the annual (25th annual
actually) Coney Island Mermaid Parade.

I almost did not go up, but I talked to Sheba in the middle of the week
and asked what she was up to over the weekend and she said she had
Saturday off so I asked if she would like to go and much to my surprise
and delight she said sure. As it turned out this would be a day full of
surprises.

We left early and headed up on the train. I had actually researched the
letterboxes in the city so we had all sorts to go after. Contacting
Crayola Posse earlier in the week, I asked for suggestions and if maybe
we could get Ravenboy's exchange. Ravenboy is a ballerina at The Met
and I had hoped we could meet up at some point. As it turned out,
Crayola Posse were going to be in the city, taking in the play the
Friday night, and they would all be happy to join us as we roamed the city.

We were a little later then expected, and headed for Peggy's Park
first. We were to meet Crayola Posse here, though when we entered the
park we did not see them, so we found the box and very slyly retrieved it.

"Damn, we were seen."

"What do you mean?"

"Look," and I pointed behind us, and there was Crayola Posse seated on a
bench.

It was great to finally meet "The Boy" after hearing so much about him.
He is a good kid, and I was very impressed by him.

We headed up to Union Square and found Halloween Adventure before
hopping on the subway for Central park. After many visits to the city I
am still amazed by the size, scope and variety of it. It is just
massive, and when you emerge from the subway it takes a minute just to
get your bearings and figure out just where you are.

Another amazing thing is that even with a million other people about no
one notices what you are doing, or at least cares. Sheba and I sat down
right next to imagine, retrieved it, stamped in, and returned it and no
one knew it. There is always talk of stealth and being careful that
others do not see you, and to an extent that is very true, but I have
found that people will look at you and see right through you.

So we picked up Spook Central and Imagine. Ravenboy had already left
for the theatre, and True Indigo and RBGisMe had to head out to, so we
parted ways and Sheba and I headed for Coney.

The Mermaid Parade is something that has to be seen to truly explain
what it is. I can not do it justice in any explanation. It is
described as an "art parade." What the art is, I don't know, but it is
a celebration of everything that is Coney.

Coney is on the verge of massive change, change that I personally think
has to come, though many disagree. Much of the property has been bought
by several investment firms for redevelopment. They want to put a
combined residential and entertainment complex there. While I have my
apprehensions about this redevelopment, if it is done as planned it may
bring Coney back to the days when it was THE place to be in the summer
before the Disneys. We will see, but enough of my prattling on.

Coney was packed, wall to wall people, and we were there late. we
finally found a spot where we could get a taste of the parade, and I
managed to actually get a few pictures too. Sheba loved it, and next
year would like to return in costume herself.

We went and checked on my Coney Island Mermaid. I had gotten a report
from someone who had already found it that it was missing, so I expected
the worst and carved a new one. As it turned out it was not missing and
was right as it should be, very well hidden and dry. I replaced the bag
and placed it back while about twenty people were right there with no
problem.

We went to get something to eat. I had been trying to get my friend
Danielle on the phone with no luck, all the lines were clogged, so we
figured we would get Deno's Thrills and then head back into the city.
As it would happen, Danielle and a few of her friends from the Polar
Bears got in line right behind us.

It was great to see Danielle again. She is simply a wonderful person.
We ate, and Sheba and I found Deno's while Danielle and her friend Laura
headed to the car to change. Laura offered us a ride back into town,
which we gladly took. Laura is a neat girl. She has these wonderful
tattoos all over, colorful and some great images.

We got back into town and headed uptown to find Fog, a box we wanted to
get earlier but did not because we were running late. Fog is hidden in
a tiny little park overlooking the East River, and it has a fantastic
stamp to boot.

This, however, was not the end of the day. As we were walking towards
Penn Station we decided to divert and visit Grand Central. I had never
been there, and it is really a remarkable structure. The space is
amazing in its scale and grandeur. We shuttled over to Times Square
then and walked down to Penn from that point. We got to the station and
stepped on the train just as it was about to leave.

--
Nathan Brown
