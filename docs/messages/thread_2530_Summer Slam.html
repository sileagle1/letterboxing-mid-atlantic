From: thedoubtfulguests | Date: 2007-06-25 20:14:05 UTC
What a brilliant collection of puzzles, a fabulous collection of
stamps, and a fun tour of Alexandria.

This pub crawl gather concept shows that you can have a great time with
urban boxing for a day, even though you couldn't expect those stamps to
last very long in the wild. How great it is to combine indoor, outdoor,
and everywhere.

Notable highlights:
- Beautiful weather.
- Meeting over brunch before start on a comfortable patio. Beer, food,
exchanges, cooties, homages, talk, reunions, galore. Adventure Seekers
appeared with new baby, even though they couldn't stay. First chance to
see them since they moved to Nern VA.
- Fascinating binder of clues, photos, info, and more. Mysterious
envelopes, hidden clues, fun jokes. Great planning. The 'one official
log book per team" idea really worked great.
- Non linear route. Teams went off in every direction.
- Fun encountering other teams on the trail, but seldom being in the
same place at the same time.
- Outside boxes too, even in parks. The former railroad tunnel was
awesome.
- Founder's Park -- "Make sure they don't see us find the box." Except
that they had already found it right under our noses, and we never did.
Well done! I now know every Lone Tree Root to be found within 150
yards.
- Team Work, Team Work -- Multiple people put muliple pieces together
to find one answer. Some clues had that many pieces to the puzzle,
which made them really interesting.
- Such Variety of Clues -- directions, ciphers, observation, gadgets,
maps, history. Brilliant.
- And Great stamps!

Thanks for all the fun,
Scarab of the Doubtful Guests

From: Lady Hydrangea Prisspott nee Hedge | Date: 2007-06-27 14:17:44 UTC
A cautionary tale about progression.
Her Ladyship is involved with an organization that holds an annual
retreat. The main entertainment at this retreat is a "No Talent Show"
which began very humbly but which was immensely entertaining. For a
few years it got better and better each year, then sort of slumped at
which point her Ladyship was tapped to host (actually she flat out
asked). Needless to say, her Ladyship decided to raise the bar a
little. More lavish costumes, more elaborate set, higher production
values, etc. Her Ladyship continued to contribute to the enthusiasm to
raise the bar and it continued to be raised. Every time some new
extravagance was added it became expected the next year. The show now
involves construction of a full professional stage including wings and
an overhead super-structure for professional lighting a professional
sound system, wireless mics, set decoration, hours and hours of
planning, setup and money; lots of money. Yes the show is fabulous and
yes it's the most anticipated event of the year for our little group
but it's seriously in danger of collapsing under it's own weight. It
very nearly ended one year ago when the hostess wasn't up to the task
and failed to do any of the planning and prep work. The show was a
fiasco partly because of her failings and partly because of events
beyond her control but the progression to greater and greater heights
cannot be sustained and I'm now in fear of the monster I helped create.

I would urge you all to seriously consider the notion of a progression
in the pub crawl tradition toward bigger and bigger and better and
better or the whole thing could come crashing down in a blaze
disappointment, regret and resentment. It very nearly did in our "No
Talent Show" tradition. Let each person make it their own and keep it
special; meaning, I would urge you to try to keep it to once a year.
There's a reason Santa only comes once a year...

For Her Ladyship the pub-crawl has become a wonderfully fun way to
letterbox and meet others. It would be a tragedy to see it get out of
hand. Look at letterboxing in general everyone's enthusiasm for the
hobby makes them want to make it bigger and better but in her
Ladyship's opinion it has now gotten so big it's unmanageable to the
point where we now have factions, splinter groups, play-my-way-or-not-
at-all secret groups etc.

I urge everyone to keep a long-term perspective, keep the pub-crawl
manageable and fun and intimate. It's perfect now, beware the beast
that lurks behind the enthusiasm.

LP
