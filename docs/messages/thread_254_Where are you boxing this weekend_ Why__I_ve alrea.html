At 11:01 AM 8/7/2004, you wrote:

>Did you guys go left down the hill at first:? That's the way I read the
>clues so Dude and i headed in the totally wrong direction and spent 30
>minutes on the wrong 180 degree path. Of course there were twin trees
>there and a tree that fit the description so I was totally perplxed. I
>finally decided to go in another directed, went to far, was on the wrong
>180 path, etc. Am amazed I actually found it! But how sweet it is!!!

Nope, we pretty much walked directly to the box!

Recently, we went back to a box that I had searched for *twice* and had
been unable to find, back when I was just getting started letterboxing a
couple of years ago. This time, I zeroed in on it really quickly and was
amazed at how I could have spent so long looking in the wrong place the two
times I had tried to find the box before. It wasn't even a difficult clue
or an unobvious hiding place.

I like to theorize that you can develop a "sixth sense" about letterboxes
after you've found your first hundred or two -- and this then helps you to
figure out where a letterbox is on a more instinctive, intuitive level
instead of following the specifics of the clues. I saw evidence of this
theory last year when I went letterboxing with Princess Lea & Amanda from
Seattle out in CA, and was constantly surprised at how Amanda would walk
directly to the letterbox we were looking for, with no pause to take in the
final leg of the clue. She would know what side of the big tree on which to
look without having to walk around the whole tree.

Another thing that helps as you find more and more letterboxes is getting
to know the clue "style" of the person who planted a particular box. After
you've found several boxes planted by the same letterboxer, you can get a
sense of how their clues are meant to be deciphered -- do they like to
throw in tricky parts or are the clues very direct, are their paces a
little shorter or a little longer, would they be the type to direct us to
that deer trail that's barely visible off the side of the trail or should
we go on to the next major path instead? You can gain this kind of insight
from experience. Of course, some letterboxers like to mix it up in clues to
their different boxes, so knowing something about the clue writer doesn't
always provide you with any "tells."

Anyhow, just musing here -- back to my day's chores now!

to the challenge!
