Anniversary in West Virginia

From: tworstaggering | Date: 2007-07-09 13:11:19 UTC
It occurred to us last week that with one child at camp and another
on a church trip to Mexico that the three remaining teenagers at
home could manage on their own, so my husband and I drove to West
Virginia for a 3-day getaway to celebrate our 27th anniversary. And
to please me, my noxer husband agreed to some letterboxing!

Hah! "Some" never satisfies an addict! We ended up finding 13/15
that we searched for, and my dh tracked down a few of those himself!

We really appreciated the historical and geological sites that we
visited looking for the boxes, plus a series of 1 and 2 mile hikes
in absolutely gorgeous forests, plateaus, bogs, and meadows.

Our very last box required a second stop and had my dh and I "spruce-
diving" up and down a rocky ridge for an hour, and just as we were
about to give up [again] because twilight was setting in, under the
most unlikely of trees, there it was. While I stamped in, dh
interpreted the clues from the end backwards, and, well, we really
disagreed with the first clue which sent us a half mile in the wrong
direction, but we choose to be forgiving in victory!

I have a new anniversary digital camera, too, so I'll try to post a
photo...

Mommo

Where in WV did you go? Personally, I feel that WV is the only place
to spend an anniversary ;) My family has had a cabin in Pocahontas
Co. near Minnehaha Springs for the past 36 years (I'm almost 36, btw)
and we have spent family vacations there every year while my Dad was
alive. After my grandfather passed away it was up to us to help
grandma go up once a month to keep the grass mowed, maintenance, etc.
So needless to say, when I got married I insisted that we spend our
honeymoon at the cabin. Now, every chance we get we spend our
anniversary there, and get in a little boxing ;) Too bad I didn't
know about letterboxing when we got married -- at the time of year we
spend our honeymoon there nothing was open because it was before
Memorial Day!! So hubby had to put up with me going to used book
stores ;)

midlandtrailblazer

We went to the Canaan Valley area. We stayed at a
wonderful B&B in Davis, the Meyer House. One day we
did rafting in little "Duckies" on the Cheat River [a
