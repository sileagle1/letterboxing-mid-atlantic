Lost & Found - Found Stamp...

From: jerseytrailblazers | Date: 2007-07-17 16:41:31 UTC
Hello all, yesterday while doing some boxes in Washington Crossing
State Park, I crossed the water into PA and did the box "Bucks
Covered Bridge Series #2 - Van Sant Bridge". After stamping in and
re-hidng the box I went back up to the bridge and looked down as I
was about to cross back over to my car. In the middle of the path
that I had just walked on was a stamp...it was as clear as day from
above..even though I walked by it below without noticing it. I went
down and retrieved it and it appears to possibly be someone's
signature stamp. It is a red hat which is made out of the small
combination of letters "rh" and there are dozens of them which make
up the shape of the hat. I have seen the name "Red Hat Boxers"
mentioned before - although I find no listing on LBNA or AQ. (Except
for one variant of the spelling on AQ that has only 1 plant logged).
I did not look through the whole log in the box I had just stamped as
I was already tired after a long day and just glanced at it...but I
believe they had just stamped in days before me. I was about to go
back down to the box to see if there was more info on the page they
stamped, but as luck would have it, a group of children w/Mom came
along and were hanging out and playing near the bridge. I waited
awhile by my car but they were going nowhere fast as the woman was
talking on her cellphone while the kids were running all over the
area (and also picking up rocks and tossing them in the area from
right near where I found the stamp...luckily I found it first). So,
since my day was running late and my energy and water was low..I
headed off for the 1 hour drive back home. The point of this post
should be obvious by now...is anyone famaliar with the owner of this
stamp, so that I can make contact and mail it back to them if they
would like it returned? Any assistance would be appreciated for this
poor little lost stamp.

-John
