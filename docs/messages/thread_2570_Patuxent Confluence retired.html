Just a note for folks boxing in Maryland!

I had some excellent correspondence this week with a relatively new boxer who also
geocaches, psbales. They had attempted my Patuxent Confluence box but could not locate it,
and they kindly emailed me. A few days later they stopped by the park office for another
reason and noticed my letterbox sitting in the office. Once again they kindly emailed me.

Yesterday I was able to retrieve the box from the office and have a nice conversation with the
park official who had picked it up. It seems like some folks had opened it up and strewn the
contents around. When the official, who was on trash detail, found it scattered all over the
place, he at first thought it was trash, but then realized what it was, and so kindly looked in
the general area for what parts he could salvage.

Inside the box were the stamp and my "this is a letterbox" notice, but no logbook.

I thanked this man profusely for his kindness! And I am deeply grateful to pbsales as well.

All this to say that 1) Like Blanche DuBois I am dependent on (and thankful for) the kindness
of strangers, and 2) I am retiring the Patuxent Confluence for now.
