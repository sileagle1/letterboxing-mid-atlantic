nice noxer husband

From: tworstaggering | Date: 2007-07-28 23:11:33 UTC
I was feeling really blue this morning, probably a delayed response
to all the emotional upheaval with my daughter's accident [last week
she got hit by a car while riding her bike, 4 vertebral fractures,
but she's been released, is walking and DOING GREAT!]. Anyway, I
was just sitting like a blob this morning with absolutely no energy
to do anything. Finally my husband coaxed me out for a twenty
minute walk. He saw me glancing into the woods where I have a box
hidden. He said why don't you check on it? I hadn't looked since I
planted it back in the winter. I sat on a bench and it was a real
treat to see the stamps and read the kind comments.

After we got home I perked up and took him up on his offer to drive
with me to letterbox this afternoon, which I hadn't felt up to
before [now that is really down in the dumps!] There was an elusive
Box#3 in a series of five that was bugging me. We hiked the three
mile loop and then this dear patient man spent TWO HOURS with me
combing the woods. You know the drill--the clues don't seem to fit
the terrain, so basically you are just searching through the forest
for the final tree that might fit the description. Well, two hours
later all I had found was a snake under a rock..eek...so we gave it
up and continued hiking on the loop. And much much farther down the
trail, suddenly the clues began making sense, and OH HOW EASY it is
when you are in the right place deciphering clues!

We were the first to ever find that 3rd box, and then I rechecked
the fifth box in the series, and the First Finders Certificate was
still there, as everyone had left it for whoever it was who actually
had found all five. And now that was me!

What a great pick-me-up this afternoon was, and what a great guy!!!

Mommo
with the very patient Warrior77 who said it was worth it all to see
the big smile on my face!
