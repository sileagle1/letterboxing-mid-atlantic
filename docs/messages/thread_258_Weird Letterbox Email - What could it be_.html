From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2004-08-08 21:55:02 UTC
I got this really weird email today....

FROM: MR JOHN PUJEH
PHONE: +229-8675309 (LETTERBOX REPUBLIC)
EMAIL: xxxx@...

I am MR JOHN PUJEH from SLOBOVIA, a Country in an undisclosed
location. My father is MR MOMOH PUJEH, former MINISTER of
LETTERBOXING and COMMUNICATIONS and one time DEPUTY FINANCE MINISTER
in Slobovia. My father and mother were arrested by the government of
my Country and put in detention since 28/09/2001. My parents were
accused of keeping large quantity of LETTERBOXES in the house, and
also privately hiding large quantity of LETTERBOXES abroad. You can
verify this from SLOBOVIAN EMBASSY in your country.

I escaped to a hide out in PENNSYLVANIA COMMONWEALTH, a State in
America with 1 Letterboxes containing about 1 stamp, a log book and
Forty Six Million virtual U.S. dollars (USv$46,000,000.00). I kept
the boxes in a SECURITY & TRUST COMPANY in ALLENTOWN Capital of
LEHIGH REPUBLIC. For my safety and that of the boxes, I did not let
the company know that the boxes contain a stamp and a log book. I
told them that the boxes contain FAMILY TREASURE.

I cannot move about freely now. I need Your help urgently for both
SAFE STAMPING and LOGGING of this letterbox in your logbook. You are
one of the three email contacts given to me by a Cyber Cafe' operator
on my request for a letterboxing contact. She did not tell me your
name or country of origin. Though I did not tell her why I needed the
contact of any letterboxer.

Because of the urgent and confidential nature of this business, you
are advised to keep everything secret for now. if you are interested
in doing this business with me, kindly find this mini-letterbox and
stamp in. When stamping, include your trail name and personal stamp
for easy verification. From the Bridge of Bogert, continue 60 deg on
the trail of the South Bank until you find the last planet. At tree
at 115 deg brings you closer, find from there a crossing point at 80
deg. Across this step across and you shall be rewarded behind a
stone of light tan along the bottom of the wall of stone. Thanks for
your anticipated [cooperation].

Yours faithfully,
MR JOHN PUJEH

From: Silent Doug | Date: 2004-08-09 00:55:15 UTC
Jennifer and I only had 12 finds today, but we spent a couple of hours
working on narrowing down some mystery (or otherwise challenging) boxes, so
we got a good workout on the trails nonetheless. Here's what we found:

Giant Peach
Hungry Termites
Jump for Joy
Catch the Wind
Great Blue Heron (where we picked up the Happy Duck Hitchhiker)
Who Is the Author? (all 6)

All were in good shape

I hit two milestones today -- ending the day exactly at F400, and reaching
PFX500 (http://alum.wpi.edu/~p_miner/Letterboxes.html#pfxclubs) on the
day's second letterbox.

The day wasn't all good, though -- I got stung by a yellow jacket or bee at
the last box in the Who Is the Author series as I was putting the box back
in place. There may be a nest nearby or even in the box's hiding place, so
others should use caution there.

Doug
