Thursday, March 25, 2004

Loose mountain lion a rare sight in Jersey

Mahwah police confirm sighting near pond

A mountain lion, a species of large cat that has not been seen in New
Jersey for more than 200 years, is being sought in Mahwah after
police officers and other witnesses saw it hunting geese and lounging
around a pond yesterday.

Unsure whether the animal escaped from a private compound, was let
loose or is an amazing natural phenomenon, police called in the state
Division of Fish and Wildlife after confronting the cat about 3:15
p.m. and watching it run into a forested area. State conservation
officers are monitoring the area on Route 17 where employees at a
Federal Express building first reported seeing the cougar earlier
this month.
