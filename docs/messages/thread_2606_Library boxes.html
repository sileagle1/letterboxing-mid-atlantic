Hi, Miz Scarlet! Thanks for noticing our letterboxing project! I've
never letterboxed personally, but I just might start now. I had heard
about letterboxing and geo-caches in various ways and thought it was a

little tweaking, some poetic license and some unfortunate delays, this
is what we came up with. The kids seem to be having fun with it. I'm
planning to write up an article for a library publication, so we'll see
if we can spread the word a little further. Thanks for your interest!
-- Jeanne

From: Team PM JET | Date: 2007-08-12 00:45:43 UTC
SSB,

You are correct. Get a Clue is the National Summer Reading program for Libraries sponsored I believe by the ALA. My wife is the Children's Librarian in Lambertville, NJ and has done a huge amount of programming through the Get a CLue program. Each year there is a national theme and they have workshops as well as a binder of ideas.

A Library Letterbox was one of the suggested activities particularly for teen readers. I personally think it is a great idea for this theme. I read over the suggestions in the binder and they all pretty much followed what I have learned on these boards. I have to check but I think it also recommended checking out LBNA and atlasquest FAQ's before planting. Unfortunately, I did not have the time to plant one for her so she did not have a box for the theme but will eventually:)

Anyway just a little background for you.
