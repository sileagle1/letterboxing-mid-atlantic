From: winpax | Date: 2007-08-12 18:54:41 UTC
Okay, so I am out today boxing (of course) and had a few questions. If
a log book is full, do you take it and replace with another new logbook
(then contact owner so you can mail it to them)? This was not the case
with any log books but one was pretty close. Also, if I swap out
logbooks, what if the planter has a beautiful hand-made one and mine is
all generic? So question- swap or leave?

Second Question:
One box was swapped with Geocache stuff- they even logged it (I know,
it broke my heart too). It kind of made me laugh though, the note said
they loved the stamp so they are leaving two mini-stamps in its place.
Now with this box, do I take it so I can mail it to the owner, or just
leave it? In this case I left it. The geocache is less than 5 ft away
and I'd hate for a future letterboxer to go hunting and walk away
without finding the letterbox and instead gets some geocache box.

Anywho, these are my two questions. Anyone have an answer? (Oh, if this
turns out to be a flame war- SORRY FOR ASKING! I just want to do right
in the eyes of the majority).
Win-pax

From: Hikers and Hounds | Date: 2007-08-12 19:20:48 UTC
No flames on this list so no worries.
 
The logbook thing happens alot. I try to carry extra paer or even 3x5 card logs in my pack for this reason. If the book is full and there is enough room for both, I just add the new blank logbook, if there's not enough room for both I will replace withte blank and contact the placer for an address to return their original to. Either way contact the placer.
 
The geocache thing also happens quite a bit and I've had a stamp "swapped" out from one of my boxes. Again, contact the placer and tell them whats going on and see what they would like you to do, if it's possibel for you to return should they ask you to. I have taken to writing on my boxes "This is NOT a geocache! Do not remove or "trade" any of the contents!!" or something like that.
 
Good questions and both are common occurances. Others opinions may very but it's all good in this hood.

Re: [lbma] I have a couple of questions- can you assist?

From: Otis' Friends | Date: 2007-08-12 20:10:47 UTC
Add new log book and leave old one (space providing).  (Same advice if you find a soggy mess log book, provided you can seal it off from the other contents.)  If they have a fancy schmancy log they'll probably want to put in another fancy one, but in the interim the finders after you will appreciate it and chances are what you leave will be better than some random scrap of paper.  I've taken to carrying spare boxes, bags, and logs.  If there's space for only one log book it's a judgment call.  And, of course, always contact the placer.
 
Re: Geocache snatches - there's nothing worse than finally figuring out a mystery and discovering the stamp gone.  It's an honest but uninformed mistake, and you have an absolute privilege to curse as loudly as you can when you see it.  Some people have had luck in retrieving the stamp .  It's a good idea to record the name of the person who took the stamp and try to contact that person through the geocaching website.  Where the box and cache are so close together, someone's gonna have to move or this will continue to happen.  That's up to the placer, though.

From: JuneMcAllister | Date: 2007-08-12 20:19:12 UTC
Welllllll, no it's not "cute". You don't take a stamp from a box. Period. Family or not, cute kids or not. Can you imagine how you would feel if you spent hours carving a stamp, and some of us do just that, and then someone took it because they thought it was cute? And left some kind of crap or commercial stamp in it's place? That stamp will probably wind up under some kid's bed trapped in dust bunnies.

It's something you just do not do. You don't take a stamp from a letterbox. This is something I for one take very seriously, and I think other boxers do as well. I appreciate that you ask these questions. Lots of people just start boxing and don't bother to find out anything.

MissMoon

From: winpax | Date: 2007-08-12 22:25:00 UTC
I agree completely w/not taking stamps. The folks that took the stamp
though thought it was a geocache 'trinket' and didn't know any
better. Also, I truly do feel horrible for the carver and planter-
They're really great and nice- I've written them back and forth a few
times- I hated telling them what happened.
My very first stamp, which was going to be my signature stamp and is
now a personal traveler, snapped and broke in the corner (1/3 of the
stamp broke off). It's too big for a sig. stamp anyway and was pretty
bad, but still, I was crushed. I tried gluing it and adding a backing
so it works, but how long will it last? No idea- This was my first
stamp though so hopefully it'll last a bit longer.
Bottom line, I've dried out wet boxes, replace a busted box (it took
2 weeks until I got back out there, but it got done), I've replaced
countless baggies, I write everyone after boxes are found and let
them know the condition, I leave no TRASH (see main board), wipe
backs of stamps after stamping in(with unscented wipes), plant better
than found, even replant where it was supposed to be- 1 was close but
not where it was supposed to be- and let the owner know just in
case. . .I really am trying my best to do this great hobby justice. I
don't want to be one of those boxers that some might roll their eyes
or shake their heads at. I understand your last email as anger
towards the geocachers and not me- I just hope you and other
understand too that I don't agree with what they did but I do see why
they did it.

Contacting Geocachers (WAS: Re: I have a couple of questions- can you assist?)

From: rozebud.rm | Date: 2007-08-13 17:08:52 UTC
Ok, the ONLY good thing here is this: geocachers are all about
logging their finds on-line. You or the planter simply need to
create a user ID on the geocache site, find the listing for the
nearby cache, and try to determine who the "guilty" parties are.
They may well still have the stamp and would probably be happy to
return it if the situation were explained to them!

Here's the link to their official site to get you started, or to pass
on the the placer: http://www.geocaching.com/

Good luck!
