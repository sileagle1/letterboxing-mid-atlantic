From: Jill | Date: 2007-08-12 23:50:29 UTC
Just wanted to take a moment to introduce myself. I live in Delaware
and have been letterboxing for about a year and a half. I also
geocache.

Not in defense of geocachers that take stamps, just maybe an
explanation. These may also be the same geocachers that other
geocachers complain about. They are new to the sport, unaware of
letterboxing and not really paying attention to the rules. They can
also leave trash in the box and not hide it properly. In otherwords,
don't dump all geocachers into one catagory. Trust me, we are really
nice people. Yes, there are a few bad apples, probably in both
sports. So ease up or you'll make me sad.

I geocache because there are a lot around me. I letterbox because I
like to hike in the woods. Both are treasure hunts. I really enjoy
both equally.

There is an issue of where you place a box. If you want to safe guard
your box, join geocaching.com and watch the placement of boxes near
yours. It's very easy to do. There are maps, one that can bring up
all the geocaches in an area. You can get an idea of how close they
are to your box and whether you need to check on it more often. Also
gives you a quick way of locating a geocacher that takes your stamp.
Nearly all geocachers log in. You could also email me and I would
check the area for you.

I know there are letterboxers here in Delaware but I haven't met any
yet. I did try to get a little help from someone about one of his
boxes, but his response was not very helpful. Do letterboxers help
when someone has trouble with clues or is that just not done? Just
wondering. I haven't tried asking anyone else, now I just put it in a
list of "forget about it, maybe someday". Really, that's my heading.
As for logging my finds, I really do that for myself, to keep track
of what I've done and what I need to do. Sometimes I email the owner
when I find a box, I know I should but just don't always think of it.

Perhaps I can get to an event. I'll be on the lookout.

jillalexandria
