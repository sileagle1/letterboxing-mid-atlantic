Random thoughts

From: brendan_hickey13 | Date: 2007-08-13 14:07:08 UTC
Some interesting discussions going, just a few odd ideas - maybe some
free association, if my colleagues still use it...

I think that the geocacher/letterboxer relationship is similar to
that
of mountain bikers and hikers - two groups using the same resources
in
ways that are similar but not quite the same, bound to come into
occasional unintended conflict.

Yeah, poking around 2.5 MPH on foot, I'd rather not hit a blind turn
and face a biker doing 15 MPH, but if I were on the ground with a
twisted ankle and 8 miles from help, I'd rather see a biker.

Same dynamic applies to horses & hikers - scary to suddenly share a
narrow trail with a half-ton or more of animal, especially if you're
not familiar with the equine psyche.

In terms of helping with clues, most folks will give you a nudge - a
placer wants the box to be found. Given the hours that it takes to
carve a stamp, maybe assemble a log, scout and plant the box, and
write
the clues, yeah, please, find it and enjoy it.

Mystery boxes are a different scene, though. If I'm trying but I'm
stuck then I might hope for a little help, but not so much that I
don't
get the satisfaction of figuring it out.

Bottom line on all of it for me is that this is my hobby. At work, I
trade occasional distress and distaste for money to survive. In
letterboxing, I'm out to have fun and there are some really cool
people
and amazing places and challenges in this. It will get you to think
about stuff and go to spots that you'd never consider on your own.

Psychiker
