as "cuts" means "to throw up," as in "I think I'm gonna kutz."

From: jbzfarm | Date: 2007-08-28 22:03:33 UTC
Do you think it counts if your Grandmother took your aunt to see a
pow wow doctor in the early 50s? My aunt had a lump on her head and
the pow wow told my grandmother to say a few words and throw salt
over her shoulder. The lump did go away (probably would have
resolved itself on its own). This same grandmother tended York
Central Market at a stand that backed up against the Wilbert Hess's
family stand. The same Wilbert Hess that was involved in the
Rehmeyer Murder. My Grandmother tells me that Wilbert's mother
would lay kernels of corn at the edge of their tables at market. It
was meant to keep others who worked at the market from "hexing"
them, a sort of protection. My grandmother said they were a
very "hexy" family and not very good bathers either.

I always wanted to do a hex series of stamps. I carved a distelfink
once and DH gently recommended that I try again.
