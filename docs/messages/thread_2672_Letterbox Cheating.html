From: JuneMcAllister | Date: 2007-09-11 22:16:49 UTC
Actually there is a certain amount of cheating going on. Event boxes are
listed on AQ as regular boxes , as one example. People count postals and
personal travelers, HHs on tables at events as finds in their own logbooks
even if AQ doesn't. I recently removed quite a number of "finds" from AQ
of event boxes, after checking with my conscious. But all those other
people who stamped into those same boxes and listed them as finds didn't
have that problem. And I don't even know why it's become this big thing to
keep count on AQ, but I do it. I get a kick out of seeing them all listed,
such as they are. What can I say?

As for the complainers, they fall into the catagory of get a life in my
opinion, and if that gets back to anyone who did the complaining and you
have a problem with it, feel free to e-mail me at any time. But be
advised, anyone who comes into my mailbox uninvited and gives me a hard
time is not received well, if you get my drift. Don't sweat it. Any large
group contains a certain amount of jerks and we have certainly have our
share. Just the fact that it bothers them pretty much says it all about how
they look at the hobby, and what do you care what they think?

MissMoon

From: jbzfarm | Date: 2007-09-11 22:31:35 UTC
If someone is cheating, who cares? My conscious is clear. I think
it is very sad someone would cheat in Lbing. What do they gain? A
high F count? If they want to check off all of the boxes, be my
guest.

I am not impressed by high F counts. I am rather impressed by
clever clues, artistic boxes and awesome hikes. I am grateful and
highly regard the people that take the time to create the journey
for others. Artful and creative planters carry much more weight and
value than someone who speed stamps or falsely claims to speed stamp
via F counts. I also think the issues surrounding event stamps vs.
traditional finds and F count are non issues because there is no
standard that is followed. IMHO, this dillutes the importance of F
count even more.

From: davyschris@aol.com | Date: 2007-09-11 22:38:58 UTC 
I think that if you read the FAQ on Atlas Quest, it says that the F-count on 
the ribbon only counts boxes you find out in the wild, not event boxes or 
hitchhikers.

I don't remember; I read it a long time ago.

I think that's right, though, because I keep an Excel spreadsheet of what 
I've found (yeah, I'm a NERD) and I don't count anything but boxes I find out in 
the wild, not hitchhikers, travelers, etc, and it's pretty close to my AQ 
count.  My AQ count is actually lower, because I'm sure I missed inputting a box 
or two here or there.

I don't really care if people inflate their F counts.  I mean, what does that 
get you?  I feel kind of bad for the people who feel the need to do that, 
actually.  I know what my count is, approximately.  I don't really pay attention 
to anyone else's, because I'm only competing with myself.

Actually there is a certain amount of cheating going on. Event boxes are
listed on AQ as regular boxes , as one example. People count postals and
personal travelers, HHs on tables at events as finds in their own logbooks
even if AQ doesn't. I recently removed quite a number of "finds" from AQ
of event boxes, after checking with my conscious. But all those other
people who stamped into those same boxes and listed them as finds didn't
have that problem. And I don't even know why it's become this big thing to
keep count on AQ, but I do it. I get a kick out of seeing them all listed,
such as they are. What can I say?

As for the complainers, they fall into the catagory of get a life in my
opinion, and if that gets back to anyone who did the complaining and you
have a problem with it, feel free to e-mail me at any time. But be
advised, anyone who comes into my mailbox uninvited and gives me a hard
time is not received well, if you get my drift. Don't sweat it. Any large
group contains a certain amount of jerks and we have certainly have our
share. Just the fact that it bothers them pretty much says it all about how
they look at the hobby, and what do you care what they think?
