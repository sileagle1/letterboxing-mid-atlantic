Mistaken Box (bomb)

From: cleverestpig | Date: 2007-09-11 11:17:14 UTC
Hey guys, has anyone every had this happen before? I just got word
from my mom that a box I planted where she lives (very small town in
Western Kansas) was found to be a suspicious item. Brought in the
police, fire department, dogs, and bomb squad. Now, I can't even
believe they have a squad in this little town of 5000 people but who
knows. So a lady who works across from the little park where this was
planted made them put it back! Seems she knows the librarian, (there's
a box in the library too) and knew that it was game/hobby. I'm
thinking I ought to notify the police department about the one at the
courthouse?

FWIW the box was a lock-n-lock covered in cammo tape with the official
atlas quest letterbox label printed in bright yellow and black.

wow.

From: brendan_hickey13 | Date: 2007-09-11 15:31:38 UTC
Were a bit nervous about a recent find near a Coast Guard station but
the Coasties didn't seem to care.

However, I have run with Hash House Harriers a few times and do
suggest taking a lesson from the folks who were busted after marking
a trail through an IKEA in Connecticut. I think that will get
settled without the felony charges and not the first time that it's
been a concern - a Philadelphia HHH was cited after marking telephone
poles - near the mint, if I recall correctly.

I'm not sure how to avoid this other than being smart about plants
and hoping that finders are smart when retrieving and replacing.

From: nattybumppolbna | Date: 2007-09-12 12:10:58 UTC
The morning of the big Boston bomb scare - where Cartoon Network had some people attach
battery-powered magnetic LED light signs promoting one of their shows that shut down big
parts of Boston for most of the day - I got calls from 2 non-letterboxing friends who know I
have this weird hobby where I hide things in cities. They wanted to know if the strange
boxes the Boston police found might be mine.

I of course replied, "I dunno. Could be." One of those friends works in TV and passed the
info to a news producer who called and wanted to interview me about letterboxing.
