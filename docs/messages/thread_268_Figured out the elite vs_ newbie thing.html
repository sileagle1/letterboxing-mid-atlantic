Figured out the elite vs. newbie thing

From: cahillymom | Date: 2004-08-10 19:00:39 UTC
1998 - article in Smithsonian magazine. The type of person that
reads Smithsonian takes to the woods, compass and rubber stamp in
hand.
1998-oh,2000ish - promotion by EMS & other outdoorsy-type places and
publications. Hikers and other outdoorsy-types go back out to the
woods with rubber stamps added to their compasses.
2000ish-2004 - various articles appear in the Family outing/things
to do/Oh, isn't that interesting? sections of regional newspapers
and magazines. Families looking for interesting things to do take to
the woods in said regions, compasses in hands.
2004 - article appears in Time magazine

The demographics keep expanding.
Not seeking flames; I just had an "Ah-Hah!" moment last night
talking about LB with a neighbor when I found myself mentioning the
two very different magazines in rapid succession. It ties into Wanda
telling me at a gathering last summer that when she started it was
much more a "Mensa-like" thing to crack clues.

So, newbies - get out there and read Smithsonian.
(kidding kidding kidding.... I love newbies - they give me more
boxes to find....)
Aud
