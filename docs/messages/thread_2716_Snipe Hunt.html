From: nattybumppolbna | Date: 2007-10-02 18:14:04 UTC
For me, these are the best people I know in the whole world. I may not agree with them
on sex, drugs, politics, war, God or anything else, but we do agree that letterboxing is
something special. Special enough for all of these people to devote so much of their
precious time and lifeforce for our mutual entertainment and edification.

So when conflict arises - as it often does with my particular brand of humor - I always
assume it is a misunderstanding or my fault and not an attack. An explanation, discussion
or apology goes a long way toward maintaining group harmony. For what profit a man (or
woman) if he should win the argument but lose the respect of the group?

We never all agree on anything. But we always seem to respectfully disagree. Which
makes this place different from the "Big Board." We share a love for a game that has no
rules. Only mutual respect for each other and the customs and mores of the group can
this continue. Letterboxing is a tenuous thing at best. One only need to look at the
missing box situation in CT to see how easily it could all go away.

Nobody should withdraw from this group. But perhaps we should all recommit ourselves
to a policy of "disagreeing without being disagreeable."

Personally, I am recommitting myself to not drinking red wine and getting preachy in the
middle of the day. Perhaps I should forego recommitment and just be committed.

As an apology, I will now write a book clue based on this long and miserable post.
Something good should come of it, I think.

Natt-
