From: sewsowbizzy | Date: 2007-10-04 12:59:19 UTC
I agree with MizS - MasterCarve is crummy and toooo thick - takes up
valuable log book space.
I have found at my local ACMoore they have some 4x6 pink stuff on the
shelf and I run in every week with my 40% coupon and scoop up a piece
at $5.49-40%. And oh how nice it was for carving again. Albeit white
PZ is only 1% less enjoyable

If you are using a laser printer (toner not ink) then try transferring
with Acetone (I use a can from HomeDepot not nail polish remover that
has other oily ingredients). Print, lay rightside down, lightly rub
with tissue wetted with Acetone. Too much may smear image - but then
just wipe rubber clean and try again with new printed page/image.

Absent the Acetone -- like when flying - I use the No.2 soft pencil
method but I just print on bond, run the pencil around the lines, flip
and rub.

Both of these methods have the printed image flipped right side down
on the material so Instant Reversal of text -- no backwards letters --
now if I could only learn to speal.

ssb

MasterCarve will melt, melt, melt. We needed a rubber stamp here at
work, for a fundraising event involving alcohol - if you were old
enough and paid admission, we stamped our logo on the back of your
hand. PR person wanted to send out for the stamp, and I was
like "No! Let me carve it!" I brought my little craft iron to work,
laser-printed the logo, hit it with the iron and got...a mess.

Fortunately, the mess is only stuck to the paper, so I flipped the
rubber over and did it again, the old-fashioned way.

Perhaps MasterCarve would be ok with a lower-temp setting on the
iron, but the craft iron has no temperature settings, just on/off.
