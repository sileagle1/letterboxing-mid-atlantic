The double bag was a 40 degree bag and she had 0 degree mummy inside to stay warm. As for the kids, when temperatures approached freezing during Fall/Spring camping, they got to sleep in their snow suits.
 
I try to teach my first year Boy Scouts (11 year olds) about the second law of thermodynamics. How two objects in contact tend to equalize in temperature. That they need a closed cell foam pad to stop that process, not because I'm concerned with their comfort. What I usually end up with is "The earth is a really big heat sink. Get a foam pad that is hard to squeeze or you won't pass Tenderfoot requirement 1."  We work on the finesse items for winter camping after they master this skill.
