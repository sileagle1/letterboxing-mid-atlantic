Protective Headgear - Beware the Mast!

From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2007-10-07 21:45:40 UTC
Does anybody have that favorite old hat, all white from accumulated
sweat (like the certain hat I am prone to wear when out boxing - Bug
points to whom remembers what it says)?

Does it serve purpose, but to shield the balding pate from summer's
rays or protect it from winters cold breezes?

Well, yesterday it did. Twas walking back from a short saunter at
Green Lane Park in Raindear Country, when some breeze caught an Oak
Tree during what must be a mast year (lotsa fruit compared to other
years). As the acorns came running down I thought "I wonder how
much protection this slimy old hat provide if I would get hit on the
bean?"

Well, my question was soon answered. Thwock! An acorn of unknown
size and origin hit me on the left side of my hat covered head.
While it smarted a little, I think the old slimy did add another
layer of protection to the old noggin! So, you can add
acorn/hickory nut protector to sunblock and windscreen to the
purposes of the slimy old hat. Although, I think the ubiquitous and
much more massive black walnut may be a bit much for its defenses.
Not as prim as Cyclonic's fine fedora, yet also not a hat of red,
but a slimy hat worn with pride.

No clue was written into this silly post and Jenny's area code is
610, natch.

Cheers, LB
