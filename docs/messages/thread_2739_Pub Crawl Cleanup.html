Pub Crawl Cleanup

From: Suzanne Coe | Date: 2007-10-12 06:43:00 UTC
(This would be the story of the day after the pub crawl.  I think I'd better go ahead and tell it, because my blogging friend still has us in San Francisco, and he's got 3 more days of the California trip yet to go....  Sheba)
                                 
                                        *   *   *   *   *   *

So I called my friend Cyclonic and said, "Bro, you know what we are doing on the first day of my vacation?"

"No," he said warily, "what are we doing?"

"We are going south," I said.  "We are going to do some boxing in the Glorious South, and then we will stay the night at your girlfriend's, and then we will do some more boxing in the morning before we head back so you can go to work.  And at some point in there we are going to have a drink at Stack's House, because after 3 years of looking at the clues, I FINALLY know where it is."

"Actually, that's not a bad plan," he said (sounding much relieved).  "Schizokeet & I are going to Great Adventure the day before, we can swing by & pick you up.  And while we're down that way, we'll take a trip back to Bulldawg's box, because I'm pretty sure I know how to get into the logbook now....  How's 7AM sound?"

                                      *   *   *   *   *   *

Which is how Becky & Nathan & I ended up at the Four Secrets box on a lovely morning on the last day of September.  I have to say I'm not at all sure I'd have figured that one out on my own, but it was definitely worth the trip.  The box is freakin' ingenious & the stamps are awesome.  May it live long & prosper!

While we were considering where to go next, it occurred to us that the pub crawl boxes might still be in place.  But where could we get the clues?  Whom could we call??  Why, of course--Tech Support South--Sewsowbizzy!  Fortunately Judy was home, so that was our next stop.  She had been going to pull the boxes, but suggested that we could do it instead, take all the boxes back to Becky's place & stamp in, and then she'd meet us for dinner & pick them up.  Brilliant!  Judy gave us her clues, called Gallant Rogue to determine which boxes had already been pulled, and sent us off with a shopping bag to visit Old Town Manassas.

What fun!  We enjoyed following the clues, finding the clever locations, then--no stealth needed, just pick up the box & pop it into the bag.  At the pubs we told the bartenders that we were the cleanup crew.  The bookstore decided to keep theirs, so we stamped in & then did a little shopping....  then back to Becky's to sit around her table with our loot.  What a variety of beautifully crafted boxes-- kudos to the carvers!

Judy arrived and we handed over our booty, then the four of us went out to dinner.  At Stack's House!  I was a little worried when Judy ordered a letterbox along with her meal and the waitress didn't seem to know what that was, but after a slight delay it was located & brought to our table.   How cool to see the stamps from years ago.  Another 'boxing goal achieved....

                                      *   *   *   *   *   *

The next day we weren't in much of a hurry to go home.  After Becky left for work, Nathan & I took a walk down the trail to get the Bluemont Junction box.  It was a little farther than we'd figured, so we weren't sure we would have time for anything else.  But some inspired parking brought us within reach of the Ghost Castle of East Church Falls--a very satisfying find to finish up with!

So I was sorry to miss the pub crawl, but I have to say I had some terrific boxing  down south that weekend anyway!  Hope to get back there soon....
