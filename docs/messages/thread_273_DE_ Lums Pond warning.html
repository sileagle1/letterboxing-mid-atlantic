DE: Lums Pond warning

From: hsc_pride | Date: 2004-08-13 12:28:02 UTC
Head's up to anyone searching for or planting boxes at Lums Pond.
Apparently, the Lums Pond staff has removed all geocaches hiden in
the park. This is what was posted to each of the cache logs:

" August 3 by LumsPondStaff (0 found)
The staff at Lums Pond State park is asking that all Geocaches are
removed from the park. Our park is extremely environmentally
sensitive, and we fear that going off trail will compromise that
environment. We have to be responsible stewards, and this activity
can create unwanted trails and damage. We are asking that you remove
the cache within two weeks. "

I don't know if they view letterboxes the same way was geocaches, but
my hunch is that they do.

Coach of HSC Pride

Re: DE: Lums Pond warning...it may get worse.

From: hexacat | Date: 2004-08-13 16:21:08 UTC
the News Journal will be writing an article on letterboxing. A
journalist from the newspaper contacted me last week. she was looking
to interview local letterboxers and get a bead on the hobby in
general.

not that i'm a pessimist, however, once the News Journal publishes
something it usually has some negative fallout. Certainly the
Delaware State Parks department will react to the article and become
aware of letterboxes hidden in "their state parks". The reality is
they probably already know about letterboxing and hoping it will go
away quietly, but once the masses learn about the hobby their knee
jerk reaction will be to remove as many as they can to try and
prevent hundreds of people trampling off trails to look for
letterboxes.

it is not out of the question that a mandate is sent to all of DE
State Parks to pull the environmentally unfriendly boxes. and of
course park rangers will get upset that they would be forced to get
out of their vehicles and actually hunt down a letterbox.

granted i'm a newbie to this group and i may be talking out of line
here. however, one of the appealing aspects of letterboxing is the
small and obscure group of people partaking in the hobby. The News
Journal article will most definitely negate this facet. how they
print the article will be interesting. we can only cross our fingers
and hope for the best.

we need a secret handshake.
-hexacat

Re: [lbma] Re: DE: Lums Pond warning...geo Vs. lbxing - park control

From: The Doubtful Guests | Date: 2004-08-13 17:26:29 UTC
This brings up the old geocaching vs. letterboxing. 
 
I have always thought geocaching more potentially environmentally damaging then letterboxing.  I do both. 
 
In geocaching the GPS only leads you to withing about 30 feet or more of the location.  From there you often find yourself tromping all over, turning over every log and rock, to find the darned thing.  I read of one method called the "spiral search". 
 
I prefere in letterboxing where the clues may be clever, or mysterious, but usually once you get to the right place you're told pretty precisely where it is.
 
In reality nature recovers quickly from visitors. 
 
Park rangers would prefer that the park be a museum.  That people stay out and only observe from the sidelines.  But though we may go off trail we are not snowmobilers, dirt motorbikers or even mountain bikers.  As a class letterboxers clean up more trash and attract more positive nature appreciation than many other park users. 
 
The park managers should recognize that this is hard to stop and it is better to join them then beat them.  If they establish clear rules for permissions and placement, we would follow them, and they would have more control over caching and boxing than they do now. 
 
Scarab of the Doubtful Guests
