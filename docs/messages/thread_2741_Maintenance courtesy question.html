Maintenance courtesy question

From: Victor E. Ramirez | Date: 2007-10-15 13:32:54 UTC
As Abita (darcynr) posted, she sometimes takes wrong turns. Saturday
was no different, and we went straight, more or less, from box 1 to
box 3, skipping box 2 (which we bactracked and found later). It was
still a very enjoyable hike. Just ask my legs.

We got to box three, and as we looked toward the fallen tree that
should have hidden the box, we could see that the box had been
vandalized. The top & bottom of the box and its contents were spread
out near the tree and we feared the worst.

Fortunately, everything was intact and in good shape (which was a
great relief to us--the carvings are stunning!) Apparently, whatever
woodland creature decided to open the box thought a logbook and
stamp weren't worth eating.

(We don't think it was a human vandal, because I can't think of any
humans that would leave teethmarks like that, except for maybe Bat
Boy, and last I heard he was up in New York somewhere.)

The box was in pretty good shape, if you ignore the teethmarks, and
after stamping in we replaced everything and hid the box with a big
(BIG!) rock on top of it. I'll tell you about Abita digging up that
rock later.

When Abita logged the find in AQ, she included a note to the planter
stating we had found the box in that shape. In the reply, the
planter indicated she had planned to do a check on her boxes next
month but did this need maintenance now? We've replied it doesn't;
the box sealed up well. Heck, even the bags were in good shape.

As a matter of course, we carry extra bags with us should we see
some in bad shape along the way. But what about boxes? Would it be
considered rude if we replaced a box we felt had been chewed so
badly by an animal (or damaged some other way) that it was no longer
watertight, or should we just inform the planter and let them take
care of it?

--Victor (Ratinabox)

From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2007-10-15 22:17:21 UTC
What's in your box repair kit?

 About 15 baggies for logbooks and stamps.
 A bunch of papaer towels for drying off salvagable innards.
 A fair bit of paper for fashioning a replacement logbook (usually
some sheets from a Jeff Foxworthy calendar).
 A spare box sometimes.

Sometimes, we need to consider a box so trashed that we recover what
we can and contact the owner. We did that for the "Luck Be Lady"
box near Harrisburg in early July, who was grateful to get their
logbook back.

Box repairs are a given, any boxer worth their salt carries a kit.

Cheers,

LB

From: jbzfarm | Date: 2007-10-16 00:21:39 UTC
We actually remove bags from outside of boxes that are wet and muddy
inside the bag due to tearing. We then email the owner to let them
know the mess we found and how we tried to improve the situation (not
adding another bag to the outside of the box if it is a water tight
box). It is then up to them as to whether they want to replace the
outside bag. We normally carry, bags, logbooks, decon and paper
towels. The situation is sticky when someone uses a pencil box that
has a gaping slit in the back in its design and then puts that box in
a plastic bag. It is only a matter of time until the entire contents
are wet. It seems that when school starts we see more of these leaky
containers being used.

I have to admit, the best boxers are those that contact us with
specific information about the status of our boxes after they have
found them. We are very grateful to those that send an email
regarding the contents of the box. Recently we received a delightful
one from Froggy JG who found two of our boxes that were previously
reported missing. These were only 2 in a series of 6 that he found
and he gave us an in-depth report on each find. This was particularly
meaningful to us because we would have had to carve out a full weekend
to replace those boxes that were reported missing (not to mention the
time taken to re-carve). I think it is just as important to
acknowledge the status of a box if it is in fine shape as it is to
email details about a missing/wet/destroyed box. I find it difficult
to truly know if a box is missing because someone emails and tells us
so. I have had numerous emails with one person who explains the
details of where they were, what they did, where they looked and they
are in the right area and can't find the box. We go out, and there it
is, just as we left it.

From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2007-10-16 12:07:36 UTC
A "decon" is an army surplus decontamination container. About 3" by
5" by 2", made of a rigid plastic shell and a polyethylene lid. Very
watertight, if closed properly. Their smaller size and black plastic
make them easier to conceal. Favorite of Central PA letterboxers, and
some of us in South and East PA use them as well, but our supplier is
in Central PA. They are sometimes also referred to as "Posseware"
after the Crayola Posse (the source).

Cheers,

LB
