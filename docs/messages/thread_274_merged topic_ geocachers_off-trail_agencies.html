See how this all pulls together?
Yes, we are very much the same as geocachers, in that we go out and
play hide-and-seek in the woods with Tupperware (or whatever that
brand was Doug recommended). And so we should pull together in our
PR attempts with park folk.
BUT, my fear is that by emphasizing the sameness, we don't
communicate the DIFFERENCE between the hobbies, and the difference
is what makes letterboxing gentler on the environment than
geocaching, and potentially more acceptible to the powers-that-be.
To say it in the words of a whiny child,"Why should WE be kicked out
when THEY did the damage?"
The difference that must be emphasized is this:
A geocache clue says, "The cache is here, at (x,y); please use the
trail over here."
A letterbox clue says "Take this trail this far, turn here, turn
there and look."
We HAVE to take the trail because we don't know where we're trying
to get to. A good planter will choose a trail avoiding environmental
damage, so even the careless finder is led along a "safe" path. A
good geocache planter will do the same, of course, but since the
finder knows exactly where he's going and has a gadget in his hand
with a big arrow saying, "It's over there," he doesn't HAVE to.
