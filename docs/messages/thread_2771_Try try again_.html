From: darcynr | Date: 2007-10-29 03:06:26 UTC
Well, my first planted box has gone missing, after all of one week.

I'll be the first to admit that I didn't spend enough time scouting
the location...it was in trees between a playground and housing
development, and I was afraid it was a little too high-traffic an
area. But I mentioned it as a possibility to my nine-year-old niece,
she started coming up with a stamp & clue theme, and I'm an indulgent
aunt and didn't want to damped her enthusiasm by making her wait til
spring.

It just occurred to me that I could recarve the stamp and try
again...either in the same place, hoping that this was a fluke, or
maybe hidden hopefully more securely nearby.

If a box disappears that quickly, is that a sign that it's A Bad Spot,
or is it worth gambling another Lock & Lock?

I appreciate the insight of the experienced.

Thanks,

From: sewsowbizzy | Date: 2007-10-29 13:24:47 UTC
Well we did finally get some rain - maybe it washed out into the open
and was picked up by some kids or a critter. I'm always put my boxes
too close to water!! or on a slope!!

Did you cover your LnL with camo duct tape (carefull, the newer tape
is more like plastic tape and doesn't tear or stick very well. It
looks like oak leaves and the old looks darker) or just use black duct
tape. I leave the bottom untaped because inside the box I have the
"You have found a letterbox, not a geocache - Please do not remove"
notice printed up and taped -facing out- to the bottom -- so if it is
found it won't be mistaken for trash, a bomb, or a drug drop.

Good luck with your next plant and the one after and the one after...

sewsowbizzy

From: jbzfarm | Date: 2007-11-02 22:47:53 UTC
We have a series that is dedicated to families with kids and all of
the boxes can be found in playgrounds and parks. Of the series of 6
or 7 we lost one to a flood and one went missing 3 times. We had been
warned by other boxers that the park was difficult to find a hiding
spot that reduced the incident of something happening to it. We have
finally found such a good hiding spot for the one that went missing
repeatedly that it has remained safe and sound for many months now.
Most of the boxes in the series have been out there for a few years
with no problem. Smart hiding is key to these areas, but do not be
discouraged by kid friendly areas. It can be done successfully.
