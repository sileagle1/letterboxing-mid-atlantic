Hello avid posters and timid lurkers. We are looking into hosting a
Primitive Camping Event in Central PA, but need an idea as to how many
folks would actually be interested in primitive camping (no modern
facilities). Many primitive camping areas are quite limited as to how
many sites are available and the number of people and tents allowed
and so that the best accommodations can be made, we would like a to
hear from you as to whether you would consider camping a little closer
to mother nature for an event. The event would include daytime
letterboxing activities as well as night boxing for those spending the
night.

Would you be interested in a primitive camping event in Central PA?

How many in your party would be interested in overnight primitive
camping accommodations?

We realize that AQ provides Event pages, the level of interest from
this board will help determine the location of the event. Your
comments, suggestions and thoughts are very much appreciated.

From: jbzfarm | Date: 2007-11-04 22:59:36 UTC
Primitive camping means there will be no running water (no pit
toilets, showers, Christmas lights, loud radios, ATMs, vending
machines, generators or mini-golf). You can carry water in or bring a
filter for a nearby stream. I realize this isn't for everyone, and
that is OK! If you are a first timer and want to try primitive
camping, this would be a great opportunity. This event will most
likely be in the fall.

I need to get an idea as to how many folks would even consider
primitive camping. That will determine the location of the event so
that I can post it on AQ. Smaller numbers mean a more primitive area,
larger numbers mean a location that can accommodate larger parties
(but still primitive). Comments and the level of interest in primitive
camping for an event will determine the proper venue.

From: jbzfarm | Date: 2007-11-06 19:32:15 UTC
The location of the sites will be determined by the volume of
interest. The most desirable location will require a "walk-in" to
the camping site(s) and allow for complete privacy as no other sites
outside our group will be seen, heard or within walking distance from
the event. Backpacking could be included in the event. Generally,
when primitive camping, folks do not bring a bunch of stuff beyond the
necessary because you have to carry everything in. Food (many times
dried), water, tarps, tents and maybe a small camping chair takes
priority over heavy and bulky items like coolers so that you can make
it to the site in as few trips as possible.

From the emails I have received, I think there is enough interest to
do this at most preferred location.
