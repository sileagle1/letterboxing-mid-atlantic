Battle of Trenton - 1776

From: jerseytrailblazers | Date: 2007-11-12 01:09:59 UTC
Did some boxing today and finally made it out to see if the two Battle
of Trenton Boxes were still in place. The "Victory" box placed in
10/2003 is ok - no problems. The "Captain Moore's Insignia" box placed
in 4/2004 has not fared as well though. The box was visited in 4/2006
and then not again until 9/2007. The last finder wrote that it was
waterlogged and stated that they tried to dry it on site, but it was
then left in place. Well, today the log was still soaked through and
the book has rust stains from the rings across the pages. Many are
barely readable. I have taken the log in an attempt to salavage some
pages. The stamp itself is still in fairly good shape. I dried the box
out and have left some index cards in place for anyone who may come
along to stamp in on. I was not preapred with spare logs today. I
will be out in the next week or so to replace the log and return the
salvaged pages to keep the history of the box going. I may also switch
the box for a lock & lock. If anyone knows the original placer "D-man"
and could let them know the status of the box it would be helpful. It
does not seem that the box has been checked on in the last year. I did
send an e-mail through the LBNA link. Thanks, John
