Hey there CageysCrew, it's your friend Wood Thrush here. I'm guessing
the reporter's either with the Baltimore Sun or one of the Howard
County papers (like the Columbia Flyer or the Howard County Times)?

cover with the reporter. I wonder where that is online---does anyone
know?

boxes when the mainstream press articles come out (and I'm in the
public relations business myself!). Having said that, though, if the
newspaper is bound and determined to write *something* about boxing,
I agree that it is best if they do talk to a seasoned boxer. I can
understand why you are uncomfortable that they called you out of the
blue, since you are kind of new to the pastime.

Did you get the feeling they were going to write this article "come
hell or high water" or were they just kind of curious about boxing?

Yes. And look what happened. One article set a continent on fire. We've more than doubled
the number of boxes in Dartmoor- ever - and spread them to just about every county in the
US in less than 10 years. Imagine it doubling again here in the US.
I firmly believe that this game can only continue as long as it remains below the radar. When
the general public becomes aware of letterboxing it will be banned in all the places where we
now commonly play. There is a tipping point where we will cross into public consciousness
and the game will either become corporate or banned. I don't know where that tipping point
is, but I'd like to stay away from it as long as possible.

I understand how this game grabs you. I understand the desire for more boxes... more
boxes... more boxes. I am not asking that we keep it a secret. Growth is good. We need
growth - but slow, controlled growth.

That is why I will continue to urge restraint when dealing with the mass media.
