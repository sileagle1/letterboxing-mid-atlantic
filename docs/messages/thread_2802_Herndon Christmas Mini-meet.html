From: Arielle K. Masters | Date: 2007-11-17 18:20:32 UTC
Omia's sounds fine. I've never eaten there, and it's just barely still
in Herndon - it's at the corner of Dranesville & 7 by the double-decker
McDonald's - but it's worth a try. I'll pencil in Sunday, Dec. 23rd at
Omia's - it looks fine now but I may have to bow out, as some relatives
are due in town around then and who knows when they're coming or what
we'd be doing if they're here that day.

Is this main LBs only or whole families? And how/where would we plant
the new ones? If it's whole families, I'd suggest an early dinner - 5
or 5:30. If we're planting that afternoon, we should meet somewhere
else first, say at 3 or 4, to plant and THEN go to dinner. Perhaps we
could all plant in the same park/part in/near Herndon and call it the
First HALB (HALF?) series.

I haven't been paying a lot of attention to this, so I'll ask rather
than checking the site: are there downtown Herndon LBs yet? We could
put one near and/or in the library, one near the depot, one in Great
Harvest Bread if they'll let us, one near the caboose, one along the
W&OD but still downtown... maybe one by the HCC... Almost drive-bys
except we'd encourage people to park and then walk the series since it's
in a small area. We could each pick a location and do a box to match.
If we did that, I'd suggest eating downtown afterward rather than
driving to Omia's. My vote would be the Tortilla Factory (plenty of
room; not sure about music). Joe's Dogs is right by the depot and would
be a good place for a late lunch if you get there early. Yes, it's a
hot dog place.

Yoo-hoo! & crew
