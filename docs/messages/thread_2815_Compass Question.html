From: thedoubtfulguests | Date: 2007-11-26 18:53:34 UTC
Still using your Dad's Boy Scout compass?

The idea of liquid filled is so the needle doesn't swing around so
much. The liquid keeps it more stable. At least that's what I remember.
I think SILVA became famous a couple decades ago with liquid filled
compasses mounted on a rectangular base with straight edges and an
arrow, to help with orienteering. The system really makes it easy to
use a compass with a map. I've used one like them for like forever now.

Example:

To walk at 145 degrees for 15 steps, "Turn the dial on the compass to
the compass bearing (145). Line up the north needle to the north lines
below it. The compass base's arrow will now point in the direction you
should go." blah blah

To record a bearing, "Look down the base's arrow towards the rock you
hid the new lbox under. Hold it in place while you turn the dial until
the north needle lines up with the north lines under it. The compass
now reads the bearing to your newly hid box."

It gets even more fun with a topo map using the compass' straight edge.
Oh to triangulate your position from far, visible, mapped landmarks.

I know this is more than you asked for but it might help someone. :-)

Scarab of the Doubtful Guests
