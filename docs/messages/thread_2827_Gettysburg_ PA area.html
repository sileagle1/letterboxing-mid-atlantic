Hi-
A few weekends back the RedHatBox'rs rented a cabin in Pine Grove
Furnace and did the "Sunset Rocks" box [it's on LB.org Cumberland
County , Shippensburg]

It was a hike straight UP the side of a mountain. But this ol'broad
actually made it to the top and with screaming knee caps back down.
The view from the top was gorgeous -- maybe less so now with all the
leaves gone?? Which will also make the trail slippery having fallen
wet leaves to contend with. Just be carefull and go for it!

We only did the one box and then we took the 2.5 hr audio/guided bus
tour around the Gettysburg battle field.

From: alwayschaos | Date: 2007-12-04 12:29:46 UTC
I definitely recommend hiking poles for Sunset Rocks, especially this
time of year! It is a great view, even in winter (when I got the
box), though I imagine Fall must be a glorious sight from the top.

Kings Gap Environmental Center is also in the general area of Sunset
Rocks. "Following the Paw Prints" is there--I've never found it, so
I can't vouch for it.

Don't forget to visit Hanover in addition to York--there have been so
many additions to Hanover in the years since Silent Doug planted Mary
Ann's Furnace (and it's closer to Gettysburg, to boot, if time is an
issue).
