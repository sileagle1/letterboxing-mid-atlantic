New Experience

From: Victor E. Ramirez | Date: 2007-12-26 18:53:22 UTC
Abita and I had a new experience on our hike the other day and I
thought I'd share. We were pulled over by the police.

Parts of the Seneca Creek Greenway Trail (http://www.mc-
mncppc.org/trails/trails_maps/Seneca.shtm) pass near our house and
we're slowly hiking the whole thing, a bit at a time. Christmas Eve,
we started at a part near our home and headed in a direction we
hadn't been before. As we came to a point we had been to before
(letterboxing from the other direction, where we orignally turned
around), we decided to try to find a letterbox we hadn't found
before. Abita still had the clues for the box with her, so we kinda
split up looking for the hollow stump...or something. The landmark
mentioned in the clue, you know?

I heard a bit of a commotion from the hill above us, and I looked up
to see two of Montgomery County's finest, one of whom was losing her
footing and falling into a fence, while her partner asked if she was
alright. I would have laughed, except they were armed and I don't
like to consider myself stupid; instead, I just yelled out something
along the lines of, "Be careful, officers! There's lots of barbed
wire 'round here!"

She picked herself up and I saw she had a citation book in her hand,
and she yelled down at me, "You're wearing (blaze) orange--that's
good! You're not hunting are you, although I see you're not!" and I
confirmed we, indeed, were not. Abita later noted a trained officer
could probably tell my hiking pole wasn't a rifle, even if I hadn't
had one end stuck in the ground, and we speculated on what the heck
that was all about because they disappeared right after that.

My best guess is that someone saw some people in blaze orange
(namely, us) along the trail from their house (you can frequently
see houses thru the trees hiking these trails...stupid over-
development) and called the cops on us.

--Ratinabox

From: wood thrush | Date: 2007-12-26 21:03:22 UTC
Wow, what a story! I had a similar experience in '06 down Silver Spring way. I was trying for
the old "National Park Seminary" box, which had been planted on the incredibly decrepit and
spooky grounds of a former girls' school near Walter Reed hospital. [I believe currently this
property is being renovated for condos, etc.]

It was noontime on a weekday, when the place was at its least spooky, but that is not saying
much. I followed the clues but felt I was in some danger from the nature of the place, the
abandoned buildings, overgrowth, etc.

A couple of older teen boys ran past me at one point, hightailing it through all the vines, up
and down the hills, etc. Minutes later came the cops, several cars full. They questioned me
about whether I had seen any "activity." They had followed the teens, suspected drug dealers,
into the place---which as it turns out was a favorite hiding place for them.

At that point I got my butt out of there. By the way, I found the site of the box, and it was
long, long gone. The clue's still on LbNA, but believe me, the box no longer exists. I am glad
to see I was the last attempt on this one!

wood thrush
