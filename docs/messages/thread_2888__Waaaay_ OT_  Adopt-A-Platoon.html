Hi There.

In December I decided to put my money where my liberal pinko mouth is and adopt a platoon of Marines through Adopt-A-Platoon .org  This organization is run by mothers and other relatives of servicemen and women who find forward-serving platoons of all branches of the armed forces who might need a little extra personal and moral support while in Iraq.

I was fortunate to be assigned a platoon of Marines of Delta Co. 2nd AABN (Amphibious Assault Brigade) stationed in Fallujah.  If you would like to see them please check out my web site:  WinstonSound.com .  There is also a link to a video made by one of the Marines which shows just how deep in the sh*t these guys are.

I know many people - both pro-war and anti-war - feel a little helpless about the situation in Iraq and are looking for a way to help.  I know I was.  So this is what I decided to do while I ask our government to get them out as soon as possible.

So I am soliciting support from anybody interested in helping. There is information on my website  about items I am collecting to ship over to Iraq.  If you would like to contribute in any way, please let me know and we'll make arrangements to collect them.  Any type of support is appreciated.  Even if you would like to write a letter or email to be included with our shipments -  I am told letters from home are great for morale.

My contact with the platoon, MSgt. Reed, emailed yesterday that it just snowed in Fallujah and freaked everybody out.  It is a welcome change, he says, from summer when it was 143 degrees in the shade and they were patrolling in 75lbs of equipment and body armor.

Anyone who contributes (money, email, letter, spicy beef jerky, anything) will get the clue to my new WOM box. 

I was going to send a letterbox over and ask them to hide it as my first international box.  But as the biggest problem is IED (improvised explosive devices), a letterbox just might cost somebody his life.  So I will hide something here in tribute to the Marines of Delta Co.  Maybe something at the end of a long hike.

Thanks for your kind attention.

Natt-

My Dad was stationed in `Nam. Mom was home with seven young ones
under the age of 10. She learned not to let people know that Dad was
in `Nam, because it was not a popular war. She relied on the other
wives and her dear sister who came to live in California while Dad
was overseas (rest of family was in Pennsylvania). She has many
stories of tough times from those years. We made it through and Dad
made it home. Many were not as lucky or had a tough time adjusting
when they returned.

These men/women were not appreciated for doing their job. They didn't
necessarily want to do it (my dad was drafted into the military), but
it put food on the table and paid most bills and it was their job
that they did for their Country.

To this day my Dad always greets anyone with a `Nam shirt, hat, logo,
etc with a "Welcome Home Soldier" It always brings tears to Mom's
eyes.

Dad said if they could do what they were supposed to do; the war
would have not lasted as long! D*** Politicians!

I have had my oldest Niece and Niece's Husband in the current
conflict. They fortunately made it home safely.

Please support these guys/gals no matter what your position is in
politics.

Every year my oldest Brother's gift to the family is a donation to a
Veteran's group that sends phone cards to soldiers overseas. It is
one of the most appreciated gifts I receive. I remember talking to
Dad and having to say "over" on the phone when you finished a
sentence because it was "patched" through to us (no cells back then).

So send me your address (offline of course) and I'll send a

I remember this with my husband not that many years ago (feeling
old)! God love ham radio operators--they would connect with the ship

(otherwise satellite phone was something like $10 a minute). The ham
radio operator would call the family member and I'd always forget to
say "over".

New spouses and military members these days don't seem to fathom that
they are actually lucky to be able to speak with their spouses as
often as they can. This can cause it's own problems (for instance
spouses at home who burden the military member with things they don't
need to know about- ie plumbing problems etc- instead of just taking
care of the problem)

Kudos to you Natt for this! They need all the support they can get,
it just keeps getting harder everyday to hold their heads up when
some are on their third or fourth tour!

I sponsor a co-worker's reserve unit until they return in May and we
support his wife here at home. Another soldier my division at work
sponsored and wrote to was killed in November by a sniper attack. We
received his last letter to us a few weeks ago. Somber day, that...
