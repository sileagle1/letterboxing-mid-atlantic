From: Otis' Friends | Date: 2008-01-30 02:15:00 UTC
I find that the 40 degree mark seems to be the magic number with little ones.  Your mileage may vary.  Snacks and other bribery are also helpful.  As long as things aren't buried under ice, there's not necessarily any reason to wait until March or April if the conditions are right (but February tends to be brutal).
While you're starting out, you might want to try the city and county parks, which usually have paved or gravel paths.

Re: [lbma] Re: New to the group

From: Otis' Friends | Date: 2008-01-30 14:45:14 UTC
French Creek is on the rugged side.  Most of the paths are quite rocky and some of them have significant grades.  The reason there isn't as much mud is because of the rocks and because many of the trails aren't level enough to collect water.  My 5-year-old (now 6) has done OK on a few of them but we did enjoy a fine whine.  And, of course, when it's cold enough the mud freezes, anyway.
If you are close to French Creek you probably aren't that far from Reading - quite a few paved and gravel paths in some of the parks there, but some of them are quite steep.  You might also want to pop over to Lancaster County.  A few nice short ones close to Chester County.  Are you checking both lbna and Atlas Quest?

Re: New to the group

From: four.suits | Date: 2008-02-01 18:06:45 UTC
as a mom of 2 boys - 4&7, go whenever the weather is mild enough for
a hike. Remember the hiking will warm you all up & mittens & hats
help too. Last winter, I waited until the weather warmed up for some
local boxes that turned out to be wrapped in poison ivy & then they
were muggled before this winter. I also love that there are no
toys/germs being shared, so it's a great way to have a playdate when
your kid has that lingering cough - also a great way to introduce
lboxing to others (so you can explain the etiquette) - and the extra
kids help motivate each other to keep going (toward the next big tree
or the snack waiting in the car!)

Ridley Creek is great to take kids (& dogs), lots of choices, many
short hikes. chester springs has a couple in a great nature area
(check atlasquest).

As for the mud, after watching our kids & the keystone khans' walk
fully clothed into french creek's lake @ the alienteering gather this
summer, all I can say is, "it's good to be a kid." Pack boots &
sometimes a change of clothes & change b4 you get back in the
car. :)

enjoy! 4suits
