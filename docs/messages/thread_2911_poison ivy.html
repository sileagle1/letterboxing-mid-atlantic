I will never forget when I took a community plant ecology course in the winter in California.  Two professors shared teaching the course:  one who taught us to identify trees by their bark and one who conveniently ignored that it was winter and taught us to ID plants based on leaf shape.  One of the students in my group insisted on plucking and carrying a stick back to the prof with us (he was about a 2 mile hike away) despite protests from the rest of us that it was indeed poison ivy.  Needless to say, she paid for the folly of not believing the rest of us!

On the plus side, at least in winter there is less exposed to the poison ivy.
