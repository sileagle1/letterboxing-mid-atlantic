Oh yes it does! We've played letterboxing BINGO in the Great Lakes
region in 2006, 2007 and now again in 2008. The first straight line
BINGO is usually called after 30-40 words. The first full card BINGO
normally takes about 3.5 months. We always start on January 1, so
the game does in fact take us through the whole winter, ending
sometime in April.

However we play with only 125 total words to choose from, and a few
more general terms. The Mid-Atlantic version has 155 possible words,
and less general terms, so I think it will be an even SLOWER game.
However maybe SSB will give us some bonus days, with perhaps an extra
word on Valentine's Day (hint, hint).

From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2008-02-11 19:52:10 UTC
It all depends upon what one enjoys getting out of the hobby. Some
people enjoy the challenge that comes with a particularly mind-
twisting puzzle and an exceedingly well hidden treasurebox. Some
people like something straightforward.

As to whether a box is worthy, I guess that is in the perspective of
any individual or group of boxers that seek. Many of the locations
are unique in some special way, be it the beauty of the spot or the
backstory involved.

It all boils down to whether one gets that sense of accomplishment for
having put the grey matter to work to having figured one of these
things out and gone out and gotten it. Sometimes it takes more than
one person to collaborate on these things (that whole diversity of
thought thing).

Speaking just for myself (althought some may share my
opinion), "Bigarren!"

Cheers,

LB
