Box Protocol ?

From: sethompson2 | Date: 2008-02-25 17:11:31 UTC
Hello everyone,

As I am still learning the ropes I have a question to pose. On our
outing yesterday we found one of the boxes basically destroyed by
weather. The top had come off, the baggies were ripped and the logbook
ruined. All that was left was the stamp and ink pad. Rather than
leave it there in that state I took it home with me and posted a note
to the placer on Atlas Quest. I haven't heard back yet. Should I have
left it there and then contacted them? Or should I just repair it and
return it myself? As a footnote, the 2nd box in the series was not in
much better shape. The baggies were ripped, top leaking and stamp
missing. I was able to close that one back up pretty well and informed
the placer about this one too.

Thanks!

There are a many answers to this question. Personally, I would have
tried and repaired as best I could on the trail and then contacted the
placer when I got home. It is not my box to remove, even with the best
of intentions. Just remember that sometimes people are out of contact
and don't log on all the time. If the box is close, you can offer to do
repairs for the placer if they are not able or willing to do them yourself.

Re: [lbma] Box Protocol ?

From: Ed Bindler | Date: 2008-02-25 19:21:10 UTC
Hi,

I've come across some the same way. I try to repair them as best I can.
I have gotten into the habit of carrying extra freezer ziplock with me. I
haven't gotten to the point of extra logs though. I also try to have some
paper towels to "attempt" to dry out soggy books.

Sometimes they are just beyond the beyond. I came across a stamp in
Waldwick NJ, the container and bags were in absolutely horrible shape. I
could only stamp my book. The logbook was SO soggy that it was crumbling
into mush.

There wasn't anything I could do but write the owner. It is an old box
and I was sorry that I couldn't leave my entry. I didn't get a response
but hope it he/she was able to repair it.

What series of stamps, can I ask? If I haven't done it, I will try to
remember to bring replacements with me.

Ed B.

I don't bring extra logs, but a few index cards with

out there. It still had good pages in it once it was
dry.

From: Stacy Becker | Date: 2008-02-25 22:20:34 UTC
Re: [lbma] Box Protocol ? I have stood at a box MANY times trying to decide what to do. In 2 cases – the boxes were so small that I had to remove the wet and in 1 case frozen log book to get note cards in.  In another case the boxer that planted it live several states away and I just decided that I would take the chance and replace the whole thing. In all cases – everyone has been super nice about me removing their logbook. I always dry them out and mail them to the owner on my stamp. I only do it if I really feel the need and can replace it. Honestly I would hope someone would do the same for me, cause I can’t always get out right away to replace things.
