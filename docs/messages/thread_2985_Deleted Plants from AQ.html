Deleted Plants from AQ

From: Nathan Brown | Date: 2008-02-26 19:35:33 UTC
I was looking at someone's profile on AQ today. It said they had 152
planted boxes, but when I looked at their logbook, I found that all but
two boxes had been retired. I thought, that isn't right, those boxes
should not be counted, in my thinking anyway, as plants any longer.
Sure, they were planted at one time, but they are no longer there, no
longer being watched over, and no longer being found, so should they
really count toward PFX?

Now, it really is up to the individual person, it really only matters to
them, but I decided to go ahead and delete a bunch of my own boxes which
are retired. Anyone that had found them would have already logged them,
since they were all retired some time ago, so I decided out they go.

Just thought I would let anyone interested know, and maybe spark some
discussion as well.

From: sewsowbizzy | Date: 2008-02-28 22:52:53 UTC
As WI Hiker pointed out - leaving a clue in place kinda inhibits
someone else from planting in that area. Although your clue indicates
"MISSING" in the title (that is always appreciated) the clue doesn't
indicate whether you plan to re-plant or if the location is up for
grabs, i.e "Box will NOT be replanted". Besides, with "MISSING" in the
title, I would think most folks wouldn't bother to look at the clue so
they wouldn't know about this lovely garden and your intention is for
naught.

HOWEVER, on LB.org an archived box will no longer be available thru
SEARCH and those who only record their finds once a decade will not be
able to record that find once it is archived. Those who had promptly
recorded the find will maintain their F count, but they won't see it
any longer on their list of finds/attempts and might be easily
confused?!?!

In which case I think box clues should be clearly marked (MIA or
MISSING) in the title and the clue text should clearly indicate at the
beginning if the box will be or not be replanted.

And missing boxes should only be archived 10 years after they went
missing!!
