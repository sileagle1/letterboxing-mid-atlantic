For those who don't listen to Car Talk on NPR, this week's puzzler:
   
The math class is studying perfect squares.  The teacher gives them a formula for determining if a given number is a perfect square, and assigns each student a number.  The assignment is to use the formula, NOT their calculators, to prove whether their number is a perfect square or not.

Johnny comes home & tells his family about the assignment.  He is somewhat upset--his assigned number is 334,912,740,121,562.  But his older brother just laughs.  "Your teacher gave you an EASY number," he says. "I can tell you right now whether it's a perfect square or not.  What's it worth to you?"

The puzzle, of course, is--is it a perfect aquare? And how do you know?
