Missing and Abandoned Boxes

From: The Doubtful Guests | Date: 2004-08-23 18:27:06 UTC
I am trying to think of a way to track missing and abandoned boxes. 
 
I am increasingly frustrated here in the DC/VA/MD area by missing boxes that are not marked as such.  Nor does it help to attract people to the hobby. 
 
When the owners are responsive it doesn't matter.  We all have trouble with missing boxes.  I know that boxes are often reported missing when they aren't really.  I know that owners cannot rush out and check them immediately. 
 
But some boxes around here I know have been missing for many months or a couple years.  Abandoned by their owners they never get marked. 
 
I would be willing to keep a file posted in LBMA's file section of missing reports in the DC/VA/MD area.  It could note a date a box was reported missing, whether confirmed or not, and if it was reported missing multiple times.  Maybe note that the placer has been notified but did not respond. 
 
One DC box had been missing for almost two years and continued to attract visiting victims.  I finally asked to adopt it just so I could mark it missing and eventually delete it.
 
I am hoping that someday the LbNA web site could have features for this issue much like the geocaching site does now. 
 
Any ideas out there?
 
Scarab of the Doubtful Guests

From: Judy B | Date: 2004-08-23 21:48:14 UTC
The LB-usa has a database to post boxes up for adoption -- not much
action tho, probably folks who don't bother to maintain wouldn't
bother to post either. However, starting with the LBMA area such a
database - all members able to edit - could allow for a box to be
named, seeker ID, date not found. Since database sorts on first
column naturally, maybe that should be a State Column. I'm not sure
if it then sorts to second column (county?), third column(city?)
etc. And I might suggest including a Post# column (LBMA#375 or
LBusa#44368), that way database data can be kept to a very minimun
(more lines per screen) and the details could be in a Post. It is
quick enough to look up a Post # in the messages.
