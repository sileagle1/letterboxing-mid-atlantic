Re: FCPL Letterbox Challenge

From: brendan_hickey13 | Date: 2008-03-21 00:32:25 UTC
We drove to Delmar to get this box on the day before the Polar Bear
Plunge. As soon as we entered the library, we got a strong, "You are
strangers and you don't belong" vibe from the staff.

It did not take long to blow up.

With one of the staff watching, I tried to use the catalog computer a
few times, figured to need that to find the book. I finally realized
that the computer was off but hey, Saturday morning, rural area,
quiet winter day, just hasn't been used yet.

So I turned it on. Big, big mistake.

The other librarian threw a tantrum that would give reason for
applause to any 3 year-old in a department store. She was at least a
half-century too old to act like she did, but the upshot was that the
computer was broken and so I might have had some problems with my
search, which might have inconvenienced me, I suppose - but we really
had no right to be in the public library in Delmar, being strangers.

I took this tirade with calm and good manners because I did not want
to be thrown out of the library - came to find the box - or to
jeopardize the placement.

She then offered us the general Internet computer, but she knew that
we could not access them because we had no library cards or accounts
because, yes, we were strangers.

We searched that library for the next 75 minutes (not even close to
our record for futile library searches) while waiting for Sheriff
Bubba to show up. The staff watched us from their little control
booth.

We finally decided to ask these elite professionals, the
intelligentia, nay, the illuminati of Delmar (is the sarcasm coming
through?) so that we could find this box and be done with it. When
they learned why we came to their town, they eased up a little and
pointed to another corner of the library, but we still could not find
the box.

I emailed the placer, who turned out to be TheDelmarLibrary, a few
times after we returned to safety and home but received no reply.
The box was probably part of a summer reading program and LBNA has
since moved the listing to the basement.

Delmar...Don't Bother
