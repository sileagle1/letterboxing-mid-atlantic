June gathering announcement

From: Indigo Vulture | Date: 2008-03-27 10:30:44 UTC
Let me take this opportunity to announce:

"O" Boxer Where Art Thou?
AKA:  "Do Not Seek the Treasure!"
An Orienteering-Themed Letterbox Gathering

Location:  Hickory Run State Park, White Haven, PA
Date/Time:  Saturday, June 21, 2008, roughly 10:00 am - 4:00 pm
Hosts:  Lightnin' Bug & Indigo Vulture

Come to one of the largest and most beautiful state parks in PA to test out your map skills, and find some wonderful stamps.  Boxes will be planted near controls along the permanent orienteering course at Hickory Run.  You'll need to get a park orienteering map (we're still working out details on that), and simple clues will lead you from the controls to the boxes.

The pavilion at the Sand Spring Lake has been rented.  We'll gather there.  The orienteering course is a short drive away from there.  The plan for lunch will be pot luck.

Here's the really fun part:  All boxes will be themed around the Coen Brothers' movie O Brother, Where Art Thou?  As I recall, the possibility of a gathering based around that movie had some traction in the past.  I've already got some ideas for stamps, but we would love to get stamps from many different people.  If you'd like to carve a stamp for it, email me off list.  I'll need all stamps by the end of May.

That's about it for now.  Watch for more info.  There should be an Atlas Quest event page up in the near future.

Indigo Vulture

Hickory Run actually has three different courses on its orienteering
brochure. One beginner, one intermediate, and one advanced. The
French Creek course (the one Alienteering uses) is a beginner course.
So it depends on which course the placer chooses to use.

Mapsurfer #10 uses the advanced orienteering course at Hickory Run
for its clue and it is legitamitely advanced. I am fairly experienced
in orienteering and I got misplaced a couple of times. I don't
remember my exact time to complete the course (I may have written it
in the log book), but I remember it being quite poor.

I don't have the brochure in front of me, but I recall that the
beginner course was just as straight forward as the one at French
Creek, and the intermediate course was at the easier end of what
would be considered intermediate.

Your milage may vary.

Mike
