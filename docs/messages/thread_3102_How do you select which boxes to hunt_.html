Re: [lbma] How do you select which boxes to hunt?

From: Katrina Lustofin | Date: 2008-04-03 11:41:59 UTC
Oh, the idea of having boxes as close as 15 miles away - you have no idea how wonderful that sounds.  If I only boxed that close to me, I'd never box.

Before I moved, I definitely had favorite placers whose boxes I would eagerly anticipate;  I haven't found enough letterboxes here to develop a sense yet of who has really awesome hikes and who doesn't. 

Mostly I have two methods for choosing letterboxes.  The first is to find boxes along a travel route.  So, for instance, when I lived in Illinois and would be driving to Michigan or NY to see family, I would find clues for letterboxes at a nice stopping point along the way.  We found some really nice parks this way and it is always good to get out of the car and move around a bit.  The second method is if I'm home with nothing planned for the weekend, I find clues for those boxes closest to home - which currently means about a 45 minute drive.   Then I select for maximum bang for my buck - or the presence of multiple boxes in one location, preferably with multiple planters.

Once I've selected an area, that's when I get choosy.  I generally don't go after boxes if I know the stamp is store-bought (what can I say, I'm a snob - hand carved are so much more interesting to me, even the really crude ones).  I don't tend to go after urban or drive-by boxes because they aren't nearly as much fun for the dogs and if I don't bring the dogs, I have to carry stuff myself.  I prefer hikes of a decent length, or at least the option to keep hiking in that area after I've found the box.  And now that I bought myself a season pass for DE state parks, I'll probably show a marked preference for boxes in those parks :)

Katy

I don't... they seem to select me. It's like an obsessive compulsive
disorder. There I am scanning AQ looking at recent posts and seeing
