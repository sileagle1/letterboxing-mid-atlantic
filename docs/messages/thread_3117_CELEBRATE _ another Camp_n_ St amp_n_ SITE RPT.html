From: sewsowbizzy | Date: 2008-04-10 13:03:51 UTC

Road Trip!  Headed over to Tuckahoe SP yesterday to check out the campsites and plot a few plants. 

It should be noted that the Bay Bridge is under construction.  They have scheduled lane closures that could back up traffic  EZ Pass always helps.  Here is a web site you might want to book mark for future travels over the bridge.

 http://www.baybridge.com/

  CLICK on "Drivers Toolbox" "Traffic Advisories"

The campsites are nice and not too far apart (0.3 mi loop).  They only have small grated fire pits but there is a fire ring in the area where the overflow parking/canoe launch is.  Only I couldn't figure out why you would want to put a canoe in the marsh!  Parking is still limited there as you have to squeeze in between the trees.  I asked at Park Offc and didn't get any objection to our using that fire ring for central cooking/gathering.  Will need folks to bring some folding tables as there are no picnic tables in this area and of course your own camp chairs. 

NOTE: No outside firewood is allowed to be brought in.  Park Offc sells wood  however, at the Dec Tyler gather someone brought scrap pine boards from their home projects/crafts.  It burns faster than logs, but still if you want to clear out that workshop. . . there isn't any risk of the evil beetle.

Of the three boxes that are listed on LB.org for Tuckahoe SP only the two 2007 ones are there and in good order.  "Underground Railroad" from 2002 is either missing or the clues are way off.  All the sign posts on the Lore of the Land Nature Trail appear to be newer and probably in different locations.

I'll be posting clues early for the boxes I plot  but the stamp/log won't be in place until May 30.

 ssb
