Burholme Mansion Seasonal Stamps Box
This box is in a great hilltop location in a 68 acre park, with 30
mile views on clear days.
Take time to enjoy the Ryerss Family Legacy.
The stamps, & location will change seasonally.
Start at foot of red sliding board. Walk 345 Magnetic to 4 trees
short of brush land. Check base of inner tree for stamp. Then 105
Magnetic about 70 feet to a rock in grass on left . Old Gray a
favored horse is buried here, the marker was "lost" in 1986. His
painting is in the library. Check out the small animal pet cemetery
on top of hill at side door of mansion.
WHEELCHAIR ACCESIBLE: The box is on ground, the chair will need to
cross about 600 feet of park grass.
The Mansion, Museum & Library on top of the hill are opened & FREE
almost all weekends on Friday, Saturday & Sunday 10AM to 4 PM.
Map & webpage: http://www.fairmountpark.org/RyerssMuseum.asp
Museum Photo Tour Link: http://ryerssmuseum.org/
Wheelchair accessible, grassy & paved areas. Playground. Rest
facilities until 4PM off season weekends & longer hours all days in
summer, (accessible).
Location: 7370 Central Ave
Philadelphia, PA (United States)
RYERSS'LARGE ANIMAL INFIRARY
8670 Krewstown Rd ***
Philadelphia, PA (United States)
8600 Krewstown Road NE Philly 19115. Yahoo Map shows right Driveway
location@ 8670. Driveway is NE of Creek & Railroad underpass on
Krewstown Rd. Google map somewhat off. Take road to Right away from
The Krewstown House to the stable area. About 300 yards. NO REST
FACLTIES AT THIS SITE. Wheelchair accessible, grassy & paved areas.
Folks in a wheelchair may need a friend or a long arm to reach box
itself. No CARS on Grass. Park near stable end of first horse ring
(corral)
The hike length assumes you walk around this great hilltop location &
look at things. The stable by the second ring was a police barn in
the 1980s, The park tree crews & Rangers have offices here now. The
hilltop is used for equine & other recreation. No horses live here
now. But I see horses & dogs on most visits.
Map & Web page: http://www.fairmountpark.org/KrewstownRoadHouse.asp
Robert Ryerss was President of SPCA & the Ryerss Infirmary. The SPCA
placed many water troughs for horses around the city, they were stone
& engraved with the words " A kind man is kind to his animal".
The Ryerss Farm is a retirement home for older & rescued horses now
in Chester County, it moved from this area in 1955. Link:
http://ryerssfarm.com/
The Park boundary to the NE is Meeting House Rd. (dirt). The Ryerss
Infirmary extended NE from that Rd. to Corner of Bloomfield Ave. &
Verree Rd. It was over 200 acres. It is full of 1950's homes now.
You should look under (in) a water trough for a magnetic letter box.
Rehide in shadow with camouflage facing out.
On leaving you should check out the Krewstown Road House Built
between 1730 and 1750, this house is an excellent example of rural
colonial architecture and one of the oldest documented residences. To
the NE on Krewstown Rd. is the 300+ year old Baptist Meeting House &
Cemetery.
To the S. of Krestown Rd. is Pennypack Creek in the park, with
parking & scenic views of 2 historic bridges, one 90 feet high. Enjoy.
