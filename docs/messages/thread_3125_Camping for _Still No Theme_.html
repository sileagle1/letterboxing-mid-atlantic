Camping for "Still No Theme"

From: Nathan Brown | Date: 2008-04-16 16:38:14 UTC
A Good news, bad news moment:

I was over at Washington Crossing State Park today, and unfortunately
the sites that were booked for the gathering's weekend are now confirmed
and paid for, so there will be no camping at the park.

However;

I talked to Mark (PM Jets) over there about it, and about the idea of
camping just up the river at Bull's Island, and the fact that they
needed two nights to reserve a site. He made a call over to a friend at
the park, and they said they may be able to reserve two sites, possibly
together, just for Saturday night. They will get back to me on this
very soon. So there may be camping after all!

In other news, and this is the bad news park, as you know WCSP is one of
the park on the list for cuts. Mark's job will be cut if the Corzine's
plan to cut the parks go through. If you are in the area of any of the
state parks in Joisey, please stop by and sign the petition there to
keep these parks open and operating. The parks are very kind to
letterboxers on the other side of the Great Divide, and they allow us to
plant pretty much at will, with a few reasonable exceptions. So please
help out.

Also, there will be a rally at the capitol in Trenton on April 23, noon
to two. If you are free, please come out and show your support.

--
