From: Mary Erickson | Date: 2008-04-27 21:47:06 UTC
I wasn't there, but I did spend Saturday with my noxer
husband at Codorus State Park in PA, and it was a
great day of boxing. We also saw a beautiful box
turtle, 4 sets of Canada geese parents with their
oh-so-cute goslings, and only six ticks between us.
Five on my husband...the tick magnet.

But my favorite critter was when I set aside a bunch
of old sticks off a box at the base of a tree, then
lifted the box up, and there staring at me, wondering
what just happened to his roof, was a little deer
mouse. He froze for ten seconds, then scampered off.
The box had been untouched all winter...I was sorry to
disturb his Tupperware-roofed home!

The best box for me was one that we had the most
trouble with. We had puzzled and puzzled over
matching the terrain to the clues, and my sweet noxer
was ready to move on. In deference to marital harmony
I acquiesced, but...oh well, you know that *I* would
have been happy to backtrack a mile and try it all
over again! However, the very next box we looked for
happened to circle us RIGHT BACK to the troublesome
place, and with renewed vigor [and absent the pesky
fishermen] we gave it one more try, and VOILA! right
where my beloved noxer said it absolutely wasn't. Tee
hee hee. Oh, those suspiciously laid piles of
sticks...

Mommo
