"Abandoned boxes"

Those boxes and clues for which the owner no longer even answers
questions about them.

This is a long standing and difficult problem in letterboxing.

Over on the LbNA letterboxing.org site this became a huge problem.
If someone wrote to the website about an abandoned box, often an
angry complaint, it was the webmasters who had to deal with the
problem. As the hobby aged and more and more boxes were abandoned,
they got tired of answering angry questions about them. So, one year
they archived all clues where the owner could not be contacted.

This was when adopting boxes came into being. Many of
these "orphaned" boxes were good boxes still enjoyed by many. But
LbNA rightfully insisted that someone had to take responsibility for
them. A lot of boxes were adopted by others willing to maintain
them. If the owner returned to the hobby the adopters would happily
give them back.

A letterbox, and the clues for it, are the property of the placer.
But the webmaster is responsible for the website and takes the rap
if there is no one else to be found.

Scarab

P.S. There are still a lot of abandoned and archived letterboxes out
there in the wild, unadopted and unlisted. LbNA has an archived site
with the clues still posted. They call it the "basement" or
something. Some boxers have fun tracking these down to see if they
are still there. Finding a forgotten box is a big thrill.
