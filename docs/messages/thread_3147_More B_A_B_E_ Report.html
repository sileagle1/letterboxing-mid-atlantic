More B.A.B.E. Report

From: June McAllister | Date: 2008-04-28 15:39:35 UTC
As it turned out, there were actually more than the 10 spaces that were posted about because you could park on the edge of the road. *And* as it turned out, at least as far as the impression I got, there was no parking problem because it wasn't a big gathering like I expected. I don't know if not everyone showed up or there was no problem just due to people coming and going. And you did have to drive to most of the boxes not at the pavillion unless you had all the time in the world to hike around and do them all in a very leisurely fashion, which we did not. And many of them were a hike of some kind after driving to the location. But it seemed to me like a regular sized gathering, not a so called big "national anniversary" gathering. I have a tendency to get overwhelmed when there are too many people and too much of a stamping frenzy and I never felt like that while I was there. It was a nice gathering with a very good, friendly feeling to it.
 
I guess we were at the pavillion from around 1???.... untill around 3???? We had driven from Roanoke and then did 3 boxes in Hot Springs before we got there. After we left the pavillion we went and did the event boxes and only got 8 of them, mostly because of the time factor and the fact that we were very tired. I have an issue with hiding the event stamps all over the place but that was her choice. People coming from a long distance had time and traveling issues that I don't think were taken into consideration. People who came and stayed for 2-3-4 days had time to do it all with no problem but not everyone can do that with a so called national gathering. But it's over and done with now. She has the right to do what she wants to. All in all it was a good gathering and I really enjoyed meeting some very nice people from a different part of the country. I also finally met the Gallent Rogue himself!!
 
And it never rained on Saturday as far as I know. If there were any scattered sprinkles we never saw them.
 
We went back and did Max Patch the next morning before leaving the area. We had stayed in Newport Tenn, about 26 miles away and did the comfortable bed, steak and crab cakes for dinner at Ruby Tuesday's boxing thing as opposed to the hard, cold ground boxing thing. ++A boxer does not a camper have to be. There's room for those who enjoy sleeping on the cold hard ground and for those that enjoy a nice mattress.++
 
Max Patch is an easy box to get  once you get up to it, but the drive willllllll kill ya!! LOL But it's BEAUTIFUL!!!!! Then we stopped in Bristol, Virginia and did two boxes before the rain started there. We didn't have time to wait till it stopped and do the other two. So by the time 4survivors and I got back to DC and I took the train baaaaaaaaaaaak here, it was midnight. So guess who took advantage of being a civil servant and called in sick today?! God I love not working in the private sector where you actually have to go to work every day!! LOL
 
Did I mention that North Carolina is extraordarily beautiful? North Carolina is extraordinarily beautiful! I hope I can go back down there and see more of it.
 
Missmoon

From: BullDawg | Date: 2008-04-28 18:00:02 UTC
ahh...B.A.B.E......what a perfect weekend it was...The weather was amazing.  The area and scenic drive was nothing short of breathtaking.  The campground, dare I say, was as good as any I've been to, especially with the river sounds all night.  The hospitality of the locals was a breath of fresh air.  The event organizers and all that I met were incredible. 
 
I'm gonna put this out there and say that neither the event nor its location was "foolish" in anyway.   All the concerns about crowds, and parking was quite foolish itself.  I've had harder times parking at all my other events I've been too...Ever try parking in Old Towne Alexandria, or Manassas?....sheesh!  The parking was plentiful..totally amazing!  Awesome job setting this entire event up!..kudos, and nothing but respect for you all. 
 
Yes...I do recall an event box, there....yes, Six Secrets.  It's quite the puzzle.  It was a pleasure to be apart of it with great friend "X marks the spot.  He is leaving this coast soon, and it was our last meeting for some time.  Either way it was great to brainstorm with him in collaboration with that box and 4 secrets....I'll mss him, although Trailing Emerson and I showed him something that even shocked him...I'm not sure he'll ever be the same again...either way, its in our hands for now, here in VA..doing some final touches on it.
 
Thanks to Mountain Scorpia, and others involved in setting this event up.  Thanks to all those I chatted with, and a special thanks to all those that stayed up, and the main headquarters cabin.  Gallant Rogue, thanks for the beer, and other beverages.  We need to make that a more often occurance, as we are miles apart up in VA.
 
It was great finally meeting Dixie...what a sweetheart of a lady. Who could not enbrace the kindness in her? This hobby I brought so many great folks into my life, and that is what I will always take from it.
 
Thanks again!
BullDawg
