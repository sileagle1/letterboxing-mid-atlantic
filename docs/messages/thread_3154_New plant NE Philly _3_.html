The Flying Horse
This short walk will take you along 300 year old roads, past a toll
house for the Bustleton Turnpike & into a 102 year old park which was
almost all industrialized area in the 1800s.
This box & stamp were at a nearby location for Burholme Event of
4/5/08
Park on Bustleton Ave. or Strahle St near the Pegasus Riding
Academy. They provide a therapeutic Riding Program for many
illnesses. Their link for website & map:
http://www.fairmountpark.org/Pegasus.asp
The old building on the corner was a toll house for the turnpike, it
also offered accommodations for travelers, and horses could be
accommodated in the stable behind the house.

4,000 FOOT WALK(shorter walk route below): As you walk NE on the old
Bustleton Turnpike you will pass the newer building used for the
riding program. This Parkland was by the Fairmount Park Commission &
the Philly Water Dept. to make a buffer zone removing industry from
the city's water supplies.

Then Right on Axe Factory Rd. (the 2 lane paved driveway (FPC Park to
R. & 1990s McHomes to left)) this road was authorized 300 years ago
as a short cut from Lower Dublin Rd. (Welsh Rd.) to the Turnpike. It
was unnamed for a long time then called Factory Rd. then named Axe
Factory Rd. There are 2 blocks of 1950s homes on the east end near
Welsh Rd & Old Aston Rd. intersection. The rest is park with paved
bike path along creek, & this section of horse path & driveway as you
will see it.

Pass the horse rings, & fronts of houses,
pass backs of homes & yards, to end of last yard & fence on left.
See the SILVER POLE on L. berm & SILVER TREE on R. berm, follow line
from pole through tree 35 steps (from tree) to next silver metal pole.

R. @ 45 about 20 feet is very gnarly tree with a mostly not gnarly
branch, 45 to L.@ 15 feet is a broken tree trunk 2 foot above ground
w/ a live branch growing straight up. 7' left of straight up live
branch inside the horizontal trunk under bark is the home of the
Flying Horse.
Lots of park users in area; try not to attract attention & rehide
well.
Kindly report finds & any problems via AQ. Good Hunting to all!
The Axe Factory Rd. is about 9,000 feet end to end if you want to
follow it. It's a nice walk that gets better as you go SE. Cross the
large creek on steel bridge, R. on paved bike path is Axe Fac. Rd.
Past highway overhead, pass path to L. @ small bridge. When you cross
the stone bridge (medium sized 60') over the Axe Factory Run (creek)
go straight uphill to park limit & homes on street to Welsh Rd.
1000' FOOT WALK TO FLYING HOUSE: Drive NE on Bustleton Ave. to R.
on Bergen St. to end @ Large St.
Park. Enter park along yard fence to R., to dirt road @ berm road
shoulder. Start from Silver POLE & TREE in clue above. 4-30-08
