New to posting....

From: Amanda Sellers | Date: 2008-04-30 13:44:17 UTC
Hey all...
I've been looking in on these posts for a while, and figured there is no time like the present to throw my two cents in.
 
I guess, I should first say introduce myself.  So, hello...I'm a Marylander who started boxing a year or so ago.  I have started very slowly, (only a box a month, I'd say) but now I'm starting to really love the hobby -- the beautiful stamps, the great places it introduces me to -- and I'm trying to step up my game.
 
I think I agree with Mommo.  I started off at LBNA, then learned of AQ.  AQ was great for the profiles, logs, etc, but after seeing all this extra stuff -- postals, LTC's, -- I have to admit I was scared off a bit.  It is overwhelming when all I want to do is go take a hike.  But now I see in this list a sense of, ahem, *strong dislike* for AQ.  I can get behind it, but as a relative newcomer, I'm worried that there will be no way for a newbie like me to meet, and learn, from pro's like you all.  Because I have no chance (at this point) of finding the mapsurfer's of the lb world, so I guess I'm just part of the herd at the moment.
moooo....lead me to better pastures, people....moooooo.  =)
Mandy of
Gaurdians of the Rose

Welcome!

I think that for as much as some people try to streamline the hobby
and make rules, there are still no rules and you can do with it what
you enjoy, what makes you most comfortable, and so on. I've been
boxing about 5 years now, and I've only found about 100 boxes, but I
don't think there's a correlation between F count and quality of the
letterboxer.

Just have fun with it :)

Anybody is "worthy" of whatever they set their mind to and what is

> And I'm really not trying to be snarky here, just asking a question.

And it is a good question.

I think I need to clarify my thinking here a bit. All of the drive-bys
and short walks and little boxes are fine, and they certainly have a
place. Heck, I search them out myself, but I do not get the same joy
out of them as I do going for some of the longer hikes, or figuring out
some of the mysteries that are out there.

You don't have to be "hard-core" to go out and find the "good stuff."
Everyone can enjoy it, and I don't think anyone here restricts the boxes
that they place. Sometime clues are hidden, but if you are clever you
can usually find them. And the good stuff is all in a matter of how you
look at it. For some folks it is a long hike, or a good puzzle, or a
nice stamp, or simply to find as many boxes as one can.

I guess I am just lamenting the fact that so many folks only seem to go
for the short, easy stuff, and don't even give some of the more creative
stuff a second look. I look at people's profiles and I find them rife
with the easy boxes, the little boxes, and very little in the way of
some of the more difficult stuff. I find those boxes and simple keep
seeing the usual suspects logged into them. I think that is what I am
trying to get at.

Everyone that sets their mind to it is worthy of finding these boxes.
