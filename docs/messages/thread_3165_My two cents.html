My two cents

From: Heather Siple | Date: 2008-05-02 03:21:16 UTC
I've been mulling over this whole conversation and came to a couple of counter points:
 
1) Posting public comments on AQ -- I set mine to moderate and figured I'd see what happened.  It has been my experience that very little gets posted on guestbooks, save by spammers and friends who want to make the site look important, so I expect that, at least until the spammers break in, no one is going to bother with a public comment anyway.  However, As I took a loooong hike into the woods yesterday, I thought about one of the harder boxes I've found.  The placer has retired, but the boxes are still around, mostly.  One may have been removed recently because of construction on its long-derelict home.  Telling the placer is pointless, but I can't let anyone else in the community know that it is not longer around, either.  Would be nice to have a way to let people know, before they tear their hair out over a box that doesn't exist anymore, that it is MIA for good.
 
2) It is not the length of the hike, but how you enjoy it.  Sometimes I need a really long hike, but most of the time I have the kids with me and they have very short legs yet.  I would have been thrilled to find a drive-by on our weekend outing last week, because I had to take frequent stretches for my broken body, but there were none to be hunted.  There seems to be plenty of room for library boxes, and they do not require hiking at all.  Sometimes ya just gotta go out and solve a puzzle, no matter how bad the weather is.
 
3) And, finally, in defense of AQ's search system, I found a zillion clues on AQ for French Creek tonight with no trouble at all, but had a devil of a time searching on LBNA because the park straddles county lines!!!!
 
Heather Siple

Good points, Heather, thanks.

I guess what I don't understand is--if you're going to have your clues all on your own website (like Nathan, Rich, & Mike), what's the difference between providing a link to your clues through LBNA and providing one through AQ?  OK, I've heard "I don't like all the bells & whistles," but--who said you had to use them?  Don't put any icons on the box; if it's not a mystery just put in the town, county & state; click on the buttons that disable ratings & blue diamonds & comments.  If there's too much prattle on the AQ boards, don't read them.

Suzanne

From: Randy Hall | Date: 2008-05-02 04:36:28 UTC
Hopefully everyone celebrated in the appropriate way, or
at least found what they were looking for. For those of
us unfortunately and unavoidably trapped by the mundane,
there is always the next cycle, or a future sabbat, for
which plans involving my friends HD and GM, among others,
are already being made.

And, of course, for collectors of small tokens with David
Lynch's three favorite colors, this major sabbat affects
a changing of the rules, as we know. Perhaps the next
major sabbat will affect another change, only the Wheel
knows.

So as not to be accused of intellectual preening by friends
of the father and other charlatans, we'll keep it brief; its
been a long day --

> perhaps I'm not "hard core" enough yet to be considered
> worthy of the good stuff? And I'm really not trying to
> be snarky here, just asking a question.

Some web site(s) require you to log dozens if not hundreds
of finds of boxes you may not be interested in to get to
the "good stuff" that you may be interested in. Obviously,
that is their right and their prerogative, tho those of us
who actually celebrate beltane obviously find that idea
somewhere between silly and preposterous. So, for _that_
"good stuff", you're up against a wall, I'm afraid. The
solution is to write an http client, or download one, to
fake out said web site(s), much easier than logging 100
drive-bys when you don't even believe in logging.

OTOH, the other "good stuff" or "not so good stuff", as
the case may be, generally doesn't have that restriction.
I mean, it wouldn't be "good stuff" if it did, now would
it? The good news is that you are in control of your own
worthiness. There are no artificial or unrelated barriers
and other annoying constraints. That would be my
definition of "good stuff", even if the actual "stuff"
turns out to be "lame".

Good luck.
Randy
Class of '98
