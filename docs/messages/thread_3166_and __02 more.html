3) And, finally, in defense of AQ's search system, I found a zillion clues on AQ for French Creek tonight with no trouble at all, but had a devil of a time searching on LBNA because the park straddles county lines!!!!
 
This is why having the two sites is very helpful. Yes, they overlap, but, sometimes they don't. I would ask that those of you that threatened to remove your clues from AQ rethink this.
AQ has certainly morphed into the 'all things for all people' site - perhaps those of you who are premier members or have an ear of the powers that be could suggest that the site not be so much. I would love to see just Letterboxes and gatherings on one site (for the purists) and the other etherial pursuits for those that have more time on their hands. While these other things such as LTC's, virtual boxes, postal boxes, etc. can make for a more interesting hobby, many of us are purists and just enjoy a good jaunt in the woods, etc.
My husband and I put our clues on LBNA, because we didn't have our own website and that was what there was. There are others who started 'boxing when there was an AQ and in fact that was all they knew. We eventually listed our letterboxes there so that everyone would have a chance to find the 'boxes. That's why we hide them, right? I know that we don't take hours finding just the right spot, the right stamp image and check and double-check the clues so that the 'box just sits somewhere looking alot like someone's lost lunch.
