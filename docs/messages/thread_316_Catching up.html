From: Cyclonic07@aol.com | Date: 2004-08-27 01:16:55 UTC
Well, I have been away, and have been having computer problems so I have been a bit out of the loop for the last few weeks.  So, I am going to try to catch up a bit here.
 
Doug - Fantastic site.  Great work, it is very comprehensive and has some great information on there.
 
Irene - I am glad Carman is OK.  No one got seriously hurt or worse in the accident, and that is much to be thankful for.  How is Dude?
 
I finally got some hunting done myself, out of state nonetheless.
 
I was in Indiana this weekend, and picked up several boxes.
 
-On Friday I found Papillon and Cicada Land in Fort Harrison State Park outside Indianapolis, IN.  There is another box and a HH hostile in the park, but it was raining and getting worse and I decided not to go for these boxes.  Also, the park charged four bucks to get in (despite the rain and lack of any other patronage), which, well, put lightly, blows.
 
-On Saturday I found two boxes.  The first was Cairo Skywatch, hidden next to a Korean War era monument in a dot of a town called Cairo, which was in the middle of nowhere, IN.  Nice monument, and the letterbox is well know by the local vets, as they also log into it when they visit the monument.
 
The other box was Morel Mushroom in an unremarkable local community park in West Laffiett, IN
 
-On Sunday I had some luck on a three box series in Fort Wayne, IN.  It was the Johnny Appleseed series, planted in a small park where Johnny's gravesite can be found, surrounded by...you guessed it...apple trees.  Nice little place, but I only found two of the three boxes.  One was defiantly MIA, as other log entries had also indicated.
 
-Finally, on Monday, I went on my biggest, yet most unsuccessful hunt, in Beura, OH.  I was going for the eight box "Sound of Music" series, yet only found the first box.  Either I am really bad at this, or the rest are MIA.  On top of that, I went after a close by micro box, and also came up empty handed :(  Oh well, I'll live.
 
I also repaired and released the Sputnik HH and picked up a new one, which I have plans for :)

some days off

From: Myke Scholtes | Date: 2004-08-27 01:45:03 UTC
Message
I have a week off from work, so I went letterboxing yesterday and today.
 
Yesterday, I went to Berks County, PA, and found:
 Mount Penn: Pagoda
 Mount Penn: Penn Tower
 Reading Pagoda
 Southeast
 Southwest
 
All were in good condition.  I think I walked through 7,433,984 cobwebs at French Creek Park looking for Southeast & Southwest though.  I hope one of the spiders that bit me was radioactive!  And I got WAY lost looking for Reading Pagoda.  Ended up performing the most treacherous downward rock-climbing I've ever done.  But after 1.5 hours, I found it!  I also found some fascinating graffiti along the way:  "Embrace life for what it's worth, not what you want.  Greed is the root of all that is evil."  Maybe that's good advice for those letterboxers who are like me, and get really frustrated when the hunt is unsuccesful or when the clues just seem to make no sense.  After all, no matter what happens, we're out in the woods surrounded by beauty and nature!
 
 
Then today, I hung out in Montgomery County, and found:
  Ski Bee (first finder!)
  Peaceable Kingdom
  Under the Sea
 
All were in good condition.  Under the Sea had some water in the outer container, but the bags were dry inside.
 
My wife and I are heading up to Cape Cod for the weekend to visit with friends.  I don't know, but there might be some Massachusetts letterboxing in my future...
 
Peace,
Myke
"The Indigo Vulture"
