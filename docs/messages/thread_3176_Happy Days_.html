Happy Days!

From: Katrina Lustofin | Date: 2008-05-07 12:47:05 UTC
Today is the last day of final exams for me!  I have three students taking a conflict, then I have to grade the bloody things, and I am free for the summer.   Of course, the forecast here says beautiful today and then rain for the rest of the week and weekend, but I guess that will give me plenty of time to carve stamps for the event at the end of the month. 

The sad thing is that I'll be leaving Maryland in a little over a month and moving to Ohio.  Since I have a month with minimal obligations (I do have to find a house, pack, etc, but that's all), I plan to do a great deal of letterboxing and kayaking.  So what boxes would you say are the ones that I absolutely positively *have* to find before I move?  I've found most of the Brandywine boxes in Delaware, but haven't found too many boxes in my home state of Maryland - most are on the western shore, and I hate crossing the Bay Bridge, but for a great box, I'd do it.  Looking through your logbook, what boxes stand out in your mind as truly memorable (preferably in a good way)?

I don't guarantee to find all the recommended boxes, but I'll make an honest attempt (probably more so than some of my students on their finals, unfortunately).

Katy

From: Otis' Friends | Date: 2008-05-07 14:53:05 UTC

Heading East, I recommend taking a stab at the Delaware Games boxes in Kent County (Delaware) and Sussex County.  You'll like Cape Henlopen park and Bombay Hook, which are filled with all sorts of insects, a few of which are not even mosquitos.

 

I assume you've been to Blackwater already, but you should definitely go if you haven't.  There are one or two boxes there.

 

On the other side of the bridge and then some, the mountains of Western Maryland are very beautiful and offer nice, challenging hikes.

 

Don't forget the mysteries.
