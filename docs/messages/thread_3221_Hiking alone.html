Don't you just hate when you freak yourself out! I'm glad I'm not the only one. I boxed Tuesday be myself. Nothing new there - I like boxing be myself. I get to the park - a cute little place. Up a head of me another squirrel - I start to think about the Charlie and the chocolate factory and the squirrels. Oh no - they know I'm a bad nut! Didn't scream like a girl but I pretty much ran back to my car.  
I think for me I was boxing in a new pace for me and since I didn't know the area I freaked myself out.  I go to Nolde Forest by myself and don't think twice

From: gryandrowansmum | Date: 2008-06-06 15:15:18 UTC
You need a dog!

I box alone, deep in the woods, and I can't tell you how much better
the dog makes me feel. She can smell/hear people coming before I can,
and she's fairly big. Also, we adopted her, and her one "issue" is
that she doesn't like men. She will bark at them, and sometimes
growl. She's pretty intimidating, really! If I get attacked by a
woman or child, I'm just SOL, but I think you would have to be one
crazy man to take on Cleo.I hear rapists look for crimes of
opportunity, and with my big dog, I'm not a very good opportunity.It
kind of stinks because I can't take her around lots of people (like
to Campin and Stampin!) or Starbucks, etc, but as issues go, its not
a bad one to have.

Hit the ASPCA and get yourself a hiking buddy!

However Cleo is completely useless around *animal* predators. We met
a quite large Black Bear in Washington state out in the woods, and
she looked at the bear, looked at me, and went back to sniffing the
weeds. Totally useless with the bear. The bear just wandered off, but
it was pretty scary! Ditto coyotes, groundhogs (they are actually
shockingly big when you are right next to one!), snakes. Totally
useless.

Only the 2 legged predators scare her.

stargazermomma

From: Katrina Lustofin | Date: 2008-06-06 15:28:26 UTC
So true, stargazermomma.   Last summer I letterboxed solo (i.e. without the dogs) for the first time ever.  I was out of town for work and needed a way to spend my time outside of the job, plus get some exercise.  Most of the boxes I went after along the same greenway and as I got to know it, I felt much more comfortable, but that first day out, I felt so vulnerable and easily spooked.  I hadn't realized just how much I rely on the dogs to keep me informed about what's going on around me, particularly when I'm stamping in.

My LB buddy in Illinois also had a dog and the 5 of us - two females plus 3 dogs - would hit the trail together all the time.  Someone once asked her husband how he felt about two females alone on the trail - did he really think we were safe?  His response was that a person who was not deterred by a German Shepard and a pit bull would also not hesitate to attack a large group of people.

Katy

From: Mary Erickson | Date: 2008-06-06 20:59:07 UTC
I was out boxing this afternoon and after I found the
three boxes in the series I took the wrong way home.
Came to an unfamiliar road and then realized I had to
just backtrack all the way to the car. Reversing
course, I stuck my hand in my pocket and realized my
cell phone was gone. Sinking feeling.

I kept thinking, if only I had a cell phone, I could
call my cell phone! It was hard to erase that
interesting but idiotic thought. Since I had to
backtrack anyway I searched each spot where I had
stamped in, to no avail. I hoped I had left it in the
car, to no avail.

I then began driving, looking for somewhere that had a
pay phone. 15 minutes down the road, at last, a 7-11.
Took $2.00 of pay phone money [Did you know they
charge you fifty cents even when you don't make a
connection??!?] to call my teen with instructions to
WAIT 20 MINUTES, and then start calling every 3
minutes for a half an hour.

Sped back to the woods, down the trail where I could
hear my phone ringing 50 feet off the trail at the
first place I stamped in. 16 missed messages from my
teen! She thought starting earlier was helpful, but
I'm just glad it was a fairly non-traveled trail. I
know *I* would certainly investigate a phone ringing
in the woods!

So that is a downside of hiking by yourself, but I'm
not quitting anytime soon.

Mommo
