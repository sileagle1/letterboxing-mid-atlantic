Not the most erudite response of all time, but when you
see your name, you feel compelled, don't you? (And what
do you expect when the current CD is "Jimmy Buffet: Songs
You Know By Heart", and you really do know them all by
heart?)

In any case, such that the post isn't a total train wreck,
it seems possible, tho not necessarily likely (depending
on personal logistics, guest carver(s), and the ever
skyrocketing price of petroleum products such as tupperware,
all of which are not necessarily 100% certain at this point)
that I will have a day-of WOM box at HR, so the interested
need to set up the necessary infrastructure on AQ to make
that F count increment and all that other logging stuff
and really important details that make American letterboxing
what it is, if they want to.

There may even be a bonus, but to make sure it is actually
used this time, it will expire day of. So the interested
need to be prepped. The uninterested need to ignore me,
which I think is always good advice in any case, uninterested
or otherwise.

In other news, in talking to a couple of folks, it occurs
to me the tremendous amount of mischief that could be played
on AQ (is what I'm responding to part of that?), if one had
the interest and inclination. Unfortunately, after not-so-
careful deliberation on the matter, I do not.

From: rozebud.rm | Date: 2008-07-07 20:18:58 UTC
Hey SSB,

I wanted to answer you before, but have been "sworn to secrecy" about
this event for a while...us local folks in St. Louis have been
involved, to varying degrees, in the planning of LB Con 1. As for
me, I've mostly lurked for several months due to extremely pressing
work issues (70-hour weeks, anyone?) but hope to start participating
more other than the occasional email. Heck, for one thing, i could
get out and plant those dozen letterboxes sitting in a drawer at home!

At any rate, while there will be vendors and classes, no one is
looking to make money on this or make it commercial. But the idea IS
to have something different than a regular gather: walk over to a
table and buy PZ Cut directly from Webfoot? Can do! Learn how to
carve with a needle? They will have that too. And I am REALLY
looking forward to that, because I can't for the life of me figure
out just how one DOES carve with a needle! ;-p

Yep, hot and sweaty is St. Louis in the summertime. I believe the
basic plan is to have LOTS of indoor activities scheduled for late
morning - early evening, and perhaps plan some early morning or night
boxing outside...because really, boxers have to box, right?

I hope to see all my mid-Atlantic friends here in the Lou next
summer - thanks!

~rozebud
