General Lafayette's Sword

From: foundingfather1776 | Date: 2008-06-25 20:27:55 UTC
Next time you feel like taking a potshot at the French, please remember it wasn't George
Washington who forced Cornwallis to surrender at Yorktown, but the French fleet waiting
offshore.

And Gen. Marquis de Lafayette volunteered to fight and despite being wounded at
Brandywine, kept fighting through the end of the war - even using his own money to buy
much-needed supplies for his American troops.

His most unsung role was perhaps his ability to cheer Geo. Washington. That was not an easy
proposition as our first president was always a dour fellow. In defeat after defeat, it was
Lafayette who kept Washington from despair, keeping him warm at Valley Forge, safe at
Barren Hill and getting him drunk on good beer during the retreat from Germantown.

Freedom isn't free. In fact, ours cost many French francs.

From: von der Insel | Date: 2008-06-26 03:36:24 UTC
Many millions.  The French king bankrolled the Continental Army's payroll for several years.  It was expedient for him, of course, since he wanted to aggravate the English.  English law also discouraged their colonies from having a gunpowder industry (making them dependant on buying their gunpowder from England), so during the War of Independance, we got about 90% of our gunpowder from France.
