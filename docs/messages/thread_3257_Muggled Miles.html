NOT the first finder, after all

From: Mary Erickson | Date: 2008-06-30 14:25:11 UTC
After I dropped my son off at camp in West Virginia, I had a few hours of daylight left to find a box or two in a new place. I drove to Front Royal to look for some boxes in the Elizabeth Furnace area.

I enjoyed the mile or two on an up-up-up trail, but then was dismayed to spot the box sitting completely exposed on top of some rocks, five feet from the supposed hiding place. Who could have been so careless, I wondered?

I took the box back down the trail a ways to stamp in, and lo and behold, I was the first finder? So why would the PLACER leave the box exposed? When I was replacing the contents I looked more carefully at the lid. Gouged into the new plastic were five distinct CLAW marks, and what looked like more scratches or teeth marks along one edge.

So apparently I WASN'T the first finder, after all!

And yes, I heard bears in the woods all the way back down the trail, lol.
