A Poem By Bill Lewis

G____ __N grin and gurn
From blackened beams
that creak and groan
as ancient houses dream
and are swayed by
wind in branches
long since snapped.
Foliate Faces flower and the
memory of an antique hour
unwinds beneath
a carpenters craft;
masons too, saw their shape
sleeping in the stone.
So all is forest then,
vegetable, mineral, flesh, bone.
The world tree becomes
the column of my spine;
eyelids leaves of oak;
fingers ash and pine.
I am lost within a wood
that is lost within me.
G____ __N grin and gurn,
for no one knows more than they
what is and is not tree.

Who is he? A mystery of tree? Would ya like to meet him?
Well, visit the village of Revel Grove. Enter the gates and stroll
the lanes of the Faire.
Where the pipe organ plays it mighty tune, turn right. A chance for
directions can be
found amongst the bubble fairies, for their Master is he who hides.
Follow the lane down
the way. Seek the purveyor of fancy fairie facades for your face.
There are two
establishments of this kind. Seek the one on the right, near the
wandering maze and
betwixt and between the interlacers of tresses and those crafty
celts. In the place created
by the miscellaneous oddiments, there is a wall of those leaf faced
lords and ladies. Stand
and meet their gaze, read and learn and then look up. Corbeled in the
ceiling lie two more
of there kind and nearby where you stand, just above your eye, nested
behind the vine,
you will find an emerald green box perhaps unlike any other you have
hunted. If the shop
is filled with unknowing customers, than perhaps retrieve yourselves
with a wink and a
nod to the the knowing crew. However, if time allows, tell them "I
would like to meet the
G____ __N" and then perhaps Papa Woodwose himself will present you
with the treasure
and then you will exchange your marks with one another.
Peaceful Trails,
