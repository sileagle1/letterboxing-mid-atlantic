From: Indigo Vulture | Date: 2008-07-27 15:37:18 UTC
My two cents about the crazy Tupperware gatherings:

Cyclonic wrote:

"While there is nothing wrong with people doing all these other
things, I question what it all has to do with letterboxing."

I agree...that's my big complaint too. I wish Atlas Quest would start
calling itself "a place to have adventures and meet fellow
adventurers" or even "a place for quests and fellow questers" or
something like that. They could accurately say that "SOME of the
quests listed here are part of a hobby called letterboxing" or
something like that. I have no problem with it all existing, just
with referring to it as letterboxing. However, this is probably the
same sort of thing that led some of the earliest American boxers to
give up the name and call their hunts "treasure boxing" or something
like that. So perhaps it's US who should lay down our pride, and
change the name of what WE do. I don't know. (And don't get me
started about how I think there's a parallel here with current
Christianity...but I digress a LOT.)

Miss Moon wrote:

"And this all comes back to all the publicity that so many people
thought was just honky dory."

I still don't agree with this, because it was through a newspaper
article that I discovered letterboxing. Because it was in 2004, the
article led me to letterboxing.org. And I learned to box from the
people who were then big on that site. (Those were the days when
Mapsurfer clues were still listed there.) The rub is that current
publicity probably leads most people to AQ, and when you discover that
as the current face of the hobby, you learn how to box AQ-style.
Since many of us (including me) have abandoned that site, new folks
don't hear our views until later (if ever), and just see us as whiny
veterans.

The answer? Oh, if I knew that, I'd bottle it and sell it.

I.V.
