I still don't agree with this, because it was through a newspaper
article that I discovered letterboxing. Because it was in 2004, the
article led me to letterboxing.org. And I learned to box from the
people who were then big on that site. (Those were the days when
Mapsurfer clues were still listed there.) The rub is that current
publicity probably leads most people to AQ, and when you discover that
as the current face of the hobby, you learn how to box AQ-style.
Since many of us (including me) have abandoned that site, new folks
don't hear our views until later (if ever), and just see us as whiny
veterans.

The answer? Oh, if I knew that, I'd bottle it and sell it.

From: lunastrixae | Date: 2008-07-28 04:52:13 UTC
As a newbie I mostly use AQ, but I serach for clues on lbna too. I
know nothing of the politics, etc involved between the two sites.

Why AQ? I often find the 'last found' stat critically helpful. Since
sometimes it's hard for me to get someplace being a non driver. So if
I make arrangements to go someplace, I want to know at least there is
a good potential that there is a box for me to walk right by and not find.
