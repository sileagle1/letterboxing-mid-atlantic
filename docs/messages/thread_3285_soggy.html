We'd already found 3 boxes at Iron Hill (oddly enough, the park and not
the brewery - who would guess?) with rumbles of thunder but we pushed
for a fourth find and stamped just as the rain started. Just sprinkles
until we reached the road and then blinding rain from that point back
to the car.

There's really nothing like being afoot during a thunderstorm in a park
built on an old iron mine. "This will be a good story if we live!" :)

Curiously, we were and were not almost in a similar situation Saturday in York County.  Check with the elevation, iron ore, and impending storm.  The key difference is that natural preservation took over before we found all boxes in the park, leaving enough time to make it back to within 50 feet of the car as we watched the wall of rain head toward us from the other side of the tree line.
 
So, P&S got the better story out of it, but we were comparatively dryer.  Both lived to box another day.

As they say at Boys Town, good judgement comes from experience and
experience comes from bad judgement. The modern addendum is that
bad experiences become good stories, or at least blog posts.

Twice, I've been on Adirondack summits at the time of pop-up
thunderstorms. When you're standing on the highest point for some 3
miles in any direction and there are 2/10ths of a mile of bare rock
face, begging to become a sliding board, between you and tree line,
it's amazing how fast you can move.

It also changes your perspective on where you need to park at the
mall on a cloudy day. "Wet" looks pretty good when you've
contemplated "wet yet crispy." :)
