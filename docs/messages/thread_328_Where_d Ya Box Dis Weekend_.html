Re: Where'd Ya Box Dis Weekend?

From: cahillymom | Date: 2004-08-30 13:05:54 UTC
I set out with one son on Saturday to be a letterboxing angel and
replace a box we'd found with decayed stamp and book. So, if
anyone's after the "Back to the Future" boxes - 1, 2, and the new 4
are there. We still couldn't find the third.
Drove up and down the wrong Woodbridge Ave. for a while, then got on
the right one and got Mary Moore, which brought me to F98. Seemed
like the whole church was there for a workday; hard to be subtle,
but we managed.
Drove through a park looking for Meuly Woods. Saw folks setting up
for a birthday party for a 1 yr. old, according to the very large
banner. There must have been at least 40 "goody bags" (HUGE -
baskets full of school supplies!) set up waiting for guests. That
must be SOME baby! But we moved on...
Wandered around the VanWickle house looking for the five plank
bridge. The nine-planked one didn't work at all. Gave up.
Heading for the East Brunswick parks, I ran over something. After
that, the car made horrible noises while turning, so I stopped
promptly at the nearest service station. Waited in the smoke and
tire smelling room watching Olympics and developing a headache.
Turned out it was some piece of trim under the car which got bent
back to where it rubbed on the tire. They used a little tie-down to
pull it back, didn't charge me, and I was out of there - with a
headache, so I just headed home.
Yesterday afternoon we (w/husband this time) nabbed Warcat's new
boxes in Franklin Lakes to reach F101!! Then, Wayne thought we
needed some more hiking, so we went to the Palisades Interstate
Park, Stateline Lookout Section, to hike down to Peanut Leap Falls.
I've never hiked past a "Warning: next 2 miles of trail very
difficult" sign before, have you? I HAD been thinking a box was in
order, since the name lends itself to a cute stamp image, but I
really don't think I want to be responsible for box maintainance!
Aud

Well....we managed to actually get in a little boxing dispite the
intense fact that it was opening weekend of the MD Ren Faire, my wife
and I run two business there with 6 employes to manage and opening
was terriblly hot. I create sculptural leather masks and let me tell,
this was not a wknd where many folks wanted to strap leather to their
faces....no sir-re. However ,with 10,000 thanks to Scarab of the
Doubtful Guests, we managed to be 1st finders on his two new micro-
boxes at the faire. Now let me tell, as we are involved with the
faire, we can be on grounds during the week.The faire has good
security with photo IDs. The grounds are mostly empty during the
week, we know mostly everone who would be there then. It would be a
simple matter for us to hunt these boxes with absolutly no-one
around. But that wouldn'nt be the same now would it? So lovely Lady
Woodwose and I, left the booths in our "faeries" capable hands and
took baby woodwose for a long walk on Sunday to see if we could get
her asleep and to hunt the boxes. Success for both! Let me tell,
these were fun retrivals! We have the added advantage of knowing the
faire really well, and I didn't have to follow all the compass
directions for the "Brass Dragon"(Whew, The look fun however!) But
pulling a micro in such a packed setting, we even went during the
height of the day, was awesome! Of note, Take him seriously about the
poison ivy vine!!!! As we are in garb and folks will watch you to see
if you are a faire character and are going to do something, then this
was really hard. I made an exceptional boo-boo by dropping the stamp
over a railing and being forced to climb over and retrieve in! (I
can't believe I just admitted that, how embarassing!) I can't say how
much I appreciate getting to box at my little village of Revel Grove!
We did two other boxing things. We launched our "Face in the Woods"
box at the faire. Sort of a personal traveller for our booth. The
clue are rather easy, but I think the box is could be one of the more
unique ones folks might come across so I hope to see ya. I wanted to
create at box that worked within the setting of the faire. Here I am
again, planting a box where I get to meet other boxers!(See
the "Green Rose Labyrinth" box, located at our home studio)
And one last thing,
Hey Scarab, I took Johnny Fox to the box on Monday. he had read the
TIME article which had attracted his attention because he had heard
about the box last year! He tought it was cool and signed in!
We look forward to checking back on the boxes during the faire season
to see who comes, although I will expect all who do to hit the "Face"
as well, ya won't be disappointed if ya like art boxes! And if your
there and ya spy a young guy, (almost 32, I can still say that?)
wearing alot of green and maybe a cornacopia basket as a hat, goatee,
and longish hair, well ask and see if it might be me,
Papa Woodwose,
aka: Shane "Brambleberry Woodwose, Master Mask Maker, Master of the
Bubble Fairies, Little Faery King of Revel Grove, and Abbot of the
Order of the Greenman" Odom
Peace.
