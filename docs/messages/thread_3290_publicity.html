I have to chime in on this one!
 
I agree with Miss Moon 100%. (now that's something I never thought I'd say!)  :-)
Many of us have had the opportunity to "sing the praises" of our favorite hobby.
But why? Publicity about letterboxing was equated to the following from an enigma from the north:
 
"It's like having your own private fishing hole - teaming with fat, plump trout in an idyllic setting. Then you tell your friends about it; who tell other friends, etc. By the next time you return, the fish are gone, the wild flowers are trampled and there is litter all over."
 
I see this as almost a generational thing (although age doesn't really enter into this - it's more like the years you've been letterboxing. ) Those of us who have been boxing for 3+ years feel differently about bells & whistles and publicity. Those new to this hobby know nothing different than all that Atlas Quest has to offer.
 
I was on Atlas Quest several months ago and happened to read a post where Erik Davis was mentioned. "Who is Erik Davis" several of the newbies mentioned? That really shocked me.
 
Like Miss Moon (I've said it again!) I do enjoy some of the bells on AQ - the map that shows where some of the boxes are located within a town. Makes for effiecient planning when visiting new locales but with well over 2000 finds, Sue & I have done fine without it though all those years prior to Ryan develoing this. But it is kinda cool.
 
Maybe we (the vets) need to do a better job with educating those new to the hobby. It's something we've tried to do on the Newboxers list. But - newbies need to have the desire to learn. Therein may lie the rub.
 
Thanks for reading my post. I'm enjoying this thread.
