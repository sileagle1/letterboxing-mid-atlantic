From: John | Date: 2008-08-27 01:03:36 UTC
The following (see below) is too long and does not quite fit the
parameters of the LB Poetry Contest. But I thought I'd post it
anyway. I first posted it over a year ago and I have since 'tweaked'
it a bit. I hope you get a kick out of it. -John
--
Escape - "with me" (The Letterboxing-allota Song)

This version is definitely not by: By Rupert Holmes
Lyrics partially borrowed and butchered w/o permission but with a
good sense of humor by: Jersey Trailblazers

Sing to the tune of the "Pina Colada" song:


I was tired of my boxing partner, we'd been together too long.
Like a worn-out old inkpad, or a clue sheet that's wrong.
So while she lay there sleeping, I read Family Fun magazine in bed.
And in a letterboxing classified, there was this letter I read:

"If you like Letterboxing-alotta, and getting caught in the rain.
If you're not into geo-caching, if you're a little 'Bumppo' insane.
If you like carving stamps at midnight, of all kinds of weird shapes.
I'm the trail partner you've looked for, AQ-mail me, and escape."

I didn't want to box with my partner, I know that sounds kind of mean.
But me and my old partner, had fallen into the same old boxing
routine.
So I wrote a letter to the editor, about the Family Fun ad.
And though I'm not a Mapsurfer, I thought it wasn't half-bad.

"Yes, I like Letterboxing-alotta, and getting caught in the rain.
I'm not much into orienteering, and my box clues make others complain.
I've got to meet you on the trail soon, and cut through all this
cammo tape.
Or at an upcoming Cyclonic gathering , where we'll plan our escape."

So I 'inked up' with hopes, then she walked in the place.
I knew her LBNA patch in an instant, I saw her compass in place.
It was my own lovely lady, and she said, "Oh, it's you."
And we exchanged cooties for a moment, and I said, "I never knew"..

"That you liked Letterboxing-alotta and getting caught in the rain.
And the thrill of being first finder, and that you had another trail
name.
That you like carving stamps at midnight, of all kinds of weird
shapes.
You're the trail partner that I've looked for, come with me, and
escape."

(Chorus)
"If you Letterboxing-alotta, and getting caught in the rain.
And the thrill of being first finder, and you're a little insane.
If you like carving stamps at midnight, of all kinds of weird shapes.
You're the trail partner that I've looked for, come with me, and
escape."
