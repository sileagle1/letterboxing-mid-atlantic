Levels of boxing

From: wood thrush | Date: 2008-07-30 15:46:39 UTC
Perhaps one way to think about the current state of boxing is that it
is like a video game. At the highest expert level is the Mapsurfer
approach and philosophy. I think Randy espoused that extremely well in
his most recent post.

At the most beginner level would be something like a drive-by tot lot
box with straightforward clues designed for very young children that
you find on AQ with lots of comments and recent find reports.

And then there is everything in between.

You experience boxing at whatever level you're capable of. These days
many start at the beginner level. The hope of those of us who are
devoted to the pastime is that people will be intrigued enough to gain
experience and skill and explore the more difficult levels that open
up to them.

Some never do. They may lose interest after a time. Or--like my son
when he's playing a video game, they may prefer to kick butt on a
lower level for a long time rather than grow in the skill and
challenge of the higher levels. Or maybe something about their life
situation precludes them from going further. So be it--they still can
enjoy things where they are.

And those of us who like the rarefied air of more difficult and
challenging levels of boxing enjoy where we are too.

Having said this, I do acknowledge the nagging fear (already
expressed) that the growing number of people indiscriminately boxing
on the lower levels will overexpose and endanger the pastime. How best
to slow the slide towards and yet prepare for that possibility is, I
think, one of the biggest challenges the boxing community faces.

Thanks for the conversation!
