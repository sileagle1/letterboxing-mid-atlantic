Re: Toner Heat Transfer Question & Skull2

From: sewsowbizzy | Date: 2008-08-22 14:33:10 UTC
Finally - having a detailed stamp to carve for Skullduggery 2 - I
tried your heat method.

1. Remove paper when still hot -- but peak first to see if toner is
transferring thoroughly -- since the toner acts like a glue the image
won't shift if you only lift 1/2 - press - lift the other 1/2 -- when
pleased lift off

2. In addition to the image paper, add a clean sheet on top so the
iron never meets the rubber directly

3. I have one of those heavy GE irons - so I let the weight of the
iron do the work. I had tried pressing down and I ended up with the
outline of the iron in the rubber - right thru the image!! Flip sides
and do again.

4. The rubber will absorb and hold heat -- set it out on heat proof
surface - flat - no ridges/lines - to cool 100% - 10min about

5. If there is already an image on part of the rubber - suggest you
cut it away. When ironing image #2 - image #1 transfered back to the
clean sheet of paper!! but only slightly

Thanks for reminding us of this technique -- the images are super
sharp for carving and I was almost out of acetone anyway.
