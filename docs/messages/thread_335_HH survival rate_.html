From: Flame Thrower | Date: 2004-08-31 13:21:36 UTC
The ADG has released about 12 hitchhikers and 3 of them were sent
back to us. Those 3 had traveled quite a bit, most of them made
their way up to New England for a while. It took about 2 years each
to get the hitchers back. We tape an envelope into the back of our
hitchers. The envelope contains an address to a PO box and stamps.
This encourages people to return your hitchers to you. And we
always seem to get the stamps back unused. People may bitch and
moan all day on the main list but at heart most boxers are kind and
unselfish. (now watch them go prove me wrong with more rants)

Of the other 9 hitchers, about half of them are still moving about
and have been spotted in the last 6 months. Based on our
experiences then I'd say the hitcher retention rate is about 50%.
However we may have gotten lucky. My recommendations would be keep
the log small and write instructions about the return into the
logbook. We have also found that the home-made logbooks are really
appreciated and boxers tend to treat them with a little more respect
than a spiral bound book of 3X5 cards.

Tammy
ADG #1
