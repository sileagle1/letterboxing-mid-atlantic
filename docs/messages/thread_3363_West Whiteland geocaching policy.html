I prepared myself to be mildly interested in a story of the discovery of the wonderful world of geocaching....but it turned out to be a report that West Whiteland's new geocaching policy.
Article follows:

Geocaching approved for West Whiteland parks
By JOE LANGDON, Staff writer
09/14/2008

,WEST WHITELAND — The Board of Supervisors approved a policy on Wednesday to allow recreational "geocaches" to be placed in township parks.


Geocaching is essentially a worldwide, high-tech treasure hunt in which participants use Global Positioning Systems to locate small caches, commonly clear plastic containers, whose coordinates have been posted on Web sites such as www.geocaching.com. Once the caches are found, participants remove a trinket from the container, such as a foreign coin or small toy, replace it with one of their own and sign the cache's logbook.

Geocaches have been approved for Miller Park and Boot Road Park, and enthusiasts will now meet with parks and recreation officials to determine the exact type and location of the caches. Officials will ensure that the caches will not be confused with dangerous items, like bombs, and will not have an adverse effect on environmentally sensitive places, like the butterfly garden in Boot Road Park.

"From what I understand, these things will be placed five or 10 feet off the road, not 100 feet off in the woods," said Supervisor Keith Choper.

Township Manager Michael Cotter noted that unlike conventional orienteering, in which participants use compasses to navigate an area, geocaching tends to be minimally invasive for the environment.

According to accepted guidelines in the geocaching community, caches must be moved or removed after three years and may not be placed within a tenth of a mile of other caches, to prevent oversaturation of an area. All caches must be placed in accord with township approval and regulations.

West Whiteland will join several other townships, including Pennsbury, Warwick and New Garden, that participate in geocaching.
