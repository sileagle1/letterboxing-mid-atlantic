From: Christine Bamberger | Date: 2008-10-16 13:30:46 UTC

As my daughter learned in her math class, this is a case of “not enough information”.  How many is a ‘bunch’?  How old are they?  Where do they want to go hiking?

 

Our early letterboxing adventures (and probably our current ones depending on how judgmental you are) were not the most stealthy, but kids playing in the woods often makes a decent cover for our activities.  Our first letterboxing hunt was with five kids ages 6 and under, but we chose a park that wasn’t highly traveled.  Nobody was around as we looked for the box.  One of our trips soon after that was with a group of about 15 family members.  Disguised as a post-Thanksgiving walk, we wandered another park looking for a box.

 

It can be done, but the best “sure thing” is one you hide yourself.  I’ll carve and send a stamp if you can get more information.

 

Chris

of the Scattered Showers
