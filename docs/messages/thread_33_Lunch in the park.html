Lunch in the park

From: Cyclonic07@aol.com | Date: 2004-04-02 01:26:31 UTC
I had some time yesterday, so I decided to take my lunch in Evansburg park.  It was a miserable day, so the park was empty.  But that was OK, it meant for some nice quite time.
 
I strolled down the trails of the park, slowly devouring a ham and cheese sandwich.  The path was a little wet from an earlier rain, and the numerous streams quickly flowed into the Skippack Creek.
 
As I walked along, I crossed a small bridge that spanned one of these streams, I heard a rumble from beneath.  I slowly descended to the stream bed to see what it might be, fearful that a bridge troll may suddenly jump out at me.  Fortunately, there was no troll, but instead a fuzzy little caterpillar, sitting on the bank.
 
I was a cute little guy, I picked him up and took him to a nearby table.  I wanted to keep him, but instead pulled out my small notepad and made an image of him, so that I would remember him, and then placed him back under the bridge, where he can one day turn into a beautiful butterfly.
 
It did not take long, for as I continued to roam through the park, suddenly from a tree, came fluttering a beautiful butterfly.  It landed in my hand, at the tip of my fingers.  I took an image of it as well, and then sat it back into its tree, where it can wait for the next traveler.
 
I hear there is also a dog roaming the park, but I had finished lunch by this time, and could not go and see if I could find him.  Maybe next time.
