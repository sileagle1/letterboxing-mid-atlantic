From: Katrina Lustofin | Date: 2008-11-17 17:31:42 UTC
That reminds me of the time I was letterboxing in Georgia, a few summers ago.  The town has several parks connected by a greenway;  both parks and the trail had boxes for which I was hunting.  Some of the stretches of trail were more popular than others, and at one point I found myself following a gentleman with a backpack and trekking pole.  I was walking faster than him, but kept getting behind when I would stop to snag a box.  There was no one else around and we were not close to any of the parks, so I started wondering if he was the placer of one of the boxes I was after.  I was catching up again and contemplating asking him if he was a boxer when he came out of the woods in a place that did not match any of my clues - so I assumed he was a geocacher...  But after finding the box for which I was looking, I wandered over to where he'd been, and found a letterbox!  No one had stamped in recently, so I assume he was the placer, doing a check, but by then he was gone.  I've never seen the box listed anywhere, nor have I found anything posted by that trail name, so it was truly a surrendipitous find.  The box, appropriately, was named "Joy"

Katy

From: Stephen | Date: 2008-11-18 01:40:57 UTC
I don't usually chime in on these threads, but this subject I find interesting since there seems to be much distain regarding AQ with many of the 'Old timer" letterboxers.

As a letterboxing newbie (been at it for only two years now), where does one go to find these "so many clues hosted on neither AQ nor LbNA"? WoM's are great, but hard to come by.  While I find that both sites are littered with boxes that aren't worth reading the clues let alone going out to find the plant, they are the most accessible way to find boxes, plan & track events, plan for travel, or keep logs for plants and finds (without fear of losing them as I have read about happening to others on this chat group). 

Seems like the mass usage of these sites makes them the "Center of the Letterboxing Universe" (perhaps the mass usage is the reason for the distain).  Am I wrong to guess that most letterboxers (Old or New) spend most of their searching time on these two sites??  Of course, being the center does not mean that policies of this hobby are set there, but I can see why so many think that they are the place to look for "the letterboxing codes and rules".  

For me, "There are no rules only personal choices, it's letterboxing after all."

DC Stones

From: Nathan Brown | Date: 2008-11-18 16:05:37 UTC
nattybumppolbna wrote:
> I shudder at the thought of decisions being made on AQ about the future of letterboxing.
>


Here is the thing that I have found over the past year or so, it doesn't
matter. Really, it doesn't matter what others say or do because the
future of the hobby is what you, personally, make it.

I read the boards on AQ mostly because it is like watching a train
wreck. Very often, when I post, whatever I say, my opinion, gets
lambasted. People don't get it, or are just plain ignorant, as to why I
post things.

For example, in the thread that Rick here was first talking about,
personally, I think the listings for boxes that are abandoned by folks
not coming back to the site should be deleted. I think only the planter
of the box has any rights to ownership of a box and that the box should
never be forcibly given to someone else, even if that someone else will
take care of the box or the box will simply become trash in the woods.
It is amazing how many folks seem to think it is OK to steal someone
else's box, change the clues or the box or both. Heck, I used to even
think that, so I do somewhat understand, but sometimes a box simply has
to die.

I could go on and on about the stupid things that go on there, and are
said there, but really, what difference does it make, that is not going
to change, and at least there are functions to ignore most of the
non-sense.

This is not to say that there is not good as well. The new groups
function has opened up lots of new options on box clue ideas and the
sharing of thoughts and opinions. But I think that the sharing of those
ideas, and the good that can come of it, is far overshadowed by the
nonsense and utter stupidity that goes on. That is why a list like this
still works so well, and will continue to work so well.

I just find myself totally disgusted by how so many seem to worship Ryan
and everything he does. At least I know he doesn't like it either,
unlike other list owners that seem to relish being propped up as such.

The original purpose of the site, to be able to more easily search for
box clues, is great, and works well. I often wonder, however, just how
many people use the site for that purpose, and how many simply use the
site to gab about any and everything. I ignore most of it, and go about
planting and finding my boxes, but I see very few people out there
finding even the simplest of my own boxes, and often wonder if the site
is really about letterboxing anymore.

--
Nathan Brown
