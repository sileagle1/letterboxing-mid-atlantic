Some Updates and Boxing this Weekend

From: Nathan Brown | Date: 2008-12-01 14:03:40 UTC
Well, first a few updates. For those interested, I planted a new box
earlier this week, "Winter in Bucks"
(http://www.penncoasters.com/letterboxing/winter.htm). This is a limited
time only box that will be available only until January 6. Afterwards
there will be a permanent box planted at that location.

I have also finally changed and updated "Beast from the Barrens"
(http://www.penncoasters.com/letterboxing/beast.htm). It is in a new
location and the clues have been altered. This box is a favorite of
mine and the clues are based on a true story.

Schizokeet and I had a nice weekend, though we went boxing on only one
day this weekend. We went in search on the new Remembering 9/11 series
in Lower Makefield at the Garden of Reflection. A very nice park. The
clues turned out to be more challenging then we expected and Becky
needed the facilities before we found the last box. The hides are very
well done.

We then made our way to Lambertville to get some beer at River Horse,
then across the river where Bowman's Tower was actually open (five bucks
to go up, forget that!) and my box is still in great shape. We then
headed home to have an early dinner before watching a tree lighting with
fireworks, which made Becky very happy.

We also ran into other boxers on the trail. We are sitting, logging
into the first box, when I see folks off into the woods. I say they
look like they are letterboxing, I think they have clues. Becky says,
no, they are way over there and would have gotten this box already, but
we are first finders (nice little FF prizes BTW, Becky liked). They
must be geocaching, they are looking under every log, stump and tree.
They get really close to us, within 50 feet, and still do not see us
(this is why stealth is overrated), and finally Becky calls over to them.

Turns out they are out of town boxers, Snap Z from NYC and Sunny Sal
from Maryland. They are headed to NY and boxing along the way, and not
having much luck with this series. They had skipped the first three
boxes having not been able to find them, and were looking for the forth
(which would be difficult because you would need to find the first three
first to find the forth). So we got exchanges and went on to find two
of the other three boxes while they continued to poke around for the
second box.

Speaking of seeing folks on the trail, I hear Celtic Cat and Wednesday
Bowler ran into an interesting fellow on trail on Sunday.
