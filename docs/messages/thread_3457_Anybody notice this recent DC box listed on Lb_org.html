Monument Scouting Report:

North Face Clear of F.O.D. (Foreign Object Debris aka "letterbox")
Recent Masonry and Joint Repairs not weathering well.  Nowhere to hide a box.  None loose enough to be pulled and placed back as a cover.
2" Expansion Joint at base of Monument installed during the post 9/11 security upgrades
Expansion Joint Material installation holding up well.  Tight Bond.  No Loose Spots.  Entire joint checked.
The are two round access handholes at either corner of the north face.  One accessible, one need a screwdriver to open.
The accessible handhole was checked.  No F.O.D. found. (This would be a nice place for a plant! Wouldn't last long, but those who could get it, would love it!)
On the East Face, they have built an entrance to the obelisk.  There are 2 PVC pipes on the North Face of this "lean to" addition.
Both pipes checked.  No F.O.D. found.

Conclusion:
Unless box was placed during the 2002 civil improvements to the base of the monument, the box is not there.  But perhaps I overlooked something.....It wouldn't be the first time!

Stephen, I came back to this just because it really struck me and
brought back memories.
In the US Army, FOD is Foreign Object Damage. It is the result of
something "foreign" striking someplace it would have been better had
it not: entering a turbine engine and nicking the blades (not blowing
it up as a Holywood aircraft does) or damage to a propeller, main
rotor, tail rotor, even the occasional maintenance stand creating what
is also known as "hanger rash", the maintenance stand being foreign to
the airframe.

But the term takes on a life of it's own as an acronym (verses an
initialism such as "CIA"). We had to do a daily "FOD Walk" down the
flight line. Were we looking for FOD? Noooo, we were looking for stuff
that could cause FOD.

After I became a pilot, we still looked for FOD around the aircraft
(uh, its a screw that could CAUSE FOD) and on the aircraft for FOD
(Not happy when found on a pre-flight inspection because it wasn't
caught in the last post-flight inspection or by the crew chief, but
happy it was found). While keeping an eye out for, you gussed it, FOD
on the aircraft that that could cause FOD to the aircraft. Confused yet?

All work spaces in the hanger had a, can you guess? A "FOD can."

Thanks for bringing back a smile.

Ron
