Saturday's quests

From: Silent Doug | Date: 2004-09-06 03:08:53 UTC
Jennifer and I had a busy day today -- we found Cyclonic's Mount Penn boxes
and checked in on my Reading Pagoda box, and all were fine.

Our Letterbox Rescue Mission program also scored another success. We
recovered and adopted a forgotten box from the letterbox basement, the
Exeter Scenic Trail Rocks in Gibraltar, PA. It hadn't been visited for a
year, but it was in fine shape. The clues are now available at:
http://www.letterboxing.info/box/diverdm/exeter.php

Unfortunately, we didn't have as much luck with two other abandoned boxes:
The Eagle in Reading is definitely gone, and our search for Stream vs. Road
in Birdsboro was inconclusive. (If anyone has any information on Stream vs.
Road, please let me know!).

We closed out the day by spending nearly 4 hours at Nolde Forest searching
for shredmtb's 3-box series in the park there. It was fiendishly clever,
and kept us busy, both from a hiking and brainpower perspective, and we
both found it to be one of the most satisfying (and exhausting!)
letterboxing forays in recent history. We highly recommend it to anyone
who's looking for a new challenge!

Monday's report

From: Silent Doug | Date: 2004-09-07 00:01:00 UTC
Jennifer and I each logged 8 today, pushing me past F500!

Here's what we found (and didn't), starting with boxes in NJ:

National Mystery Letterbox Project: New Jersey
(Does everyone know about this box? It's not been visited very often, I
guess cause it's kinda hidden on letterboxing.org. You can find the clues here:
http://embers.cox.net/ae4mk/letterbox/nj_NMLBP.htm )
Van Wickle House (note to Aud: sometimes boards used for bridges get too
weathered and must be patched)
NJ Physiography 103
Delaware & Raritan Canal North
Big Green Monster #1 & 2
Music to My Ears Mystery

We didn't find the Delaware & Raritan Canal Griggstown box, and we're
pretty sure it's gone.

Then, we came back to PA and found:
Canal Bridge
Scudder Falls Bridge

We met Warcat 76 as they were hiking back from Canal Bridge -- they first
noticed the paper in our hands, and then the LbNA patch on the back of my
pack was the verification they needed to confirm that we were letterboxers,
too! We exchanged stamps there on the canal towpath.

Whew! It's been a busy long weekend....

Doug
