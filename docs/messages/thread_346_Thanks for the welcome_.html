Gosh, a flurry of welcoming messages onlist and off! Thank you!

Yes, I do plan to attend the Palmyra gathering with my family tribe. As
for refreshments, I have a garage full of Girl Scout cookies I could
bring...has anyone got a preference? (Thin Mints are gone, but I have
just about everything else!)

I learned about letterboxing from the AllGirlScouts Yahoo list.

For my introduction to letterboxing, I printed the clues to two boxes in
Western Maryland, bought a ton of carving material and supplies, and
brought the whole kit and caboodle to my week-long family reunion a few
weeks ago. I had the whole family -- four generations, aged 6 to 83! --
carving erasers around the table. Then most of us set out to find the
Meadow Mountain box in Deep Creek Lake State Park. After a wonderful hike
on Indian Turnip Trail, we found the "solitary tree," but found no
letterbox under the stump. My cousins checked every stump in a
hundred-foot radius, just to be sure we weren't missing it. We were
disappointed, but we did enjoy the hike, and we carved our own Meadow
Mountain stamp when we got back to the house.

The next day, everyone who wasn't sore from the previous day's hike went
to find Swallow Falls Swallow, and it was there! Beautiful hike, gorgeous
place!

So far, since getting home, we've found Victory and Captain Moore's
Insignia (Washington Crossing Historical Park, PA), Scudders Falls Bridge
(Yardley, PA), and NJ Physiography 104 (Smithville, NJ). We've gone
looking for the two Smithville Bicycle Railroad boxes, but they were
washed away in the summer floods, so we carved our own stamp with a
picture of H. B. Smith's Star Bicycle on it.

My trail name is She Shines. Looking forward to meeting y'all in Palmyra!


KJ Rowland
