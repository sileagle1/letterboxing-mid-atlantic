Re: [lbma] Snapware

From: Otis' Friends | Date: 2009-01-31 14:26:51 UTC
Anecdotally, I haven't been as impressed with it.  I've never used it myself and don't plan to.  It's not as airtight as a Lock-n-Lock, and the plastic doesn't seem to be as rugged.  The question is whether the reduced quality is material.  Once you get to a certain point, the marginal improvements in quality may cost more than they are worth to you.
 
It's certainly better than Gladware and Rubbermaid.

Re: [lbma] Snapware

From: Gail Metzger | Date: 2009-01-31 21:47:26 UTC
When I have run across boxes using them, it seems that the flaps don't hold up as well as LnL's.  Here in the Northeast, with the temp fluctuations, it appears that with repeated opening locking and unlocking, the hinges give way and the flaps break off.  Not great in the long run.  It would appear that a good LnL would be cheaper in the long run than replacing the knock-off on an annual basis.
