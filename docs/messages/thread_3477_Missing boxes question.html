Re: [lbma] Missing boxes question

From: Otis' Friends | Date: 2009-02-05 14:49:49 UTC
As far as things like that go, unless there's an incriminating note in the log book (e.g., "took stamp, left jagged shard of glass"), I don't think there are any res ipsa loquitur inferences that can be drawn, other than that the placement is imperfect.  (Differing levels of imperfection are almost unavoidable.)  Noxers?  Critters?  Sloppy/incompetent replacement?  Newbies not clear on the concept?  Poor original placement (e.g., animal burrows, clear visibility from the trail, proximity to places where kids obviously play and explore, lack of cover from casual observers, etc.)?  Bad luck?  All of those can be the cause.  (On the other hand, I trailed one of the numerati in a Montgomery County location a day after he went through, and I seriously doubt that a noxer was the one who literally ripped the bags to open them, sometimes leaving them on the ground, while replacing everything in the box.  In that case I'm not embarrassed to assume.)
 
If we think of this in terms of the "costs" of preventing the box from being damaged, who is the "cheapest cost avoider"?  If the placer is putting demands on finders that will predictably lead to damage, perhaps it's time to rethink replacement.  Where the spot is no better or worse than several others, and finders in the field are just making boneheaded errors, what's the best way of "idiot proofing" the boxes?
