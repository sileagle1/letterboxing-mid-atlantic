lovely day for boxing

From: Suzanne Coe | Date: 2009-02-09 01:17:42 UTC

Finally got a chance to try out the new boots!  I think they'll work for anything except Central PA mud.... ;-)
 
I wish I could say I found Wm Penn, but I didn't.  Some consolation in being able to say "Well, I didn't have the clues with me, and I don't remember the exact degrees...." I also wish I could say I found "Where's Bumppo," but I didn't.  Some consolation in being able to say, "Well, that was 5 years ago, if it's not gone then probably the terrain has changed."
 
I DID find the Amico Island Bugs--lotta fun, nice carves, boxes in great shape.  Lotta mud, natch. Stamped into box 3 on the bench at the bottom of the hill, came back up to replace the box and ran smack into a couple of birders.  Well, SHE was birding--and probably would not have noticed me had I not spoken to her--but her husband was waiting patiently on the trail.  So "what kind of bird was it?" seemed the safest way to go--since I was standing there awkwardly, Snapware in hand.... =) --and now I know how to identify Carolina chickadees & merganser ducks....
 
So how'd the rest of you spend this lovely day?
 
Sheba

From: Nathan Brown | Date: 2009-02-10 02:46:36 UTC
It was an excellent weekend to be out and about. I did find 6 boxes on
Saturday, but Sunday has a funnier story.

We planted a box on Sunday, but found no letterboxes. Instead, we found
a geocache, and also a geocacher to go with it. We are walking down
trail when I notice a stump just off trail. It looks odd, and had SPOL
piled within it. "I bet that is a geocache," I exclaimed. And sure
enough, it was. Practically right on trail too.

So we are rummaging through the logbook, didn't even look at what junk
was piled at our feet when coming down trail is a guy with his head
stuck in a GPS. Yup, there is a geocacher.

It was all good though, and his comment on the cache is pretty
entertaining as well:
