From: Randy Hall | Date: 2009-02-17 21:25:52 UTC
I read one of the articles, which seemed short on details. The
indication was that the intent was to replace gas taxes, meaning
it is possible the proposal is revenue neutral, and thus nothing
to worry about if you drive an "average" vehicle (speculatively,
drivers of fuel-efficient cars would be hurt, drivers of Hummers
would benefit). But, without any details, it is all speculation
and FUD, meaning writing to legislators might be premature.

My guess is that the drop in demand for gas (and hence revenue
from gas taxes), as more to do with the depression rather than
more fuel-efficient vehicles, but I don't have the data to back
that up.

Keep in mind that it has been demonstrated that raising taxes
during an economic depression is a bad idea. Will governments
repeat the mistakes of history? I guess we'll see.

Also keep in mind that the president just signed legislation that
will increase funding for highway repair; it remains to be seen
whether such legislation will take pressure off states to raise
revenue for this purpose, and thus take pressure off this initiative.

As for big brother concerns, at least the article I read indicated
an opt out ability by not buying a GPS and thus continuing to pay
gas taxes. Sounds like shopper bonus cards to me. Again, short on
details, so all speculation.

Cheers
Randy
