Yes, you are reading correctly, but keep it in the context of this group and this hobby.  Certainly I am not trying to justify or support getting nasty and personal about peoples' desires.  It is only letterboxing afterall, just one aspect of our lives.  Sure there may be others that think otherwise, so they may find my comment offensive and/or insensitive. 

If someone posts an "offensive" or "insensitive" comment, (which does happen whenever communications occur), why take it personally and ostracize them?  Just look the other way if you don't want to get into it, hit delete and move on.  If debate is what one craves, then have at it.  On this site, the group will police itself and the offender will either stand his ground or change his thinking (See Kirbert....sad to see he caved in to the masses, but happy to see he is happy with his decision. Oops there I go again calling out names).  By ostracizing them we may only end up shutting out some of the best letterboxers who do this hobby.  And then we all lose in my opinion.  Afterall, isn't this about the box, not the boxer?  For me, if I didn't like someone, but they planted AWESOME boxes, I'd still go find the box.  But that is just me.  I don't expect everyone to think like that, nor do I care if they do.

I've said it before and I'll say it again.  There are no rules or right way to box.  Just because someone has boxed for 10 years and doesn't choose to use AQ makes them no closer to a "real letterboxer" than a newbie who loves and respects the hobby and can see a lifetime of boxing before them.  Do what makes you happy and don't worry about what others say, think, or may tell you is the "right way" to box.  But always respect the plant and the planter.

Enough for tonight, I'm off to watch Top Chef,
DC Stones
