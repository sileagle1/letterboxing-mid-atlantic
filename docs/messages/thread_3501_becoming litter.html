OK, I missed this discussion--must have been out sick that day. =)  How does this happen exactly?  I mean, say I plant a mystery box with clues written in Latin so horrific that nobody can find it.  And then one day I develop amnesia, forget about letterboxing and never log into the site again.  So the box is "abandoned"--but if you look at _the box itself_, nothing has changed.  it's just sitting there in its hiding place, same as the day I planted it. How does my not logging into a website change it into "litter" or make it leave any more of a trace?

Re: [lbma] becoming litter

From: Kirbert | Date: 2009-02-28 03:26:45 UTC
Suzanne Coe wrote:

> I mean, say I plant a mystery box with
> clues written in Latin so horrific that nobody can find it.  And then
> one day I develop amnesia, forget about letterboxing and never log
> into the site again.  So the box is "abandoned"--but if you look at
> _the box itself_, nothing has changed.  it's just sitting there in its
> hiding place, same as the day I planted it. How does my not logging
> into a website change it into "litter" or make it leave any more of a
> trace?

Not exactly a good example, because your clues -- while difficult to
follow -- are presumably still valid. The problem arises when the
clues are no longer valid so the box cannot be found by
"letterboxing" -- it can only be found by luck or muggles. And if
the owner isn't active and therefore won't be correcting the clues,
the box is litter.

And the "leave no trace" mantra has been debated a couple of times
since you cannot leave a letterbox without leaving a trace. We each
have to struggle with that one on our own terms, I think. For me, a
letterbox is not litter because litter is A) unsightly (my
letterboxes are camo'd and therefore not unsightly) and B) discarded
(my letterboxes are carefully hidden, not discarded). But it is
leaving a trace. Not a problem for me, I never made an oath to leave
no trace.

-- Kirbert

About 3 months ago, I stumbled on a box that had no clues posted.  It was in good shape and had not been found in over 2 years per the entries in the log book.  I knew the planter and showed him the image at a recent gathering.  He was very excited to see it.  He had forgotten all about it and did want the clues on how to find it.  Very satisfying for both the boxer and the planter.  Clues are still not available anywhere that I know of, but the box is still out there.

So I would say not litter in this case.
