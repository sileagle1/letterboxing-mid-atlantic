From: Heather Siple | Date: 2009-03-02 15:48:07 UTC
Yeah, and I get the impression from Midwest transplants to Delaware who initially scoff at us that they are lacking something we have aplenty around here -- black sheet ice under the snow.  Routinely.  Predictably.  It seems to be a coastal thing.  Can't plow it.  Salt helps, but isn't a cure.  Combine that with our rather steep hills -- too small to bother building a road across and over, so they just go straight up and down -- and you have a very interesting skating rink!  I've taken my car out when the Midwesterners were scoffing and found myself unable to get up the hill -- and what about that stop sign at the bottom, on the other side????  Try that with a top-heavy, underpowered bus maintained by the lowest bidder!
