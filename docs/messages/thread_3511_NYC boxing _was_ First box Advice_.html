What I found interesting about boxing in NYC was that all those non-letterboxers milling around actually became protective coloration. We sat down right next to "imagine" & stamped in, and nobody thought anything of it because everyone else was sitting all over the park too.  Used the same technique at Coney Island. 

Really the only anxious moment we had was when we discovered that I'd left our whole sheaf of clues on a park bench at our first stop of the day.  Envisioned the wind strewing them here and there throughout the city.... When we backtracked we found them being read by a puzzled-looking elderly Gothamite, who let us claim them without any fuss. =)

Sheba

Speaking of NYC, today I read this interesting story from a Broadway stage manager:

Once upon a time, someone was working in a Broadway theater. One day this person was outside on the marquee alone, doing general maintenance, and discovered a small sealed elaborately enameled metal urn with a note attached. The note explained that the urn contained some of the ashes of someone who had worked in that theater long ago, and in their will was the request that the urn be interred somewhere within the theater. The note explained that the official request to do this had been denied, and so the note-writer took a chance and threw the urn up onto the marquee from the street, expressing the hope that either it would never be found, and so they would have at least done their best to fulfill the wish, or if it was found, that someone might unofficially honor the request. The story goes on to say that the urn, complete with note, now rests in the deepest, darkest recesses of the theater, probably never to be seen again - but at least now they're not getting rained on while stuck in roof tar.
