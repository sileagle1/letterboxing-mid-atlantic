The Daughters of Rehoboth

From: gallant_rogue | Date: 2009-04-01 03:52:56 UTC
Darkness, rain and fog. The wind whistles down a deserted boardwalk. Black mist swallows flood light from shuttered buildings. Beyond fence and dune is darkness. From the blackness beyond, almost lost in rolls and rumbles where the sea falls on the shore, a siren calls.

Stormy nights on Cape Henlopen, sailors passing near the mouth of the great Lower Slower DeLaWare Bay. Near, too near the shore. Perhaps drawn by flickering light, an illusion of safe harbor, or perhaps a sweet song and promise of warm embrace, runaground, pounded to pieces by the unrelenting black waves.

Roman writers linked the Sirens closely to the sea, as daughters of Phorcys Later writers mention both their names and number; some state that there were three, Peisinoe, Aglaope, and Thelxiepeia.

Swaggerers know them as Marylann, Ms Ball M More and Dollie
