From: sewsowbizzy | Date: 2009-04-21 16:14:57 UTC
It is all in how the pouch is assembled -- zipper part must be free from duct tape to facilitate re-sealing. And only quality (double zip freezer) bags should be used. Make sure they are big enough to hold double bagged contents (anything smaller than quart won't do except for temp bags.)

Which reminds me I need to go out and double bag all those LSS street pouches from last month!!

Re: [lbma] Containers

From: von der Insel | Date: 2009-04-21 20:50:53 UTC
It's not so easy around here to find lock&locks *not* made in China...I have this thing about that.
L&Ls are best for protecting the contents, but I've been becoming a bigger fan of either dark tarp or burlap bags hiding the boxes, which can then be hidden nicely in our local cedar trees and bushes.  I think ammo cans are just too hard to find a good spot for, even though they protect best, withstand the weight of a SPOR, and have gobs of room for hitch hikers.
 
My first box, I went out and bought a Tupperware box, 'cause I thought I should.  Once Diane found out how much I paid for it, and I found out how much other brands cost, well...that was it for me and Tupperware.
 
Dave
