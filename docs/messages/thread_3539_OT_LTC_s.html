LOL.  No, they're not letterboxing, they're stamp art.  (I could have said use the L&L's for card stock for making logbooks, of course, but thought the LTC reference would get a more humorous reaction. Not aiming for anyone in particular.)

But yes, I've done a few LTC's (although not the kind Diane does =).  I think the blame for this should rest squarely on Magical Music Man for bringing his collection to Hickory Run & letting me see how creative they were--thus contributing to the deliquency of an adult!

I see LTC's as beneficial in a way.... I think as letterboxers/carvers sometimes we get a feeling of impatience, if we carve a really good stamp, we want to put it out SOMEPLACE as a letterbox.  Sometimes I think that puts the cart before the horse--that there's too much emphasis on the stamp, and our boxes would be better if we focussed more on "this is a great place/hike/set of clues" as a reason for planting.  Maybe being able to use the stamp & "get it out there" as an LTC makes it easier to wait for the right place.

Or maybe I just like them because they're pretty.  =D

Sheba

I don't think that is the case really. I do think that folks are often
so very focused on the stamp that they do forget about the other things
that make a great letterbox great. There are an awful lot of very
talented people out there, very good carvers that I could never hope to
match, and I love finding their stamps. But in the end, for me, it is
about the entire package, not just the stamp but also the writing of
clues, beyond just directions, and the location, one that makes me want
to return. I have found many amazing stamps that were seemingly simply
thrown out into the woods with little thought of these other important
elements to a letterbox.

Re: [lbma] OT:LTC's

From: Heather Siple | Date: 2009-04-23 21:55:44 UTC
We did some boxing down in Calvert County, MD this weekend and I have a new
favorite box now. It has a very simple, store-bought stamp, but the box was
actual wood, decorated nicely by the placer, in a lovely location and even
had some treasures to go with the stamp. Do I care that it was
store-bought? Nope. Would have been an ice touch, but not necessary. Went
to another box on another day, many mile away, and found a beautiful
hand-carved stamp in a boring park, 20 feet from the car. My SHORT-hike
appreciating kids announced that the second box was, "way too easy!" We
enjoyed the stamp, but it would have been even nicer if we could have
enjoyed some special scenery too.

Mother Duck
