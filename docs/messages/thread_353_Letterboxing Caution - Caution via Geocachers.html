Letterboxing Caution - Caution via Geocachers

From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2004-09-06 23:34:10 UTC
Hi All,

I was doing some checking of my own boxes and ran into some
Geocachers that I had been corresponding with (they are new
Letterboxers as well). They had told me about a problem spreading
through their hobby - theft of geocaches. Geocachers like to use
ammo cases to stash their caches. Apparently, some slimeball has
been purloining the geocaches that are made using the ammo cases. A
fellow cacher may have interrupted who they perceive this slimeball
is when he was at a cache. Like most letterboxers, geocachers have a
sense of community and camraderie, but the suspected slimeball seemed
to have a 'God's gift' attitude and an overwhelming sense of
entitlement.

Just cautioning everyone, but hoping not to alarm.

Lightnin' Bug

The slimeball is stealing the caches in total - not a thing is left
behind. From what I understand it is the caches in the ammo cans.
It originally started in the "slate belt" area of the Lehigh Valley
(Bangor, Pen Argyl, Wind Gap) and has spread to the greater Lehigh
Valley (Allentown, Bethlehem, Easton). The local cachers are
justifiably upset and frustrated. They have some suspicions, but
cannot absolutely prove anything just yet.

Hope your are is safe,

Lightnin' Bug
