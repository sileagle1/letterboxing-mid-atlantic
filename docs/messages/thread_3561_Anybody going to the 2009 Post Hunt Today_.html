From: gallant_rogue | Date: 2009-05-18 02:16:28 UTC
Scarab, Cyclonic and myself went and gave it the old college try. We did well thru five clues. Just like letterboxing, you have to get familiar with the style.None of us had studied last years clues and solutions... which are on the Washington Post website.

http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-srv/artsandliving/magazine/features/2009/post-hunt/index.html

We thought we had a chance going into "the end game" . . . and we did have most everything solved correctly. . . but when the sixth and final clue was released . . . it was waaaay different from the other clues and we didn't have a clue what we were doing. We had not even moved away from the stage. We were still contemplating what the clue meant when they announced they had a winner
It was a multi-layered task, waaaay outside of the box and required using the solutions you had picked up in the first five clues in solving a new puzzle to yield a phone number, listening to a cryptic message that tells you how to decipher a code... running three blocks to view a poster to decipher a picture clue... that led you to a map with animated picture clues... that led you three blocks away to solve the identity of someone standing on a particular street corner. The winning team (from DC) solved it in an unbelievable 22 minutes! They finished 2nd last year so they knew what they were doing. A team from Miami finished 3rd. A father daughter team from DC finished 2nd.

Wait till next year!

From: gallant_rogue | Date: 2009-05-18 15:51:40 UTC
We solved all of the initial five puzzles...(many people did) a couple of them we solved very quickly... and felt rather smug!But we had one where we solved the puzzle but interpreted the answer wrong; we added the numbers for the presidents 15 31 34 and got 80... which was not on the clue list and threw us off. The solution number they wanted was "153,134" which we kind of picked up on from another team's list.... so we had all five clues going into the End Game... but none of us got "E I E I O" as "01313" . . . I was just singing it backwards... NOT looking at it upside down... or "turned over" so we got nada nowhere zip on that last clue... we never made a move... like three deer in the headlights... we froze. The winning team had six members... and they solved & executed in 22 minutes. We will never know how long it would have taken us to solve that last one... they read the solution about 25 minutes after the gave the clue.

Next year we will be better prepared... Scarab will get a good night's sleep and show up on time... and you and Martini Man will be with us!
