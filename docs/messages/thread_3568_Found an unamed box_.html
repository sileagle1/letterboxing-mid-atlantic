Found an unamed box?

From: Marleah | Date: 2009-05-24 01:06:42 UTC
Today, after getting soaked in my first thunder storm, I was on the trail of M.C. Esher in Jacobsburg Park, north of Nazareth. Where I think the first box in the series should have been, I found a different box, not well concealed, but it had a stamp, log book & ink pad inside. However, the front of the logbook appeared to be missing so I have no idea if it was a letterbox & if so, which one. It was a store bought stamp of a cup of steaming tea.

Any ideas about this one?

Thanks.

Marleah

Re: Found an unamed box?

From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2009-05-24 11:28:47 UTC
Interesting, we've been over to Jburg a few times and had never heard of or seen that one. Cool find. Was anybody stamped in and such and what were the dates? If they are fairly recent, it may have been a girl scout troop earning one of their merit badges and they had not gotten around to posting clues. If it was long ago, it may have been a long forgotten box.

There are some old archives out there, such as the wayback machine:

http://www.archive.org/web/web.php

where you can see the contents of a specific website from long ago or there is the letterboxing basement

http://groups.yahoo.com/group/letterbox-basement/

Where pre 2003 boxes may be found. There were a couple boxes in Jacobsburg planted in 1999 or so, and they were restored by Silent Doug in 2004 and they were also in the southern park of the park near Henry's Woods (cool old growth trees, btw).

Consider it s serendipitous find, although it may have been a word of mouth box that the friends of the planter were not careful in their replanting. It could also have been a geocache hybrid (mostly store boughts in there and other tradeable detritus), but there only appear to be regular geocaches in that particular park.

Cheers,

LB
