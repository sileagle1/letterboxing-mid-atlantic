Any guidelines that only incidently reference letterboxing and ramble on about caches and geocaching are treated as optional in my view. If they have any valuable info I try to adopt. I did like the bit about any clue having directions back to the parking lot ... that is a safety aspect. However, I'll just abide by the "Unapproved caches will be removed or a Geocache Placement Application issued if deemed appropriate by the park manager." If I get an email to pick it up, I'll let them know if and when. But the critters and water will usually beat them to the removal.

Re: New VA State Park Guidelines

From: brendan_hickey13 | Date: 2009-06-22 22:04:12 UTC
Agreed, and it's instructive in regard to states with policies that don't yet specify a required frequency of maintenance checks - it could be coming. I keep thinking of the parental/managerial attitude of "Don't force me to address this issue because you won't like how I do it."

If your box is in decent shape then it may not actually need quarterly checks and if not, if it becomes a nuisance or trash, then quarterly checks are not enough. State policies don't allow for such nuances, though.

The other part of this that I noticed and disliked was the time limit of 3 years for a box location - after that, the box has to come out. To me, any box that can survive and be worthwhile after 3 years in a particular location is in a really good spot, one probably not easily replicated - safe enough, dry enough, etc. After 3 years with no compelling adverse environmental impact, in a successful location, the box simply has to move?

Re: New VA State Park Guidelines

From: brendan_hickey13 | Date: 2009-06-23 03:08:09 UTC
I agree with you, Sewsowbizzy.

If any kind of herd path forms then the park will pull the box or tell the placer to pull it, anyway, and that can happen in much less than a year.

In fact, considering the excitement typically generated by a new box, I would take the risk of herd paths to be greatest in the first few months.

I mostly disagree with this part of the policy because if the box is associated with any sort of environmental harm then the box will have to come out immediately, so it seems a reasonable assumption that any box enduring for 3 years has not led to harm to the immediate setting.

After 3 years, the box hasn't hurt anything or been destroyed or stolen by "the powers that aren't" - it's probably in a really good spot - so arbitrarily move it and hope that the new location turns out to be as good as the old one.
