RE: [lbma] Question- Night Letterboxing?

From: Debbie Kotlarek | Date: 2009-08-18 14:17:04 UTC

Our first experiences with night boxing fall into the category of “uh-oh, the sun is setting REALLY fast and we are still out here on the trail!”.  We’ve had to find/replace boxes and make our way back with only one of those key chain LED lights.  They do work in a pinch (but your fingers get tired of “pinching” them to keep them illuminated!)

 

Since those episodes we’ve intentionally gone out at night a few times, equipped with flashlights & head lamps.  We now carry both of these in our packs and periodically check to be sure they are still working since we never know when we’ll be out when that sun starts disappearing over the horizon.  We haven’t been too worried about bugs or animals, but did once see a skunk scurrying in front of us on a trail in DE.  We   s l o w e d down to let it get well ahead of us!  Main concern is authorities spotting us.  If we see a chance of this, OFF go the flashlights and we go into major stealth mode!  Act like a possum…

 

Wisconsin Hiker
