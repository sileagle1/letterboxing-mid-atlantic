Hope MizS has a cooling comfortable excursion.

From: sewsowbizzy | Date: 2009-08-24 01:42:50 UTC
No tours, but a few shandys to shake off the heat. The Halifax Alehouse was one of the few buildings left standing after the big explosion of 1917 in the harbor.

The owner clued me in that the drink to have in heat is a "steamer".
Steam milk (via capauchino machine), add some crush mint, and let cool just a bit so you don't burn your tongue. Raises body temp and mint is refreshing so the heat doesn't feel so bad.

And per the web it doesn't appear to have been sun poisoning (no nausea, not even tender, no blisters - just big red splotches, hot to the touch, and slightly itchy - and now in 5 days it is gone!?!)

Sound the trumpets . . . I posted the clues to the boxes I planted. Although I had a heck of a time trying to remember which stamp was planted in which city and which photos were my clue notes. 3 hrs and 5 boxes later, I now know why I only carve and plant and skip the posting!!

http://sewsowbizzy.tripod.com - no I'm not going to try and move all that stuff to sewsewbizzy.
