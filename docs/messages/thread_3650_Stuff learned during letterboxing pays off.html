Stuff learned during letterboxing pays off

From: rpboehme | Date: 2009-09-19 17:56:23 UTC
We have learned a lot from this nutty hobby of ours. Knowledge that is sometimes practical, too.

Last night, I arrived at 4:59 at PHL on a flight from Nashville, TN. ('twas out in the Paducah, KY area for work this week). I called MRs. Bug to let her know that I'd be beginning the drive home. She says, cool and then calls me back in about 5 minutes to let me know that I-95 south out of PHL is backed up from DE to the Airport, the Blue Route (I-476) is also backed up and that the Northeast Extension is basically shut down between Lansdale and Quakertown. Accidents galore, according to Mrs. Bug. This would have been the way home.

So, as we ride the bus (not the short one) to the long term parking lot, other passengers and I noticed how messed up 95 is and I decided that I would try I-95 north past Philly along the Delaware. I recall from boxing gatherings at Tyler SP that you can get from 95 to Tyler via 332 and from Tyler it is a little over an hour home to Alientown via Doylestown, Quakertown and the dreaded construction through Coopersburg (still messed up in case anyone asks). So, I decide that is the was to go. Luckily, boxing has trained me to keep a PA gazeteer under the passenger seat so enable boxing locales. I checked it and drove on my merry way. Left the lot at about 6:00 and made it home at 8:00 pm. Had there not been hellacious construction traffic in Coopersburg (again, avoid 409 through there at any cost), it would have been 7:50.

So, I did manage to call MizScarlet at the beginning of the adventure and waved at HnH as I passed Bensalem, gave a salute to Cyclonic's former stomping grounds as I passed Bristol and Cornwall Heights, and gave a yo to Cherish through Doylestown and felt itchy from ticks as I passed Peace Valley. I wondered, what eevil clue could I write for this misadventure.

Cheers,

LB
