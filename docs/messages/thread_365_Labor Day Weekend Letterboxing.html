Labor Day Weekend Letterboxing

From: Hikers_n_ Hounds | Date: 2004-09-09 00:27:18 UTC
I just wanted to elaborate a little on what a great weekend I had in WV. Several members of my caving club, including Mr. Irene, went with me to Spruce Knob to plant. But before I planted the boxes they were so tickled with our club logo stamp that Carvin' Carmen carved they all stamped their ID badges and pretty much everything else with it. Then they looked through my log and saw the 2 cavers have already been letterboxing with me and have their names in my log book. So several members insisted on going boxing and planting with me to get their names in my log. Cavers always have tons of bat stuff, stickers, temporary tattoos (I of course have the real thing, a HUGE bat tattoo on my....um nevermind) and...STAMPS! So they all grabbed different bat stamps and off we went. We found the two boxes already on "The Knob" and couldn't plant the Commander Cody Caving Club box until we were done hunting as we used that as our team stamp. We all stamped in with individual bat stamps, I used my personal travellor, Dancing Bear Bat, and finally we planted the boxes. Have you ever planted boxes with a bunch of people? Lot's of opinions, but they were so enthusiastic I let them call the shots. After all, the box was dedicated to them. 
 
Then on the way home Monday Mr. Irene and I stopped and planted the Windmills of Backbone Mountain box on Route 219. He was a excited as I was. We first saw these windmills last year when we took a new route home and totally freaked out they are so freakin cool. Check em out online sometime. www.themerrycat.com/windmills.shtml
Anyway, he has been boxing with me before but it was his first time planting. He really enjoyed it. Then the whole way home, FOR EIGHT HOURS, all I heard was "Hon, you could plant there. Hey was that a park, you should research it and plant there. Wow, we just crossed the Mason-Dixon line, and there's a Memorial, you should plant there. Look at the old cemetary...." and so it goes until I'm ready to smack him one real good! Nah, just kidding. He's too darn cute. Anyway, I liked his idea about the Mason Dixon box and will be doing that one soon. Once the election is over his political activites should slow down (please lord) and he's really looking forward to boxing with Dude and I some more. I am hoping he will poke his bald head in the NJ gathering for a little while but we'll see.  
 
Anyway, what a great weekend!

Labor Day Weekend Letterboxing

From: Cyclonic07@aol.com | Date: 2004-09-10 11:18:23 UTC
 
As usual, I am behind again :)
 
Anyway, I had a pretty good weekend.  I found my first mystery box on Friday, and a hard one at that, Mat Hatter's Hitchhiker Hostel.  The only thing was, I forgot to bring along my HH, so I will have to return.
 
On Saturday I did some box maintenance, as well as hunting.  I found both boxes of the Big Green Monster.  Great location, nice stamps, and the camo really hides them well.
 
I replaced the log (actually, the whole box but for the stamp) of my Bowman's Hill Tower box, after just two months it had already filled up with 33 log-ins.  Some were from as far away as Seattle (Amada) and Oklahoma (Kingfisher).  I was thrilled with it.
 
Well, I was busy the rest of the weekend, so that was it for my boxing adventures.
