From: Otis' Friends | Date: 2009-10-06 13:26:18 UTC
Not that this is of any interest to this group, since there are no letterboxes in the National Park (That would be wrong.), and everything will happen at night, but keep your eyes posted for updates.

"Valley Forge National Historical Park officials plan to begin killing more than 1,000 deer at the park over the next four years.

The park wants to reduce a deer population now estimated at 1,277 to around 175. The sharp-shooting operations could start as early as next month.

Opponents say the park is ignoring more humane options, like contraception.

Officials at Valley Forge, site of the Continental Army's 1777-78 encampment, say the herd is gobbling so many plants, shrubs, and saplings that the forest cannot regenerate.

The shooting would be conducted by federal employees or contractors firing high-powered, silencer-equipped rifles, mostly at night. The deer will be lured to areas baited with apples and grain."

From: Randy Hall | Date: 2009-10-08 13:18:46 UTC
> It is a dog-eat-dog world where nature mandates survival of the
> fittest. Now if only humans would apply that to ourselves and we could
> thin our own herd instead

Neglecting, of course, to point out that "nature" neither
defines "fittest", nor "weak", nor does anyone else, a point
often missed by those with this ideology (and others, I
suppose). For more interesting reading, one may wish to dig
up another letterboxer's thoughts on these matters (Kirby), and
my rebuttal.

Beyond that, I would suggest to the moderator that the thread is
moving towards uncomfortable off-topic. :) (and who am I to not
help it along)

But, while I'm here, I would suggest that BullDawg's logic could
be equally applied to suggest that you should let termites eat
your shelter rather than kill them. Interesting to note that
the best practice for killing carpenter ants is to bait them with
food that is actually poison that they take back to their nest in
an attempt to kill the whole nest. But, since these things are
farther down the being chain, it doesn't apply, does it?

Who knows? There seems to be a value judgement (at least among the
majority of respondents), that the biomass of Valley Forge vegetation
is more desirable than the biomass of Valley Forge deer (certainly
those who run thru the woods at VF on a routine basis would likely
disagree, but that seems tangential). Given that it seems likely
that the cervine biomass has more intelligence and consciousness
that the vegetable biomass, I would be hesitant to quickly make
such a judgement myself. (It would be interesting to look at the
biomasses in question and make value judgements based on caloric
potential).

(In any case, for more interesting arguments by smarter people,
one need simply to google "elephant culling" Africa).

So, what is one to do? I guess keep on keepin' on, and follow the
KISS principle. Drown me in the shallow water, don't let me get
too deep. Hope that I am fit enough to survive nature this day,
and hope that this putative creature postulated by BullDawg doesn't
eat me, 'cause that wouldn't be good, would it? In the mean time,
I won't kill any deer unless I need to to survive, but I will still
reach out and pet them as I run in VF, which, unbelievably, I do on
a regular basis, given how they have been so desensitized to
predation.

As a final note, there is a bear in French Creek as well. I've
even seen it. Pretty cool. :)

Cheers
Randy
all in good fun :)
