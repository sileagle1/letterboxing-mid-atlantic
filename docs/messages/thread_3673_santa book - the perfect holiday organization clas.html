in the last 5 years my interest in the hobby has gone up, but the quality of plants has gone down,,,I dont log finds or counts, but it has decreased every year.
 
where are the boxes about history, long hikes, scenic and unknown places?...where is the need for quality over quanity?....I just dont get the new direction..
 
And most say that it is from muggles, or from people tnat do interviews for newspapers...I havent seen those....So where is the new wave or sub-par boxes coming from?
 
I can say my region has lots of newbies with store-boughts, or horrible carvings and efforts.
It has become scrapbooking in the woods, and not for reasons most feared.,,I have not seen new carvings by the creative few in the area in a LONG time...LTC's and postals have helped with that, and that is not a letterboxing activity....Again, my issue with AQ, is the lil gadgets that have changed the game, and now with even newer buttons to report people, it has become more comical...we need no 'daddy'..we need no new rules...we need to stick by what made this great to start with...Say what you want, but AQ does and has altered the game....it has gone from clues, to a forum for cheap art
 
to each their own, but the game has taken many steps back in the years......not that all newbies are bad, but they just dont understand trash from treasure......

Heather Siple wrote:
> And yet, each time a newbie writes on this list asking how to make a good box, the pat answer is always to do what they think is best. No comments as to hand-carved vs store bought (except, perhaps that it is not critical). No comments about how cryptic the clues should be or how to plant the box. The answer is along the lines of "No rules, just right." That's what a lot of people love about boxing. However, the whole complaint about store-bought stamps, disappointments about very short hikes, etc. is very likely WHY AQ has some of its gadgets in the first place. In short, if you want the quality to improve, be constructive when people ask. If you don't care to be constructive when they ask, go to AQ, look at then icons you and be more selective in your boxing.
>
>

I think, in a lot of ways, you are very, very right. I often say that
there are no rules (beyond the common sense do no harm sort of stuff),
but we don't often talk about what really is a good box, or at least not
all at once. I think it has a lot to do with people not wanting to
discourage others.

The way I figure, you can do what you want in terms of planting, that is
fine, just don't expect me to go looking for the box, and I have gotten
much more selective about the boxes I go searching for.

So, what does make a good box? We actually do talk about that all the
time, great clues, location, wonderful stamp and presentation, being
clever and creative. These are the things that make great boxes, and
not planting for the sake of planting.

I do not think this is the fault of a website, I think it has more to do
with the lack of good examples and pushing folks to challenge themselves
to not only plant great boxes but to also go looking for great boxes.
It is all about attitude. We need to inspire people to plant what we
like to find, and stop finding the boxes we don't enjoy. Quality over
quantity. It is something I think i will be mulling over more and more.
