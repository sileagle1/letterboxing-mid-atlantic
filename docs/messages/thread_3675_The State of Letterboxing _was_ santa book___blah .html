I don't think that is really the problem either.

I like boxes that are just directions with a nice little walk in a nice
park. They are fun, get you out, and all.

I think the problem I have are the boxes that could be nice but are
not. Take, for instance, "Bruno (Off Leash)"
(http://www.atlasquest.com/showinfo.html?gBoxId=115455). We found this
box a few weeks ago while camping at the park. It is the only box at
the park which has several miles of nice, easy trails. Disappointingly,
the box is not along one of those trails, but instead it is right off
the parking lot right at the trail head. I mean, really, it doesn't
take much to actually go for a little bit of a walk and plant a box down
the trail. But they chose not too, and it really then seems to be a
waste, it is planting just for the sake of planting.

The same goes for another nearby box, "Cherries on Top"
(http://www.atlasquest.com/showinfo.html?gBoxId=77208). Great historic
location, lot of trails, and this too is right next to the parking lot.

These are the boxes that I find disappointing. It doesn't have to be a
long walk, or wonderful, clever clues, to be enjoyable. But these boxes
were planted simply for the sake of planting a box, and really, why
bother. It is these boxes and others like them that are making me a bit
more selective about what we go searching for.

It is also these type of boxes that make me think about what we are
doing wrong. Maybe I need to start planting very nice, well thought out
"Little Boxes." Boxes that have easy clues but along nice walks with a
nice box at the end. At the same time I think maybe I need to create
some that are more in shape with their surroundings, boxes that are
there for a reason, and not to simply be there to have a letterbox
there. I do think it goes both ways, there is learning to do on both sides.
