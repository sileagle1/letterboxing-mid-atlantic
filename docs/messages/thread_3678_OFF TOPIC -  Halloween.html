Kids here in eastern PA do miss out on a lot of the Halloween fun we had. In most townships (including ours) trick or treat is strictly regulated to be 6-8 PM on the Friday before Halloween (lame).

Back in the day in a suburban county just east of MissMoon's current home, we would get home and hit the streets in our local neighborhoods. Had about 2 hrs from 3-5 or so, when it got dark (remember, that DST ended in October in the olden times). Then it was a quick dinner and out again to a different neighborhood until people stopped answering their doors. Of course, shaving cream and powder socks. Never egged.

Kind of like boxing. Just one more - gotta get that last one! LOL

Just had our first few trick-or-treators at the door tonight.  Our suburb used to have trick-or-treating on a Sunday afternoon near Halloween, but our subdivision always did it at night.  Eventually the city realized most of the neighborhoods were doing it at night, regardless of the “official safe time” and a few years ago Halloween got switched to when it SHOULD be – on actual Halloween night, regardless of what day of the week it falls.  So yes, people CAN have an influence to get at least some things back.

 

(BTW – When we were kids, we also used paper grocery bags and pillow cases.  We’d stop at home to dump the contents on our beds when they got too heavy, then head out for more.  We’d have to hide the better things, especially Almond Joys, because my dad would always try to sneak them.)

Just had our first few trick-or-treators at the door tonight.  Our suburb used to have trick-or-treating on a Sunday afternoon near Halloween, but our subdivision always did it at night.  Eventually the city realized most of the neighborhoods were doing it at night, regardless of the “official safe time” and a few years ago Halloween got switched to when it SHOULD be – on actual Halloween night, regardless of what day of the week it falls.  So yes, people CAN have an influence to get at least some things back.

 

(BTW – When we were kids, we also used paper grocery bags and pillow cases.  We’d stop at home to dump the contents on our beds when they got too heavy, then head out for more.  We’d have to hide the better things, especially Almond Joys, because my dad would always try to sneak them.)
