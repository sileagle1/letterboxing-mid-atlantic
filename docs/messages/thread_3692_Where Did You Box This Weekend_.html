Where Did You Box This Weekend?

From: Nathan Brown | Date: 2009-11-30 02:02:18 UTC
In an on going effort to actually get out and hike, Schizokeet and I did
not box at all this weekend, but did have a very nice hike to Mary's
Rock (see www.penncoasters.com).

Last weekend we were up in Gettysburg where we planted a couple of boxes
and found a few others. Had a very good time for our last camping trip
of the year. "Bloody Shrapnel" is an excellent hike, and well worth
it. Of course, Pink Panther and Black Swan beat us to it the day before
(should have let us know you were to be in the area, I had extra
"drinks" at the campsite). That box was my highlight of the weekend,
just a wonderful hike. Caledonia itself is a very pretty park, think we
will have to return.

Today we did do a little box maintance. A very old box, "Clark's
Crossing," which can be found in the letterbox basement, is still alive
and well, but needed some help. I dried out the old logbooks and
provided a new logbook, as well as a new, watertight box (with luck). I
have one logbook left that had been wet in the box. I will return it to
the archive in the box in a few weeks. These books are very cool to
look through, and are a who's who of letterboxers in NOVA. It seems the
box was an early find for many, and it is really cool looking through
all the old stamps. Would love to see the box taken out of the
basement, don't know what the qualifications are that put it there, but
it is alive and now very well, and I hope some new folks get a chance to
go out and visit this classic.

--
Nathan Brown

Just contact the LBNA webmasters to let them know you have found it and are willing to adopt it.  You can then keep list the box under your name, but keep all the original clues and include the original planter info within the clues.  That’s what we did for a box we found in Hawaii that was relegated to the basement.  It has been found by MANY people since we resurfaced it back to the “main floor” of LBNA.

From: Otis' Friends | Date: 2009-11-30 17:13:42 UTC
That's one of Squirrel's boxes.  All of those have been placed into the basement, as have all the boxes by The Apple Dumpling Gang, Erik, and Firefly (and probably many others).  I assume the reason is that these boxers haven't logged into LBNA in several years and are presumably no longer active.  I can personally vouch for the availability of some boxes falling into this group.

I notice that no one has recorded an online find since 2007.  I don't know what Choi's algorithm does, but the one Green Tortuga uses assigns a "Retired" category based on the find record (but only for boxes planted by inactive users).  I see now that one of Squirrel's boxes still shows up on the main floor and has a recorded find of Feb.3, 2009.  Don't know if that made a difference or if someone else gave a nudge.  Some of Erik's boxes have more recent recorded finds and have still been moved to the basement.

I'm sure some enjoy the fact that few people think to mine the basement and other techniques for abandoned clues.  From Choi's standpoint, I can understand wanting someone to be responsible for each listing.  Good luck.
