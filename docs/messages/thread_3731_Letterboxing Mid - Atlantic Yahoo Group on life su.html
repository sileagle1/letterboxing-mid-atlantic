Is the list on life support, or is it just in its winter dull drums? I
don't know. I do know that postings are way down as compaired to the
past, and that is true in all letterboxing forums, be it here, other
local lists, the "Big List" and even on AQ.

Why is that? Sure, Facebook could be partially to blame. I know I have
not been blogging because instead I write a bit on Facebook, it is just
easier then writing the code for my blog, but really, life has just
gotten in the way.

And I think that is the real answer, or at least a bigger part of the
answer. Life changes for everyone. You can't always keep up with
everything. I know I can't, never been very good at it to begin with,
though I should try and be better.

I think another reason for the decline is that there are actually fewer
people letterboxing. People have tried it a few times, maybe planted a
box or two, and then went onto other things. I can tell you that just
by judging from the finds on some of my easiest letterboxes. There are
the "usual suspects" that find the boxes soon after they are planted,
and then it goes a very long time before there is another finder, and a
new finder is very rare. And again, not all of the "usual suspects" are
even getting out there. Heck, I am not even getting out there like I
used to. That whole life getting in the way thing again (though, even
now as I type, I am in the woods waiting for Schizokeet to get done work
so we can get some new boxes).

Maybe everything that there is to talk about has already been talked
about? And talked about. And talked about. We don't seem to talk
about issues of the right type of box, or carving. Nothing on great
locations or clue writing, or our weekend adventures for that matter.
We are all culprits in that. We just don't make the effort to start a
thread much about anything.

So, lets ask the question, where are you boxing this weekend?

Nathan Brown

From: rpboehme | Date: 2010-03-20 23:58:57 UTC
I think the dynamic of the yahoo boards (particularly the main list) were influenced by a couple of factors. AQ's ascent occurred roughly at the same time that many of the original creative souls of the hobby became disgusted by the abusive behavior of some of the folks on that particular list (some of whom were held in "high esteem" and occupied positions of great influence). Folks on LBMA used to laugh at the Main list's preoccupation with image and status (but pitied the abusive behavior - just look for any post by 'pokerman', 'barace' or one of his many abusive incarnations - IP addresses will show- and you will see some examples of what drove a lot of people away). It was kind of a harsh environment for the new boxer.

AQ provided a place where the new boxer was welcomed and many of the ideas and changes (while not necessarily letterboxing, were sort of semi related - like boxing buddies and LTC's) were adopted and enabled by a helpful site creator that only wanted to please folks (I do not fault Ryan in any way - he was trying to be good guy). So most new folks gravitated to an environment that was not hostile.

Many of those folks who were drawn to that environment left many of the yahoo groups behind, despite some local communities (such as LBGLK, LBMA for example) that actually were pretty inclusive to boxers of all stripes.

The advent of facebook have boxers yet another avenue to connect. So, perhaps that is another factor contributing to the decline of the participation in the yahoo boards.

Just some perspective (although some may not like the bug's particular perspective no matter what is postulated). ;-)

LB
