Yes that handsome letterboxer would be me (if indeed I were handsome!) about 1 minute 50 seconds into that video... but I would encourage everyone to watch all those videos to see how, from letterboxing standards.... these clues are clever but not impossible.... Cyclonic, Scarab and I solved all the puzzles but interpreted one wrong... so were didnt get the correct phone number at the end.... Where we goofed was at the Memorial to Failed Monuments... we added the three together 15 + 31 + 41 = "87" instead of placing them beside each other to get "153141" ... so when we got to the end... we had one solution wrong... given time we would have recovered . . . but the winning team followed the clue correctly and completed the last task in 18 minutes... so they took home the cash. (So close yet so far!) But the event was very very well run... it wasn't overly crowded like the video may seem . . . you could solve the clues in any order... you had three hours to work on six clues... the actors presentation were ongoing... you didnt have to be there on any schedule... you just walked around the central downtown area as you needed to explore.... it was not a lot of hiking... the magazine came free with the morning paper...

I suggest Scarab, Cyclonic & I each lead a team ... five or six on a team would be a good size.... then just before the finale... we come together and compare notes!
