Re: [lbma] Tyler question

From: Otis' Friends | Date: 2010-05-06 11:48:59 UTC
If you start from the southern entrance, there are quite a few not that far from a paved trail.  The exercise trail also has some nice ones.  There are even more in various parts of the park not that far from a horse trail - I'm not sure what the biking rule is there.  I can get an off-road jogging stroller within the vicinity of most of them.  Bikes will get you through the approach to the boxing areas more quickly, but then you'll have to improvise.
 
If you do, be prepared to get off and walk them, or leave someone on the paved trail to watch the bikes.  Also be aware there are a few not insignificant hills (such as Dairy Hill).

Indeed, they are more road then trail, and some lead to private
residences within the park.

The horse trails are supposed to be off limits to bikes, but this is
taken as more a suggestion rather a rule. Most boxes are not far from
the paved trails, and thus you would have little trouble even walking
your bikes in.

I have biked and boxed at Tyler, and it does work fairly well. The park
is pretty large, and the boxes are clustered in areas. It is best to
plan ahead of time, roughly figure out where the boxes are (last time I
counted there were better then 50 in the park), and use the bikes to get
from area to area quickly.
