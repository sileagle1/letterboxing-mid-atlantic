From: idlemoon2000 | Date: 2010-07-03 00:08:50 UTC
Well, not this weekend, but Wednesday and Thursday, the 4 Spigs (soon to be BlueDelft), and I boxed Ridley Park in PA... We are newer to letterboxing, and this park is literally in my back yard. We planned to box it together but the timing was always wrong. The planets all aligned, we had fab weather. The letterboxer gods shined on us.

So many amazing boxes there! We were especially stoked to find all 4 boxes in RedHatBxr's "Color Me Red". There were boxes in that series that were marked "attempted" 7 times, last logged in 2007!

Many thanks to those who planted, many of which post here! Amazing!

Then Wednesday night we met Maiden et al at a mini meet and greet! What a fantastic week for letterboxing.

Thanks for letting me share!

Congrats to you, the way those women write clues, it is a wonder anyone
finds their boxes. I think it has something to due with the drunkiness
while planting ;-)

While in Western PA last week we found but three boxes (actually, just
one in PA, the others were in NY). I will say, reading the clues for
boxes more recently planted in the Altoona area has me salivating for a
return, and the one, "Red Arrow Train Wreck," we did find there was
outstanding. We just did not have the time, what with all the roller
coaster hopping and get soaked by some gigantic waterfall (10,000
gallons a second will do that). We did, however, find the time to plant
on a nice five mile hike, clues to follow.

Oh, did I mention Texas? It was a great trip as well, and we did find a
few more boxes down there, including one that had not been found in
sometime and with many attempts on it. Seems those Texans just are not
persistent enough. Fortunately, Schizokeet is. I planted there as
well, this time with a cuckoo clue. Figure I will keep those Texans on
their toes.

We met with Maiden and company last night as well. Great group of
folks. Many thanks to X for scouting out and finding a much, much
better location for a dozen folks for dinner. The original plan, Johnny
Rockets, was far too small, and then I commented that we could not do
the meet in the food court as we had been kicked out of there a few
years ago, besides, it was very, very crowded. Siné was a great choice.

I think that is all for now, I hear a Lager (it has a first name, or so
I've heard) calling my name.
