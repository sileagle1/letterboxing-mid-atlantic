Frances Slocum State Park

From: Otis' Friends | Date: 2010-08-03 16:29:21 UTC
For those of you who haven't heard, all trail blazes of all colors at this state park west of Joe Biden's hometown have apparently been painted over with road paint yellow.  However, a preliminary investigation has shown that at least some letterboxes in the park are still very findable.

Now for an anecdote that is an actual experience and not a clue.  We had just finished covering up a box last weekend and were walking back to the trail when we saw a middle-aged couple leaving the trail and heading toward the general area where we had been.  As I walked down the trail, I started wondering if they were potential muggles or other letterboxers looking for the box.  In the interest of protecting the box and/or getting an exchange, I turned around and headed back.  My vision is not that good and we were some distance away, but they didn't appear to be all that interested in the letterbox.  Further embarrassment was avoided when they walked behind a large rock formation.

Some minutes later, as I was stamping in/first aiding another box further down the trail, I heard an ATV driving down the other side of the rock formation, playing loud music.  (Think something along the lines of what you would expect the Xtreeeme! sports enthusiasts to listen to in Harold and Kumar Go to White Castle, rather than what was actually in the tape deck.)  Later still, when we returned to the parking lot, we saw flashing lights and a ranger writing a ticket.  And there they were.  As we were leaving the park, saw a black and white heading toward the parking lot.

Let this be a cautionary tale.
