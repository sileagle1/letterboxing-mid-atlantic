Letterboxing This Weekend

From: Nathan Brown | Date: 2010-08-29 23:07:41 UTC
You know, it is nice to get out and just spend a few hours at a local
park finding some boxes that you have just not gotten to yet. That was
today's trip to Red Rock Wilderness Overlook Regional Park (say that
three times fast, real fast ;-). there were a bunch of boxes there that
neither Schizokeet nor I had gotten, including "Erik's Box" by
Waterbird, which had been there since April 2003, and was in good shape
with a full log book.

Koyote's "Myths and Legends- Blodeuwedd" has a new logbook, though I
would like to see the old one if it still survives. Also found was
Sprite and Highlander's "Jukebox Life Lessons #36 - Smoke On The Water."

We searched for a mystery box there as well, but with no luck. I think
it is there, just did not work the clue out correctly. I'll have to
give it another try again.

Nearby we visited Edward's Landing Park for another Koyote box, "He Made
Me Do It," which was right where it should be, with a full logbook
though. This park was very nice and deserving of more exploration,
maybe a few more boxes, and with the very nice pavilion they have, maybe
even a future gathering.

Of course, a day of boxing deserves a nice belly up to the bar at a
local pub, so we had a late lunch at Vintage 50, a micro brew in
Leesburg. Good food and beer, though a bit pricey.

So, hope everyone else had a nice weekend, next week we will be in
Altoona, doing some camping, classic amusement parks, catching an
Altoona Curve game, and some letterboxing as well.

As a bonus, here is a clue to be made pubic soon:

http://www.facebook.com/photo.php?pid=1411919&l=4c3af5f76c&id=1330520506
