Nattybumppo,

Boy are you preaching to the choir here! I respect what MaryAnn says
and doubt she is one of those referenced in another email
as "Arrongant Equestrians" or something like that. But I met one
this weekend!On Sunday Dude and I were way the heck back on trail
and I had him off leash, which I know is bad but he doesn't leave my
side except to protect us both from the dangerous squirrels, which
he never catchs. He just enjoys running and barking at them, then he
comes right back. Anyway, Dude and I were sitting in a remote field
stamping into a box when out of the woods comes a horse. I had to
quick get him on leash in case he decided to wander up and say hi
to the horse. I didn't him to want to spook it. Well the rider gave
me a talking to about him being off leash. I explained to her that I
dod keep him on leash most of the time and this was the exception,
however I did not see any horse signs posted on any of the trails so
I assumed horses were not allowed in the park. Her response
was "well, they never said we can't be here" I explained that I
carefully read all signs with regard to horses, bikes, and dogs so I
know what to expect. I saw dogs signs everywhere asking owners to
clean up, which I do. So right then her horse takes a huge dump and
Dude and I are staring at here. So I told her I had lot's of paper
towels and zip locks so she could clean that up. She laughed and
said no one expects horse owners to do that! So Dude and I just
walked away from her in disgust and I was really thinking about
contacting park management about it.
Another one of my pet peeves are the canadian geese. People feed
these pests so that they have given up their natural migratory
behavior and many are now year round park residents where they crap
everywhere and severely fowl the local waters. And I show up with
Dude, who loves to bark at them, and get dirty looks from
the "enablers" feeding these year round loafers. I actually had a
confrontatin with an elderly person who said I should now have my
dog at Pennypack Park, Pine Road entrance, if he is going to bark at
the geese. I explained to her that he is just barking and that at
least I clean up after him. I asked to look around where she was
standing, it was disgusting! And the little creek there is getting
so gross!

Well, enough ranting for me. It's not safe for a woman of my
advanced years to get so worked up this late at night!

I don't know Nat, I don't think there is an answer for you but I'll
see if I can get one.

Still posting from the LBMA Group

Irene

From: MaryAnn Lockard | Date: 2004-09-15 10:07:14 UTC
One of my pet peeves is people feeding wild animals.
At the beach it's the sea gulls. When we were in
Yellowstone last year the rangers have a saying "
A fed bear is a dead bear". We then applied this to
all the wild animals out there. It was amazing to
watch parents oohh and ahh over how cute it was when
the kids fed the chipmunks potato chips. Anyway trail
use. I think we all need to be aware of the rules for
multi use trails so they can stay that way. One of the
trails I use for ridding is maintained by a "hunt" for
equestrian use but they also allow walkers with dogs.
As a rider I have had several close calls with
mountain bikers. Both bikes and horses can do a lot
of damage to trails not meant for their use.
