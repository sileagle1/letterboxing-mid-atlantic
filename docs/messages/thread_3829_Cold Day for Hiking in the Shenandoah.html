Cold Day for Hiking in the Shenandoah

From: Nathan Brown | Date: 2010-11-28 01:36:30 UTC
As was the case last year, we managed to find a cold, blustery day to
take a little walk in the Shenandoah.

It was just a short 2.5 mile hike up to Mary's Rock, where we did not
stay too long on account of the wind blowing heavily off the western
side of the ridge. It was a very nice day out though, and worth it.

On the way back we managed to find a few boxes. An old box, Old Man
Winter, was still found in fine shape near Sperryville, VA. It was just
a short walk, but since being planted five years ago, has had very few
finders. The first finders (who reside on this list) didn't find it
until two years after it was planted.

A new box was planted just last week at the Narmada winery, which is
fairly new in it's own right. So a quick, cold find followed by a
tasting and fire to warm up.

Not a bad short day, and we were home for lunch.

Oh, and I planted a box that I have been planning for a year now to
plant, clues forthcoming.
