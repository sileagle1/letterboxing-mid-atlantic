Chayda shel Chanukah - Night 2

I have a little dreidel,
I made it out clay,
And when I tried to spin it,
It gave me a "gimel" all day!

One of the most enjoyable parts of Chanukah is playing with dreidels, four sided
spinning tops. There are letters on each of the four sides: Nun,
Gimel, Hei, Shin. These letters stand for the
words Nes Gadol Hayah Sham  "a great miracle happened there", referring to the
miracle of Chanukah.

I have a special dreidel whenever I spin it, it always lands on "gimel",
meaning that I win! However, this weighted spinning top doesn't do the same for
everybody else. After having some of my friends spin my dreidel 1E99 times, I
found out that:

1) The chance of landing on a "shin" is three times greater than the chance of
landing on a "hei".
2) The chance of landing on a "nun" is half the chance of landing on a "shin".
3) The chance of landing on a "nun" is the same as the chance of rolling a two
on a single, unloaded die.
4) The dreidel always lands on one of its four sides.

Now for some data
C: The inverse of the probability of landing on "gimel" or "hei"
D: The inverse of the probability of getting nothing

"Daily Location":
- The number of sides with letters on a dreidel multiplied by the number of
pennies in a dollar.
- The first word of the name of the National Park located in Omeo.
- la calle

- Google, placemarks, lines, yay!

A micro box will be planted at this location shortly. It may or may not be
necessary to find this "MB" if you wish to find the final box.

Chag Sameach!
MMM
