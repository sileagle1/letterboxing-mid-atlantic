Surface prep for new PZ cut

From: mizscarlet731 | Date: 2011-01-20 22:20:48 UTC
Just got my 1st slab of the new PZ cut. Haven't started to carve but not so wild about it so far. Have heard a few times it doesn't take ink well. Rubbed surface with acetone, less shiny but when I tried a marker on it the ink just beaded up. Scrubbed surface with some abrasive polishing cloth I have for jewelry, same result. I was reluctant to resort to sandpaper, didn't want too rough a surface.
If I have to do so much prep to get it ready to carve I'm thinking of going back to the pink stuff, but I love the white background for transfer.
What are some of the experiences other have had with this stuff?
MizS

Re: Surface prep for new PZ cut

From: cwolf3030 | Date: 2011-01-21 11:46:20 UTC
I used 600 grit sand paper to prep the slab of new PZ. It took a laser jet transfer with xylene well. Carving the stuff with a knife can be a bit trying because the pieces you are cutting out don't break off easily like they did with the old PZ. I didn't notice any problem with inking but the one stamp that I have done on it so far was (like many of my stamps) an image with mostly small lines of inked areas, think coloring book image. I did try using a gouge on the stuff to but you need to apply more pressure than with other materials. You will have to be careful not to "slip" when pushing on the gouge.

Hope this helps

Chuck
