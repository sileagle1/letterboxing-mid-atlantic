From: mizscarlet731 | Date: 2011-01-23 14:14:20 UTC
Got my first sheet of the PZ cut firm cut, I think is called. Prepped surface with sandpaper and acetone. As Chuck said it is a lot harder than the previous PZ or the pink stuff. I used both exacto and gouge. Gouge does need more pressure and if I wasn't extra careful I over shot end of line. So my first carve has a few boo-boos.
I already had a transfer done on the old PZ(my last sheet I have been hoarding) so after I finished other carve went back to work on it. Well what a difference. After carving the harder stuff it was hard to adjust back to a softer material. I could get used to the new PZ cut, it's just learning to adjust my carving style.
Noticed Dixie has a carving material listed on her site, any one know anything about it?
MizS

I have experimented with some of the Dixie stuff that Chuck from Soulmates donated to me. Of the different ones, I am still trying to figure out the 'Smooth Cut' stuff - it is granular like the cheapest dollar store eraser, but no ability to heat transfer copier toner images to it. The more intriguing stuff was the material that had the black tire backing - similar in consistency to the pink stuff, but slightly more granular. RE: [lbma] Re: adventures in carving

From: Debbie Kotlarek | Date: 2011-01-27 04:03:33 UTC

Martini Man & I won free samples of the 3 Dixie materials at an event and I also received a sample of another experimental material last year.  Here were our comments after we carved on all of them last April:

 

OK, I finally carved on the last of our new sample materials. Here are our comments (we both like to carve with blades on PZ, so that influences our perceptions):

Staedtler-Mars eraser
Martini Man - Very soft like Master Carve. Tooled OK, but you had to be careful as the softness could allow you to cut too much.

Dixie Brown
Wisconsin Hiker – a bit too soft, transfer OK w/acetone. Prefer a better contrast such as black on white media, but it wasn't as bad as I expected to see black on brown. Inked and stamped well with Versafine black pigment ink.
Martini Man - Not as soft as Staedtler-Mars eraser material. But definitely softer than other media. Seems like it cut OK. I liked well enough.

Dixie Thin White
Wisconsin Hiker – Firm, but “drier” than PZ, transfer OK w/acetone but tended to smudge/smear while carving. Too thin – danger of cutting all the way through when creating white space. Needs to be mounted, which is a definite negative for us. Inking was Iffy at first, but was good after wiping stamp with acetone to remove toner. However surface still feels “fuzzy”.
Martini Man - VERY stiff, like the orange PZ-Kut. Probably would be good for detailed carves. Very hard to cut. You need a sharp blade and I could feel real resistance from the media.

Dixie White on Black
Wisconsin Hiker – Weird to have everything dark after you are done. Easy to see any remaining “high spots” since they are white, but harder to differentiate edges of image. Transfer OK w/acetone. Inked and stamped well with Versafine black pigment ink.
Martini Man - Also very stiff. Same impressions as Thin White stuff.

Smooth Mtn Biker MA material
Wisconsin Hiker – Transfer OK w/acetone. Carved nicely, perhaps a little bit softer than PZ, but pretty good. This was my favorite UNTIL I went to stamp the finished image. The ink beaded up on the material, creating numerous white speckles in an area that should have been solid black. I tried several times with no change in results. I then cleaned with acetone and it was a bit better, but still not good. I don’t want to mess around w/sandpaper and had no desire to sand my finished image for fear of damaging it. Final stamp is OK since it is small, but ink beading is a real negative.

 

 

Wisconsin Hiker
