Washington Post article recommends letterboxing

From: paper4trail | Date: 2011-03-10 15:06:30 UTC
Jay Mathews' CLASS STRUGGLE column features 'School is out, Learning is in - how to make the most of spring break.' #3 is "Go letterboxing: For some adventurous families, this might be as familiar as hiding eggs at Easter, but it was new to me. Susan Fontyn, a media specialist at Richie Park Elementary School in Rockville, suggested it. This treasure hunt-like activity was invented on the moors of southwestern England in the mid-19th century, but here is how the modern version works: Hobbyists hide waterfroof plastic boxes in enjoyable settings, such as a park, then give clues online to find it. You take a notebook, a rubber stamp that represents you in some way and a stamp pad. When you find the box, you stamp the notebook inside it and use the stamp inside the box to mark your book. There are many boxes in this area. All you need to know to get started is at www.letterboxing.org."

I think most of the "seven year +" letterboxers in the greater Washington Metropolitan Area (DC, Maryland, No Virginia) got introduced to letterboxing by a Washington Post article in the Weekend section. I think this is the best argument for the use of the "find" restrictions feature on AQ. Some boxes are valuable works of art and deserve protection from rookie mistakes.

From: Wisconsin Hiker | Date: 2011-03-13 04:01:37 UTC
I post all my clues on LBNA with NO restrictions, just like the clues we found in 2003 when we started boxing. I enjoy getting notes from people who found their box, similar to this one I received 2 weeks ago:

Hello Wisconsin HIker! We found your box. It was a great place and I love the stamp. We originally went to the wrong part of the park and did a 2 mile hike. About 1.5 miles into the hike we realized we weren't in the right part, but it was a great hike anyway. We then went to the correct starting spot, and we found the box with no problem. We also picked up the HH clue and plan on replanting it soon. This was our first box to find as we just started letterboxing! Thanks!

I like to make my boxes available to everyone.
