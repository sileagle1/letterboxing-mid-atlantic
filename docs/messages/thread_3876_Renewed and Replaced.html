Renewed and Replaced

From: Nathan Brown | Date: 2011-04-18 23:03:08 UTC
This weekend I was camped pretty close to a few boxes at Cape Henlopin,
so I would be remiss in not reporting on them.

I believe that Ft. Miles Tower is gone. Psychiker and Syner maybe able
to confirm this, as I think they were there to check on their box ahead
of me and there were a good number of fresh footprints surrounding the
tree in question.

On a happier note I did find Lighthouse Series Box 1, though it had seen
better days. The box had a big old hole in the top of it, and someone
had recently put a bag around the outside of the box (the bag was very
clean) but this had a hole in it as well and the box was filled with
water. I removed the box and before leaving this morning replaced it
with a new box as well as a fresh logbook, though it is lined, I did not
have my stuff with me this weekend. It should survive a few more years now.

I had a great weekend and will write more on it later, and clues to a
new box are on their way as well.

Cyclonic

From: idlemoon2000 | Date: 2011-04-19 00:13:12 UTC
We missed finding Ft. Miles tower, I swear the three of us looked for an hour for that box! We had a lot of fun looking for the answers for the clue.

We had found the lighthouse box as well, a complete mess! The old (assumed) pink log book was soaked. We replaced it as we found it (outside baggie and all) and emailed Jessica about it's condition. We searched for a lot of classic boxes this week and ran out of pre-made log books and freezer baggies. Would have replaced and or repaired if we had any supplies left over. I had hoped we would run into the planter at her family's diner to report on the classic.

Did anyone go looking for SSB's Cape Henlopen Dunes box? Looked for that as well, but did not look very long because we were being battered by the wind up on the dunes. I was really looking forward to finding it.

Lisa
