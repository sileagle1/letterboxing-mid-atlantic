Ft Miles Tower

From: brendan_hickey13 | Date: 2011-04-19 13:51:51 UTC
Ok, so here is the abridged version of recent events:

Found by new boxer a few weeks ago
Could not be found by other boxers during gathering weekend


The new boxer replaced the box under the correct tree, but in a spot different from where it had been. Because the last previous find was our box check in February, the new boxer found the box in the place indicated on the clue but did not put it back there - was close, about 8 feet away. It took us about 10 minutes of circling the tree before we found it.

So, Ft. Miles Tower is alive and well. That is very cool.

However, we moved it to different tree in the hope of preventing future confusion. I will update the clue today.

Because this is the second time that something like this has happened to this particular box, we are also going to put a small find count restriction on the clue. We've never done that before and we still don't like the idea but it looks necessary right now.

We say that our hobby does not have rules. Really, though, it has conventions, including double-bagging logs and putting a box back where you found it unless that's a really bad idea, in which case you put it in a safe place nearby and notify the placer immediately.

Anyway, that's the story. Thanks, everyone who contributed to this mystery.

While the site may be easier to use, and may have the appearance that it was made for kids, it also doesn't populate first on a Goggle search, LBNA does.  AQ comes up third, it has for a very long time, and in a search, which would you click on first?

From: Debbie Kotlarek | Date: 2011-04-20 14:19:39 UTC

That's probably true, but it's not necessarily new boxers causing a problem.  It's more likely boxers that focus on something "easy" where they don't need to think too much or do much work.  I've had people in various parts of the country tell me that LBNA is "too hard" and that they therefore don't look for clues there. 

 

I also have a box in a very popular vacation area in WI.  It's been in place for over 5 years and the last time I checked on it there were LOTS (80-90) of people logged in, of which only ~20% had electronically logged finding it.  From reading the comments, it was obvious that many new & casual letterboxers had visited it.  I was happy to provide some fun for them and didn't care that they hadn't electronically logged it somewhere.
