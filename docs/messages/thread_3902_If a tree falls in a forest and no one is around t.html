From: audcahilly | Date: 2011-07-09 14:15:16 UTC
I haven't been near a fall when it happened, but my hubby is a forensic arborist/court expert witness, so I live with these stories daily.
What I've learned:
If the tree falls off the beaten path somewhere, so what?
If the hazardous tree had been threatening to fall (as determined by a "reasonable and prudent person") on a path, and does, and does injury, the property owner is liable.
If the target is a campsite, bench or other place where people could be expected to be at rest, not moving, the property owner is toast.

Look up before you stand still.

Aud

I've seen news stories about a few recent fatalities, mostly in camp grounds, besides the one that I found about a death along the Radnor Trail. There are lots of good reasons to pitch a tent in a clearing rather than beneath a tree.

There are circumstances under which I will not go into the woods. Mostly, if the winds are high, especially if it's the first really windy day in awhile, or definitely on a windy day after several days of heavy rain, then I will find something else to do.

The drive to the park is probably more dangerous than the hike, but why take the chance?
