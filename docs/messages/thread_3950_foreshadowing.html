foreshadowing

From: brendan_hickey13 | Date: 2012-09-02 00:04:52 UTC
As a retired English major, I love foreshadowing.

This should not be confused with Groundhog Day or anything else done in burrows.

We were out to check on our Haunted Inn series - been awhile - and when I lifted the pack, I realized that I'd left the first aid kit on the sofa, where it would keep the sofa nice and safe in case of injury.

I said, "The first aid kit is on the sofa, keeping it safe, so no injuries on this hike."

We were close to the first box when I noticed the very active ground wasp nest - like 15-20 of the grouchy little buggers coming and going in the few seconds that I stood nearby and watched and recommended retreat.

I was so attuned to the ground wasps (by the way, they get pretty nasty when they know that cold weather is coming, like now) that I missed the stinging nettle behind me.

Oops.

Caught that sucker on bare calf, felt like a wasp sting at first, but it wasn't. After that, about every 6 steps, "OWW!"

Syner, who had innocently walked right by the nest, walked into greenbriar on the way out.

So, Haunted Inn is probably still out there, great series to check/seek around Halloween.
