Pouches

From: Maryann Lockard | Date: 2012-11-03 12:44:10 UTC
What is the general consensus on these things? I can't stand them. Have found a bunch recently and just do not care for them. Why? Well they are messy, if it has rained recently mud just clings to them, dirt flies everywhere when you open them. You have to be super careful when you open them so as not to damage zip on bag. I wonder what will happen to them in freezing weather when plastic tends to become brittle. Closing them is often a nightmare trying to get the zip lock closed. Yes they do fit in small spaces but that's about all I can say.
MizS

Re: [lbma] Re: Pouches

From: Katrina Lustofin | Date: 2012-11-03 13:55:37 UTC
When I moved to SE Ohio, I was surprised at how many of those pouches are in this area.  It turns out one of the most prolific planters in the area uses them and now every one here thinks that is the norm.  Personally, I am not fond of them.  I agree with MizS that they are messy.  I find that mud and gunk always ends up in under the flap, which is somehow much messier than just on the outside of a box.

Plus, most of the time I find it a tight fit for the stamp and logbook so it is very difficult to leave a hitchhiker in a pouch, which means I end up carrying a hitchhiker for much longer (and sometimes end up forgetting I have it once I find an actual box).

On the other hand, they are probably a lot cheaper than buying good boxes so maybe it allows more plants?

Katy

In June we vacationed in the Smokies, and down there folks just seem to
love those pouches. Don't like them either, they are always messy,
often wet inside, and too prone to failure. It is too easy for the
zipper to break or for it to not be sealed correctly. The effort to
make them, to me, seems to outweigh the savings over Lock&Locs, which if
you hunt around online, you can get at pretty good prices.

From: sowbiz | Date: 2012-12-07 13:52:13 UTC
Haven't been on the LBMA board in months!!
But I am one of the "pouchers" . . . but only when I have to fly - since I also have a very large stash of Dougerware and LnL knockoffs. But with one checked bag at 50LB, and 7 (Italy) - 30 (AK & PNW) stamps to haul, pouches are my only option. Oh, and I just left 4 in Austin. . . . Now where are those clue notes???

One thing I noticed, is that if the duct tape covers the zippy part it is next to impossible to get it to re-zip. I always leave the zippy part free and make a bigger flip-flap. Maybe folks shouldn't plant them in the mud!

But I agree with Zorket -- when I get to the 'box' and it is a pouch, I still want it!
