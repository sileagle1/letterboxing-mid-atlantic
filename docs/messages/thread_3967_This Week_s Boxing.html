This Week's Boxing

From: Nathan Brown | Date: 2012-11-27 01:59:04 UTC
We've really been getting out a bit.

On Thanksgiving morning we left very early and stopped by Brandywine
Creek State Park to go "Into The Woods." I had found the series several
years ago, but Schizokeet had never found it. It really is a great
series, and we had fun, even her singing as Rapunzel did each time we
passed one of the towers.

While there we found "Love Bench" as well as one other box (I rather not
give it away). We managed to eke out four miles on the morning. We
have been trying to do more.

On the way home from dinner we made a quick stop to find a box hidden in
the Delaware rest area. Nice enough box, but no logbook? There was
plenty of room where it was hidden (even though it was in a hidious
pouch, at least it is in a protected location), and it is not part of a
series, so I don't see a reason not to have one.

Today I went looking for "Day Hiker." It is along an easy stretch of
the AT out of Keys Gap on the VA/WV boarder. I found it, the first to
do so in two years. Box was in great shape. The walk was indeed easy,
with a rewarding view as well.

This leads to something interesting. We found a HH in DE which I left
in "Day Hiker." I did not even look at it until I went to plant it.
The HH originated from this area, managed to move around a little here
before jumping to CA, then very soon back to PA, DE and finally right
back where it started. Can't think that that happens too often.

I then made my way to Harpers Ferry where I found a few more boxes,
including "Screaming Jenny," "The Parable of the Purloined Peaks," and
"VB Travelin' WV." I did not do any of the longer hikes there, saving
that for the next trip, though I did park outside town and walk in,
roamed around a bit, and stopped at the ATC HQ to have a look.

On the way home I found "My Friend Oscar," with a nice stamp, very cool
logbook, but a location that, while for the box it made sense, it really
was just a parking lot box.

It is nice to get back out on the trail, and I feel that I am extending
myself more each time. It is also nice to get a few boxes that have
seen very little in the way of traffic and give them a little love.

--
Nathan Brown
