From: Hikers_n_ Hounds | Date: 2004-09-19 00:13:22 UTC
Welcome!

Where are you folks located? What state or area? Yes,
there are lot's of places for clues other than
Letterboxing.org but you pretty have to find them
yourself for the most part. I'm not being deliberately
cryptic but folks who don't post their clues on the
website usually want you to have to figure out how to
find them, such as search the internet, etc. I only
use the website so can be no help to you with that.

A great list for you to join also is
newboxers@yahoogroups.com - An excellent resource for
us newer boxers still figuring out this hobby.

Welcome again and hope to meet you on the trail!

Hikers & Hounds

worse....welcome to the addiction! At least it's a
healthy one.

From: cahillymom | Date: 2004-09-19 12:36:04 UTC
Welcome!
The lbna site is "the big one", but there's a new one going now too,
(quick, Doug, what's the name of Ryan's site?)
AND, when lbna changed the site to a database format a while ago, a
bunch of clues didn't get switched over, and were put in
the "basemant" at:
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/letterbox-basement/
There's a greater risk of finding clues to missing boxes there, as
if the placer didn't bother moving the clues, there's a chance they
aren't even boxing anymore and the boxes aren't maintained, either,
but there are some great "old" boxes there, too.
