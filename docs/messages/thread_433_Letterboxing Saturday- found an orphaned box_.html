Letterboxing Saturday- found an orphaned box.

From: Manda | Date: 2004-09-25 21:46:02 UTC
Well, I went out and found me some letterboxes today. I went to Lake
Anna State Park in Spotsylvania, VA. Something interesting
happened. I found the letterbox I was looking for and I recognized
that it would be a good place for a geocache, so I started looking
around. I left my GPS and all the coordinates at home so I could
concentrate on boxing. I didn't find a geocache, but I found another
letterbox. If I remember correctly, it was about 2 years old. I was
the first person to sign in because the clues aren't posted
anywhere. The box is in okay shape. Unfortunately, the placed
wrapped it in Duct Tape, probably to keep critters from chewing it.
The note in it said that it was a replacement box for the one the
critters ate. So, I'm thinking she took the clues down, replaced the
box, and then never posted the clues again. The duct tape has done a
number on the container, which is a tiny gladware container. The log
book was damp. I carry snack sized ziploc bags with me, so I did a
little bit of first aid to it.

Have you ever stumbled upon a letterbox. This box is less than 50
feet from another box, but I know it was there first. The other
boxes in the park planted by this boxer have been adopted by Scarab,
so I'm assuming that she no longer boxes. I'm going to try and
contact the placer. If I don't find her, would it be a no no to post
the clues for this box and adopt it?

Besides those two, I found 1 other in the park. I tried to find
another "over the river and through the woods". But after walking 2
miles I still didn't see a posted sign in the clues, so I'm thinking
I wasn't in the write place. I was tired and went home. I may go
out tomorrow with my boyfriend, get the other two boxes and caches in
the park.

I had fun, and I am POOPED. I walked about 7 miles total. There was
a triathalon going on in the park so it was slow getting about.

Lemme know what you think about that box?

Re: Letterboxing Saturday- found an orphaned box.

From: Mike Brooks | Date: 2004-09-28 18:23:56 UTC
For those not familiar with letterbox-basement, that group is
basically a "message database" of clues that were unclaimed (neither
the original owner created an account nor did anyone else adopt the
clue) during the changeover from webmaster-entered clues to box
owner-entered clues. That process was ongoing for most of 2003 with
the unclaimed clues finally being purged from the site and into the
basement in the fall.

My opinion is that any letterbox that wound up in the basement is
absolutely abandoned. If the owner didn't participate in the
changeover process themselves, then they are almost certainly out of
the letterboxing hobby now. There might be an exception out there,
but I doubt it.

My opinion is that you should either adopt that lEtterbox or clean
that lItterbox out of the woods.

Mike
