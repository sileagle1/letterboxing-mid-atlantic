I Love Letterboxing and the Extra Experiences They Give Me!

From: Jan | Date: 2004-09-26 19:56:01 UTC
I love letterboxing, I love letterboxing, I love letterboxing!!!
This was an absolutely perfect weekend here in Southeastern PA!
Yesterday I took my daughter, who is pregnant, along on the
Underground Railroad series in Lancaster. We found the first two
boxes and were walking around the graveyard for the third one. I
could tell she was tiring so I told her that ahead of us was a
tombstone with two steps in front of it and she could sit there and
rest while I looked for the box. Well, when I got her settled, I was
stunned to find that the grave was for President James Buchanan, the
only President to come from Pennsylvania. How excited I was, I had
no clue that he was buried there! And that was only experience number
one.
Today, is a perfectly beautiful autumn day and I convinced my
boyfriend, who is not an avid letterboxer, to take our little red
Firebird convertible out to do the Bridges of Lancaster County tour.
When we finally reached the covered bridge that matched the
description for the letterbox, I got out to find it and ran into an
Amish family beside the bridge. We got to talking and when I
explained to them what I was doing, they were very interested and
helped me to find the box. Then they walked back to our car and
offered to ride along with us and show us the park and other points
of interest in the area. How neat is that! They got to ride in a
convertible and we saw things that we never would have seen without
their help and in addition, got to exchange ideas with people from a
culture very different from ours.
....in case I didn't mention it before, I love letterboxing!

Jan

Nope, they were all Amish. We discussed it when I was stamping the
book. She told me about her home with no electricity and that they

clues. I have lived in Amish country my whole life and never would

suggested showing us around. Her husband wouldn't get in the car,
just the wife and one of the children. I think the allure of a
little red convertible was too much for her!

Actually, I live in one of the 3 school districts you drive through

too dangerous (and spread out) for the Amish to completely stay
buggy bound in Lancaster County.

Today, Mennonites range from everyday people walking down the street

Mennonites that broke off from the Brethren (Mennonite) religion
once upon a time.
