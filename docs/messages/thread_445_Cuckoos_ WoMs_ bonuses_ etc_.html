Re: Cuckoos, WoMs, bonuses, etc.

From: highlysung | Date: 2004-09-28 17:44:33 UTC
I've never seen any Cuckoos or WoM clues - (guess we haven't paid our
dues yet :)

But I have encountered bonus boxes. (Since I don't know if it's ok to
divulge that a box has bonus boxes associated with it, I won't
mention the box's name). It was a box with a very nice hike, and we
found two separate bonus box clues written in the logbook - we
searched and searched, but never found either bonus box. It was hard
to tell if it was because the original letterbox wasn't found in the
exact location the clues had led to or if they just weren't there.

I keep thinking about planting a bonus box, though. There is another
box we visited which had FANTASTIC clues that really made you feel
like you were part of the story - the hike was absolutely beautiful -
and with every step our anticipation to get to the box just grew and
grew, because we couldn't wait to see the box and stamp. As we
unearthed the box, we agreed that this had been the best letterbox
ever, so far! With the wonderful integration of the clues to the
location and story, we felt sure the stamp would really be the icing
on the cake. When we opened the box, we found a store-bought stamp
(which would have been totally fine, if it had something to do with
the theme) that was so incomprehensively NOT related (not one iota)
to the theme. We were completely dumbfounded trying to figure out
what the planter had been thinking. So anyway, I keep thinking that I
need to plant a bonus box there, just to provide a stamp that will go
with the theme! That letterbox is still one of my favorites so far,
but that stamp is just some sort of weird Twilight Zone-ish twist in
the heart! Like the planter had dipped their hand blindfolded into a
vat of stamps and just grabbed one! Or maybe let their dog pick it
out! And, please, don't get me wrong - this would be one of the top
letterboxes I would recommend for someone to find, in spite of the
stamp.

I wonder what reasons one generally has for planting a bonus box? I
would guess a great location would be reason number one - but has
anyone ever planted one in order to redeem the stamp in the original
box? Gee, I really hope this isn't making me sound terrible to you
all - I love ALL letterboxes and stamps! Really, I do!!

highlysung

From: The Doubtful Guests | Date: 2004-09-29 17:24:02 UTC
You never can know how someone will feel about someone else adding another box to their box/series/hike. 
 
Personally I would like it.  My Christmas Tree Graveyards was my first, and crudest, stamp.  Someone else planted a box nearby that said, "follow the clues to Christmas Tree Graveyards and then do this..."  It was great.  She added a beautiful hand carved stamp and a hand made log that made my "series" more worth visiting. 
 
You can always ask them:  "Hey I loved your series, the hike and your clues.  I would really like to carve a stamp to add another box to the series.    What do you think?"  Or some such.
 
On the other hand should you find someplace else that's great and start something new? 
 
Scarab

I have been working on a few bonus boxes of late. I have had
wonderful responses from the folks on whose works I have built
upon.

I would never do anything without asking permission first and so
far everyone has been enthusiatic. When I brought up some
questions about bonus boxes on the main list, someone
brought up the concept of "poaching." I know there is a great
temptation to drop a box when someone else has led you into a
great location. What are the thoughts on poaching?

While I would never wish to limit the wonderful variety that is
American Letterboxing, I am suspicious of WoMs. It seems a
little clique-ish to me. I do love the constant development of new
aspects of this game.

Keep going

plant then the end of November.
