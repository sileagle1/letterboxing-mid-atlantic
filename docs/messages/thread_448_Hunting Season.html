At 04:35 PM 9/29/2004, you wrote:
> > Is there a website I can look on to see which parks
> > allow hunting? I choose to avoid these areas rather
> > than trust wearing orange during the hunting season.
> > I am particularly interested in Bucks County- Peace
> > Valley, Graeme, and Noxamixon Parks for example.

Even if those sites don't allow it, you have to be conscious of what might
be around a park -- there might be private land or state game lands
bordering those parks. Even with the buffer zone that's supposed to exist
between legal hunting and non-hunting areas, it can be tough to know where
those are, for hunters and non-hunters alike.

The further away from Philly that you go, the more likely that state parks
allow hunting within some parts of the park.

In PA, there is no hunting on Sunday, though.

Even so, as someone once pointed out to me -- it's not usually the hunters
you have to worry about, it's the poachers who might be sneaking around in
places where they aren't supposed to be. So it's never a good idea to wear
a brown coat and white mittens when you're out in the woods in the fall and
winter, no matter where you are.

I don't think any of the state parks allow hunting in PA, except when they have spiecal hunts to thin the herds, in which case the park is closed to the public for that day.  However, many parks do have surrounding state game lands, in which you must wear 250 square inches of orange (visible 360 degrees) from November 15 through December 15.
 
You are OK with Peace Valley and Graeme, and should be safe with Noxamixon.
