Wayne discovered geocaching a few months before he found
letterboxing for me, and it's been a bit of a his/hers thing for us,
although I'm more active in LB. We found that just about any neat
place with a letterbox in NJ was bound to have at least one
geocache, too. But there are geocaches, and then... there are
geocaches. Some are just "I dare you to find this spot" with no
consideration of the value of the place in and of itself. Blech.
The web-based-ness of cache-ing has spun off one thing that we think
is super-neat: the "locationless" geocache, where the "placer" does
nothing more than post the parameters for the places, and then
the "finders" post photos and write-ups of the places that fit the
bill. Wayne did one asking for original markers on the Mason-Dixon
line, and the responses could be made into a book! Great photos and
stories that give the history of each site. And we don't even have
to leave home!
And now, a tip from experience: if you are hunting both a geocache
and a letterbox in the same park as part of one excursion, do the
geocache first. "Why?" you say, "The gps will lead right from the
box to the cache, right?" Yeah, but in a direct line, not on the
route taken by the geocache placer from HIS starting point. So after
an hour of crawling through the trackless underbrush you may break
into the clearing and then shake your head reading the other log
entries about how easy the walk was.
