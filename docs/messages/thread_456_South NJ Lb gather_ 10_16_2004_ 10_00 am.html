Re: [lbma] South NJ Letterboxing Gathering

From: Silent Doug | Date: 2004-10-10 22:41:22 UTC
Be sure to bring your work or gardening gloves to the gathering on Saturday
-- Jennifer and I hid a box there today, and found that a stretch of the
trails are pretty well covered in trash (mostly drifted ashore from the
Delaware River). We decided that we'll create a special letterbox that you
can only log in to if you collect a full bag of trash from the trail on
your way to/from the other box that we hid. We'll bring bags, but you might
want gloves to pick up the garbage along the way. This is a great way to
show the nature center that we're responsible and sensitive to the park's
natural setting.

I'll also be bringing letterboxing bumper stickers to the event -- both the
oval LB stickers ($3) and the "Get a Clue" ($2) stickers -- but all
proceeds from the sale of the stickers will be donated to the nature
center. I figure that's another good way to show our appreciation to them
for being so accommodating to letterboxing.

Also, for all you letterboxers attending the gathering from PA, here are
some driving directions (since the nature center doesn't have them on their
site): After passing through the toll booths on the Palmyra-Tacony Bridge
(there's no toll for NJ-bound vehicles), head to the left lane. At the
first light, turn left, and follow the small brown signs for the Palmyra
Cove Nature Center. You'll eventually go back west past the toll booths and
then under the highway to reach the nature center.

It was a beautiful day at Palmyra Cove today -- it's really a great
facility, and we're looking forward to a great gathering! Just one reminder
-- keep your eyes open while you're out on the trails. We found a very cute
2" long baby snapping turtle on the trail, with tiny claws and a long tail.
We moved him off to the side of the trail (which was a good thing, since
the local police came by on an ATV a short while later). The moral of the
story is to not always keep your head in your clues, but observe and enjoy
your environment while letterboxing!

Doug
