From: Cyclonic07@aol.com | Date: 2004-10-05 01:40:33 UTC
 
Amazingly, I was not worn out after work on Sunday, so I headed out to do some more boxing, this time in Upper Bucks.
 
First up is the Ring-Ding-A-Ding series.  I found both boxes in fine condition.  Nice little park.  It was a perfect day out, hardly a cloud in the sky, so there were a bunch on skydivers "dropping in" on the airport next door.
 
I stopped in Menlo Park in Perkasie, where they were having the Perkasie Fall Festival.  I finally had a chance to check out the carousel there, very nice.  You can see the photos I took here:
 
http://cyclonic.smugmug.com/gallery/241488
 
I heard there were a few Hungry Termites in the woods nearby, and had no trouble tracking them down.  Great stamp, wonderful detail in a great scene.  Those who have not found this one must get it before it moves away.
 
I also discovered the long lost city of Minas Tirith nearby.  Love the lake view.  The sun was now setting, so that was it for boxing on this abbreviated day.
 
Also, I captured the Traveling Music Note HH.
 
Not a bad weekend for me, six finds (two were very difficult ones), a bunch of exchanges from some great letterboxers, and a HH.

From: ilikebeingbob | Date: 2004-10-05 03:29:09 UTC
I went on a box check.

A Garden Box - Tropical Storm Gaston visited and put the Garden box
under three feet of water. The letterbox set sail but was blocked
from going down stream by the fallen tree that it was under. It was
dry and put back in place.

Rebellion - Gaston also visited the Rebellion Box. The brook it was
beside rose from three feet deep to 15 feet deep in one afternoon.
So the box ended up under about five feet of water. It was under a
rock so it stayed put. I did have to dig it out from teh sand and
mud that was left behind. Gaston left it's wet stamp. The park this
box is at was destroyed so the box has been removed and will be
replaced when they fix the park.

Belle Isle - Soaking wet, so box is removed for cleaning. Not sure
if it was Jeane or Gaston who signed in at this letterbox.

Huguenot Flatwater - Jeane sent floodwaters down the James so this
box was under water for a short time. Jeane left her wet stamp so it
has also been removed so it can dry out.

Robiious Landing - High and Dry. Although part of the path was under
water.

While I did take possible flooding into account, I did not take a one
in 2400 year flood (TS Gaston) into account or a river being smart
enough to find my letterbox.

Bob2
