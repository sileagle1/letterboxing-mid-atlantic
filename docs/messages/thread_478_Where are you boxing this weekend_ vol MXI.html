From: Hikers_n_ Hounds | Date: 2004-10-10 03:44:34 UTC
I tried to do some maintenance today on my Washington
Corssing boxes, Cheshire Cat and Abstract Guitar. I
knew the area had gotten hit hard per Ed's previous
post but man, did it get hit hard. The access road
behind the Thompson Neely House is closed. I parked
elsewhere and Dude and I hiked in. Abstract Guitar is
totally gone. Oh well, Carmen's first stamp and he
didn't like it anyway. Cheshire Cat was completely
ruin and the stamp seperated from the base. We'll
eventually replant but not until spring.

Then I chased the ever elusive Miss Jane Austen.
Persuasion, I think it was up the road a piece. Found
the cemetery, found the rocks, did not find the box.
But Dude and I had a great time climbing all over the
rocks and stream and checking out the gravestones. A
really neat little place that we had all to ourselves.
Emma we DID find and enjoyed that park very much too!

Tomorrow I am meeting up with Celtic Cat, Wednesday
Bowler and Jan (can't remember her trail name) and her
dog Barney to hunt some boxes in Ambler.

My PLB Truckin is home for a visit so I will be
bringing that to the gathering and Carmen is just
busting he is so damn happy with his lastest stamp
that we are making a PLB. I raised my boys on all of
the classics, Marvel Comics, Monty Python, Mad
Magazine. Carmen has created a "Mad Fold-In" image
that is really cool. It's two images, one laid out
flat, and another when you fold it in. Plus the
wording chages as well. We're brining it to the
gathering and then mailing it out.

Been kind of quiet lately, yet another death in the
family, my sisters husband. This has not been the
greatest year for my family. We're looking forward to
2005. Well, take care and see you all next Saturday.
Good Lord willin' and the creek don't rise, as my Ma
always said.

Irene
