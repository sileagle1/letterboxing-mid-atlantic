As a letterboxer living in Philadelphia, there aren't many places I
can hide boxes. I have several stamps carved and ready to be planted,
and I've been thinking for a long time about hiding them in Fairmount
Park - Philly's largest park at 9,100 acres.

Now obviously not all of those acres are accessible to people or
letterboxers for that matter. And I wanted to hide my boxes in
relatively populated areas. The issue is that there have been...not
exactly a "string", per se, but several serious crimes committed in
various parts of the Park in the past year. What's bringing this
topic to the LBMA group is that I read in the news that another body
was found in an underpopulated area of the Park over the weekend :-\
which makes me question my hiding spots...

My primary concern would be, of course, people's safety, which is why
I'd hide my boxes in well-populated areas and not let people go off
the beaten path. I could wait to plant my boxes until this "dies
down", but crimes are, unfortunately, a way of life - especially in a
large city.

I guess my question to all of you folks is - should I just skip
hiding in the Park altogether? Should I just find areas in the 'burbs
and countryside to hide my boxes? Or should I go ahead with my plan
to hide them in well-populated areas and ask people to remember
they're in an urban environment - and hope that people who are not
comfortable being in a city won't go..?

Any thoughts would be greatly appreciated...

Thanks,
FdL

I think if you are already "concerned" about Fairmount Park, you
could just put ONE LB there, in a populated area. Or put a caution
in your clues, especially if folks should not venture in alone.
Concentrating on following clues may make a seeker less aware of
their surroundings/safety. Of course it is easier for you to
maintain a box that is easy to access - so where do you like to go
that others might find interesting or fun to visit? Not everyone is a
hiker (with or without hounds) - and near a museum is just as special.
In the end if you feel safe planting the box on your own, that should
be your first consideration.

From: thedoubtfulguests | Date: 2004-04-13 00:00:19 UTC
Besides the safety issue you also have to worry about the box
lasting in a busy park. Most letterboxes go unnoticed for a long
time off the beaten trail in a woodsy park. Planting boxes in an
urban park is very dicey. If the gardeners don't get them the kids
playing will. I've seen many boxes that don't last a summer in such
a place. Also you want to avoid sensitive historical areas and such.

You will have to look around carefully and ask yourself "Can this
hiding place be easily seen? Easily discovered by chance?" Think
like a gardener or a curious kid. This only compounds your problem
of well hidden but dangerous.

I like microboxes in urban settings. You can tie them with a long
velcro strap to the branch of an evergreen bush. This keeps them
out of sight and off the ground from the gardeners. Their small
size is also less threatening to the humorless security folks, at
least I hope it is. There is a good picture of a microbox in the
main Yahoo list's photos.

What I recommend you do not do is to put out several new boxes at
once before you try out the area. It would be a shame to plant a
series you are proud of only to have them disappear quickly. There
is a great Lion series here in DC in an urban park. Two of them
were poorly located and one is already missing. I do not expect the
other to survive unless it is moved to a better location.

With all that in mind it is yet possible to plant boxes in a busy
urban setting. Feel free to write and ask more and I'll describe my
successful urban hides. Experiment and have fun.

Scarab of the Doubtful Guests
