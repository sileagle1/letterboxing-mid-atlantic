VA: New Stamp, Clues, and Bonus for Xmas Tree Graveyards

From: thedoubtfulguests | Date: 2004-10-10 17:37:56 UTC
October 13, 2004: I carved a new stamp, slightly changed the
location, and altered the clues. That makes it a New Find. Look
for the Bonus Clue during October, to lead you to the Halloween
Graveyard, and a couple more boxes.

It's only 75 cents to drive out to Robert Simon's dream, Reston, a
planned community of work, home and play, in rolling Northern
Virginia among preserved woods. These directions will take you
through a creek valley of tall trees in one of the natural areas of
Reston, Virginia.

From the Beltway, I-495, take the Dulles Toll Road, route 267, west
to Hunter Mill Road. Left on Hunter Mill and right on Sunrise Valley
Drive. Take the third left onto Cross School Road. First Right on
Midsummer Drive. On your left you will come to Dayflower Court.
There on the right is a small park. Park.

Walk between the two benches down a steep path and across a gully.
You'll pass the first Christmas Tree Graveyard. Follow the path down
into the woods all the way down into woodpecker valley. Keep an ear
open for Woody Woodpecker's relatives. Yes the big ones with the red
feather crowns live here. Did you know you can tell which way is
north in a forest by observing which side the moss grows on trees?
Be careful there is poison ivy around here. You'll come to a T at
the path that runs the length of this small valley. Turn right.

The path heads north along the narrow swath cleared of trees for the
water line. Look for tall dead trees with woodpecker holes. You'll
pass a tall manhole cover and a muddy spring. You're following a
creek way off to the side. Along the way, you will also see tall
tree, split in two and way up high. There are many parallel paths,
some made by bikers to avoid the mud and the spring. You will have
to figure out the way on your own. Just be sure to follow the
creek, along the right side, until you come to a bridge.

(You're not supposed to cross the creek any more due to erosion. I
am sure you can figure out a way around.)

At bridge 31 go north, continuing on a dirt path, still following
along the right side of the creek. Very soon you'll see a 9' tall,
rotting, tree stump. Walk around it in a U-turn and take a bearing
of 120 degrees. Walk that that direction to the root end of a large
fallen tree and the Christmas Tree Graveyard.

Be sure to bring clues for the "Furry Woodland Creature" letterbox
too, which is nearby, which, if you're clever, you can do along the
way.

Afterwards you can either return the way you came or take the paved
trailed East from bridge 31 to the road and go to the right to your
car.
