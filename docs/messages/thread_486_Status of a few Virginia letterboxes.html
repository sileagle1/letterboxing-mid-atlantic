Status of a few Virginia letterboxes

From: genealogy_va | Date: 2004-10-11 23:45:25 UTC
Hello (especially to Wordfried! 8-D),

Just posted this on the Big List, but wanted to add the more
personalized version here...

09 OCT 2004
"P-U Letterbox": Well hidden and easy to get to--especially if you
don't mind heights...If you have to drive on I-81 past Edinburg, it's
worth the quick stop. My 7-year-old accompanied me on this one, his
first. The whole experience came home for him as he scampered up the
stairs to grab the box first. Now he wants to do it, too.

"Seven Bends": Still there and in good shape. My son wanted to see
this one, too. And it was an excuse to climb the firetower on the
way.

"Woodstock Tower Seedling": I've looked for it twice now without
success. All of the fallen leaves and my lack of imagination make
this one a tough one for me. I can't tell if I was in the right area
or not. I sent email to Wordfried, so we'll see.

11 OCT 2004
"Where the Poison Ivy Leaf Grows": A little trouble finding the
correct boxwood, and lots of sticker covered runners. Box was dry
and in good shape. Logbook slightly damp, but OK.

"In the Park--Sports Series #3": Wow, this one is well-bagged. Nice
box, stamp and a good park. Everything dry and in good shape.

"Clark's Crossing": Be careful getting this one. Part of the trail
that run's by the creek looks like it's been seriously eroded during
last rash of big storms to come through. One mis-step and it's a 3
foot drop straight into 3+ feet of water...Parking lot for the park,
as of this evening, it completely torn up as new curbs and street are
going in. Lot's of "Do Not Enter" signs...;-). It's a quick scamper
though to the park behind the construction though. The box is in
decent shape, dry, but logbook is a touch damp.

Thanks,
FamilyMan
