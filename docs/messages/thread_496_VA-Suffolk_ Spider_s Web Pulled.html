VA-Suffolk: Spider's Web Pulled

From: Mike Brooks | Date: 2004-10-15 03:24:37 UTC
Based on the note in the clue by the owner and some problems in the
park, I pulled Spider's Web a few weeks ago. Bat Cave, the second box
in that series was missing. I have asked the letterboxing.org
webmasters to pull that clue. The owners have not answered two emails
on the subject. If these boxes were on your to do list, scratch them
off. I'll probably add the clues to the basement to preserve them.

More details of the problems:

ALL of the man-made objects in step #1 were removed by the park
before last year's storm, making the unmarked trailhead impossible to
find without prior local knowledge. This information was per a log by
the park ranger, who was the only person to find Spider's Web in the
last two years.

The small drainage where the letterbox was is now a swamp. The two
biggest trees in the area fell. The lack of canopy allowed the
previously clear streambed to fill with briars. The fallen trees also
dammed up the drainage, making what had been a fairly dry area, very
wet and unpleasant.

The log book is just now finally dry after several weeks. It was that
bad. It was actually sealed well, but it was 80% submersed in mud for
at least eleven months.

Unless the owners reappear and object, I'm going to recycle that
stamp into a new placement in a different park in a few weeks. Not
many people found it originally in 2002 and there are plenty of new
letterboxers in the region who might like the stamp. I don't think
there is a decent spot in Sleepy Hole to plant anything now. If
anyone feels otherwise and plants in Sleepy Hole, I'm sure I'll enjoy
trying to find it.

Happy Trails,

Mike
