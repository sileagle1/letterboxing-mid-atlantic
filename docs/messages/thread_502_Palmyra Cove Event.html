RE: [lbma] Palmyra Cove Event

From: Myke Scholtes | Date: 2004-10-17 11:45:20 UTC
Message
I'd like to thank everybody at yesterday's gathering for making me feel welcome, and for a wonderful time.  When I started letterboxing in January, the anonymity of letterboxing was one of my favorite aspects of it.  I thought it was great to just log in, and let nobody know who I was or where I was from.  I thought it was like a secret treasure hunt.  I would refuse to email anybody to tell them I found their box, assuming they should check on it to find out.  I wouldn't even leave comments in boxes.  I thought that was "too personal."

Then I planted my first box, and discovered how much fun it was to hear from people.  I started to see another side of letterboxing.
 
Then Silent Doug invited me to join this mailing list in August, and I started to discover how interesting you all are. 
 
It was with some hesitation that I decided to attend the Palmyra Cove Gathering.  I wasn't sure what I was getting into...I wasn't sure if I'd get along with you all, if I'd feel like an outsider.  But I had an absolute BLAST.  From the moment Team Lightnin' Bug invited me to join them in their search for the Red Hat boxes, I was glad I went.  The day was SO much fun.  There was such a sincere camaraderie, and such a sense of wanting to just have a good time.  I am very glad I met everyone who attended yesterday. 
 
Thanks so much, Bev, for hosting.  And thanks for the truly helpful carving tips (and the free material!!), and for setting up such a great day!
 
I've found a new side of letterboxing once again, and I look forward to seeing you all again sometime soon!
 
Peace,
Myke
"The Indigo Vulture" AKA "The guy with the 309 stamp"
