From: Art Vandelay | Date: 2004-10-18 00:23:39 UTC
I know where all of you boxed- I've been busy reading all the posts about what a wonderful time you all had at the gathering.  Sounds like a good time was had by all.  Hopefully we'll join you next time. Warcat'76 was attending Parent Weekend at DelVal but managed to do some boxing today on our way home.  Here's what we found:
 
The Keith House
Ghost of Graeme
Jump for Joy
Ring-Ding-A-Ding series
LoTR- series of 6
Catch the Wind
Hungry Termites
The Great Blue Heron
 
All were in good shape.
 
Also left 2 Hitchhikers and picked up 2 Hitchhikers.
 
A good weekend all-in-all.   We are over 140 finds now!
 
Cat

Well, I've found my first three "post-gathering" boxes.  My "309" trail stamp is really shot after all the exchanging on Saturday...I'll be making a new one soon, maybe this week.  I've been wanting to make a new one anyway...I've developed better carving skills since making it.  I'd like to call it the very first stamp I ever carved, but unfortunately, the real first one actually said something like "GOE" in a Pennsylvania route shield.  After I tested it once, I swore profusely and then realized, oh yes, one must carve the image BACKWARDS.  Duh.
 
Anyway, what I found yesterday and this morning were:
 
Robin Hood's Hat
Tunis Brady
Infinity Knot
 
All boxes were in good shape.  Special thanks to the other hairy-legged letterboxers for the hint on Tunis Brady.  And big "foot-in-mouth" for the false-alarm-MIA I announced on Infinity Knot a few weeks ago.  It's most certainly still there, and a beautiful stamp to boot. :)
 
Peace,
Myke
"Indigo Vulture"
