update to my web site

From: Silent Doug | Date: 2004-10-22 16:02:30 UTC
After I redesigned my web site, I got a couple of complaints that it was
difficult to print the clues from the new layout. Occasionally, the text
would be clipped on the right side, even if you just tried to select the
clue text (without the header, sidebar, footer, etc.) and used the Print
Selection option from your browser, or if you copied and pasted the clues
into Word.

To fix this, I added a "print friendly" option for all the clue pages. Just
click the Print Friendly button at the top, and a new browser window will
open with the clues in a format that is more easily printed. Any of the
clues from this page now feature the new print option:
http://www.letterboxing.info/box/

If you've previously printed any clues from the site but haven't yet gone
after those boxes, make sure that your printed copy hasn't cut off any
important text on the right side of the page, and get a new copy from the
site if it has.

Also, note that the footnotes may be in smaller print, but there's often
important information there, so consider yourself warned....

:-)

Doug

From: nattybumppolbna | Date: 2004-10-22 18:44:31 UTC
I was talking with some co-workers about my weekend
letterboxing activities one day. One of the younger guys I work
with overheard our conversation and asked about letterboxing.
After the quick synopsis ("It's a kind of treasure hunt game that
started back in England in the 1800's. You hide a box
somewhere and post clues on how to find it on the internet.
Yadda Yadda Yadda...") we went back to discussing this new box
I had hidden in DC. The kid then chimes in with, "So you found it,
then?"
"Found what?" I asked, not sure he knew exactly what we were
talking about.
"Your Old Man Hobby."
"My what?!"
"Your Old Man Hobby. You know. It's what you old guys do when
you can't do real sports anymore. Like Bird Watching or Fly
Fishing."
So after I had him move all my equipment up to the second floor
and clean out my van, we sat down to discuss some of the
advantages of experience over youth - like the ability to
participate properly in civil conversation with one's elders.
I then suggested that he give letterboxing a try. I knew of a box
with an 8-mile hike on pretty steep terrain if he wanted a
challenge. He declined saying that he had trouble with hikes
since he had twisted his leg up in a snowboarding accident.
As he limped away I knew the irony was completely lost on my
young friend


Natty Bumppo

Red Hat Box'rs

From: Silent Doug | Date: 2004-10-22 20:27:27 UTC
Today's Washington Post has an article on the Red Hat Society, and it
mentions the Red Hat Box'rs:

"Registered chapters in the Washington area run the gamut from nursing home
residents who participate in on-site programs to small clusters of
neighborhood friends who meet monthly for tea to huge groups of women from
all over the region who attend a potpourri of events each month. An
entertaining, often sassy, sampling of chapter names offers clues to the
groups' natures, as well as their special interests: Ladies With a
Hattitude, Le Roux Chappeaus, Twisted Sisters, Purple C.O.W.S. (Creative,
Outgoing, Wise and Sexy), Red Hat Chili Peppers, the Red Foxes, Red
Hattricks (which claims it's open only to hockey players) and Red-Hat
Box'rs (specializing in the hobby of letterboxing)."

I put a PDF of the article from the Post's web site in the files section of
the LbMA group.
