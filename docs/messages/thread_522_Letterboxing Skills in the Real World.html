Letterboxing Skills in the Real World

From: nattybumppolbna | Date: 2004-10-26 00:53:11 UTC
The Secret Service Agent at the metal detector would not allow
me to take my Leatherman into the John Kerry Rally on the Ben
Franklin Parkway. For those who are unfamiliar with the $70
Leatherman Wave, it is the ultimate pocket multi-tool containing,
scissors, pliers, standard & phillips screwdrivers, can opener,
file, and yes, a knife.
After a 20 minute hike from the car and a half-hour wait in the
metal detector line, my choice, if I wanted to see the rally, was
throw it away or take it back to the car. The Agent offered me
another option.
"Some guys just find a place to put it out there until later."
I got out of line and walked outside the Police cordon. Twenty
yards away was a 15' x 15' grassy patch with a few scrawny
shrubs. I watched for a few minutes as a parade of men walked
by the patch and, not-too-subtly dropped their pocket knives and
nail clippers into the bushes. I also noted that others were
watching this parade. My precious Leatherman would not be
added to this Swiss Army gold mine.
Next to the patch was a 6 foot wall that separated the sidewalk
from a parking lot. The top of the wall was a long-neglected
planter where flats of annuals were once placed.
I hopped up on the wall and watched the crowds file through the
metal detectors. And guess what - there was just enough space
in the gap for... a Leatherman!
I hid the tool and returned to the metal detector.
After speeches by Bill Clinton, John Kerry and (what seemed
like) every Democrat in the free world, I returned to retrieve my
Leatherman. Unfortunately for many others, the Swiss Army
cache had been raided.
Choosing a good hiding place is a crucial skill in Letterboxing -
and life. Letterboxing saved my Leatherman.

Natty Bumppo

rarely let me pass through to the gate with my apparently lethal
plastic key chain. I would excuse myself, go into the Ladies and hide
