Sorry about that! When we were at the Mercer Castle, I came across a
catalog that had images of all the tiles, and of course my first thought
was "these would make cool letterbox stamps!" I picked up a copy and
quickly carved about a dozen of them, so I've got more of them almost ready
to be planted.

>I had just last week staked out Enos Godshall, Branchwood, and Upper
>Salford Parks as new hiding spots. I kid you not. The park names are on
>my chalk board at home.
>
>So now I have new boxes to hunt, but no place to hide mine.
>If you don't mind, I have Camp Wawa and Bergey's Mill on my board too.

I also recently got a copy of Microsoft Streets and Trips, the mapping
software (and I think that you have a copy of that, too, don't you?). I've
been going through it, trying to identify local parks that might be good
letterbox hiding places but that don't already have any. Many of them don't
have names on the map, but with some web sleuthing or just by visiting
them, I've been able to identify some good possible locations. But I'll
take Camp Wawa and Bergey's Mill off the list (yep, they were on it!).

Well, I hope you enjoy finding the boxes!

Doug
