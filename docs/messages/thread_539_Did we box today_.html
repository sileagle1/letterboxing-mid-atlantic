Well,there's been a darth of posts in the last two day which suggests
that some of you have a life, or, at least another life aside from
letterboxing. How scandalous.

On Friday, my 14 year old went to a garage band concert. Mom sat all
night on the edge of her seat awaiting the call for a ride home,
which would indicate that he had, indeed, survived the experience.

Last night we both attended a large, well planned, well thought out
multi-generational Halloween party. About 50 people between the ages
of five and seventy attended. There was a scavenger hunt!

Today I went letterboxing. Finally, a valid excuse for this post. I
picked up the Five Bridges of Batsto box. A beautiful day, a very
nice trail.

Finding the trail was a real head basher. I walked to and fro through
Batsto Historic Village looking for the yellow/orange trail head. I
asked others for directions to no avail. Finally I spotted a forest
ranger near the visitors' center who enlightened me. It was all the
way over on the other side of the property. I must have
looked like I was going to cry because he offered me a ride. I took
it.

Off in the other direction. I hunted Batona Trail Surprise. I was to
walk a third of a mile and pace in five directions. I think that I
walked much further, and on the way back stopped at every fallen log
across the trail and paced the compass points. Not today. The sun had
fallen behind the trees and I hurried home so that I could fulfill
the expectation of littler hunters seeking treasure at my door.

Gillian
