Weekend Boxing Report

From: Hikers_n_ Hounds | Date: 2004-11-01 11:32:12 UTC
Well this was a very hectic weekend for me but I did
get some boxing done.

Friday night I threw a surpise 50th Birthday Party for
Mr. Irene. He was completely shocked and thrilled. It
earned me major brownie points, not that I need them.
I am after all..me!

Saturday evening I reported for duty at Graeme Park to
volunteer with their Ghostly Gathering. First I found
all of Cyclonics GG boxes but ran out of daylight
before I found Jennifer's box so will have to go back.
It was my first time there and I really enjoyed the
place. Plus I got to hear Cyclonic screaming at the
hapless tourists and scare the bejesus out of them all
night. That got over around 11:00.

Sunday Dude and I met up with Janila and Barney, Team
Little Dog, to plant some boxes in Tyler Park and try
again to find both of Muk-Luk's Schofield Ford Covered
Bridge boxes. We found box#2 easily enough but the
first box eluded us. I think we were pretty darn close
but no luck.

Now back to work, boo!!!!

No boxing this weekend, though on Saturday we went on a Boy Scout
Hike right past the South Mountain Series. As Tom did not accompany
me when I planted them back in August, I said things like "the Spring

of fame yet again! ;-) We assembled the U2/Doves box for P'ooza to
ship to Irene at lunch today.

I am totally stoked for a mystery adventure next weekend!

From: Chile-Pepper | Date: 2004-11-03 12:18:36 UTC
We had fun this weekend--there's a great little park very close to us that's ripe for letterboxes so we planted (clues forthcoming).  Interestingly, at least for me since it's a first, I found a great spot and then nearby noticed some leaves and sticks that just "didn't look right".  Well, you guessed it, we were accidental finders, but of a geocache.  I know very little about that sport, but it was more like a microbox--just a film canister with a stubby pencil and a log.  Was the pencil the prize?  Or just for the log?  Who knows.  But I thought it so ironic that in a 50 acre park we came across this 1 x 2 inch object totally by accident.
I expected a geocache to be bigger, but I guess it was a "microcache"! 
ChilePepper

Did you note the name of the geocache? They have names like
letterboxes; it might have been on the front of the log. If you're
curious enough, try a site called Buxley's Geocaching Waypoints. The
geocaches are represented by red dots on a map. Select country,
state, region. At that point, if you click on a clear area, no dots,
it will zoom further.

From: Mike Brooks | Date: 2004-11-12 00:37:15 UTC
A trend in geocaching is to go to "microcaches". The thought is that
some places are too busy to hide a full sized cache, or it can't be
camoflauged adequately, or maybe the hider just wants to make it
harder. The pencil is just for the log.

If you'd like (and haven't already looked this up yourself), post
some info on the park (name, town, road intersection), and I'll see
what's nearby among caches.

Mike
