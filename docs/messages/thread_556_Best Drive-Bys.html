Since the rat is out of the bag here, let me open this up to the group:

Can I get a head count of people/teams who might be interested in a Letterboxing Road
Rally?

The idea is this: A series of boxes in various parks in Montgomery County.
A precisely mapped circuit route and order to the collection of the boxes.
The boxes are simple drive-bys in each location.
[i.e. Park here. Walk 20 paces to an elm tree. 35p @ 220 to a fence. 10p @ 118 to a Job
Johnny, It's inside next to the toilet paper]
Contestants will be released at intervals so as to not interfere with each other.
The winner is the one who comes closest to the "perfect" time pre-determined by the
judges (so far, me.)
Of course there will be some bedevilment involved. After all, it is ME organizing it.
Route and specific driving instructions to be announced the morning of.

Of those interested:

When should we try this? December is VERY busy. January? February? Spring?
Any input or advice is welcome.

Natty Bumppo

From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2004-11-19 02:08:24 UTC
I remember participating in a few road rallies back in college. You
basically follow instructions like "CAST 45 to Riverside", which
means can assume speed to 45 until you get to Riverside..."Left".
And so on.

They are kind of like letterboxing clues, only you are trying to
match a desired time for the course. The trick is to drive about 3
mph faster than the CAST speeds and this will overcome slowdowns at
traffic lights and stop signs. The first half of one rally, we did
not do this and finished near the middle. We employed the 'slightly
faster' strategy the 2nd half and almost won. We still had very much
fun.

Maps were allowed, and you had to have a good alert navigator. Since
I had the car, my bud Larry was the navigator. He was also the
experienced rallyer, and I was just the driver!
