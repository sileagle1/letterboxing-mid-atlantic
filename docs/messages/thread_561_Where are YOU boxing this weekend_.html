So, anyways, you folks have already heard about the Hairy Leggers adventures at Blue Marsh, where we snagged not only the two stamps from Stamp Collection that are there, but also were the first finders on Like Chasing Rainbows, after it has been sitting there for a year and a half.  Not a bad day, too bad my Sunday did not go as well.
 
I slept a little later then I had wanted to on Sunday (usually I am working), and didn't get up to the Lehigh Valley until almost noon.  This was not totally a bad thing, since it was raining in the morning.  Of course the rain was a bad thing, because everything was very wet that afternoon.
 
First up was Da Pokemon Code.  First finder, a very easy box once you figure out the clue.
 
Next I set out to find the Rodale Fitness Park.  This is where thing got a little, OK, a lot wet.  I got to the boardwalk in the clues when I found that the stream running beneath it had actually flooded over and torn a three foot section of it clear out.  I would have jumped this section (OK, I am not real athletic, but I am not that bad), but the wet boardwalk was as slick as ice, so I worked my way though the woods, getting even wetter, back to the front of the park, and finally worked my way to the other side of the trail, finding the box which had somehow been saved from the deluge.
 
Next up was Lock Ridge.  After slogging though yet more water (did I mention it rained), I was disappointed to find no box.
 
Just down the road, I headed into Kalmbach Park, and easily found the box hidden there, and didn't get too wet either.
 
My next, and as it would turn out, final stop, was the Reimert Bird Haven.  I hiked up, and up, and up the hill on the trail, but could not seem to find the Wildlands box.  I kicked around for about an hour, but I just don't think I could come up with the correct location.  So I headed back down and copied down the clue for the other box to figure out later, and decided to get something to eat and call it a day.  I will try again in a few weeks, and maybe have some better luck, but I did come home with three new finds, and a new idea.
