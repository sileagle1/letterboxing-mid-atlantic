Riddle me this...

Say a letterboxer plants a box and later comes down with irreversible
amnesia. Hence, they cannot remember planting the box or anything
about letterboxing. Oh, and in the incident, lose their logbook.

A couple months later, they read a Time article or catch something on
CNN and realize, "Hey that's a cool hobby". They hop on LBNA, then
locate and find a letterbox really close to where they live.

As it turns out, they find the same letterbox that they planted...

Does this count as a find?

I had hoped to stir up the main list a bit, but there were few
nibbles.

Lightnin' Bug

From: gerania93 | Date: 2004-11-15 03:12:50 UTC
Oh, absolutely! A find of the first order. If intentionally searching
the box, it might be the letterbox type, clue or location which draw
the amnesiac to search for this prise, never realizing that the
things which cause them to seek the box are the thing(s) which may
have prompted them to plant such a box in the first place. If a
person repeatedly, accidently, happens upon the box which they
themselves have planted (Oh my. What's this?) it is still indeed a
valid find. If that person has a short term memory loss and hour to
hour or day to day has no recollection of finding the letterbox a
second, third, fourth time (we must at this point assume that the
person in question carries papers or index cards on which to stamp
and not a book, as said person might recognize a duplicate stamp)
then yes, that counts as a find. This is not my opinion. This is one
of the laws of the universe. I didn't write the laws, I only obey
them.

Gillian
