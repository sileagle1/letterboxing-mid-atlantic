Road Rally Update

From: nattybumppolbna | Date: 2004-11-20 04:24:55 UTC
Since most people are chomping-at-the-bit to go, let me just set the Tentative Date for
the Rubbermaid Road Rally for January 22 or 23.

Of course this is weather contingent. I will plan to have the boxes planted by then and we
can just watch what Ol' Man Winter has in store for us. Once the route is mapped and
boxes planted, I feel we can make a go of it whenever people feel ready.
As the object is to try and get as close to the "ideal" time without going UNDER, slower
roads should not be a problem.

As I work out the route and times I will have a better sense of how many boxes we will
need, etc. I'll post updates here. If you have any ideas, please feel free to share them with
me off-group at nattybumppolbna@.... I'll be happy to listen to anything, but don't
expect me to let you know if I'm going to use it. That way it remains a surprise for
everyone. Twists and bedevilment welcome.

Also I have never run a letterboxing event so those of you with experience, I would be
indebted for any tips you might have.

Natt

Road Rally Update

From: nattybumppolbna | Date: 2004-12-06 04:28:34 UTC
The Road Rally is On!

The tentative plan is for January 22 or 23.
All the boxes will be planted by Jan 20.

It is my goal to run the Rally on the 22 with a weather hold on the 23. Because of the
nature of this event, it is incredibly weather/condition contingent. If necessary, we can
push it off as far as Spring.

If you interested in carving a stamp for the rally, please contact me off-list at
nattybumppolbna at aol dot com
If you volunteer, please be sure you can get the stamp done and to me by Jan 15

To allow everyone to participate in the rally I will be doing all the planting for the event.
After the event, you are welcome to replant the stamp elsewhere or claim my placement
and distribute your own clue. I will waive any claim on the P.

I hope to begin team sign-ups right after Christmas. I would suggest forming teams for
the rally. Each vehicle must be separated by enough time to keep teams from bunching
up at box sites. And with many teams in the game this could go on all day.

Any suggestions or volunteers for the gathering portion of the day are most welcome.

Actually any suggestions at all are welcome.

Natt-

From: rallymastr | Date: 2005-01-11 00:40:10 UTC
Here's the scoop:

Due to forces beyond our control Saturday the 22nd is no longer an option. The current
date for the rally is Sunday, January 23rd with a weather hold on the following Sunday,
January 30th. If the weather does not cooperate, we will continue to push the date back
until we can make this thing happen.

The planned facility will not allow us to reserve the heated pavillion in advance in case the
lake should freeze. When they have 7" of ice on the lake they use the pavillion for skaters.
As we need a fixed and definite facility for the rally HQ and start point, it would not do to
simply roll the dice on this. Alternatives are being examined in the area.

The original plan was to begin and end the rally at Green Lane Reservoir Park in Green
Lane, Montgomery County, Pa We will continue to locate ourselves in or around this great
park as it offers many activities in addition to the road rally.

Road Rally Update

From: nattybumppolbna | Date: 2006-04-12 01:48:22 UTC
May 7th is the day.

At a location to be announced here soon.

But it will be in Montgomery County, Pennsylvania in the Green Lane Reservoir area so all
of last year's info on hotel/motel/campsites will be the same.

See the blog for details: http://www.roadrally.blogspot.com/

We have more spaces available for teams this year, but in case demand is higher than
availability, teams will be admitted in the order in which they apply via email for a slot.
Here's how it goes: I will announce the opening of registration on this group in the next
few days and an email address to apply to.
The number of teams allowed will be determined by the the route design. I will select the
teams that apply AFTER I make the announcement in the order they are received. All
others will be placed on standby.

When you apply, you need to have a team name and the names of the driver and navigator.
You can change these details later if necessary. Also you will request a start time: Early,
Late or No Preference. I will do all I can to accomodate your requests, but once the
running order is posted, it will be YOUR responsibility to find someone to change with if
you cannot make your post time.

I have some really great stamps this year from some of your favorite carvers and the ever-
generous Letterboxing Community has come up with prizes beyond my wildest dreams to
be donated to the effort.

Stay tuned...
