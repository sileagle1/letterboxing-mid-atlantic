Woo-hoo! H&H hits 100!

From: Hikers_n_ Hounds | Date: 2004-11-22 01:50:23 UTC
Well, who'da thunk it? Hikers and Hounds found 100
boxes in a little more than 10 months. The fact that
we have probably hunted 200 means nothing! Nah, we've
really only not found 6, so not too shabby. Dude the
Wonder Dog is not talking to me but I promised him
some boxing over Thanksgiving weekend. Mr. Irene and
I had a WONDERFUL time at the Delaware beaches. We've
been going there since we met 9 years ago (he used to
live in Greenville, DE) but never really visited Lewes
beofre. What an absolutely charming little town. We
found boxes hidden by Mix Scarlet, Hexacat, Firefly,
Jessica and Daphnesfam. They were all great and I'll
be contacting the placers with specifics. We hit our
100th box on Miz Scarlet's Red Mill Pond and almost
had the cops called on us! It was in a development but
in a really cool place. We parked and found the
box,took it back to our van and were stamping in when
a resident walked up to the window and asked us if we
needed help. We said no, we were reading our maps
before we headed home. Which really made no sense
because it was deep into this development, not
anywhere where you would pull in to read a map. But it
was the best we could come up with. Well, he must have
called the guy who owned the house next door to the
lot where the box was because as I got out of the van
to re-hide, I noticed the homeowner standing in the
driveway watching me. So I called Miz Scarlet and she
okay'ed us bringing the box home and mailing to her.
It was that or explain ourselves to the cops which I
am sure were the next people we'd see!

Anyway, great time. Glad Jan's daughter is going to be
okay.

Oh, saw National Treasure, really liked it. Harris
LOVED it. Take care all,

Irene - H&H

I've been lurking on this board for a while now.  I just started letterboxing the end of July.  I heard about it on a talk radio morning show right before I left on vacation to Alaska.  I ran out and bought a stamp and I actually found my first stamp in Alaska.  It was great and I'm hooked.
 
My daughter is 12 and she loves letterboxing too.  We don't get out as much as we would like but we are always looking for time to get out.  We live in central Delaware and there aren't many letterboxes nearby.  We have a great day letterboxing in Lewes on Veteran's day, doubling our numbers.  We found 7 boxes and 1 hitchhiker and 3 no finds.  It was hard to leave the area knowing that there were more to hunt.
 
I'd love to meet the faces that go with the names.  And I love reading this board.
 
Thanks.
JuleBee
