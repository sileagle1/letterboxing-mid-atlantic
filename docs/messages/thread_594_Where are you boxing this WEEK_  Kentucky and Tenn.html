Howdy All,

Guess who gets an all expenses paid week in Calvert City, Kentucky?
(Hint - It's chemical plant heaven near Paducah).

Your friendly neighborhood luminescent insect. I leave for Nashville
tomorrow, so I hope to snag a few boxes there and along the way,
weather permitting. There is actually an indoor box in Guthrie, KY.

Western KY has a grand total of 3 boxes, surely more will be there
once I've carved and planted. There is a wonderful area called Land
between the Lakes nearby (my hotel will overlook one of these lakes)
and I am looking forward to boxing a smidge during my downtime.

I will be returning to eastern PA on Dec 7th, hopefully a little
higher in my PFX count....

where's Silent Doug?

From: Silent Doug | Date: 2004-11-29 17:09:25 UTC
Hey, gang--

I know I've been quiet lately, so I thought I'd give you an update. Two
weeks ago, Jennifer and I took a 6-day trip to Seattle, where we were the
surprise guests at a "mystery guest gathering" and were also able to go
letterboxing with Ryan, Amanda from Seattle and Funhog. Jennifer found 60
or so boxes on the trip; I found a few less since I had a conference to
attend, but still was able to pass F600 on the trip. It was a fun trip, and
the weather was mostly cooperative (i.e., the rain was never too severe to
interfere with our boxing).

Once we got back from WA, I had to turn around and fly up to Boston and
back, and then I began my Thanksgiving travels. And both of us have been
battling the flu off and on for what seems like weeks, which has slowed us
down a bit, too.

Anyway, I'm otherwise engaged on the date of the road rally in January
(just like I was for Plantapalooza) -- sorry!

I also saw National Treasure, and it was really a lot of fun -- I highly
recommend it. (The movie does play fast and loose with historical "facts,"
but I did just discover by accident one really big discrepancy regarding
the scene at Independence Hall that would have made that particular
sequence impossible -- anyone care to guess what it is? Without giving away
too much to anyone who hasn't already seen it, of course!)

Hope you all had a good Thanksgiving!

Doug
