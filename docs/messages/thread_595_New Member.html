--- In lbma@yahoogroups.com, The Doubtful Guests
<thedoubtfulguests@y...> wrote:
> There are ghost towns in New Jersey?!

Yep, and they have letterboxes, too.
The quick history - the sandy soil and the acidic water from the
pine needles somehow combine to get iron, and it settles to the
bottom of the bogs; thus, "bog iron." Refining the iron, using
charcoal from the pine trees and shells from the nearby bay/sea was
a big industry from colonial time through mid 1800's all over South
Jersey. Then hard coal and purer iron were found in PA, the bog iron
couldn't compete and poof! no industry. Glass and paper worked for a
while, but just for a while. People left, fires went through (they
always do) and now there's nothing but cellar holes where there were
full-blown towns complete with gas lit streets.
Great boxing territory.

I wanted to update you all on my first hunt! First though, thank you
all for being such a welcoming group. The board is very active, and
everyone seems to be quite enthusiastic about the hobby. Someone
asked if my trail name was my address, actually my trail name is
somecomic, close but not the same as the address.

yes there are ghost towns in NJ, NJ is filled with wonderful reasons
to go out and explore.

OK, the hunt... I went after the 3 boxes in the Pennypack park
series by Joseph. I could not find the first 2 and was more than a
little discouraged as I am an immediate gratification kind of guy...
I was able to find the third one one, and wow... What a neat feeling
of excitement and being connected to others when I saw the other
stamps in his book. This is an enjoyable hike if you haven't taken
it yet.

Thanks for welcoming me, hope to meet some of you sometime.

Somecomic

Don't be discouraged by not finding the first two. It happens to
everyone in the beginning. After a bit you will start to get a feel
for how it works (certain hiker and/or hound types excluded, of
course ;-)) (just kidding!!), and will eventually reach a point where
you don't even need to finish reading the clues sometimes to find the
box.

I couldn't find the first box I looked for, either, and I was with a
group of equally clueless people. After I got some experience I went
back for it and walked right to it mumbling "what on earth was I
thinking last time when I went the other way?!"

Glad you were able to find the third and were able to enjoy the hunt!
