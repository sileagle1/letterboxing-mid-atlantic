From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2004-11-29 21:33:45 UTC
It depends. I usually try to match the stamps to the locales they
feature. Sometimes I see a particularly cool place (Saylor Cement
Museum, the Lenape Museum or the Pool Wildlife Sanctuary) or a place
that begs for a riddle (Fibonnacci or 867-5309) and build a box or
series based upon that. Sometimes, it is just plain random (like Da
Pokemon Code or the soon to be planted Yocco's Box), but the stamp
always matches the name (no miserable sharks, please ;-) ).

Most of my clues are pretty straightforward (I is an engineer, not a
prosaic stylist!), but I enjoy devising riddles as well.

Thanks for asking and Welcome Back again!

Lightnin' Bug

I just think of things I love like the Grateful Dead or Monty Python and go with that. > Where do you get inspiration for stamps, boxes, and clues you
create?

Most from the place itself. Some places are just too cool to keep to
yourself. They get the souvenir type stamp, usually with the place
named on it. The clue varies in type according to hike length, which
of the family are consulting on it, or even my mood of the day.
Where the hike was really short, I compensated by making a mystery
clue that would take some research time.
A couple were the "I wonder if I could..." variety, leading to my
two cut-up-stamp series. Stamp idea first, followed by "now where
could I put it?"
Rock, Paper, Scissors came from the urge to write a clue that you
either got or didn't. (wave to Art)
And my latest came from looking at stamps in the store and realizing
some of the messages would sound really bizarre if found in a
letterbox instead of on a greeting card. Another of the "where
should I put it?" variety that got even funnier when the hike,
chosen by brochure and map of what I >thought< was a familiar local
park, turned out to be the toughest I've ever taken.
