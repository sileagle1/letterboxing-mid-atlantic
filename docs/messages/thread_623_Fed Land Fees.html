Fed Land Fees

From: Hikers_n_ Hounds | Date: 2004-12-09 11:28:11 UTC
Hey folks,
 
Just an FYI. I received this info from my caving club, sent it on to Silent Doug who confirmed it's validity and provided the links below. It appears to be a done deal, signed on Monday the 6th. There seems to be some sort of grassroots effort to repeal or revise the bill but for more info you should contact your local legislature. Personally, I don't have a problem paying if the money goes to preserve and protect our lands, although it's quite a lot of money, IMHO. If it's really used that way and not mis-directed. But the fine is just outrageous. My goal here is not to encourage you to fight this bill, but to inform you so that you can plan accordingly as it seems if we want to use federal land, we will have to cough up the fee for the pass. I am neither advocating nor denouncing here, just informing Please see below.
 
"A rider has recently been added to an Omnibus Appropriations bill for
the FY2005 federal budget which will enact a fee system for *ANY*
federal land (BLM, National Forest, National Park, etc).  Users would
have to purchase an "America the Beautiful" pass at a cost of at least
$85 annually.  Possibly more.  This applies to hikers, campers,
fishermen, hunters, anyone using federal land as a recreational venue.
This does not apply to mining,  forestry, and ranching.  Not purchasing
such a pass would result in fines of $5000 and/or 6 months in jail.
Drivers, owners, and occupants of vehicles not displaying a pass will be
presumed guilty of failure to pay and can all be charged, without
obligation by the government to prove their guilt.  This is far in
excess of other such fines including: damaging fragile wetland with an
ATV bears a fine of $75." 
 
This bill was championed by Congressman Ralph Regula of Ohio and several
other Republican legislators from eastern states (states notably lacking
in vast tracts of federal land) and is universally opposed by western
legislators.  The relevant bill numbers are HR 3283 and S 1108.  For
legal wonks, here's the text of the bill itself:
   http://thomas.loc.gov/cgi-bin/query/z?c108:HR3283:
Soom good links:
