No Ride for this Hitchhiker

From: Art Vandelay | Date: 2004-12-12 21:45:28 UTC
Back in early Nov. we were checking on our Wonders of Nature boxes (NJ) and found a hitchhiker.  We left it there.  It was in its own box- a rather large one (band-aid box size).  It was sealed in the outer plastic bag with our box. In other words, very obvious. Today we checked on our boxes again and the hitchhiker was still there!  Two different people had signed into our logbook since then but did not take the hitchhiker.  We checked the logbook of the hitchhiker and found that they had not signed into it either.  The boxers' signature stamps were not familiar so perhaps they are new, but I would have at least thought they would have stamped into the hitchhiker's logbook even if they didn't know they were supposed to take it.  Has anyone else had this happen?  Maybe we should have moved the hitchhiker on ourselves, but since we won't be boxing until the 23rd, we decided to leave it for now.

From: Alan Marx | Date: 2004-12-13 03:22:07 UTC
Great timing on this post tonight.  I was going to post a similar question to the new boxers board but since you broached the subject...
 
I found my first hitchhikers today.  they were in one box.  There were two separate hitchhikers in two separate bags with 2 separate logs.  Both were left by the person who placed this particular box.  I was all set to take one and move it along to another box,  when I opened the third bag marked Hitchhiker hostel.  There were instructions in that bag to only take a hitchhiker if you leave another one, as well as it's own permanent hostel stamp.  This hostel was in a BIG bag.  Here is where the confusion set in...  Were those instructions only for hitchhikers in the Hostel bag, or for all hitchhikers in the entire box?  I decided to err on the side of caution being a newbie and leave them all.  I did stamp all the hitchhikers in my log book and intend on counting them as finds (if that is correct).  I did not stamp the hitchhiker logs as I felt by leaving them, I hadn't really passed them along and therefore did not qualify to stamp in to them. 
 
Is that too convoluted  a tale to follow?  Can you guys find my questions in there and sort them out?
 
The good news...  A day that started with the intention of finding 3 boxes and three stamps yielded 4 boxes and 6 stamps.  Confusion aside, a very successful day and my sons first letterbox hunt.  He was thrilled.
 
Thanks for the help.
