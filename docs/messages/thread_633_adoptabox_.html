Don't want to beat any dead anythings, but I just received an email from the placer of a box I couldn't find last week.  It was listed as "missing but will be replaced and the clues will be updated"--since that note was added over a year ago, I asked for an update.  The placer said "that box has never been replaced and never will be...."
 
So, assuming that someone wanted to replace the box, since the clues are still there and all--not that I know anyone who has TIME to do that--what's the protocol?

From: cahillymom | Date: 2004-12-14 02:03:00 UTC
If the clue (not just a link to a website) was in the LbNA database,
you can contact Wes Garrison and ask for the clue to be moved to
your "account". Then you can do any editing, and the Contact the
Placer button will go to your email.
If it's on a personal website, I can't see what one could do.
BTW, there's a whole gob of NJ clues that got transferred over to
Hello Kitty's account from T&E's when Elyse quit boxing, and Kitty
really only wanted one of them. Great clues and places, but not
always a box there.

From: The Doubtful Guests | Date: 2004-12-14 04:35:59 UTC
If the clue is on a personal website and that website still works, you could still adopt the box.  If the box is missing you could rewrite the clues to mention the website instead of going there.
 
If the website no longer works you might  be able to find it in the Way Back machine.  A site that archives old web pages.  I have found letterbox clues there even though their website no longer worked.  In that case you could just copy the text and paste it into the adopted box listing using a regular LbNA listing instead of the link.  Disconnect the broken link of course. 
 I don't know what the official word is so these are just ideas.
 
Scarab
