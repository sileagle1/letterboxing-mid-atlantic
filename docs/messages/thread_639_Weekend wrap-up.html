Weekend wrap-up

From: Cyclonic07@aol.com | Date: 2004-12-17 03:19:11 UTC
 
Well, now that I have finally fixed my network (router burned out while I was away), I can catch up on some emails.  I know I own a few folks some replies, I will get to them tomorrow.
 
But, for now, I'd just like to relate how my weekend went.  I got back into the DC area Sunday night, and after three days of marathoning a coaster in 42 degree whether, I was a bit tired, so I slept in Monday.  Finally, I left for home, and deciding not to do what I originally planed, mainly due to severe soreness, I instead just hit some quick boxes on the way home.
 
First up was the Ball's Bluff series, by the infamous Apple Dumpling Gang.  It was the first of their boxes I have done, and I've got to say, very impressive.  Great stamps, and beautiful hand made logs.  I found all three in fine shape, though the logs are full.  Great hidden away gem of a place too, made even better because I reenact with the 71st PA, which played a major role in that battle.
 
Heading north out of Leesburg, I hit the Clustered Spires of Fredrick, in the library in Fredrick, MD.  Still there and in great shape, really neat find too.  Again, bravo to the ADG. 
 
I also tried for High Knob in Gimbrill State Park, but stuck out on that one.
 
Finally, I easily found both the Fun at the Falls boxes in Cunningham State park, as it was getting dark.  The boxes were OK for now, but really need to be repacked, they are in plastic pencil boxes, and the bags outside are showing some signs of wear.  Also, the bag inside is too small to hold the log.  I put an extra bag on each log, but my spare bags were too small as well, and I didn't have any of my super heavy duty outside bags with me.  I will contact to placer about them.
 
Well, that is all for now, hope everyone else had a fun, productive weekend.
