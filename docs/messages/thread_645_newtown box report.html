newtown box report

From: rooindie | Date: 2004-12-20 01:32:07 UTC
Silent Doug and I made a stop in New Hope to do maintenance on
Persuasion (thanks Irene), which wasn't as quick as it should have
been because I couldn't remember exactly where I hid the box - d'oh!
We sat down to eat our lunch and plan our attack on the Battle of the
Bands, when it promptly started to rain. No worries, we have rain
gear!

So off we went to the Schofield Covered Bridge parking lot. I was to
follow clues for VNV Nation while Silent Doug did Radiohead. I read
stone bridge as "covered bridge", so we promptly missed that one.
Found Radiohead with only minor inability to follow directions.

On to Police, the clues of which I just loved, particularly "every
breath you take". Silent Doug and I had a minor disagreement about
the meaning of the word "south", which involved us taking one of the
mudiest trails I have ever seen. Crossing a small stream, I promptly
sunk in mud up to my knee, no lie. Waterproof boots really only work
when you keep the water below the top of your boot. After realizing
we were on the wrong track, I realized why Silent Doug thought it was
funny when I sunk up to my knee when he experienced the same fate,
though for him it was only mid-calf. Anyway, for those of you who
haven't found this box yet, do so; it's got a wonderful hiding spot!!

These boxes were followed by Across the Irish Sea and the Beatles,
both with no problems. Then back to find VNV Nation, with a stop for
a non-battle of the bands box, the Hop Devil.

Since it was only 4:30 at this point - there's still like 30 minutes
of dusk, right? - and we weren't completely frozen, we drove off to
find a few more boxes. Found NIN and the Grateful Dead with no
problem, but by the time we got to Frank Zappa, it was so dark we
couldn't tell which trees were dead and which were alive, so no luck
there.

All in all, not a bad afternoon; we found 10 boxes, plus one
hitchhiker, and it only took about 15 minutes to regain sensation in
my toes with the car heater turned on high.

Once again, a big shout out is due to Carvin' Carmen - nice work!!

Silent Doug and I had selected bands for the battle, but never did
our share (we suck); does it count for anything that I still have it
on my to-do list??

Thanks for the hunt!

Jennifer

Weekend letterboxing

From: Alan Marx | Date: 2004-12-20 04:10:35 UTC
T-shirt controversy notwithstanding :), sounds like many of you had a great weekend out there on the trails.
 
I was in Mechanicsville VA and Bob2 turned me on to quite a few of his boxes.  Went hunting for 8, found 6.  There is still quite a bit of debris in the trees from the storm last September in spots.  That must have been quite a bit of water. 
 
Bob2's Crump Park series was a great walk through a beautiful, historic farm site.  I saw a Heron take flight not 10 feet from me.  Also saw brand new baby lambs with some of the sheep and had a conversation with a horse.  Great series, thank you Bob2.
 
On his Mountain Road Series:  I went looking for 2 of them and was successful on both.  This is really a neat series to see a little part of VA I have never seen before in my frequent visits to the Richmond area.  Wonderful Photo Opportunities if you like interesting roadside history.  Pretty stamps too.
 
In his A Garden and A Rebellion clue, I found the first box, exquisite stamp, thank you very much.  Excellent hike.  Saw a large raptor take flight out of a tree but wasn't close enough to identify it.  LOTS and LOTS of squirrels.  Absolutely one of the prettiest stamps I have found so far.  This hike takes you far enough away from the rest of the world that I actually forgot I was anywhere near a city the size of Richmond. There was a lot of construction/rebuild going on at the second site as it seemed to have been heavily damaged in the flood so I couldn't really do a thorough search.  I will need to go back when there is less traffic there as I really want to complete the series.
 
Thanks Bob2 for an excellent weekend.  I really enjoyed your work.
 
Be good, Box well...
 
Somecomic (already up to 18 finds since I started on 11 29 04, I think I have a problem)

I was in NJ this weekend (Fri & Sat) and I had great hopes for letterboxing.  We were staying in Avalon and I headed down to Cape May Point looking for a Walk with the birds.  I forgot my bug spray and the bugs were really bad, then I got flustered and keep looking over my left shoulder instead of my right shoulder.  Once I got back to the car I realized my mistake and that I had actually looked at the stump and it didn't register, don't I feel like an idiot.  Then I went looking for MW Box #1.  I kept following signs for Higsbees beach but I never found the beach.  At this point, I'm totally disguised.  On the way back to Avalon, I stopped to find Hotrod in the Village.  Finally I was successful!!  As I was getting ready to leave the area, I realized that I had lost my cell phone.  Luckily, it was next to this letterbox and not in the swamp at Cape May Point.  At this point I figured I was short on time, and I better quit while I was still sane.  The letterboxing wasn't as successful as I had hoped but it was a wonderful day and I thoroughly enjoyed the trails at Cape May Point and Cold Springs area.  My husband wonders why I love doing this, but I'm looking forward to going back and trying again!
JuleBee

Re: [lbma] Weekend letterboxing

From: Heather Siple | Date: 2007-06-12 00:31:47 UTC
This is not the first time I've heard about flies and deet.  I've had several reports from boxers looking for Miss P's River Box that they were wearing deet bug spray and the flies went for it like BBQ sauce.  Meanwhile, since there are few mosquitoes there, we never used any bug spray and got ne're a  nibble from anything.  Biting flies really seem to be ATTRACTED to deet.
 
Full-body clothing with something underneath seems to be the best bug repellant we've found for boxing, though the bug spray is always in the backpack!
We went boxing last summer down Dover way and ended up on the wrong trail once.  High grass was infested with deer ticks.  Came out with our pants covered in them.  Fortunately, we were wearing swim suits under our clothes at the time and could shed a layer.  Managed to get just about all of them by peeling off our pants and bagging them tightly in plastic for the trip home.  Also prevents poison ivy.
 
Mother Duck

I finally got a day to letterbox, so I headed out to Bombay Hook for the Pierre's Vinyl Cut series.  I stopped in the vistor's center to see if the Parson's Point trail was opened and it was.  No eaglets this year.  The lady warned me that there were reports of flies.  I thought I was ready because I had brought my 25% deet spray.  When I hit the first trail, I got about 30 feet before I could not take the flies, so I got back to my car, put on a long sleeve shirt and resprayed myself.  I have never seen so may flies they kept getting between my eyes and glasses but I kept on.  I found 2 of 3 of the boxes and really enjoyed the tower on Bear Swamp trail.  The breeze kept the flies away.  It was a good thing that there has been so much discussion about tick removal, as I was covered in deer ticks.  So much for Deet working.  I may have to be a cold weather letterboxer, because I really hate ticks.& nbsp; But it was fun to get out and box. And towards sunset my husband and I went back out and there was so much wildlife, it was amazing.  That's what I love about letterboxing, there's always an adventure.
