While poking around LBPNW the following gift idea was presented --
and with full credit to the creator I add their post below:

--- In LbPNW@yahoogroups.com, "erfellie" <hemenekawan@h...> wrote:
> what I want for letterboxing? what every one wants! A FLAT DRY
SURFACE TO STAMP ON!!!!! . . .

"erfellie" <hemenekawan@h...>
I am not too good at giving directions so here is my pre-school
version, Picture an elephants head. with big ears. The ears are the
pencil pouches and the head would be the piece of wood. I used an
8'X8' square and drilled 2 holes in each side of it to line up with
the holes in the pouches, however you may want to cut out a rectangle
of wood that would be large enough to line all all 3 holes (I used
strong ribbon knotted sever times to attach). When your all done,
you can fold it up like sandwhich, one pouch in front, wood in the
middle, and the other pouch on the back.

Now I'm sure some of the engineers in this group may get confused,
but there isn't actually an elephant in the backpack.

And instead of ribbon/cord, I might try free style binder rings, so I
can easily replace the pencil pouches when the zipper breaks (once a
month?).
