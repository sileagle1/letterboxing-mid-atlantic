Well, boxing adventures abound today I am sure.

have some lunch and devise some gas inducing clues!

Looking forward to tomorrow's aventures....

Well, we were only mildly adventurous. Dashed into the house after
church to eat, then changed into NEW comfy warm socks, added hiking
shoes, put NEW inkpads and stamps into my fanny-pack, grabbed the
clues for the nearest unfound boxes and looked up to see the snow
had begun. So, yes, we boxed in the snow. But the hikes were short
and we were only about 15 min. from home. Moved the Cherokee Rose HH
from one boat basin to the other.
Discovery of the day: I do not like stamping in with cold fingers.
I'm thinking of holing up and just carving (my NEW Speedy-Stamp)
till spring.
Aud
Heading for more coffee, tea, cocoa, anything warm in a mug....

From: Art Vandelay | Date: 2004-12-26 23:53:14 UTC
Hi!
 
We had a great boxing day.- Went to Wayne PA on our way to bring my son back to college.  We found all the boxes of the 12 Boxes of Christmas.  A good series and very clever stamps.  Mizscarlet (creator of the series) actually met us on the trail and accompanied us for about half the boxes and an exchange of course.  Then we met two other couples on the trail- J&R from Maryland and Hexacat & Trax from PA. Exchanged with them too of course which was an added bonus to the day- freezing cold, but definitely worth the time and near frostbite!  This series will not be around much longer, so if you get a chance, go look for them.
 
Cat
