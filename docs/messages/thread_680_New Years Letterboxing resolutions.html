From: The Doubtful Guests | Date: 2005-01-02 05:05:53 UTC
Resolutions?  Nah.  Plans?  Yes.
 
1. Plant enough cool, new boxes in my own area to get people down from PA and out and about from around here.  Especially, I have a few older, crude stamps in boxes here that I can add new boxes around, to make them a series and more interesting.
 
2. Carve and plant the National Museum of the American Indian Series from Indian drawings I photographed from the exhibits.  I carved the first one today.  Turned out pretty good.  I hope to plant them before the Icebreaker gather.  By the gather there should be about 9 new boxes up this side of the Potomac river from the gather. 
 
3. Get planted the 2nd in the series of WOM adult art images and carve at least one more. 
4. Help with the spring gather in MD. 
 
5. Replace "The Inventor" with a 3rd ship in its unintended series.  This placement has been gotten by the gardeners twice.  Instead of recarving I do a different ship.  Its such a good place I don't want to abandon it.  Besides, the first stamp is in Hall's book. 
 
6. Finally do a new sig stamp. 
 
Scarab of the Doubtful Guests

I guess this is where I chime in. *Happy New Year, everyone!*

Scarab and I would like to co-host a Mid-Atlantic Spring Gather,
preferably on a Saturday in mid-May at Black Hill Regional Park in
Germantown, Maryland. This park currently has 15 published
letterboxes and many more within a short driving distance. There is
a 500-acre lake with canoe/kayak rental, 3 miles of paved trails,
over 10 miles of natural-surface trails and yes, Irene, Dude-on-a-
leash is welcome! There is camping nearby at Little Bennett Regional
Park, not to mention numerous hotels.

We want this to be big and have ideas for special temporary boxes.
We would like to draw attendants from all the Mid-Atlantic States.

upfront for the pavilion rental, I would like to get a feel for your
