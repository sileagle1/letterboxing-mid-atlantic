Road Rally Info

From: rallymastr | Date: 2005-01-10 02:26:38 UTC
Greetings Everyone,

I am the Rally Master. And I will be taking over the duties of organizing and posting
information about the upcoming Letterbox Road Rally: Where the Rubbermade Meets the
Road.

Any future inquiries about the rally should be directed to me this yahoo address. Natty
Bumppo will be forwarding any inquiries to me, so save yourself some time and send them
directly to me.

First off, the Road Rally is still awaiting final approval of facilities for January 22 [Weather
Hold: January 23]. The word will come down this week and I will post it as soon as I know.
I thank everyone for their patience.

We have some great stamps in already and we're looking forward to the others. Keep
carvin' folks. Contact me for shipping info.

Let me start with an official sign up: All those interested in participating, please email me
with the following info:

The Team Name
The Trail Names of 1) Driver and 2) Navigator
[No real names, please]
The Make/Model/License Plate of the Vehicle to be used.
[This is for safety reasons and will be explained on-site. Don't worry if you need to
change vehicles - that can be accomodated on-site as well]

You may have as many passengers in the vehicle as it may safely carry, but you MUST
HAVE at least one driver and one navigator. No Lone Wolves will be permitted for safety
reasons.

We have many people interested in the rally. We will be limited by daylight and the need
to space-out the vehicles. If the number of entries exceeds the number of time slots
available, a random drawing will be held prior to the event to determine the entrants. In
addition, a drawing will be held to determine post-time of each vehicle.
A mandatory organizational meeting will be held in the morning prior to first post-time.
All teams must attend for safety reasons. The site is great and has some wonderful boxes
and room for more. You can occupy yourself with E-ing, F-ing, X-ing and P-ing prior to
your post time and after your return before winners are announced. [I would also suggest
P-ing BEFORE your post time. You will not have time to stop en route]

I cannot emphasize the need for safety enough. Any activity involving motor vehicles is
inherently dangerous. Do not enter if you are unsure about your abilities. This project
does not require speed. In fact, anyone caught speeding, anyone caught engaging in
unsafe activity and anyone who completes the course quicker than the judges official time
will be disqualified. A significant breach of safety will result in the cancellation of the
activity. You will not be asked to do anything illegal or unsafe in the course of this
activity.

Have I scared you? I hope not. I write these things to remind everyone to be safe while
they have fun. I will be posting often this week. Keep watching.

Yours, Trudy...

Rally Master
