Papa Woodwose here in MD. Long time no post, and it's not even about

I know many folks on here are volunteers by nature so I thought I
might share a quick interest of mine that ya'll can volunteer your
computers for one of the most important cultural causes in human
history, IMHO.
To quote their website.
"SETI@home is a scientific experiment that uses Internet-connected
computers in the Search for Extraterrestrial Intelligence (SETI).
You can participate by running a free program that downloads and
analyzes radio telescope data."
If you aren't familar with SETI, they are the organization founded
in part by the Plantary Society, created by Carl Sagan. SETI is a
reputable science organization funded by Uni. Berekely and others.
They often mistakenly lumped into the UFO clump but the are by far
not. The SETI@Home screensaver is the first of what is known
as 'distributed computing', using home pc to function as a sort of
super super-computer. It uses the time your cpu would normally be
running screensavers and puts the cpu cycles to use. It is totally
safe and no more technical than running a screensaver. It analyzes
data and when you log onto the net, it asks to download that and
bring up more. If you wish, you can run your normal screensaver and
run the SETI as a background program, although I like seeing the
bargraph running showing my progress in examining the noises of the
stars. To date over 2.5 million users have loaded it and contributed
1000's of years of cpu time!!! Completely safe and doable for even
the most untech cpu user. Find it and answers at
http://setiathome.ssl.berkeley.edu/
I have always wanted to be part of this and never had a cpu modern
enough to handly it. Very proud to have contributed over 26 hours of
cpu time to the search so far and counting. Other distributed
computing programs are out there, many involving genome research.
One day we just might find out that we aren't the center of
attention.
