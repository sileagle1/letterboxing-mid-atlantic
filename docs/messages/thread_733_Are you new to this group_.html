I just joined.
 
Someone I respect on the yahoogroup DogsLovetoRun mentioned letterboxing and geocaching as something she does with her son about a year ago.
 
Then about a month ago through Freecycle I gave away a dog saddlebag to someone who wanted it for her dog when they geocaching.  I asked her about geocaching, looked into it, am not up to spending the money on a GPS unit right now, but reading through the geocaching site there was a reference to letterboxing. . .. which I looked into and was fascinated.
 
I love to hike.  My husband loves to hike.  My dog loves to hike.  We all need more exercise.  My daughter (almost 5) prefers to sit and do nothing, and then complain she's bored.  She is interested in the idea of treasure-hunting, and loved helping follow the clues for our first find.  Stamps and ink pads are also very exciting for her.  Even my husband is becoming interested (and he usually thinks everything we do is crazy).
I'm getting my friends interested, so there will be crews of us (mostly moms and kids and dogs) looking for boxes in NJ and NY.  Then we'll start planting. . . .

Re: Are you new to this group?

From: gallant_rogue | Date: 2005-01-23 19:02:46 UTC
Since we are under house arrest by the blanket of snow and blustery
winter wind...allow me to jump in and introduce myself: I'm new
here!

I'm the youngest son of a wood carving instructor, I'm an eccentric
history buff, obsessive mystery fan, compulsive follower of
paranormal culture & a Northern Virginia Native. I used to own my
own consulting firm but now have a goverment job. I recently bought
a home in Prince William Va and live their with my grlfriend her
teenaged son and two Dachshunds

I saw the feature article in the Washington Post Weekend section
back in 03. I visited the LBNA web site and bookmarked it. But it
was just few weeks ago when I finally got around to carving a stamp.
I found the hardest part about getting started was figuring out what
my personal stamp should be. I saw der Mad Stampers page and was a
little intimidated. But I've created a couple of "ok" stamps now and
I anxious to get going. I have been involved in many annual treasure
hunts at a local pub as a participant and then eventually as
organizer. I love solving and writing clues. I am very familiar with
the history and terrain of NoVa. I'm real anxious to get out and
start solving clues and finding boxes. I look forward to placing
boxes of my own.

I've only hunted for one box and I couldn't find it. I don't know if
I looked in the wrong place or it simply wasn't there. I suspect it
was removed by non letterboxers because the area was so open. I love
hiking and exploring but before I set out for other boxes I'd like
to have some reasonable expectation they are still in place.

I'm not aware if anybody I know Letterboxes, so I welcome the advice
& suggestions from members of this group.

Thanks in advance!

Gallant Rogue

From: The Doubtful Guests | Date: 2005-01-23 20:03:05 UTC
Cool.  Glad to have more Nern VA boxers!
 
I am not very familiar with the Prince William Boxes.  The Pirates of Sunset Grace were there last summer.  Some of the Scooby series, in the same park are also there but I understand the planter is working up a new expanded series there with guest carvers (including me). 
 
First Manassas was in place last summer but you never know.
 
I believe Witchiepoo is still in the hobby.  I think I've seen her stamp around recently.  But a lot of people go dormant through the winter so don't be surprised if people do not respond. 
 
I just did the Tribute to the Beatles series at Lake Accotink.  That was a very satisfying series. Search for an earlier post of mine probably titled, Where are you boxing this weekend, for a list of boxes I just visited. 
 
Come to the Icebreaker gather on Saturday and can get a lot of information. 
 
Scarab of the Doubtful Guests
