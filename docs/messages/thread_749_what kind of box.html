Hikers & Hounds was what my son, my sister and myself came up with. My sister and I were hiking our 3 dogs and stumbled across a poorly re-hidden box off trail. So while we were researching letterboxing we saw the various team names on the Search for Boxes list and new we needed one for ourselves. Everyone in my family has a dog (and many cats) and I knew I wanted something generic enough so that whichever sibling, niece or nephew came along, we could use the same stamp. When my brother came out from Colorado last year he and his dog Chance went with us. My husband, sister(s) and their various hounds have all come along but mostly, Hikers & Hounds is Irene and Dude the Wonder Dog.

I love this topic! It is so interesting to see the background of
everyone's trail names!

Here is ours:
Team SuddenTurkey - the name has no particular meaning, and we're not
necessarily particularly fond of turkeys: it came about after I was
trying to decifer a note someone left on my desk one day - I gave it
to several people to help me out, and "sudden turkey?" was the
response I got from several of them. Well, in our family, we're always
trying to come up with funny band names (my personal fave - "Bologna
Snowflake" - don't you remember making bologna snowflakes as a kid?
You know, you fold the bologna in half twice, and then bite out a nice
pattern, when you unfold the bologna - you've got a bologna snowflake!
Hey, I think it's better than Vomit Launch!) We thought "SuddenTurkey"
would make a cool band name, but hijacked it to be our family website
name and then our letterboxing team name. (By the way, the note
actually said "see you on Thursday" - go figure!)

My trail name: highlysung - it's actually been my email address since
email was invented - or at least since the days of astro, telnet and
gopher when I got my email address! At the time, I was a voice major
at Temple (classical and opera) - I thought highlysung referred
appropriately to my voice, but, even better, sounded suspiciously
close to highly-strung - hmmmm. Who - me?

Eric's trail name: iplaytunes - Well, he plays tunes. He's a
pianist/musical director for musical theater.

Caite's trail name: Gwenllian - she's proud of and interested in her
Welsh heritage, and so named herself after a princess of wales from (I
think) the 13th century or so. So when you see her cute personal stamp
with the whale wearing a crown, you'll understand why she signs
"Gwenllian, princess of "wales" next to it!

Carley's trail name: Lil'E - she's only 5 weeks old today! Our last
name starts with E, and Eric's students often call him "Big E" or "E",
so Carley is little E, hence "Lil'E". She went letterboxing when she
was only 9 days old! I'm still working on her personal stamp.

highlysung of Team SuddenTurkey
