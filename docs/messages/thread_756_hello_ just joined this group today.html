From: silver_vikings | Date: 2005-01-27 04:33:10 UTC
Hello!
I just found and joined this group today, but we have been
letterboxing for almost exactly one year now. We - our family of 5 -
go by the name Silver Vikings, although we're not that old yet (at
least 3 of us *G*) and neither are we Vikings .... well depends from
where you look at this :) We are originally from Germany and moved
to the USA about 4 years ago. So maybe you could say, we're kind of
very Southern Vikings, although we were all born in the part that
used to belong to the Roman empire long long ago, so we look more
like those Southern Europeans, nothing Nordic at all ... I don't
really know how we got this name, it was a brainstorming effort of
the kids and they like to play "Age of Empires", so who knows? :))

Nonetheless we're here, love treasure hunting um, letterboxing and
are happy that sometime over the summer our area here in SW-PA got a
bit more active than it seemed to be when we started out. Several
new boxes got planted, a few more people got involved .... okay, it
still is no comparison to Eastern PA and everywhere else near there
or in the New England States :) Nonetheless, with the very first box
we un-dig from the snow and ice one year ago, we found a hitchhiker
(Travelling Pants) and were sooooooo excited. Especially as it had
taken us sometime to dicover the right spot ;) We usually start
looking for the boxes on the wrong side of the path or get the
directions a bit mixed up .... fortunately we have the good excuse
of language problems ;) .... but up to now, we never had to give up,
fought nesting ants, undig several trashbags before the right one,
wandered endlessly around hundreds of rocks to find the two rocks on
top of each other .... each time the dad wants to call it quits and
nonetheless we persevere ....and succeed. It is fun!

I knew about letterboxing for a long time. I think I read this first
article in the National Geographic a long time ago, but somehow
never got into it.
3 years ago a new neighbor told me at the school bus stop that they
enjoyed that their move brought them to a new area for
letterboxing .... I was fascinated .... and forgot again about it.
Almost 2 years ago - I think- I read another article in an Airline's
Magazine ... and this time there was the web adress of lbna added. I
wrote it down. Again it took some time until I checked it out, but
finally ... kaboom!

We are not the most avid letterboxers around, too many other
competing activities. Best is when we can go letterboxing while
riding our bikes (there are a few impatient family members who need
to go fast)*G*

I am glad I found this list. It sounds like a lot of fun and
inspiration. I definitely would love to find out about a meeting in
case it ever took place in a not too distant place ... who knows...

Wishing you all a happy "hunting" season!
Ulrike
(which btw is a Swedish name,now maybe here is a clue *G*?)
