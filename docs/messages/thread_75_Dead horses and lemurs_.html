Re: Dead horses and lemurs?

From: Tammy, Cat, and Sarah | Date: 2004-05-10 16:22:22 UTC
I really am fascinated by the interactions on the main Yahoo site.
When we began boxing in 2002 I was terrified of expressing a strong
opinion for fear of flames and backlash. We entered the group soon
after one member had been ousted for hate mail and we were far too
excited about our new hobby to chance being ostracized. But sitting
by watching people abuse each other became extremely difficult,
especially when my friends were being bashed.

For a while I was really torn up about it, wanting to catch up on
the goings-on but fearful of reading yet another slam. It was
making my stress relieving hobby a real drag. My solution was to
ignore as much as I could and to be sarcastic and subversive
whenever that was not possible. People take themselves way too
seriously so I tried to poke a hole in their egos with a little
absurdity.

So I guess the whole point of this long-winded puff piece is this: I
am sarcastic and a bit off center. And I talk a lot, which on this
site means I will post a lot. And most of it will be garbage,
really strange, or dripping with sarcasm. (and occasionally in
incomplete sentences, and always with parentheses) Consider
yourselves warned.

Tammy
ADG #1
The sarcastic one

Re: Dead horses and lemurs?

From: Tammy, Cat, and Sarah | Date: 2004-05-10 19:56:39 UTC
Now there's something I hadn't considered: beating a dead lemur with
a dead cat! "101 and uses for a dead cat" was required reading when
I was in grade school. I don't remember beating lemurs as one of
the options but maybe it was too progressive for the time. However I
do remember the day when dead lemur beaters would be put in the
stockade or pummeled with stones. (lottery, anyone?)

In 1889, my great uncle, Horatio Lemures Moneke the Third, was
caught beating dead lemurs in public so they hung him upside down
and threw snakes at him until he repented. Be thankful that dead
lemur beater persecution has ended and don't forget the martyrs that
got us here.

Tammy,
ADG #1
