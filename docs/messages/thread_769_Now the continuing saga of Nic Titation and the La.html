From: Alan Marx | Date: 2005-01-30 04:57:21 UTC
Nic always kept her pad and pen close by.  She kept most of her notes in her head, she had an IQ that made Steven Hawking sit up and take notice.  Her attachment to pen and paper was more of a ruse, a distraction slyly used to conceal the three missing digits of her left hand.  Her disfigurement was a constant reminder that Speedball's warning to carve away from your hand was more than just a statement made to prevent liability, it was in fact, prophetic.
 
"Why the interest in the Twelve Days series" queried Nic. "All Twelve of those boxes were retired last week."
 
"There were thirteen boxes" said MissMoon.  Nic knew that, her "faux" faux pas was intended to test her visitor's knowledge of the case.  She had been on the trail of that series long before they were retired.  Her mind raced back to the Muggle traffic, to MizScarlet, to a case long since closed.  "There were thirteen of them, there wasn't a single clue I could figure out and I promised my boss I would find them before she returned from Dartmoor". 
 
"Who's your boss?" asked Nic.
 
"Hounds and Hikers" answered MissMoon, the name flowing from her lips in seemingly slow motion.  As soon as the word Hounds spilled forth from her crimson lips, Nic knew how it would end; the boss's name, the rest of Nic's day, and the case.  She knew none of it would end well.

MissMoon’s sobbing made Nic nauseous.  She hated crying.  Oh sure, there was a time when Nic would burst into tears at the drop of a hat, but those days were gone, long gone.  She had come up the hard way.  Cyclonic had been her mentor and her tears dried up forever the day he found her sobbing softly in the woods, her bearings off due to electro-magnetic fields from overhead wires.  He had slapped her hard that day.  Slapped her and exclaimed “There’s no crying in letterboxing”.  She could still feel the sting and the humiliation she felt like it was yesterday.  She could also still hear his next statement. “look sweetie, instead of crying over your faulty compass readings, why not close your eyes and try to feel where the letterbox is hidden”.  That was the day she stopped crying and started to rely on instinct when hunting for boxes.  MissMoon’s tears only provided further evidence that, when push came to shove, she was the first one out of the hunt.

 

What Nic couldn’t figure yet was what the 13the box from a retired Christmas series could possibly have to do with the Groundhog Day wild goose chase hunt that Hikers and Hounds was preparing.  She also wasn’t sure she wanted to know...
