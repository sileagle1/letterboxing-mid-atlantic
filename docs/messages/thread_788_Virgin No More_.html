My first time was painful, embarrassing and messy.

I had read about it alot - and had even seen some pictures of people doing it in
magazines.
(Smithsonian April 1998 - Ooo La-la!)

It started out okay. There was all that weird excitement and nervous energy at first.

But then it was kind of confusing: Where to get it? Where to put it? What to do with it?

And finally it was just humiliating: Are you okay? Was it fun for you? Wanna try it again?

But like so many things that begin that way, you keep doing it and it gets better. And
soon, you can't get enough of it and you do it all the time.

And if you're not careful you get... poison ivy.

From: silver_vikings | Date: 2005-02-01 15:16:09 UTC
Our first was Northmoreland Park. Just me and the kids. The fun
thing was that there was this couple we didn't seem to be able to
get rid of as soon as we approached the place where we were supposed
to look at the side of the path. So we kept stopping and blowing our
nose, so they had to pass us. Then they stopped and cleaned their
shoes, so we needed to pass them. Then we stopped and scratched our
heads and they needed to pass .... :) .... get the picture?
This kept going for a while until they and we looked at each other
in complete exasperation. And then in recognition ;)) The lady just
said "letterboxing?" and it turned out that we had run into the
placers who where on a maintenance trip!
They let us look, though, but inexpereinced as we were, the kids
always ran too far into the woods and I could always see the
placers' bewilderment and kept calling the kids back. Finally we
found it and Alan (the placer) even helped us to get it out from
under the ice and rock as we were not able to move it at all. It was
a fun experience and so nice to meet people who were already into it
and could point us to other interesting boxes in the area.
But we still get hilarious when we box and start coughing and
scratching and blowing noses when we get closer to the hiding spot,
just in memory of our first trip.

The Bandits' first trip was to a 2 box series which has since been pulled by the National fun-Police Service... The day started out on the chilly side, as it was late March in the Hudson Valley, where May flowers sometime don't show up until June.  No sooner than we hit the trail, the sky clouded over and the gentle breeze off the Hduson turned into gale force winds.  Not to be deterred, we pressed on and in the freezing drizzle found our very first box.... decided it was too cold and would withstand the delayed gratification of box #2 another day.
 
We've been back to Vanderbilt Mansion many times and always marvel at how short the walk to the site of the first box really was.  Now that we are experienced boxers, we would probably cut 40 minutes off that box!

HEAR YE! HEAR YE! HEAR YE!

On this Groundhog Day, February 2nd, the year 2005 on Gobbler's Knob, Punxsutawney, Pennsylvania, Punxsutawney Phil, King of the Groundhogs, Father of all Marmota, Seer of Seers, Prognosticator of Prognosticators has been summoned by Groundhog President William Cooper. Phil gleefully exited his burrow at 7:31 a.m. to be greeted by his longtime friend and handler, Bill Deeley, who held him high so he could greet the large throng of Faithful Followers. He wished them all a "Happy Groundhog Day." Bill placed him atop the old oak stump where he surveyed his surroundings. He then turned to President William and in Groundhogese directed him to the scroll which reads:

Natural disasters have been a terrible thing; tsunamis,
downpours, floods, landslides, heavy snowstorms, avalanches,
frigid temperatures, sleet and freezing rain.
They all cause much destruction, suffering and pain.

When it seems like too much with which to cope;
The coming of spring brings us new hope.
