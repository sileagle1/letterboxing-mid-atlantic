New letterbox: Persistence of Winky
Philadelphia, Philadelphia County, PA
Placed by: Winky
Box webpage located at:

Clues:
The Philadelphia Museum of Art has the great privilege to host the only
U.S. showing of the Salvador Dalm exhibition. Over 200 of Dalms works will
be on display from February 16th through May 15th, many of which have never
been shown in the United States. Commemorate this event as only a
letterboxer can and come find the Persistence of Winky.

Your search starts in the Azalea Garden in Fairmount Park. Find the Pergola
on the East Side of the circular parking lot and stand in the circle in the
center between the two benches. (No real reason for this other than the
face that it exists!) Face the fountain and head up the sidewalk South
East.

Do you see the place where Bob the Builder hangs out? Take the paved path
that runs past his hangout. Along the path you will pass some brave and
surly characters. Don't listen to Friedrich - he will point you in the
wrong direction. John Paul appears to be looking for this letterbox too and
Casimir will be happy to keep an eye on your car.

After you pass Casimir you will see the Welterweight Steps. (See if you can
resist the urge to practice your Rocky impression here) At the top of the
steps, find the Mariner and Merchant. Facing this, look for the Gazebo not
to far in the distance. Shame the eagle didn't want to hang out on this
one.

Head up to the Gazebo and admire the view of the Schuylkill River. When you
are finished taking in the view, turn back around and find Mona Schneidman's
bench. Looks like a pretty good place to sit down and stamp, huh? Good
thing you are so close now. Standing at Mona's right arm - take 8 steps to
the two trunked tree. Look deep into the Yucca to find your treasure.

Please take care in removing and replacing the box as this is a pretty well
travelled area.

While you are there, go check out Salvador Dalm. Like this letterbox, he
will be here only a short while. This letterbox will only be available for
the duration of the Dalm exhibition.

Please shoot me an email and let me know how the box is holding up.

Happy Letterboxing!

Winky


"I believe that the moment is near when, by a procedure of active paranoic
thought, it will be possible to systematize confusion and contribute to the
total discrediting of the world of reality."

- Salvador Dalm
