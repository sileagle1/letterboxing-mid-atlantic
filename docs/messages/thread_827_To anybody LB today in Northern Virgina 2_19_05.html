To anybody LB today in Northern Virgina 2/19/05

From: gallant_rogue | Date: 2005-02-19 16:20:26 UTC
It's freezing cold but the skys are clear blue... I've spent three
consecutive weekends terrorizing Lake Accotink ... I think I have
found everything there is to be found... (including that very old
A&O RR LB/Geocache). I heard back from the placer of the Lion Witch
Wardrobe Series... the Lion & Wardrobe are definetly MIA... however
the Witch was there if anybody is interested. Today I'm heading out
to a new location (for me) to see if I can find some other two year
old letterboxes.

I have found some mystery boxes that are alive & well:

"Top Secret"... Jem Heist's brand new "Cardinal" and (after
reporting it missing!) I did manage to find "Bastion"

If anybody else is out in NoVa today... share your results please!
BTW...don't tell me where it is if you have... but has anybody
solved the Temporary Haiku Mystery?

From: The Doubtful Guests | Date: 2005-02-20 04:23:22 UTC
Night Owl and I were drawn out by the bright sunlight.  Armed, or rather legged, with extra flannel jammies under our jeans we enjoyed a Suburban Saturday in Algonquin Park.  The park isn't large but it is full of spooky vines and pools.  I wonder when the best spooky time to visit would be.  We enjoyed the pure "suburbanness" of the stamps.
 
"...and on the weekends we would barbecue, and say 'delicious, delicious.'"
 
I really enjoyed those Accotink boxes too.  I have a theory that there are more in the O&A RR series.  It is a Letterbox/Geocache hybrid.  I remembered seeing somewhere that the geocache was a series.  Maybe the other geocaches have other stamps.  I just have to get down there with my GPS sometime. 
 
I want to go back in the spring to stand on the pedestrian bridge over the railroad and watch the trains go by.  I once saw the Southern Crescent in green liveried steam pass by there. 
 
Scarab of the Doubtful Guests
