boxes we sought were there, unfound for months and years with the
exception of one (one of the SpringChicks, but we were distracted by
Hurricane Ivan's flood). These are, as one would expect, very low

say, the Pinnacle. Sometimes the hunt and the company of friends
are paramount to finding the box. But, oh what a rush it is to find
them!

Give it a try, you may even find a couple close to Gotham!

Re: "Priceless" Query

From: Lightnin Bug | Date: 2005-03-03 15:51:05 UTC
Sorry if I make it casually sound easy. It is not. It takes
time/effort (in some cases significant effort) to solve the puzzle
and then one needs to coordinate with the availability of several
like minded people to go and seek it out. Once you feel you have it
is when you make the decision to go.

It is far easier to just grab the son and go (yes, we are vehicle
enabled and having lived near NYC some time I completely understand
the impracticality of owning a car there), but we collaborate with
several people to solve these things and it would be kind of scaley
and reptilian to just use them for their insight and get the boxes
solo. Our situation is complicated by our lives outside of 'boxing -
there are many demands that do take priority (breaking out the worlds
smallest violin for myself). So, we make the most of when we can.

LB
