Guess I am a little clueless on that one.

Anyway, for those who don't know me, here is my little bio. I hail
from Rocky Mount, NC. I am 27 (yes, really) which seems to be rather
young in the letterboxing community. I have an 8 year old son named
Andrew and a "significant other" named Joe. We three make up the
Penguin Patrol. I have about 2 and a half years of boxing
experience, but I am probably not the avid boxer most of you are.
The boxes in eastern NC are very sparce and we don't have alot of
money to travel alot with. Most recent trip was to D.C. (Found a few
boxes there, but got hung up on a clue with a nasty cypher from
Scarab, so no time for other boxes) I have found around 50 boxes and
planted around 10. I like boxing for the adventures (or
misadventures), the thrill of the hunt, and the art of the stamp. My
personal life revolves around the college life right now. (I'm back
in school...again) As for my job, I am an English tutor at the
college I attend. I am also a den leader for my son's cub scout
pack, which eats up alot of my time. My free time is spent reading
funny mystery books, hanging with my dog, a Boykin Spaniel (look it
up) named Riley, in front of the T.V., camping if it is warm, and
whatever various kid activities are on the calendar at the time. I
have never meet anyone in person from the letterboxing community,
but look forward to getting to a gather one day soon (Maybe Maryland
Tempus Fugit) or throwing one (probably at Pocahauntas State Park in
Richmond, VA in June 2005). I am a little leary of throwing one
though because of lack of experience. If anyone wants to cohost one
with me I WOULD LOVE THE HELP AND THE OPPORTUNITY!! I would even
consider a different location... (please email me if interested at
letterboxer@...).

Now that I have spilled my guts, does anyone want to tell me a
little about themselves? Does anyone live in the Richmond, VA area
or Norfolk, VA area that would like to partner up for boxing? :)

Liz
of Penguin Patrol
