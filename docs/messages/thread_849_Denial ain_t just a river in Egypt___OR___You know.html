From: Hikers_n_ Hounds | Date: 2005-03-04 02:42:00 UTC
Just how addicted to this insanely, wonderful hobby have you become? Here's my most recent epiphany. I actually turned the stove on and cooked the other day and there were some left overs. Being the slob that I am and knowing I have a son who works 2nd shift I left them on the stove for him and/or the trash or both. I woke up the next day and *gasp*!! SOMEONE USED ONE OF MY LETTERBOXING CONTAINERS TO PUT THE LEFT OVERS IN!!! I was freakin' out and my husband looked at me like I have 5 heads, which is just crazy 'cause I only have 2, and says "Hon, that's what those things were MADE FOR". And I swear, for about 1/2 a second I thought HE was nuts and stared at him as if he was speaking in tongues when it hit me, OH YEAH! That's what I used to by these little plastic containers for! I honestly had forgotten that little fact for a moment.
 
So how 'bout you?

Re: Denial ain't just a river in Egypt...

From: nattybumppolbna | Date: 2005-03-04 15:59:13 UTC
So our guest room has become Road Rally Central with all the boxes, stamps, bags, books,
labels, maps, clue lists, camoflage, return boxes, plates, cups, forks, knives spoons,
trashbags, etc. etc. laid out in preparation.

Last weekend we had guests. Their reaction was something like I would imagine you
would get had they walked in on a meth lab. They were probing my wife with questions
along the lines of, "Is there treatment available?" and "Have you considered an
intervention?"

My son asked a friend if he wanted to go letterboxing with us. I answered a phone call
from a concerned parent who wondered why I wanted to take his son out in the woods. I
explained and directed him to the web site for more info, but he declined. I am sure if I
had wanted to take his kid to a more wholesome event - like WWF wrestling, he would
have been fine with it.

I was on a job with some people who know I letterbox. We were going to be going to
some highly secure buildings. They sat me down to make sure I wasn't going to be
leaving anything behind - like "one of your boxes."
I joked, "You can never be sure."
They became very serious and indicated that leaving a letterbox behind would get me 10 -
20 years in maximum security.
I explained that it would do no good for me to put a letterbox in one of the buildings as
nobody could get to it.
They didn't look like they bought that story. Frankly I don't blame them because -
wouldn't you know it - I spent the whole time thinking about how I could possibly hide a
microbox in such a way that the general public might be able to.....

Perhaps an intervention is in order.
