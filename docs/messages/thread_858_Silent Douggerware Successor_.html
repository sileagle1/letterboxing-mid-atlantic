From: nattybumppolbna | Date: 2005-03-09 02:18:45 UTC
I'm just back from the Natty Bumppo Test Kitchen. Here are the results:

On the theory that warm water would expand materials differently - and thus put the
rubber/plastic containers to a real test - I submerged 3 containers in warm water for 1
minute in my kitchen sink.
I tested the SureFresh 34oz, the Rubbermaid Seal n' Saver 1.7 pt and the Milan Classic
"Silent Douggerware." All 3 came out with dry paper inside.

However all 3 leaked air after 30 seconds which leads me to believe that all 3 will collect
moisture through heating and cooling out in the real world. I know we have some
engineers with us here, so they may want to check my logic.

The SureFresh appeared to leak the most followed by the Rubbermaid and then the Milan
Classic.

Natty "Einstein" Bumppo
