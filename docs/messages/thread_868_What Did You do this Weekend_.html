From: Jan | Date: 2005-03-13 20:55:31 UTC
Well, since no one asked, I will pose the question because I finally
got to go outside boxing again. The birth of my little grandson has
kept me busy the past few weekends oohing and aahing over what a
clever little sleeper he is, but today, I had to go outside for a
while. I headed to Ashland and found Mother and Black Diamond hidden
in about 4 or 5 inches of snow. Then I moved up the road a bit and
was first finder of True Indigo's Waiting box in Danville. While I
was in the general area, I also was first finder for True Indigo's
mystery box, Fire Sprite. Hey, Lightnin' Bug, can you believe I found
a mystery box all by myself! Well, not exactly by myself, Barney
helped.

The weather is gorgeous today, perfect to be outside.
Jan of Team Little Dog

From: divinelotus73 | Date: 2005-03-14 00:45:23 UTC
Sunday was a perfect letterboxing day. Dad and I got up early (dare
I say 5am??) Started the morning at Rose Tree in Media. Went for
the "ode to good joe" box. Met up with Pennsylvania Smith for some
Irish Spirit in the afternoon, headed up to the Lehigh Valley. Ended
up at Vynecrest Winery. Great little stamp LB, and really nice
wine!! We had lunch at "Yoccos" and came home. We left our "two
peas in a pod" HH along the way, and also left behind "a Drifting
companion" HH and "a rose is a rose" HH, so all in all 200 miles on
the car and 6 stamps. I think Silent Doug had the right idea of
moving to Connecticut. Lotus

I had a pretty decent weekend.  Sat. I marched with my Civil War unit in the Trenton St. Patty's day parade.  It was nice out, so the parade route was packed.  The people get a kick out of it when we fire our guns.  It is great fun.
 
Sunday I headed downtown and managed to find two boxes, Where's Ben, a WOM, and The Persistence of Wink.  I had no trouble finding Where's Ben, great spot, and fantastic stamp.  I really had no trouble with The Persistence of Wink, except I had to be persistent, as the area was packed.  Also, fantastic stamp.  It is a massive box as well, I thought it would be a micro, or I would have dropped a HH into it, but I left the HH at home.  I did, however, find a HH, Peeps Bunny, brand new, and will soon move it along.
 
Next week, Tommcat and I are headed up the Lightn' Bug territory on Saturday, and Sunday Somecomic and I (and hopefully Son of Comic) are headed to Coney Island to get our butts kicked on the Cyclone, as well as get a little urban boxing in ahead of time.
