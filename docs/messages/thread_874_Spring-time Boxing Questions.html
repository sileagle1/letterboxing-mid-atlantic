Spring-time Boxing Questions

From: Elizabeth Grace | Date: 2005-03-15 14:56:35 UTC
Hi everyone!

After a winter hiatus, I'm back on the list. We had too much going on -
vacation, holidays, birthdays, and a couple of other things - I just
couldn't keep up with my mail. But the weather is getting warmer and my
kids have only two more weeks of "regular" school before we start our spring
schedule, which includes one day every week for hiking. Basketball is over,
too, so now we'll have Saturdays as well. (Can you picture me drooling? I
am SO ready to get outside!)

So, what I need are box recommendations in Southeast PA (including the
nearby NJ & DE area) that won't be too muddy. Moderately is okay (muddy
feet, maybe even a bit wet feet), I just don't want to have three
mud-encrusted children for the ride home.

First on our list for a weekend is to head back to Nolde Forest and finish
the other two boxes in the series. We also need to finish Who's the Author?
in Doylestown IF my youngest can overcome her fear of the the yellowjackets.
She still freaks when I even mention the word "letterboxing". I'm sure it
will only take one trip out to cure her, but we may have to tie her up
first. *grin*

How muddy is Tyler State Park this time of year? We could get some serious
mileage in one or two trips out there! Plus, its very close to home which
makes it a great week-day destination.

I'm also curious about French Creek State Park. We'd like to do our first
plant there and do some general hiking. Thoughts? Is it better to plant in
the Spring (before leaves) or in the summer or early fall when the foliage
is in full flight?

If we do manage to get our series planted in French Creek, we'll probably
need someone to adopt it for us. Most likely we'll be moving out of the
area before summer and I wouldn't want to leave them unattended forever.
What's the protocol for adoption?

If your still with me, thanks for any suggestions you have! Any and all are
welcome.

Happy boxing,
Elizabeth
aka The Mama Fishy

Pennypack park in Philadelphia has 7 0r 8 boxes, not too far off trail, and unless there was a major storm the day before you go out, the mud should be at a minimum.
 
look for the Ben Franklin Series, the Pennypack Park Series, the Birdhouse Letterbox.  There may be more that slip my mind, but it is a nice day long hunt for multiple boxes.

I was just at Tyler yesterday, and it wasn't too muddy at all.  I brought extra shoes and socks, like I usually do, but I had no need to change when I was done!  There are about 10 billion boxes there, so that's a good place to go!
