Word from the husband (notice the prevalence of gadgets here):
"If you have a book where both covers can be bent all the way back,
do that, and place the pages firmly on a board. Using a sharp drill
bit, a small one, drill a hole at one corner of the intended
opening. Unhook the blade from your scroll saw, thread it through
the hole and reattach it. Then saw out your opening. If you don't
want the opening to appear right away, fold some pages back with the
cover on each side."

I found some in RI that were really wooden boxes with a few added
touches to make them look like books. Much easier, I'd think, than
carving out a page.

But if you still want to carve a real book, clue all of the pages
together first. I did this going away to camp when I was young and
needed a hiding spot for my chocolate :) I used rubber glue on the
edges of the book, all 3 sides, and that bound them together well
enough that I was then able to use an Xacto to cut through the book
relatively easily.

I just did one a few months ago for a box. I would say that the method you are using is the
best way to insure good results. What commonly happens though is half-way through
your cuts become uneven and you nearly cut through to the page edge.

I used spring clamps to hold the pages firmly in place as I was cutting and just slowly
worked a dozen pages at a time. This way also gives you a pretty good interior edge for
gluing and - if you choose - lining with felt.
