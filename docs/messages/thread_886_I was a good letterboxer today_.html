I was a good letterboxer today.

From: cahillymom | Date: 2005-03-19 22:36:55 UTC
So. Up the 100 steps. huff, puff. (although we didn't count) Over
the crest of the hill and down, looking for the rock with the faded
blazes jutting into the path, begging to be sat upon. All the way to
the bottom of the hill, which meant we missed it. To go on to the
second box, or to go back for the first one? How tired am I? How
much do I want that box?
UP the hill again. huff, puff. This rock? That's jutting? Take the
bearing, get off the trail, fall down the hill, find the other rock,
take the bearing, clamber over to the... crevice?... here?... there?
Wrong rocks. Scramble back up to the trail and up the hill again.
huff, puff.
This rock? Take the bearing, get off the trail, fall down the hill,
find the other rock, take the bearing, get all scratched by bushes
going over to the... crevice? Wow, there sure are a lot of crevices
here. This one? That one? Wrong rocks. Scramble back up to the trail
and up the hill again. huff, puff.
This rock? Oh look! Over there's a BIG rock at exactly the right
bearing. (duh) Stand on big rock, turn to second bearing, and, wow,
that's a baggie in that crevice right in front of that letterbox
that's SUPPOSED to be behind a rock.
The baggie contained the stamp to the SECOND box. My guess is the
last finders mistakenly took it along with them while walking the
clues backwards, and just left it there. Bad letterboxers!! For
shame!! They should have gone right back down and up those hills!
It's a good thing it all happened today and we were right behind
them. That poor stamp was very exposed.
So, to hasten on to the happy ending, we stamped in and left a HH.
Which means going through the whole 'did I stamp the HH into the
box? box into HH? me into HH? me into box? box into my book? HH into
my book?- no, this is the 2nd time I found it...don't need that
one...' routine. Put the forlorn stamp into my bag. Went BACK down
the hill on the trail, UP the other hill (huff, puff, of course),
enjoyed the view, found the 2nd box after only finding the wrong
pine tree once, stamped in and left the stamp where it belongs.
So I persevered through my huffing and puffing, and put a wrong to
rights. I fear something really humiliating is about to happen to
make me stop patting myself on the back. (Proverbs 16:18)
Aud
