Cicadas

From: Mary from Virginia | Date: 2004-05-21 12:03:07 UTC
I'm certainly glad to hear that someone else loves the cicadas. I
thought I was the only one! The people I work with are so creeped
out by them that they start squealing when they come across one. They
cover their heads and look around nervously when they go outside, as
if the cicadas were divebombers or bats seeking to nest in their
hair. Even talking about cicadas makes them shudder. Every
encounter with a cicada is a near-death experience. So I guess it's
a good thing that this happens only every 17 years. I can stand the
bugs. The people are annoying me.

Mary

Re: Cicadas

From: Mary from Virginia | Date: 2004-05-21 13:39:19 UTC
Well Gillian, you NJ'ers have a lot to look forward to! Don't even
think that the cicadas confine themselves only to trees. Do you have
ivy, flowers, perienniels, weeds? They will be all over those. They
climb the walls of your house. They will climb the telephone poles,
mailbox posts, fire hydrants. Do you park your car in a driveway?
They will cover the tires. Yes, you will be sweeping a path to your
car, or doing what I do, which is to enjoy the crunch of their little
carcasses. Be sure to wipe your feet before you go in the house!

Mary

From: The Doubtful Guests | Date: 2004-05-21 13:52:59 UTC
Night Owl, Cozy, Acorn and I LOVE the red eyed bugs. 
 
They are harmless, clumsy and cute.  We love their song.  We like to pick them up by their wings.  The males will chatter at you.  You can then toss them in the air and watch them fly clumsiliy away. 
 
I haven't had the nerve to fry any up.  I do have one in a jar on my desk at work with a shell and some grass.  A mini terrarium of nature.
 
We are so excited about them we rushed out to Tempus Fugits cicada letterbox as soon as it was posted.  A beautiful stamp.  Hope to collect some more.
 
Scarab of the Doubtful Guests
