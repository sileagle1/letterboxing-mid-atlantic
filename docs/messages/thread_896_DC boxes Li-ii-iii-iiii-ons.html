From: Poison Ivy Hedge | Date: 2005-03-25 12:56:29 UTC
Rogue, how very gallant, you snag them,
and then we can arrange a meet to exchange?

Here are the particulars....

Li-ii-iii-iiii-on, Placed by: Poison Ivy, Washington, DC
Number of boxes: 4

Clues
Just South of the Woodley Park Metro Station, at the corner of
Calvert and Connecticut you are at the North end of the Taft Memorial
Bridge which is beautifully cornered with four lions. When no one is
about and their guardian services are not required, each of the lions
retreat to different bridges.

Down below the bridge is the beginning of a jogging/exercise trail
that roughly follows Rock Creek. From the Taft Bridge, go down to the
paved exercise/jogging trail that is beneath it and follow the path
downstream. You can't actually see the creek from here because of the
parkway, but soon you'll come to a footbridge that allows you to
cross the creek. Don't cross it just yet. When you get to the
footbridge, stay on the righthand side of the creek and follow the
rocky path just a little to a sewer cover. From the sewer cover head
North 50 steps up a minor trail to a big tree. Twelve more steps in
the direction of 260 degrees will bring you to another tree. At the
base, in a hole, under a large triangle shaped rock you'll find the
Lion in Spring.

Return to the footbridge, cross it and turn right, headed down
stream. After a while along this trail, you'll cross the stream again
on another footbridge. At the end of this bridge is a sign for Lyons
Mill where the next lion moonlights. There is also a sign about the
cemetery. You can see it from the path through the razor wire and
fence line. (People are just dying to get in there.) Anyhoo,... 25
steps South up another minor rocky path you'll come to a cool tree
with a split trunk that was filled with rocks. Kinda neat. The tree
stands at the tip of a gully, and just South beyond it is another
tree IN the gully and to the left of THAT is a tree on the hill next
to the gully. Behind that tree under some rocks sits the Liion in
Summer.

Go back to the path and turn right. Follow it under another large
bridge. None of the lions found an appropriate resting place here so
keep going to the next bridge. This bridge is guarded by Buffalos and
Indians. Go past the bridge, turn around and look up. The 10th indian
from the left Looks down at the last stationary rock on your right.
The Liiion in Fall rests there.

Standing on the path beneath the middle of the bridge, count 85 steps
(in the way you were originally heading) and you'll find a rooted
path on the right that leads up to the top of the unadorned and
rather plain P Street Bridge. Go up and carefully cross P Street to
the green metal plaque. Around behind the plaque and a little down
the hill on the left next to the bridge wall lays the Liiiion in
Winter in a hole with rocks.

Now you're at P and 23rd, just a few blocks from the Dupont Circle
Metro at Q and 20th Streets NW.
