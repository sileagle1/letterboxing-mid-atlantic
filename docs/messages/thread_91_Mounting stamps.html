Hi Manda, and welcome to letterboxing!

The reason that most (all) commercial stamps have backing is because
they're made out of very thin rubber and all of the little lines are
raised generally less than 1 mm off of the "carved out" areas. If you
glue them directly to wood, it's very difficult to apply even pressure
to the entire stamp (for anything over 1" square), especially the
middle, and to apply enough pressure to get just the lines and not the
background areas. Trust me :). The cushion makes it all even out.
The alternative to having a cushion on the stamp is to cushion the
paper you're stamping on to - like putting a magazine under it.

In general, most hand carved stamps are from thick, rigid material (the
carving block; plumber's gasket might be the exception), and the cuts
you make are deep enough that you don't get impressions from the bottom
of the carved-out areas (from the little ridges you create in the
carved-out area is another story). You generally shouldn't need to
mount them at all, and if you do, you shouldn't need to worry about a
cushion.

As someone said (I think Nathan), if you think your stamp needs a
cushion, you can use the Fun Foam from the crafts store that's less
than a buck for an 11x17" sheet - and comes in tons of fun colors!

HTH,

break after only a bit of use. Of course, it depends on the material; for
instance, the Speedy-Cut stuff really doesn't hold up very well at all.
