Shoichi Yokoi, a Japanese soldier on the island of Guam in WWII never got his orders to
surrender. He lived in a cave for 26 years, surviving on a diet of nuts, berries, frogs and
rats.

George Donner and 87 men, women and children set out for California from Illinois in
1846. After a brutal journey of over 2500 miles they became trapped in the Sierra
Mountains by heavy snowfall. After eating their cows and horses, they tried eating bark,
twigs and even their own shoes and belts. When the leather ran out, they ate each other.

Ernest Shackleton was trapped on the frozen Antarctic ocean for 15 months. Then he and
his men sailed over 800 miles across brutal seas in open boats to a small mountainous
island. After scaling the mountains and crossing the island Shackleton returned to rescue
his men.

Wussies. All of `em.

They never could have survived
