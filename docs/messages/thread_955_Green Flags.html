From: The Doubtful Guests | Date: 2005-04-11 03:33:09 UTC
Click on the Green Flags.  You can now log your finds on LBNA with the date found, including old dates.  People looking at the clues could see when it was last marked as found.  As a placer you can designate a status of the box.  You see them as long as you are signed in.  It will then count your Founds, etc. on the Member Services page.  There are various options including something about turning it off for your boxes if you wish. 
 
Have fun going through your log and marking.

Here's a good explanation that was posted on the Great Lakes regional board:

From: "SpringChick" <springchick@...>
Date: Wed Apr 6, 2005 11:24 am
Subject: Re: [LbGLK] Woa, what's going on at letterboxing.org?


The latest upgrade to letterboxing.org added status logging. As a finder you
now have the ability to log the boxes you have found (found or attempted),
and
as a planter you have the ability to log the status (i.e. condition) of a
box
you have planted. To log a find or plant status, you click on the green
flag.
For boxes whose clues are hosted at letterboxing.org, the little green flag
is
found on the clue page. For boxes that automatically redirect to an
alternate
site for clues, the flag is found next to the box name in the region list.

When a box has comments (i.e. somebody has logged a find), the status
comments
will display under the flag -- it will show the name of the person who last
recorded a find OR attempt and the date. If you do not wish for these status
notes to show up for the boxes you have planted you can disable this by
going to
the Member Services page (it is at the bottom of the page). Even if you
disable
status for the boxes you have planted, you can still log your finds and make
status notes on your own boxes -- these will not show up anywhere if you
have
status disabled (I think the default is enabled).

The state maps are also being updated and some new search features will also
be
added as part of these upgrades. Most of the other changes are behind the
scenes and will hopefully make the site more stable and reliable.

SpringChick
