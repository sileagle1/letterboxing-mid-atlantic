From: Silent Doug | Date: 2005-04-19 18:50:51 UTC
As you may know, I carved a whole bunch of stamps based on the tiles
on the floor of the PA State Capitol in Harrisburg. The tiles were
made by Henry Chapman Mercer's famous tileworks in Doylestown.
Unfortunately, I only got to plant a few of them before we moved to
CT. Since it doesn't make any sense to plant these stamps outside of
PA, and we're not going to get back there often enough to plant 9 more
letterboxes, I was wondering if anyone
would each be interested in planting one of these boxes in the state
as a team effort with Jennifer and me. We've already carved the
stamps, made the logbooks, and put it all together in a letterbox --
if you'd like to volunteer, we'll send one to you, you can plant it
and then write the clues for it.

I did plant three in SE PA, so it would be great if some of them could
get planted elsewhere in the state.

Anyone interested?

Doug
